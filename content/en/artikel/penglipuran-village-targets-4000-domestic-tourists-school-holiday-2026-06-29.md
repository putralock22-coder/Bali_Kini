Penglipuran Village Tourism in Bangli Regency is entering the school holiday season with an ambitious goal: welcoming between 3,000 and 4,000 visitors per day. The managers of this globally recognized traditional village — renowned as one of the cleanest villages in the world — are focusing specifically on domestic tourists, who become the dominant market segment during Indonesia's school break period.

## Domestic Visitors Take Center Stage

During international high season, Penglipuran typically draws large numbers of foreign tourists, particularly from Australia, India, and Europe. But as the national school holiday kicks off — running from late June through mid-July 2026 — the visitor profile shifts markedly. Families from Java's largest cities, including Jakarta, Surabaya, Bandung, and Yogyakarta, are expected to make up the majority of arrivals.

Village management noted that in previous years, the school holiday period reliably generated a sharp uptick in domestic visitors. For 2026, organizers are optimistic the trend will repeat itself, buoyed by improved transport connectivity and growing public awareness of cultural heritage destinations as quality travel options.

## Special Family Holiday Packages

To prepare for the expected surge, Penglipuran has curated a range of family-focused tourism packages. Highlights include:

- **Guided village walks** led by local guides who explain the philosophy of the Bali Aga community and the distinctive architecture of traditional Penglipuran homes
- **Cultural workshops** such as crafting miniature penjor ceremonial poles, janur (young coconut leaf) arrangements, and traditional Balinese handicrafts
- **Gamelan and Balinese dance performances** staged in the village's open-air areas
- **Local culinary experiences** featuring traditional snacks and signature beverages prepared by village residents

These packages are designed to encourage visitors to do more than just arrive, snap photos, and leave — instead, they are invited to genuinely immerse themselves in the daily rhythms of Penglipuran's richly traditional community life.

## What Makes Penglipuran Unique

Penglipuran is widely considered one of the most orderly and pristine traditional villages in Indonesia, frequently cited by international media as among the cleanest villages in the world. Uniform ancestral homes with bamboo-woven courtyard gates, meticulously maintained stone pathways, and a ban on motorized vehicles within the core village area create an atmosphere unlike anywhere else in Bali.

### Community-Driven Tourism

Penglipuran stands as a success story in sustainable village tourism — proof that cultural heritage and economic benefit can coexist without one undermining the other. Tourism income is managed collectively by the village community, ensuring that benefits are distributed equitably among all residents.

The village head has emphasized that while visitor comfort remains a priority, the community is equally committed to preserving its cultural identity. Visitors are asked to respect local customary rules, including wearing appropriate attire in sacred areas.

## Getting There from Denpasar

For first-time visitors, Penglipuran is approximately a 1.5 to 2-hour drive from the Kuta and Seminyak areas, accessible by private or rental vehicle. Perched on a hillside in Bangli Subdistrict, the village enjoys noticeably cooler air than Bali's southern coastal areas.

Village management recommends arriving early in the morning to enjoy a quieter atmosphere before peak visiting hours, which typically run from 10:00 a.m. to 3:00 p.m. local time.

With thousands of expected daily visitors and a refreshed lineup of cultural attractions, Penglipuran Village is shaping up to be one of Bali's most compelling domestic tourism magnets throughout the 2026 school holiday season.
