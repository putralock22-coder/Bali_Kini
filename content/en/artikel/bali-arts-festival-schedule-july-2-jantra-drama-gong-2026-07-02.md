The 48th Bali Arts Festival (Pesta Kesenian Bali / PKB XLVIII) 2026 rolls into its 20th day on Thursday, July 2, 2026. From dawn to dusk, residents and visitors alike are invited to experience a rich lineup of traditional arts performances, competitive games, and music workshops spread across iconic venues in the Art Center area of Denpasar.

## Morning: Traditional Sports at Renon Field

The day opens at **7:30 AM WITA** with **Jantra Tradisi Bali — Kelik-kelikan Demonstration** at the East Field of the Monument of the People's Struggle (Monumen Perjuangan Rakyat Bali), Renon. Kelik-kelikan is a rare traditional agility game that showcases physical dexterity—a living heritage piece being revived through PKB's platform.

At **8:00 AM**, the same venue hosts the **Traditional Sports Competition (Pacentokan)**, featuring three events: Matajog (Men's), Terompah (Women's), and Hadang (Men's). These disciplines blend competition with community spirit, preserving ancestral Balinese athletic traditions for younger generations.

## Afternoon: Gamelan Workshop and Nusantara Performances

At **10:00 AM**, Kalangan Angsoka hosts a **Gamelan Gambang Workshop (Kriyaloka)**. Gamelan Gambang is one of Bali's oldest and most sacred musical ensembles—mastered by very few artists today—making this workshop a critical session for preserving this rare art form.

At **11:00 AM**, Kalangan Ayodya welcomes a **Performance of Music and Dance from Indramayu and Cirebon** by Sanggar Seni Nusantara from West Java. Their presence underlines PKB's spirit of national unity—celebrating the diversity of Indonesian culture on Bali's grandest cultural stage.

## International Spotlight: Korea at the Bali Stage

At **2:00 PM**, Kalangan Ratna Kanda hosts the **Bali World Culture Celebration (BWCC)** featuring a **Traditional Bongsan Talcuhm Performance** from the Seoul Institute of the Arts, South Korea. This traditional Korean mask dance brings a cross-cultural dimension that reinforces PKB's identity as a world-class cultural event—not just a local festival.

## Evening Highlight: Drama Gong Grenggeng Maya

The most anticipated event of the night is the **Drama Gong Grenggeng Maya Performance** by Paguyuban Peduli Seni Drama Gong Lawas Padsmagol from Kesiman Kertalangu Village, East Denpasar. The show begins at **7:30 PM WITA** at the Open Stage Ardha Candra.

Drama Gong is Bali's traditional musical theater—a captivating fusion of spoken dialogue, dance, and gamelan accompaniment. The "Grenggeng Maya" story carries timeless themes of courage, wisdom, and sacrifice—values that resonate across generations and cultures.

### About PKB XLVIII 2026

The 2026 Bali Arts Festival runs from **June 13 to July 11**, under the theme **"Atma Kerthi: Jiwa Sidha Parisudha"**—a reflection on the purification and elevation of the human soul toward its highest, most luminous form. More than 955 participants from across Indonesia—including delegations from Jakarta, East Java, Yogyakarta, and Central Kalimantan—are showcasing their regional arts and cultures throughout the festival.

For visitors in Denpasar tonight, the Drama Gong performance at Ardha Candra Stage is unmissable.
