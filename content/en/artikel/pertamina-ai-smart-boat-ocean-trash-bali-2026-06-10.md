Indonesian state energy company **PT Pertamina (Persero)** officially launched an AI-powered autonomous ocean trash-cleaning vessel at **Pantai Sekeh, Badung, Bali** on Wednesday, June 10, 2026, in conjunction with World Environment Day celebrations. The innovative boat — named the **Autonomous Trash Skimmer** — was unveiled by Pertamina's Board Chairman **Mochamad Iriawan (Iwan Bule)** as part of the company's Corporate Social and Environmental Responsibility (TJSL) program.

## Cutting-Edge Technology Meets Ocean Conservation

The eight-meter-long boat with a catamaran dual-hull design was co-developed with the **Institut Teknologi Sepuluh Nopember (ITS)** in Surabaya. Unlike conventional cleaning vessels, the Autonomous Trash Skimmer integrates an advanced suite of technologies that allow largely independent operation without requiring a full crew at all times.

Ultrasonic sensors, high-resolution cameras, GPS, and artificial intelligence work in tandem to **detect and identify floating waste** in real time. The collected data is then processed to determine the most efficient trash collection routes, reducing energy waste and operational downtime.

### Powered by Renewable Energy

The irony of using fossil fuels to clean the ocean was deliberately avoided in this design. The vessel relies on an **electric motor supported by a hybrid system** combined with solar panels as a renewable energy source. This approach makes operations low-emission and far more environmentally friendly than conventional diesel-powered alternatives.

At the center of the boat sits an **automated trash collection net system**, paired with a plastic shredding machine to support on-site recycling. An electric pulley with a 500-kilogram lifting capacity makes it easy to haul larger debris from the water's surface onto the deck.

## Coastal Waste Reduction Targets

During the initial operational phase, Pertamina has set a target of **reducing coastal waste by approximately 1 ton per year** in the operational area around The Patra Bali Resort & Villas and the Ngurah Rai area of Badung. This figure was established following initial surveys of waste conditions in the coastal waters of the target zones.

The same program will also be replicated beyond Bali. At the TBBM Kotabaru facility in Kalimantan — specifically Semayap Village and Rampa Village — the waste reduction target is set at a far more ambitious **20 tons per year**, reflecting the higher waste generation potential in that region.

## Strategic Importance for Bali's Tourism Industry

The launch of this smart vessel carries strategic significance that extends well beyond a simple cleanup operation. As a world-class tourism destination, Bali faces mounting pressure from its marine waste problem — from land-sourced plastic carried to sea and from marine tourism activities alike.

Ocean debris not only devastates coral reef ecosystems and marine biodiversity, but also directly diminishes the visual appeal of Bali's beaches — the primary draw for millions of international visitors each year. AI-powered tools like the Autonomous Trash Skimmer enable more consistent and efficient waste management without total dependence on manual labor.

### Industry-Academia Collaboration

The partnership between Pertamina and ITS stands as a concrete example of meaningful **collaboration between major industry players and academia** in producing innovative, real-world solutions. The prototype vessel was developed through years of laboratory research at ITS before undergoing trials in actual coastal waters.

Iwan Bule stated that Pertamina is committed to scaling up similar innovations across all company operational areas as part of its long-term sustainability agenda. "This is an investment in the future of our marine environment," he said during the Pantai Sekeh inauguration ceremony.

## Aligning TJSL with Sustainable Development Goals

Pertamina's TJSL program that houses this project directly aligns with the company's commitments to the **Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)**, particularly SDG 14 on Life Below Water. Pertamina places coastal ecosystem preservation as a top priority across all operational areas adjacent to the sea.

Going forward, Pertamina is exploring opportunities to replicate the Autonomous Trash Skimmer technology at additional locations facing similar marine waste challenges — from Java and Kalimantan to other Indonesian islands facing escalating plastic pollution in their coastal waters.
