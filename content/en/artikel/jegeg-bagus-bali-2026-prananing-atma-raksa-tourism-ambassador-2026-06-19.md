Nine pairs of finalists representing every regency and city in Bali were officially introduced to the public at the prestigious Jegeg Bagus Bali 2026 selection event. The launch ceremony — a Press Conference and Sashing Ceremony — was held at the Amphitheater of Living World Denpasar on Friday, June 19, 2026.

## A Theme Rooted in Balance and Soul

This year's selection carries the theme **"Prananing Atma Raksa"**, a Balinese-Sanskrit phrase that translates roughly to "Guardianship of the Breath of Life" or, more poetically, *The Sanctuary of the Living Soul*. The theme underscores the importance of maintaining harmony between the outer and inner dimensions of life — a philosophy that has long been central to Balinese culture.

By adopting this theme, the competition signals its intent to go beyond surface-level representation. Finalists are expected not only to embody grace and poise, but to genuinely carry and articulate the values of Balinese cultural heritage in a rapidly modernizing world.

## Nine Pairs, Nine Regencies

The nine finalist pairs were selected through a rigorous screening process and represent Bali's nine regencies and cities: Denpasar, Badung, Gianyar, Tabanan, Bangli, Klungkung, Karangasem, Buleleng, and Jembrana.

The Head of Bali's Provincial Tourism Office emphasized that the selection is a key pillar in the province's strategy to develop cultural, quality, and dignified tourism. Winners of the Jegeg and Bagus Bali titles will be expected to actively support government tourism promotion efforts and advocate for Balinese cultural preservation both nationally and internationally.

## Advocacy Programs That Address Real Issues

One of the most distinctive features of Jegeg Bagus Bali 2026 is that each finalist must present a concrete advocacy program addressing a specific challenge facing Bali today. The range of topics presented reflects the island's diverse social and economic pressures:

- **UMKM digitalization** — helping small and micro businesses leverage digital platforms to reach wider markets.
- **Empowerment of female artisans** — supporting economic independence among women in Bali's craft and art sectors.
- **Cultural preservation** — safeguarding Bali's traditional arts and performance practices from fading into obscurity.
- **Source-based waste management** — building community and tourist awareness around responsible waste disposal.
- **Tourism village development** — unlocking the potential of local communities as alternative, sustainable tourism destinations.
- **Creative economy strengthening** — building a thriving creative industry ecosystem rooted in Balinese local wisdom.

These programs demonstrate that Jegeg Bagus Bali is far more than a beauty pageant — it is a platform for critically minded young Balinese who are serious about contributing to their island's future.

## Grand Final Set for August 7 at Taman Budaya Bali

The entire selection process culminates in the **Grand Final, scheduled for August 7, 2026, at Taman Budaya Bali (the Art Center) in Denpasar**. That night, the next Jegeg and Bagus Bali will be crowned and will begin their role as the face of Balinese tourism.

Before reaching the Grand Final, all finalists will undergo training sessions covering personality development, cultural immersion, and public communication skills — ensuring that the eventual winners are truly prepared to represent Bali with confidence and responsibility.

## Bali Needs Ambassadors Who Are More Than a Face

As Bali's tourism continues its strategic shift from mass tourism toward quality and sustainability, having strong, well-rounded tourism ambassadors becomes increasingly important. The Jegeg and Bagus Bali are not merely promotional mascots — they are expected to serve as agents of change who can educate visitors about the ethics of responsible travel and the cultural norms that must be respected on the island.

In choosing "Prananing Atma Raksa" as this year's theme, the organizers have made clear that the future of Bali's tourism rests on a foundation of cultural integrity — a heritage that must be actively guarded and passed on by the next generation.
