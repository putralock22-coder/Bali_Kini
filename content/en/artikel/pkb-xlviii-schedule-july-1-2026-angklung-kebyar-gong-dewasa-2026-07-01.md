The 48th Pesta Kesenian Bali (PKB) — Bali's grand annual arts festival — continues its vibrant run on Wednesday, July 1, 2026. Today's program at Taman Budaya Bali (Bali Cultural Park), also known as Art Center Denpasar, showcases two highly anticipated performances: the Angklung Kebyar Parade and the Adult Gong Kebyar Competition.

## Angklung Kebyar Parade Kicks Off the Afternoon

The afternoon session begins at 3:00 PM local time with the **Utsawa (Parade) Angklung Kebyar Khas**, where two distinguished cultural representatives will go head-to-head on stage. **Sekaa Angklung Kerthiyasa** represents Denpasar City, while **Sanggar Seni Taksu Murti Kemanisan** carries the banner of Badung Regency.

Angklung Kebyar is a dynamic form of Balinese gamelan ensemble characterized by energetic rhythms and intricate melodic interplay. Each region across Bali has developed its own distinct style, making these parades a fascinating showcase of local artistic identity.

## Adult Gong Kebyar Closes the Day

The evening's highlight is the **Utsawa Gong Kebyar Dewasa** (Adult Gong Kebyar Parade), featuring **Sekaa Gong Tirta Nirmala Hulundanu Batur** — a gamelan ensemble hailing from the Batur caldera area of Bangli. The adult category is considered the most prestigious division in PKB gamelan competitions, demanding technical mastery, group synchronization, and expressive depth.

## About PKB 2026

PKB XLVIII 2026 runs from June 13 to July 11, 2026 under the theme **Atma Kerthi: Loka Bali Bhuwana**, meaning "the spiritual breath that sustains the world of Bali." The festival brings together over **23,000 artists** from all cities and regencies across the island, making it one of the largest performing arts festivals in Southeast Asia.

Entry to PKB performances at Taman Budaya Bali is free and open to the public. Visitors are encouraged to arrive early to secure seating, as popular events regularly draw large crowds of both locals and international visitors.
