A foreign national is suspected of stealing more than Rp 1.1 million in cash from a minimarket in Badung Regency, Bali, using a deceptive technique to distract the cashier. The incident was captured on CCTV and is now under active police investigation.

## The Incident in Desa Munggu

The theft occurred on Tuesday (June 16, 2026) at around 6:40 PM local time at a minimarket in Banjar Pempatan, Desa Munggu, Mengwi District, Badung Regency.

The suspect — whose identity has not yet been determined — entered the store appearing to be a regular customer. He selected two ice creams and approached the cashier, handing over a Rp 100,000 bill to pay for his purchases.

## The Deception Method

During the transaction, the suspect allegedly exploited the cashier's momentary distraction to reach into or manipulate the cash register, removing approximately Rp 1.1 million in the process.

Store CCTV cameras recorded the entire sequence of events, from the suspect's entry into the store to the moment of the alleged theft. This footage was immediately handed over to police as the primary evidence for identifying the perpetrator.

### Police Investigating

Law enforcement officers visited the scene to collect witness statements and review security footage. As of the latest reports, the suspect's identity remains unknown and investigators are working to trace him through the CCTV recordings.

Police have urged anyone with information about the suspect to come forward to the nearest police station.

## A Pattern of Foreigner Misconduct in Bali

This incident is the latest in a series of cases involving foreign nationals committing crimes in Bali throughout 2026. Earlier cases this year included the theft of donation money from a temple, petty theft at various tourist spots, and a hypnosis-based scam targeting market vendors.

### Bali's Crackdown on Bad Tourists

The Bali Provincial Government and immigration authorities have stepped up enforcement against foreign nationals who violate Indonesian law. Those convicted of criminal offenses face deportation and permanent entry bans to Indonesia.

Governor Wayan Koster has consistently emphasized that Bali welcomes quality tourists who respect local laws and Balinese culture. The island's status as a world-class destination depends on maintaining safety and order for both residents and visitors alike.

Retail business owners across Bali are being reminded to stay vigilant and ensure proper cash-handling procedures to avoid becoming targets of similar schemes.
