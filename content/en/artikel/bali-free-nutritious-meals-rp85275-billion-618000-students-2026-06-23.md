## Bali's MBG Spending Hits Rp852.75 Billion in First Half of 2026

President Prabowo Subianto's flagship Free Nutritious Meals (Makan Bergizi Gratis/MBG) program has recorded remarkable progress in Bali. As of **June 23, 2026**, total MBG spending in Bali Province from **January through June 2026** reached **Rp852.75 billion**, disbursed through **277 Nutrition Service Units (SPPG)** spread across all regencies and cities on the island.

This achievement marks a significant milestone in the government's effort to improve nutritional quality among Bali's younger generation and strengthen the foundation of human resources for long-term development.

## 618,390 Students Benefit from the Program

The realized funds have enabled MBG in Bali to serve a total of **618,390 beneficiaries** across various educational levels. Elementary school (SD) students make up the largest group with **248,198 recipients**, followed by:

- **Junior high school (SMP) students**: 137,036
- **Senior high school / vocational school students**: 105,157
- **Kindergarten (TK) students**: 38,562
- **Early childhood education (PAUD) students**: 2,985
- **Teachers and education staff**: 1,244

This distribution shows that MBG in Bali extends across the full spectrum of basic and secondary education, while also considering the welfare of educators.

## SPPG Distribution: Denpasar Leads the Way

Of the 277 active SPPG units in Bali, their distribution reflects each region's population density and number of educational institutions. **Denpasar City** tops the list with **49 SPPG units**, followed by:

| Regency/City | SPPG Units |
|---|---|
| Denpasar | 49 |
| Buleleng | 46 |
| Tabanan | 38 |
| Gianyar | 32 |
| Badung | 31 |
| Jembrana | 31 |
| Karangasem | 25 |
| Bangli | 13 |
| Klungkung | 12 |
| **Total** | **277** |

Buleleng, Bali's largest regency by area, ranks second with 46 SPPG units, reflecting the high demand for nutrition services across its large student population in northern Bali.

## Environmental Integration: Zero-Waste Kitchens

Beyond ensuring nutritional quality, MBG operations in Bali have also incorporated environmental considerations. Several SPPG units have adopted eco-friendly waste management practices within their kitchen operations, aligning with the Bali Provincial Government's commitment to environmental sustainability.

This integration is particularly significant given Bali's strict waste regulations, which will be further reinforced through mandatory waste sorting requirements starting July 1, 2026.

## Challenges and the Road Ahead

While budget realization continues to grow, the MBG program still faces challenges in ensuring equitable service in remote areas, particularly in parts of Karangasem, Bangli, and Klungkung, where SPPG coverage remains relatively limited relative to the number of schools in those areas.

Going forward, the government is expected to continue expanding SPPG capacity and maintain high standards for nutritious, halal, and hygienic food for all beneficiaries in Bali. With Rp852.75 billion spent in just the first half of 2026, the program demonstrates the government's tangible commitment to building Indonesia's Golden Generation 2045, starting from the classroom.
