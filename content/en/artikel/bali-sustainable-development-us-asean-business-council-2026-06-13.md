The Bali Provincial Government presented its culture, environment, and economy-based sustainable development model to international business leaders at the **US-ASEAN Business Council Executive Briefing 2026** in Kuta, Badung. The forum was held under the theme *"Impact of Innovation on Bali's Economic Development: A Showcase of U.S. Companies Initiative."*

## A Century-Long Development Blueprint

Representing Governor Wayan Koster, **Luh Ayu Aryani** presented Bali's long-term development vision enshrined in **Bali Provincial Regulation No. 4 of 2023** — the 100-Year Future Development Guide for the New Era of Bali (2025–2125). This strategic document forms the foundation for all of Bali's development policies for the coming century.

The vision rests on three mutually reinforcing pillars: culture, environment, and economy. Rather than pursuing economic growth in isolation, Bali ensures that every development step aligns with cultural preservation and environmental sustainability — a model that has attracted growing international attention.

## American Companies' Contributions to Bali

The US-ASEAN Business Council highlighted the innovations and initiatives of American companies contributing to Bali's economic development across four key areas:

- **Human resource development** through training programs and local workforce capacity building
- **Environmental sustainability** via green technology and eco-friendly business practices
- **Tourism sector development** through investment in internationally standardized facilities and services
- **Digital transformation** to improve efficiency and competitiveness of the local economy

American companies operating in Bali contribute not only through direct investment but also through knowledge transfer, technology, and governance standards that raise the quality of local industries.

## Bali as a Model for Indonesia's Regions

The USABC Indonesia Chief Country Representative stated that best practices developing in Bali are expected to inspire other regions across Indonesia to build more inclusive and sustainable economies. USABC reaffirmed its commitment to supporting public-private sector synergies in realizing this vision.

The forum also served as a platform for Bali to expand its international investment network, particularly with American companies holding strategic interests in infrastructure, tourism, and digital economy development across ASEAN.

## Economic Resilience Amid Global Uncertainty

Despite global geopolitical turbulence, Bali remains an attractive investment destination thanks to government incentives, distinctive cultural identity, and a world-class tourism track record. Bank Indonesia projects Bali's economic growth in 2026 at **5.4–5.9 percent**, among the highest in Indonesia.

Bali's strategy of merging local values with international standards makes it a uniquely relevant model — not only for other Indonesian regions, but also for developing nations seeking to balance economic growth with the preservation of cultural identity.
