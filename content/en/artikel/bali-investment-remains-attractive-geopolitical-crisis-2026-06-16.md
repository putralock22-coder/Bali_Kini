Despite mounting global uncertainty driven by geopolitical conflicts, Bali's investment climate is holding firm. **Bank Indonesia (BI) Representative Office for Bali Province** has confirmed that the island continues to attract strong investor interest, backed by government incentive packages and a robust pipeline of infrastructure projects.

"Investment performance in Bali keeps strengthening amid global uncertainty," said **Achris Sarwani**, Head of BI's Bali representative office, speaking in Denpasar in June 2026.

## Tourism Numbers Bolster Investor Confidence

One of the clearest signals of Bali's economic resilience is the tourism data. Foreign visitor arrivals through Ngurah Rai Airport in the January-April 2026 period reached approximately **4 million people** — a jump of around **10 percent** compared to the same period in 2025. This robust visitor growth reinforces Bali's standing as one of Asia's most sought-after destinations and makes the island a reliable bet for tourism-related investment.

## Economic Growth Outlook

BI Bali projects the island's economy will grow between **5.4 and 5.9 percent** in 2026, even as geopolitical headwinds slow growth in some sectors. Governor **Wayan Koster** has set an even more ambitious target of above **6.1 percent**, aiming to diversify the economy beyond its traditional reliance on tourism.

For context, Bali's economy grew **5.58 percent year-on-year** in Q1 2026, supported by holiday spending during Nyepi, Eid al-Fitr, and the Chinese New Year, plus a steady uptick in foreign tourist arrivals.

Looking ahead, BI Bali projects that **Q2 2026 growth will be driven** by school holiday season (generating significant domestic tourism), the agricultural harvest period, and government infrastructure investment.

## 22 Investment Opportunities in the Pipeline

Together with the Bali Provincial Government, BI has compiled an **investment catalogue** covering **22 potential projects**. Four infrastructure projects have been designated as 2026 regional priorities:

| Project | Location | Purpose |
|---------|----------|---------|
| Parking Building, Ulun Danu Batur Temple | Bangli | Visitor management at sacred site |
| Raw Water Supply System, Tukad Unda Reservoir | Klungkung | Water security |
| Nusa Ceningan–Nusa Lembongan Bridge | Klungkung | Island connectivity |
| Jimbaran Underpass | Badung | Traffic decongestion |

The combined value of these four priority infrastructure projects is estimated at **Rp788 billion** (approximately USD 48 million).

Beyond infrastructure, the catalogue also features **Sanur Health Special Economic Zone (KEK)**, smart street lighting upgrades, and an **electric vehicle (EV) procurement program** as part of Bali's clean energy transition.

## Pitching to the World: Bali Jagadhita VII

During the **Bali Jagadhita VII 2026** economic forum, BI Bali facilitated direct meetings between the owners of **21 investment projects** and **6 ambassadors** from friendly nations, along with **35 other potential investors**. The event was designed to translate Bali's investment pipeline into concrete deal flow and foreign direct investment.

BI Bali has also made **green economy** a central pillar of the Jagadhita forum, promoting sustainable investment models that align Bali's growth ambitions with environmental stewardship — a positioning that resonates strongly with ESG-conscious global investors.

## Central Government Support

The central government has reinforced investor confidence through targeted incentives for the aviation and tourism sectors, including:
- **Airfare discounts on economy class** with VAT covered by the government
- **Zero percent import duties** on aircraft spare parts
- Streamlined licensing for new tourism businesses

The combination of Bali's inherent appeal as a global destination, a well-structured project pipeline, and meaningful government support creates what BI Bali describes as an investment environment that is resilient, attractive, and future-ready — even in turbulent times.
