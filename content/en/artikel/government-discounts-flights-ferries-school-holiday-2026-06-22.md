As Indonesia's school holiday season kicks off in late June 2026, the government has launched a comprehensive transportation discount package covering flights, trains, passenger ferries, and sea crossings. The program is designed to ease the financial burden on families traveling during the peak season — including the millions heading to Bali.

## 100% VAT Relief on Economy Flights

The standout benefit in this package is a **full waiver of Value Added Tax (VAT) on domestic economy-class airfare**, effective from **June 24 to July 5, 2026**. This policy, formalized through **Ministerial Regulation (PMK) No. 24 of 2026** issued on April 21, 2026, works through a mechanism called *PPN Ditanggung Pemerintah* (VAT Borne by Government, or PPN DTP).

Under this scheme, the government absorbs the VAT portion of the base fare and fuel surcharge for all domestic economy tickets. Passengers booking through airline apps or travel aggregators will see prices that no longer include VAT — no discount code required.

**Important note:** Additional components such as extra baggage and seat selection remain subject to regular VAT.

## 30% Off Train and Ship Tickets

Beyond aviation, the government also offers:

- **30% discount** on economy-class train tickets for departures between **June 20 and July 5, 2026**
- **30% discount** on economy-class passenger ship tickets, valid from **June 20 through August 15, 2026**

The extended validity for sea travel reflects the slower pace of ferry tourism compared to flights, giving more flexibility to budget travelers exploring eastern Indonesia.

## Bali-Specific Ferry Routes Included

Several sea crossing routes directly relevant to Bali and Lombok travel are included in the discount:

- **Ketapang–Gilimanuk** (the main Java–Bali crossing)
- **Lembar–Padangbai** (Lombok–Bali)
- Merak–Bakauheni, Kayangan–Pototano, and several eastern Indonesia routes

The Ketapang–Gilimanuk crossing in particular is expected to see a significant surge in vehicle and passenger traffic as families from East Java head to Bali for the school break.

## Why This Matters for Bali

Earlier in June 2026, airfares to Bali had spiked sharply due to high holiday demand — a recurring issue that frustrates domestic travelers. This VAT relief program brings prices closer to an accessible range for middle-income families who make up the bulk of Bali's domestic visitor base.

Bank Indonesia had already projected Bali's economy to grow positively in Q2 2026, largely driven by school holiday tourism. The transport discount program provides additional stimulus, potentially pushing hotel occupancy rates and UMKM revenues even higher across the island.

## Safety Inspections Also Underway

Concurrent with the holiday discounts, the government has launched **safety inspections (rampcheck) at passenger terminals and bus pools** from June 22 to July 12, 2026. The goal is to ensure all commercial vehicles operating during the peak season meet minimum safety standards.

## How to Get the Discount

Travelers can book directly through airline websites or major travel apps — the VAT relief is automatically applied at checkout for qualifying economy domestic tickets. For ferry crossings, the discount is available at official ASDP Indonesia Ferry counters and authorized partners.
