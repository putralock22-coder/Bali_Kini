Another incident involving a foreign national has put the spotlight back on tourist behaviour in Bali. An Australian woman identified by her initials **ALB**, aged 55, was taken into police custody in the early hours of Wednesday, July 1, 2026, after causing a loud and disruptive scene at a hotel in Seminyak.

## What Happened

The disturbance broke out at approximately **4:35 AM local time (WITA)** at **Casa Dasa Legian Hotel** on Jalan Nakula, Seminyak, Kuta, Badung. A local resident named **Hari Sedana** called the emergency hotline **110** to report that a foreign woman was screaming uncontrollably in the hotel lobby.

Police and civil enforcement officers (Satpol PP) were immediately dispatched to the scene. Upon arrival, they found ALB visibly intoxicated — believed to have consumed a large amount of alcohol — and still agitated. She was promptly detained to restore order and prevent the situation from escalating further.

## Guests Disrupted in the Middle of the Night

The late-night commotion caused significant disturbance to other guests who were asleep. Hotel staff were unable to manage the situation on their own, prompting the call to authorities. Witnesses described the woman as shouting loudly in the lobby, making it impossible for other guests to rest.

## Social Media Backlash

The incident quickly spread across Indonesian social media platforms and attracted a wave of pointed commentary. One widely shared reaction read:

> *"She must have run out of money — just deport her."*

The remark reflects a broader frustration among Balinese residents and online observers who feel that some foreign visitors treat Bali as a destination where normal social rules do not apply.

## A Recurring Pattern

This incident fits into a troubling trend of foreign national misconduct in the Kuta-Seminyak-Legian corridor — one of Bali's most densely packed entertainment districts. Recent months have seen a string of similar cases, from a physical altercation in Canggu to a shoplifting incident captured on CCTV in Badung, and numerous documented cases of public drunkenness and disrespectful behaviour at sacred sites.

Bali's provincial government and law enforcement agencies have been stepping up surveillance in known nightlife areas. The island's **tourism levy of Rp150,000** imposed on all international arrivals — introduced in early 2024 — was designed in part to signal that Bali expects visitors who contribute positively to the island, not those who create problems for residents.

## Legal Process

As of the time of reporting, ALB remained in police custody for questioning. Authorities will determine the appropriate course of action following her examination, which may include administrative sanctions or deportation proceedings if immigration violations are identified.
