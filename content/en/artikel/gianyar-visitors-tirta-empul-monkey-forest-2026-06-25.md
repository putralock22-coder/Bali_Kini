Two iconic destinations in Gianyar Regency — **Tirta Empul** in Tampaksiring and **Monkey Forest Ubud** — have once again demonstrated their timeless appeal. Through the end of May 2026, both sites topped Gianyar's most-visited list, as the regency recorded a combined total of **1.22 million tourist visits** in the first five months of the year.

## The Numbers: Tirta Empul Leads the Count

According to data from the Gianyar Tourism Office, **Tirta Empul** registered **274,364 visits** through the end of May 2026, making it the single most-visited destination in the entire regency. The sacred spring temple — where visitors can witness and participate in the melukat purification ritual using water flowing from 30 holy fountains — continues to draw tourists seeking an authentic spiritual and cultural experience.

In second place, **Monkey Forest Ubud** (officially known as Taman Wisata Alam Wenara Wana) recorded **255,249 visits** during the same period. The 12.5-hectare urban forest home to more than 700 long-tailed macaques remains a near-mandatory stop for virtually every visitor passing through Ubud.

### Declining Compared to Last Year

Despite these impressive absolute numbers, the overall total of 1.22 million visits is **notably below the same period in 2025**, which saw 1.69 million tourists — a drop of roughly 28 percent. This mirrors a broader trend playing out across Bali, where global economic uncertainty and geopolitical tensions are cited as the primary contributing factors.

The Head of Gianyar's Tourism Office noted that while visitor numbers have declined, **tourist interest in Gianyar remains strong**. The shortfall is largely attributed to external factors beyond the regency's control rather than any diminishment of destination appeal.

## Ambitious 5-Million Visitor Target for 2026

Despite the softer first-half performance, Gianyar's local government is maintaining its ambitious **full-year target of five million visitor arrivals** in 2026 — a higher bar than was achieved in prior years, and one that will require a meaningful acceleration in the second half of the year.

**July is historically Bali's peak international season**, when visitors from Australia, Europe, and the Americas descend on the island in large numbers. This high-season momentum is expected to compress the gap between current figures and the annual target.

### Strategies to Boost Arrivals

Multiple strategies are in play to accelerate achievement of the annual target:

- **Intensified digital marketing** across social media and international travel platforms
- **Strengthened cultural tourism packages** integrating Tirta Empul, Ubud, and surrounding arts villages
- **New attraction development** beyond the Ubud core to distribute visitor flows more evenly
- **Collaboration with the hospitality industry** on exclusive Gianyar overnight packages

## What Makes These Destinations Irreplaceable

Tirta Empul and Monkey Forest have not maintained their top positions by default. Each offers a genuinely distinctive experience that is extraordinarily difficult for any other destination to replicate.

**Tirta Empul**, built in 962 CE during the reign of the Warmadewa Dynasty, offers an authentic spiritual experience that blends the beauty of traditional Balinese temple architecture with living religious practice. Visitors are not passive observers — they are invited to participate in the melukat ritual, immersing themselves in water that Balinese Hindus believe has been blessed by the god Wisnu.

**Monkey Forest**, meanwhile, delivers something entirely different: a wildly unpredictable, laugh-inducing encounter with Bali's natural world right in the heart of Ubud. The three monkey colonies that inhabit the forest — Kulon, Tengah, and Kangin — ensure that no two visits are ever quite the same.

## Ubud: Bali's Most Complete Tourism Ecosystem

Gianyar's resilience as a tourism destination is underpinned by **Ubud's unrivaled position as Southeast Asia's deepest cultural travel hub**. Within walking distance or a short drive of Ubud's center, visitors can access world-class art galleries, farm-to-table restaurants, Balinese cooking classes, daily Kecak and Legong dance performances, traditional spas, and breathtaking rice terrace treks.

As mass-market beach destinations around the region struggle to differentiate themselves, Ubud continues to deepen its advantage as a place where culture, nature, and spirituality intersect in a way no other destination on earth quite manages — an edge that will not be easily eroded in the years ahead.
