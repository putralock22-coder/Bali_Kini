Bali Governor Wayan Koster officially launched the 48th Pesta Kesenian Bali (PKB) — the island's largest annual arts festival — on June 13, 2026 at the Taman Budaya Art Center in Denpasar. This year's edition brings together an unprecedented **23,936 artists** from **307 sekaa (troupes), studios, and arts foundations** representing local, national, and international communities.

## Theme: Honoring the Wholesome Soul

PKB XLVIII carries the theme **"Atma Kerthi: Jiwa Saking Parisuda"** — an ancient Balinese philosophy translating to the elevation and purification of the soul. This theme reflects Bali's ongoing commitment to nurturing the harmony between nature, humanity, and Balinese culture as an inseparable triad.

Governor Koster used the opening ceremony to affirm that Bali will never tire of safeguarding its civilization. He described PKB not merely as a cultural spectacle, but as a living declaration of Bali's resolve to preserve its artistic heritage and pass it on to future generations.

## Grand Opening Parade

The opening Peed Aya (Grand Parade) drew the most attention, featuring **3,430 artists** marching through the streets in a spectacular procession. Notably, the majority of participants were children and teenagers — a powerful sign that Bali's cultural regeneration is thriving at the grassroots level.

## Ten Main Activity Categories

PKB XLVIII encompasses Balinese arts across **ten main categories**:

- **Peed Aya** — Grand Opening Parade
- **Rekasadan** — Stage Performances
- **Kandarupa** — Fine Arts and Crafts Exhibition
- **Utsawa** — Thematic Parade
- **Wimbakara** — Inter-group Arts Competition
- **Kriya Loka** — Workshops and Masterclasses
- **Widyatula** — Cultural Symposium
- **Adi Sewaka Nugraha** — Arts Dedication Awards
- **Jantra Tradisi Bali** — Regional Cultural Week
- **Bali World Culture Celebration** — International Cultural Festival

## Karisma Event Nusantara Recognition

PKB XLVIII 2026 has been awarded the prestigious **Karisma Event Nusantara** distinction — a national recognition bestowed by the Indonesian central government to outstanding events across the archipelago, placing Bali's arts festival among the country's most celebrated cultural gatherings.

## Boosting the Local Economy

Beyond the arts, PKB serves as a significant economic catalyst. This year, organizers provided **free exhibition space** to **124 Small and Medium Industries (IKM)** and **72 local craft SMEs** for the entire month-long duration of the festival — creating meaningful economic opportunities for Bali's artisan and creative communities.

## Running Until July 11, 2026

The festival runs from **June 13 to July 11, 2026**, offering visitors nearly a month of daily performances, exhibitions, competitions, and workshops. Admission is free and open to the public, making it an unmissable experience for anyone visiting Bali this season.
