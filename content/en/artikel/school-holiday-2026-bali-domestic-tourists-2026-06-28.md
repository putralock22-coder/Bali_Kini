Indonesia's school semester break officially began in late June 2026, and Bali has once again emerged as the number one destination for millions of Indonesian families. The tourism industry on the Island of the Gods has moved quickly to prepare attractive offers to welcome what is expected to be a substantial surge in domestic visitors throughout June and July 2026.

## Bali Remains the Top Family Choice

In surveys of preferred destinations for the 2026 school holidays, Bali consistently topped the list for Indonesian families. The combination of beaches, cultural richness, family entertainment options, and strong flight connectivity makes Bali a destination that remains hard to beat.

The Ministry of Tourism launched the campaign **"Liburan Cara Baru #DiIndonesiaAja"** (A New Way to Vacation — Stay in Indonesia), encouraging the public to explore domestic travel options that are closer, more practical, and more affordable. Bali is one of the priority destinations featured in this campaign, given its comprehensive appeal to travelers of all ages.

## Hotel and Ferry Discounts

In response to the school holiday season, travel platforms are offering substantial discounts. **PRISM** is providing room booking discounts of up to **25% off** using the SCHOOLVACAY promo code, valid from June 22 to July 5, 2026, applicable across property categories from budget hotels to full-service premium resorts.

Beyond accommodation, the government has also introduced **discounted ferry crossings** to ease travel for families crossing from Java to Bali via the **Ketapang–Gilimanuk** route. This initiative aims to reduce the overall cost of travel for domestic tourists, particularly those driving their own vehicles.

The Deputy Minister of Tourism emphasized that all destination operators have been urged to ensure every aspect of their service runs at optimal capacity to deliver the best possible experience for incoming visitors.

## Shopping Tourism Gets a Boost

Beyond nature and cultural tourism, the government is also promoting **shopping tourism** as an additional drawcard during the holiday season. The Ministry of Tourism is mapping shopping tourism destinations across the country — including Bali — to promote them more aggressively to both domestic and international visitors.

The target for domestic tourist movement during the 2026 school holidays is set at **3 million trips**. Bali is expected to capture the largest share of this movement, underscoring its enduring status as Indonesia's premier tourism icon.

## UMKM and Local Businesses Poised to Benefit

The school holiday season represents a golden opportunity for Micro, Small, and Medium Enterprises (UMKM) across Bali. Souvenir vendors, local eateries, licensed tour guides, and traditional transportation providers all stand to gain from the influx of visitors.

Data shows Bali is home to approximately **439,382 UMKM**, with nearly half connected to the tourism sector. This means that every surge in visitor numbers directly translates into increased income for hundreds of thousands of families whose livelihoods depend on Bali's tourism ecosystem.

## Travel Tips for the School Holiday Season

For families planning to visit Bali during the school break, here are some practical recommendations:

- **Book accommodation early** — hotel occupancy in Bali is expected to peak during July 2026.
- **Take advantage of government transport subsidies**, including the discounted ferry crossing for those travelling by car.
- **Visit popular attractions early in the morning** to avoid crowds, especially at Tanah Lot, Uluwatu, and Ubud.
- **Diversify your itinerary** — beyond beaches, Bali offers cultural tourism, mountain landscapes, and agrotourism suitable for all family members.

With thorough preparation from the government, the hotel industry, and local tourism operators, Bali is confident it can deliver an unforgettable school holiday experience for millions of Indonesian families in 2026.
