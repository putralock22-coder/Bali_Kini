## Government Launches BINA Holiday to Energize School Holiday Economy

Amid the school holiday season running through July 2026, the Indonesian government has launched **BINA Holiday and Back to School 2026** — a large-scale initiative to drive domestic spending, strengthen UMKM (micro, small, and medium enterprises), and boost tourism activity simultaneously. The program officially runs from June 8 to July 12, 2026.

BINA stands for *Belanja Indonesia dengan Harga Terjangkau* (Shopping Indonesia at Affordable Prices), and represents a synergy between the Ministry of Trade, major businesses, shopping centers, the tourism sector, and local UMKM operators.

## Massive Scale: 80,000 Outlets Across 24 Provinces

The program mobilizes an extraordinary ecosystem of trade and tourism:

- **~800 brands** across multiple product categories
- **80,000 retail outlets** spread across Indonesia
- **414 shopping centers** in **24 provinces**, including Bali
- **Discounts of up to 50 percent** across various product categories

The transaction target for this program is set at **Rp30 trillion** — a figure that underscores the enormous potential of Indonesian household consumption during the school holiday period.

## Bali: A Strategic Hub for Shopping Tourism

Bali emerges as one of the program's strategic focal points given its status as the top destination for both domestic and international tourists. During June and July, Bali enters its peak holiday season, drawing a wave of domestic visitors from Jakarta, East Java, and Kalimantan.

Shopping destinations such as **Ubud Art Market**, **Pasar Sukawati**, and **shopping malls in Denpasar and Badung** are among the areas most directly impacted by this program. Bali's signature UMKM products — from hand-crafted silver jewelry and local woven textiles to traditional Balinese culinary specialties — gain greater reach as visitors look for authentic local shopping experiences.

### Bali's UMKM Operators Gain a Bigger Platform

Small businesses have historically struggled to compete for consumer attention against large brands. The BINA Holiday program opens a valuable window for Bali's UMKM to promote their products on a broader platform — both physically through in-mall exhibitions and digitally through e-commerce channels participating in the program.

Food vendors, artisans, and traditional market traders in Bali are being encouraged to take advantage of this momentum by offering attractive pricing and special holiday packages for incoming tourists.

## Impact on Bali's Economy

Bank Indonesia (BI) Bali representative office had previously projected that Bali's economy in the second quarter of 2026 would be lifted by two key drivers: **school holidays** and **rising investment**. The BINA Holiday program reinforces this projection by stimulating household consumption, which has consistently been the primary engine of Bali's economic growth.

Bali's economy grew by **5.58 percent** year-on-year in the first quarter of 2026, supported by the accommodation and food and beverage sector which expanded by 6.44 percent. With additional stimulus from BINA Holiday, second-quarter growth is expected to surpass this figure.

## Aligning with Bali's 2026 Tourism Direction

This initiative aligns closely with Bali's 2026 tourism policy direction, which emphasizes not just visitor headcount but the **quality and economic impact of tourist spending**. When tourists purchase locally made UMKM products, the economic benefits spread more broadly across Balinese society — rather than flowing primarily to large corporations.

Deputy Minister of Tourism Ni Luh Puspa has called on all tourism destination managers to enhance readiness for this holiday period, including ensuring comfort, cleanliness, and adequate services for the millions of expected visitors.

The BINA Holiday 2026 program is concrete evidence that tourism and trade can move in tandem to build a more inclusive and resilient local economic foundation for Bali and its communities.
