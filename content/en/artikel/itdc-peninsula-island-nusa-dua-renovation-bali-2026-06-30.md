State-owned tourism enterprise PT Pengembangan Pariwisata Indonesia (**ITDC**) is accelerating the rejuvenation program at **Peninsula Island** within The Nusa Dua complex, Badung Regency, Bali. The multi-year renovation aims to elevate the destination's standing as a premier venue for world-class international events and quality tourism.

## Latest Construction Progress

Based on the most recent project updates, works in the **Water Blow** area and the broader Peninsula zone have reached **74.91 to 78.5 percent completion**. Meanwhile, road access works from Gate AC leading into the Peninsula Island area stand at **30.48 percent** completion.

Active construction on the ground encompasses a wide range of improvements, including:

- Installation of **andesite stone** paving along pedestrian pathways throughout the complex
- Construction of **rainwater absorption units** along The Bay road corridor
- Addition of a new **wedding venue** at the Water Blow area
- Construction of a new **performance stage** for arts and cultural shows
- Upgrading public facilities including **tourist restrooms** and **bale massage** shelters for local community groups

## Five New Programs for Visitors

ITDC has introduced **five new visitor-focused programs** designed to enhance the overall Peninsula Island experience. These programs address landscape beautification, destination identity strengthening, and the development of facilities better suited to hosting international events.

Peninsula Island has long been celebrated as one of Nusa Dua's most popular attractions, particularly the natural phenomenon of **Water Blow** — dramatic ocean surges where powerful waves crash against the cliff face, creating spectacular spray formations that draw both domestic and international visitors.

## A New Chapter After 52 Years

This rejuvenation project is part of a comprehensive overhaul of **The Nusa Dua** — a premium tourism zone with over 52 years of history. The transformation is planned as a phased program spanning **three years**, from 2026 through 2028.

The long-term objective is to position The Nusa Dua as a premium destination fully equipped to host high-profile international forums, MICE (Meetings, Incentives, Conferences and Exhibitions) events, and other global gatherings on a sustainable, year-round basis.

### Strategic Investment at a Critical Juncture

ITDC's decision to invest heavily in Peninsula Island's infrastructure comes at a strategically important moment. As competition intensifies across Southeast Asian luxury destinations, Bali needs to continuously upgrade its facilities to remain competitive as a venue for international events.

Bali has recently hosted a string of prestigious global conferences, including the **WEF Ocean Impact Summit** and various international diplomatic forums. High-quality destination infrastructure is a key factor in winning bids to host such events in the future.

## Economic Impact on Local Communities

Beyond its tourism benefits, the multi-billion rupiah project is expected to generate positive economic ripple effects for communities surrounding Nusa Dua. The construction phase is absorbing local labor, while the upgraded facilities are projected to attract higher-spending visitors — boosting revenue across the surrounding hospitality and retail ecosystem.

With construction progressing on schedule, ITDC targets the Peninsula Island area to be operating with its renewed look before the end of the first renovation phase in 2026.
