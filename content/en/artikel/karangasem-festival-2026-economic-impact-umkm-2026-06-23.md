**Amlapura, Karangasem** – Karangasem Festival 2026 has closed on a record-breaking note, generating a total of **Rp1.445 billion** (approximately USD 88,000) in economic transactions over its four-day run from June 19–22, 2026, at Taman Budaya Candra Bhuana in Amlapura. The figure makes it one of the most economically impactful regency-level festivals in Bali.

## Strong Numbers Across UMKM and Culinary Sectors

The festival's economic footprint was driven primarily by the food and beverage sector. **85 culinary vendors** collectively recorded **Rp1.087 billion** in sales — a testament to the enormous appetite visitors had for authentic local Karangasem flavors, from traditional snacks and grilled seafood to herbal drinks and traditional Balinese sweets.

Meanwhile, **32 UMKM (small and medium enterprise) participants** contributed **Rp358 million** in transactions, showcasing traditional textiles such as *endek* cloth, handcrafts, and processed agricultural products unique to eastern Bali.

Beyond direct stall vendors, the ripple effect spread across transportation, accommodation, and ancillary tourism services — all of which benefited from the increased visitor traffic over the four festival days.

## Closed by Bali Deputy Governor Giri Prasta

The festival was officially closed by **Bali Deputy Governor I Nyoman Giri Prasta**, who praised the event's ability to translate cultural celebration into measurable economic benefit for ordinary citizens.

Giri Prasta highlighted Karangasem Festival as a model for community-based economic development — one where artisans, food vendors, and local entrepreneurs are primary beneficiaries rather than passive spectators. He also applauded the spirit of collective cooperation (*gotong royong*) that characterized the festival's organization.

## Amlapura's 386th Anniversary Theme

Karangasem Festival 2026 was held in conjunction with the **386th anniversary of the City of Amlapura**, under the resonant theme *"Tidak Mudah, Tapi Harus Bisa"* (Not Easy, But We Must Succeed). The motto reflects the resilience of Karangasem's people who have long navigated natural challenges — from living in the shadow of active Mount Agung to economic pressures faced by rural communities — with determination and solidarity.

## Cultural Tourism Meets Creative Economy

Karangasem is traditionally known for spiritual and natural attractions: Pura Besakih (Bali's revered Mother Temple), Tirta Gangga water palace, the diving haven of Amed, and Tulamben's famous USAT Liberty shipwreck. Karangasem Festival adds a vibrant culinary and artisan dimension to this landscape, inviting visitors to experience the regency as a living community with its own distinct creative economy.

The festival served as a creative stage for artists, traditional food sellers, craftspeople, and young entrepreneurs to perform, transact, and engage directly with visitors from across Bali and international tourists.

## Setting the Stage for Greater Growth

With Rp1.445 billion generated in just four days, Karangasem Festival 2026 sends a clear signal: eastern Bali has the creative and economic potential to stand alongside the more heavily marketed southern tourism belt. Local government officials and organizers hope this milestone will attract greater investment in future festival editions, while inspiring other regencies across Bali to develop similar community-centered cultural events.
