Despite mounting global uncertainty stemming from geopolitical tensions, **Bank Indonesia (BI) Bali Representative Office** has declared that investment conditions on the Island of the Gods remain robust and continue to strengthen. BI Bali Chief **Achris Sarwani** expressed confidence that Bali's economic foundations are well-positioned to weather external shocks that have disrupted many other regional economies.

## Economy Projected to Grow 5.4–5.9 Percent

BI Bali projects the island's economy will grow at **5.4 to 5.9 percent** in 2026 — a solid performance that outpaces many comparable tourism-dependent economies. Key factors underpinning this optimism include:

- **Stable inflation** maintained within acceptable bounds
- **Strong business confidence** among local entrepreneurs
- **Government incentives** from both central and provincial levels
- **Bali's enduring global competitiveness** as a premier destination

Together, these pillars signal that investment opportunities in Bali are expanding, even as other markets face headwinds from geopolitical instability.

## 22 Infrastructure Projects in Investment Catalog

Working in tandem with the Bali Provincial Government, Bank Indonesia has compiled a comprehensive **investment catalog** covering **22 strategic infrastructure projects**. Key entries include:

- **KEK (Special Economic Zone) Health Sanur** — a world-class integrated health and wellness zone
- **Electric vehicle procurement projects** — supporting the green energy transition in tourism
- **Street lighting infrastructure** — improving basic services in strategic growth corridors

These projects were spotlighted at the **Bali Jagadhita Investment Forum 2026**, where all 22 strategic projects from Bali and Nusa Tenggara were formally presented to **35 prospective investors** from domestic and international markets.

## Kura Kura Bali SEZ: Indonesia's Dubai Ambition

Among the most ambitious initiatives is the **Kura Kura Bali Special Economic Zone**, a 100-hectare development designed to become an international financial center comparable to Dubai's DIFC. The zone offers a package of legal and tax incentives crafted specifically to attract global institutional investors, positioning Bali not only as a tourism powerhouse but also as a serious financial hub.

## International Corporations Leading the Way

Foreign interest in Bali's economy is being translated into concrete programs. The **US-ASEAN Business Council** presented multiple initiatives by American companies supporting Bali's growth across tourism, human resources, sustainability, agriculture, and digital transformation.

**Marriott International's** flagship program, **Bali for Bali**, illustrates this commitment: since its 2022 launch, the program has generated more than **Rp14 billion (approximately USD 860,000)** in local procurement — directly strengthening Bali's domestic supply chain in the hospitality sector.

## Long-Term Infrastructure Push

The central government has positioned Bali as an **international tourism superhub** over the next two decades, backed by a pipeline of integrated infrastructure upgrades. Priority projects include:

- **Jimbaran Underpass** — alleviating traffic bottlenecks near the airport
- **Pesanggaran–Canggu national road** — connecting the south coast corridor
- **Klungkung–Karangasem tourism road** — opening eastern Bali to development
- **Northern Bali Ring Road** — linking the underserved north coast
- **Logistics ports in Karangasem and Klungkung** — boosting maritime supply chains

Indonesia's House of Representatives Commission V has backed Bali's proposal to accelerate these projects, lending political weight to the island's infrastructure ambitions.
