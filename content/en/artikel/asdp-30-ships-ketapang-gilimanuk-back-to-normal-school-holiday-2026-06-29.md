The Bali Strait ferry crossing between Ketapang and Gilimanuk has gradually returned to normal operations. After enduring queues stretching up to five kilometers in the middle of last week, PT ASDP Indonesia Ferry (Persero) successfully brought the situation under control by deploying up to 30 ships per day — a measure that took effect by Sunday, June 28, 2026.

## Queues Peaked at 5 Kilometers

Traffic volumes began surging on Sunday, June 21, 2026, coinciding with the start of Indonesia's school promotion (end-of-year) holiday period. By Wednesday, June 24, the queue of vehicles heading toward Ketapang Port in Banyuwangi had reached its peak — stretching approximately five kilometers.

The congestion was fueled by a wave of tourist buses carrying family holiday groups and heavy logistics trucks vying for space on one of Java's most critical cross-strait routes. Passengers found themselves waiting for hours before boarding.

In response, ASDP's management issued a formal public apology on June 25, 2026. That same day, queue lengths began to ease to around 2.5 kilometers as emergency measures kicked in.

## The 30-Ship Strategy and Buffer Zones

ASDP implemented a multi-pronged approach to restore normal flow. The fleet operating on the route was scaled up to between 28 and 30 vessels daily, including support ships stationed at Bulusan Pier, the LCM jetty, and additional pontoon berths to maximize loading capacity.

Traffic management was also overhauled. Private vehicles and heavy logistics trucks were directed to separate berths to reduce bottlenecks and speed up turnaround times at the port's loading areas.

Additionally, ASDP partnered with local traffic police to activate a buffer parking zone at Bulusan, capable of holding approximately 400 heavy vehicles. This staging area allows freight trucks to queue off the main arterial road, keeping access to the port clear.

## Multi-Sector Collaboration

The recovery was made possible through coordinated efforts between ASDP, the Ministry of Transportation's regional office, and law enforcement. ASDP's director noted that the combination of traffic engineering measures and operational adjustments began delivering visible results within days.

By Sunday, June 28, the Ketapang–Gilimanuk crossing had returned to orderly conditions, with vehicles moving steadily through the port.

### Discount Program Still Active

As part of the government's transportation stimulus for the school holiday period, ASDP's port service fee discount remains in effect from June 20 through July 5, 2026. Passengers and vehicle owners enjoy a 100 percent waiver on port service charges — equivalent to roughly a 21.9 percent reduction on the total ticket price.

The discount covers 14 ports across seven national ferry routes, including the Ketapang–Gilimanuk corridor. Travelers heading to Bali can still take advantage of the promotion until early July.

## Tips for Remaining Travelers

For anyone still planning to cross to Bali during the school holidays, ASDP advises booking tickets in advance through their online platform to skip port-side queues. Choosing off-peak departure times — such as late night or early morning crossings — is also strongly recommended for a smoother trip.

With the Ketapang–Gilimanuk crossing now stabilized, the route is expected to support the steady flow of holiday visitors heading to Bali for the remainder of the school holiday period, which runs through mid-July 2026.
