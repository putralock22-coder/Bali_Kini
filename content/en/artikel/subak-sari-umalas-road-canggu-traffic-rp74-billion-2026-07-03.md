Traffic congestion in Canggu has long been a persistent complaint among tourists and locals alike. The narrow lanes along the Jalan Raya Subak Sari corridor — connecting Batu Bolong with Umalas — are now set to receive a lasting fix. Badung Regency Government is pushing ahead with a multi-billion-rupiah road bypass project to permanently address gridlock that has plagued this popular area for years.

## Rp74 Billion Project Already Underway

Head of Badung's Public Works and Spatial Planning Agency (PUPR), I Nyoman R. Karyasa, confirmed that the budget ceiling for the Tibubeneng-Umalas bypass reaches **more than Rp74 billion**. Funding comes from a Non-Bank Financial Institution Regional Loan (LKBB) for the 2026 fiscal year.

"We are building and upgrading the road along the Subak Sari or Batu Bolong corridor, Umalas, through to Jalan Semer," Karyasa was quoted as saying by Detik Bali.

Physical work on site is already in progress. Contractors are currently carrying out land compaction and pedestrian path layout along the planned new road corridor.

## A Strategic Link Between Two Key Areas

The new road is designed to directly connect **Tibubeneng** in the north with **Umalas, Kerobokan Kelod** in Kuta Utara District. These two areas were previously linked only by narrow lanes that clog up easily — especially during morning and afternoon rush hours when workers, tourists, and local residents compete for the same limited road space.

This new route complements several other strategic road projects currently being accelerated in Badung, including:

- **A connector from Berawa/Batu Bolong to West Teuku Umar**
- **Access from Uluwatu to Melasti and South Ring Road**
- **A route from Ayana toward Uluwatu, Pecatu, and South Kuta**

All four routes form part of a broader package of new road access construction in Badung Regency, all targeted for completion by end of 2026.

## Why Canggu Desperately Needs New Roads

Canggu has long been the epicenter of Bali's lifestyle tourism scene — a magnet for digital nomads, surfers, young travellers, and expatriates seeking the sweet spot between work and leisure. But that popularity carries a heavy price: vehicle volumes in the area have surged sharply in recent years, far outpacing the capacity of existing roads.

Traffic studies show that Canggu's congestion isn't just a nuisance — it poses a direct risk to the area's appeal as a premium tourism destination. Property investors and hospitality operators have increasingly begun factoring traffic into their business risk assessments.

## Timeline for Completion

The project is targeted for completion within the coming months. Once finished, the new Subak Sari-Umalas road is expected to serve as a major alternative route that meaningfully reduces vehicle density in Canggu and its surrounding tourism corridors.

Badung Regent I Wayan Adi Arnawa has made new road infrastructure a top priority. "Every year, land prices in Badung rise. So we must move fast to acquire land and build new roads before costs balloon further," the Regent has stated on multiple occasions.

With Tibubeneng and Umalas directly connected for the first time, traffic flows to and from Canggu are expected to spread more evenly — giving much-needed relief to a zone caught between its own popularity and the limits of its infrastructure.
