The 12th Asian Open Water Swimming Championship 2026 officially wrapped up on Sunday, June 15, after three days of competition in the waters off the InterContinental Bali Resort at Jimbaran Bay, Badung Regency. The event gathered 78 athletes from 18 nations across three competition categories.

## China Dominates the Podium

China emerged as the undisputed champion of the championship. In the men's 5-kilometer event, Tianchen Lan claimed gold with a time of 56 minutes and 57 seconds. Silver and bronze went to Vietnam's Huy Hoang Nguyen and Tran Tuan Anh Mai, who finished within less than a second of each other.

In the women's 5 km, Wu Shutong of China led the race with a time of 1 hour 2 minutes 7.6 seconds, followed by her compatriot Chen Yijin in second place. Vietnam's Vo Thi My Tien claimed bronze.

In the 4x1,500 meter relay, China again topped the podium with a time of 56 minutes 42.3 seconds. South Korea surprised many by taking silver at 57:07.9, while Vietnam settled for bronze. Indonesia finished in eighth place with a time of 1 hour 4 minutes 32.7 seconds — roughly 8 minutes behind China.

## Indonesia Without Medals, But PB Akuatik Looks Forward

Despite fielding seven athletes in the competition, Indonesia did not win a single medal across any of the three events. However, the Indonesian Swimming Federation (PB Akuatik) chose to frame the result as a starting point rather than a setback.

"We don't see this as a failure — it's a learning process and the beginning of a more serious regeneration effort," said a PB Akuatik representative following the closing ceremony.

### Scholarship Program Announced

In a significant move, PB Akuatik announced a scholarship program to support open water swimming athletes who wish to train abroad. The initiative aims to improve their competitive edge, increase their international experience, and ultimately close the gap with Asia's best.

One standout example is Hefrisyanthi Izzy Dwi Faiva, who already received a World Aquatics training scholarship and is currently training in Paris, France. Her story is now held up as an inspiration for younger athletes to pursue international opportunities.

## South Korea Inspired to Bid for Next Hosting Rights

The championship's success in Bali left a strong impression on several participating nations, most notably South Korea. The Korean delegation expressed high enthusiasm and said the Jimbaran event had inspired them to pursue hosting rights for the next Asian OWS Championship.

South Korea is now preparing to compete against Oman, Macao, and Hong Kong for the opportunity. PB Akuatik described this as proof that Bali had successfully merged sports and tourism into a compelling international package.

## Bali's Sport Tourism Profile Rises

For Bali, hosting the Asian OWS Championship once again reinforced its reputation as a world-class sport tourism destination. The event attracted athletes, officials, and delegations from 18 countries, generating meaningful economic activity for hotels, restaurants, and local businesses around the Jimbaran area.

The Indonesian Ministry of Tourism has identified sport tourism as a strategic priority for 2026, and Bali continues to be the most visible proof of concept — combining natural beauty, world-class facilities, and cultural richness into experiences that go far beyond the finish line.
