Indonesia's Ministry of Tourism (Kemenpar) has launched an ambitious initiative to boost domestic travel during the 2026 school holiday season. In partnership with **40 industry players**, the ministry unveiled **95 tourism packages** priced as low as **Rp10,000** — making quality family travel accessible to Indonesians at nearly every income level.

## The "New Way of Holidaying #DiIndonesiaAja" Campaign

The package launch forms part of a broader national campaign called *"Liburan Cara Baru #DiIndonesiaAja"* — which roughly translates to "New Way of Holidaying, Right Here in Indonesia." The initiative aims to shift the travel habits of Indonesian families away from international destinations and toward exploring the extraordinary diversity of their own country.

The Minister of Tourism emphasized that this program is more than marketing — it's a practical effort to channel holiday spending into local economies across the archipelago during each school break cycle.

## What the 95 Packages Offer

The packages span a wide range of travel styles and interests, ensuring there's something for every type of family:

- **Nature Tourism** – national parks, beaches, and highland retreats
- **Cultural Tourism** – traditional villages, batik workshops, and heritage ceremonies
- **Adventure Tourism** – trekking, white-water rafting, and diving experiences
- **Historical Tourism** – visits to ancient temples, colonial buildings, and museums
- **Marine Tourism** – snorkeling, island hopping, and underwater exploration
- **Urban Tourism** – city tours with food trails and contemporary art experiences

Destinations covered by the program span the country, with **Bali** prominently featured alongside Central Java, DKI Jakarta, West Java, West Nusa Tenggara, Yogyakarta, Kalimantan, and South Sulawesi.

## Bali Remains a Top Draw

Despite the national spread of available destinations, Bali continues to command the greatest interest among domestic travelers. The island's combination of well-developed infrastructure, cultural richness, and global recognition keeps it firmly atop the family vacation wishlist.

The school holiday packages focused on Bali include everything from cultural immersion tours to Besakih Temple, cycling through the UNESCO-listed Jatiluwih rice terraces, to marine adventures around Nusa Penida and Menjangan Island.

### Inclusive Pricing as a Strategic Move

Setting entry-level prices at just Rp10,000 was a deliberate policy choice to make domestic tourism genuinely inclusive. High costs have historically been a barrier for middle and lower-income families considering a proper holiday. These packages break down that barrier, giving families real options without compromising on the quality of their experience.

## How to Book

Travelers can browse and book all 95 packages through the Ministry of Tourism's official platform at **jadesta.kemenpar.go.id**, where full details on activities, itineraries, and prices are available. Early booking is encouraged, as package quotas are limited and demand is expected to be high.

## Ripple Effects on Local Economies

Beyond the holiday experience itself, this initiative carries significant economic implications. Every family that chooses a domestic tourism package contributes directly to the livelihoods of local guides, small businesses, traditional food vendors, and guesthouses in the destination area.

The government's projection is that heightened domestic travel during the 2026 school holidays will help redistribute tourism revenue more equitably across Indonesia's regions — reducing the over-concentration of tourist spending in a handful of established hotspots.

With the *"New Way of Holidaying"* campaign gaining momentum, the 2026 school holiday season looks set to become a landmark moment for Indonesia's domestic tourism sector.
