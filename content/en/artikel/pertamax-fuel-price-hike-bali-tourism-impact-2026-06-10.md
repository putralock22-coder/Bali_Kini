Starting Wednesday, **June 10, 2026**, the price of Pertamax — Indonesia's non-subsidized RON 92 fuel — officially surged to its highest level in recent years. PT Pertamina Patra Niaga set the new price at **Rp16,250 per liter** for Java, Bali, and Nusa Tenggara — an increase of Rp3,950, or approximately **32.1 percent** from the previous price of Rp12,300 per liter.

The sudden jump has drawn mixed reactions from the public, particularly from tourism operators and small business owners across Bali who depend heavily on vehicle mobility for their daily operations.

Subsidized fuels — Pertalite and Biosolar — **remain unchanged**. The government has ensured these products stay affordable for lower-income households.

## Why Did Pertamax Jump So Sharply?

The price adjustment was made in response to **global crude oil market dynamics** and Pertamina's periodic pricing evaluation that considers the Indonesian Crude Price (ICP) benchmark and the rupiah exchange rate against the US dollar. As a non-subsidized product, Pertamax pricing is inherently market-linked and subject to fluctuations.

## Impact on Bali's Tourism Sector

The Pertamax price hike is expected to directly affect **tour package costs** in Bali. Tourism operators and transportation providers using Pertamax-fueled fleets must now reassess their operational cost structures.

Even before the official increase took effect, Bali's tourism industry associations had already voiced concern. If fuel prices rise significantly, operating costs for tourist vehicles — minibuses, tour buses, and rental cars — automatically increase. This chain reaction could push tour package prices higher, potentially reducing Bali's appeal to domestic tourists who are price-sensitive.

### Online Ride-Hailing: Minimal Impact

In contrast to tourism transport, online ride-hailing (ojol) drivers in Bali face relatively little disruption. Data from industry association Garda Indonesia shows that approximately **95 percent of ojol drivers** still rely on subsidized Pertalite fuel for daily operations, with only about 5 percent using Pertamax.

## Pressure on Bali's SMEs

The Pertamax increase adds another layer of pressure on **small and medium enterprises (SMEs)** in Bali, particularly those relying on goods distribution:

- **Catering and food businesses** that use delivery vehicles
- **Local artisans** distributing products to shops and art galleries
- **Farmers and fishers** using vehicles to move produce or catch to markets

Rising distribution costs could push up the prices of SME products. If passed on to end consumers — including tourists shopping at traditional markets or art galleries — purchasing power may weaken.

## Risk of Local Inflation

Economists have warned that a significant non-subsidized fuel price hike — nearly one-third above the previous price — risks triggering **higher regional inflation**. As transportation and distribution costs rise, goods and service prices tend to follow, squeezing the middle class further.

That said, Indonesia Deposit Insurance Corporation (LPS) Chief Executive Purbaya Yudhi Sadewa stated that the impact of the Pertamax increase on the broader economy is "**relatively minimal**," since Pertamax is not consumed by the majority of the population. However, for Pertamax users — predominantly middle-to-upper-income earners and business operators — the impact is very real.

## How Bali's Business Community Can Adapt

For tourism operators and SME owners in Bali, several practical steps can help manage the impact:

1. **Reassess operational cost structures** and adjust selling prices gradually and transparently
2. **Optimize delivery and distribution routes** to reduce fuel consumption
3. **Explore more fuel-efficient vehicles** or those eligible to use subsidized Pertalite

The June 2026 Pertamax price surge is a reminder that Bali's tourism sector — as powerful as it is — remains exposed to macroeconomic shocks. The agility of local businesses in adapting to rising costs will be key to maintaining Bali's competitive edge as a world-class travel destination.
