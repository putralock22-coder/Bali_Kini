The Denpasar City Government, through its Creative Economy Agency (BKraf Denpasar), is gearing up for the **6th edition of the Denpasar Youth Festival (D'Youth Fest 6.0)**—a flagship event designed to nurture and celebrate the creative potential of Bali's young generation. This year's festival carries the theme **"Feel The Growth"**, an invitation for youth to tap into genuine growth within their communities, artistic works, and broader creative ecosystems.

## Peak Days: July 11-12, 2026

Following weeks of preliminary activities that began in mid-June, D'Youth Fest 6.0 will reach its crescendo on **July 11-12, 2026** in Denpasar. The two-day peak event will unite creative industry players, cross-sector communities, culinary micro-entrepreneurs (UMKM), and arts enthusiasts under one vibrant roof.

The Denpasar city government has allocated a budget of **Rp 2.3 billion** for this edition—a clear demonstration of institutional commitment to building a sustainable youth-led creative economy.

## Headline Performers

The main stage on peak nights will feature performances from notable local and national musicians, including **Ari Lesmana** and **Bagus Wirata**, both beloved figures in the Balinese and Indonesian music scenes. Their presence is expected to draw large crowds and amplify the festival's message: Denpasar is a city that invests in culture and creativity.

## More Than a Concert

D'Youth Fest 6.0 is deliberately designed as a **multi-dimensional growth space**, not just a music event. It spans diverse creative sectors including performing arts, esports, popular culture, street murals, filmmaking, creative community showcases, and innovation-driven creative economy.

At least **20 culinary UMKMs** and **5 food trucks** will operate as partner vendors throughout the festival, providing both marketing exposure for small businesses and a stimulating economic pulse for the local community. For young entrepreneurs in Denpasar, D'Youth Fest represents a direct pipeline to young, engaged consumers.

## A Greener Festival

The 6.0 edition brings a renewed environmental commitment: an **integrated waste management system** will be implemented during the entire festival, serving as a practical education platform on environmental responsibility for Denpasar's youth. This aligns with the broader "Regrow" philosophy—reviving what communities already have, while rebuilding it more sustainably.

### Denpasar's Creative Economy Vision

D'Youth Fest is not a standalone event. It is part of BKraf Denpasar's long-term strategy to position the city as the **creative economy hub of Eastern Indonesia**—one that anchors its identity in innovation, collaboration, and youthful energy. Each edition builds on the last, expanding its reach and deepening its impact on Bali's creative ecosystem.

For young people looking to create, collaborate, and grow—D'Youth Fest 6.0 is the moment.
