## Day Two of PKB XLVIII: A Full Day of Cultural Performances

Sunday, June 14, 2026, marks the second day of the 48th Bali Arts Festival (Pesta Kesenian Bali / PKB XLVIII), which was officially inaugurated the evening before with a grand ceremonial parade. Today's program shifts from the spectacle of the opening procession to more focused competitions and intimate performances that are deeply rooted in Balinese tradition.

PKB XLVIII carries the theme **"Atma Kerthi: Jiwa Sidha Parisudha"** — a philosophical concept meaning the complete purification of the soul. Held at the Taman Budaya Art Center in Denpasar, the month-long festival runs through July 11, 2026, engaging more than 23,000 artists from across all regencies in Bali.

## Gender Wayang Competition: Morning of Refined Harmony

The day opens at **10:00 AM WITA** at the Kalangan Ayodya stage with the **Wimbakara (Competition) Gender Wayang**, bringing together three distinguished ensembles from different parts of Bali:

- **Sanggar Semara Lango** — representing Bangli Regency
- **Sanggar Candra Metu** — representing Jembrana Regency
- **Sanggar Kembang Waru** — representing Denpasar City

Gender Wayang is a sacred gamelan ensemble traditionally used in Balinese religious ceremonies, particularly to accompany shadow puppet (Wayang Kulit) performances. Its metallophones produce a distinctively soft yet expressive sound, making it one of Bali's most revered musical traditions and a recognized element of intangible cultural heritage.

## Barong Ket Workshop and Afternoon Arts

As the afternoon unfolds, the festival expands into additional venues. At **3:00 PM WITA**, the Kalangan Ratna Kanda stage hosts a showcase of **Talent Management in Dance and Palegongan Percussion** by Sanggar Gita Mahardika, collaborating with trainees from the Bali Provincial Cultural Center.

A highlight drawing significant interest is the **Barong Ket Dance Workshop**, where young artists receive direct mentorship from master practitioners, learning the technical intricacies and spiritual philosophy behind this sacred lion-like figure — a protective spirit at the heart of Balinese cosmology.

## Bali World Culture Celebration: Timor Leste Takes the Stage

At **5:00 PM WITA**, the prestigious Ksirarnawa Hall becomes the venue for the **Bali World Culture Celebration (BWCC)**, featuring a performance of **Classical Dance from Timor Leste**. This year's PKB welcomes approximately 10 international art groups comprising over 260 foreign artists from countries including the United States (New York), China, South Korea, Japan, India, and Hong Kong.

The inclusion of international performers reinforces PKB's status as a world-class cultural exchange platform, far beyond a local celebration.

## Tonight's Highlight: Kids' Gong Kebyar — Klungkung vs Tabanan

The evening climax arrives at **7:30 PM WITA** on the open-air Ardha Candra stage with the **Utsawa (Parade) Kids' Gong Kebyar**, pitting two talented young ensembles against each other:

- **Sekaa Gong Kebyar Anak-anak Candra Nirwana** — representing Klungkung Regency
- **Sanggar Seni Iswara Murti** — representing Tabanan Regency

The Kids' Gong Kebyar competition is consistently one of PKB's most beloved events, showcasing child musicians who have already mastered the complex techniques of Balinese gamelan. Watching these young performers demonstrate the precision, discipline, and artistry that Bali's musical tradition demands is a testament to the island's living cultural inheritance. Admission is free to the public at Taman Budaya, Denpasar.
