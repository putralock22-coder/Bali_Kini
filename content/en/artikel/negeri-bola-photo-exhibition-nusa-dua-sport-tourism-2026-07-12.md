Against the backdrop of Nusa Dua's stunning beachfront, a photo journalism exhibition is bringing Indonesia's football journey to life in Bali. The national news agency Perum LKBN ANTARA launched **"Negeri Bola: Dari Lapangan Kampung Menuju Panggung Dunia"** (*Nation of Football: From Village Pitches to the World Stage*) at Peninsula Island, The Nusa Dua, Badung — an initiative that fuses documentary photography with Bali's sport tourism ambitions.

## 62 Photographs, Four Deeply Reported Stories

Running from **July 11 to 24, 2026**, the exhibition displays **62 individual photographs** by ANTARA's top photojournalists, along with **four long-form photo stories** tracing the arc of Indonesian football from grassroots origins to the international spotlight of the 2026 FIFA World Cup.

Each image was selected to capture a defining moment — the raw emotion of a local child training barefoot, the electric atmosphere of a packed stadium, the tears and triumph of Timnas Indonesia reaching the global stage. Together, the photographs build a visual chronicle of a dream once thought impossible that is now becoming reality.

### A Football Field Beside the Sea

What sets this exhibition apart is its distinctive design concept. The entire layout is arranged to resemble a **football pitch**, with the natural beauty of Nusa Dua's white-sand beach serving as an open-air backdrop. The result is an immersive experience that blends fine art photography, the spirit of football, and Bali's scenic environment into something genuinely unique — and entirely free to visit.

## Governor Koster Visits in Show of Support

The exhibition drew attention at the highest level of Bali's government. Governor Wayan Koster paid a personal visit on Sunday, July 12, taking time to walk through the gallery and view the photographs firsthand. His presence sent a clear signal of the Bali Provincial Government's commitment to developing sport tourism as a core pillar of the island's evolving tourism identity.

## Free World Cup Watch Parties Bring Communities Together

Alongside the exhibition, ANTARA organized free public World Cup 2026 viewing sessions, creating open social spaces where local residents and international visitors could come together to watch the world's biggest sporting event side by side. The events were designed to be inclusive — welcoming football fans of all backgrounds to gather, cheer, and connect in the spirit of the game.

This combination of high-quality photojournalism and live football-watching experiences reflects ANTARA's active role in helping bring Bali's sport tourism scene to a wider audience.

## Why Sport Tourism Matters for Bali's Future

Bali has historically been defined by its beach culture, temples, and spiritual heritage. The island is now working to broaden that identity by weaving sport into its tourism calendar, and the results are becoming increasingly visible. In 2026 alone, Bali has hosted an Asian Open Water Swimming Championship, international cliff diving competitions, multiple fun runs, and a string of padel and water sports tournaments.

The 2026 World Cup provides a particularly powerful catalyst. The global excitement surrounding the tournament is drawing football-mad visitors from dozens of countries — travelers who are also spending time on Bali's beaches, staying in its hotels, and eating at its restaurants.

### A Template for Tourism Diversification

Events like "Negeri Bola" and the World Cup watch parties demonstrate how Bali can use major international sporting moments as anchors for homegrown cultural programming. Rather than simply broadcasting football on a big screen, ANTARA's initiative adds layers of storytelling, artistry, and place-making that give visitors a richer reason to be in Bali specifically.

For tourism planners, this model — where sport, culture, and destination experience are woven together — points toward a sustainable approach that attracts higher-value visitors and encourages longer stays, both of which are central to Bali's ongoing tourism transformation.
