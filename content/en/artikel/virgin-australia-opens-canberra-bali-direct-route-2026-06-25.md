Bali has further strengthened its international aviation network. Virgin Australia officially launched a direct route from Canberra — Australia's capital city — to Denpasar, marking a new chapter in air connectivity between the two destinations.

## Inaugural Flight Lands Successfully

The maiden Canberra–Denpasar flight took place on Monday, June 22, 2026. A Boeing 737 MAX 8 aircraft operating as flight VA127 touched down at Ngurah Rai International Airport at 9:02 PM local time, carrying 165 passengers from the Australian capital. The return flight VA128 departed for Canberra at 10:39 PM carrying 68 passengers.

This route became the 41st international route served by Ngurah Rai Airport, which is now connected to 43 airlines from around the world.

## Three Flights Per Week Schedule

During the 2026 summer season, the Virgin Australia Canberra–Bali service will operate three times weekly — every Monday, Wednesday, and Saturday. This regular schedule is expected to greatly benefit Canberra residents who previously had to transit through Sydney, Melbourne, or Brisbane to reach Bali directly.

## Australia Leads Connectivity to Bali

The addition of the Canberra route further solidifies Australia's position as the country with the most direct routes to Bali. With this new service, Australia now has 12 direct routes to Ngurah Rai, covering Perth, Melbourne, Sydney, Brisbane, Adelaide, Gold Coast, Darwin, Cairns, Newcastle, Avalon, Sunshine Coast, and Canberra as the newest addition.

This dominance in air connectivity aligns with visitor statistics — Australian tourists have consistently ranked as the top foreign arrivals to Bali throughout 2026.

## Positive Impact on Bali's Tourism

The opening of this new route presents a significant opportunity for Bali's tourism sector. Canberra is Australia's government hub with a relatively large middle-to-upper-class population known for their enthusiasm for international travel, particularly across Southeast Asia.

Direct air connectivity is expected to drive a surge in visitors from Australia's capital region, potentially boosting revenue for hotels, restaurants, and the broader tourism industry. This comes as Bali continues its strategy to attract higher-quality and higher-spending international visitors.

### Ngurah Rai Airport's Expanding Network

Ngurah Rai Airport management welcomed the new route as part of ongoing efforts to enhance Bali's global connectivity. The airport continues to develop its infrastructure and service quality to accommodate the growing number of international airlines choosing Bali as a destination.

With an ever-expanding network of international flights, Bali is cementing its status as one of the best-connected tourism destinations in Southeast Asia.
