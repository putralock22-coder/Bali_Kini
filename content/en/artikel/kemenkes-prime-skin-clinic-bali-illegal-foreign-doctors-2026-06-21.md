The Indonesian government has sealed a foreign-owned aesthetic beauty clinic operating illegally in Bali. Known as **PRIME Skin Clinic** — previously operating under the name Elasto Beauty — the facility was found guilty of three serious violations and officially closed by Indonesia's Ministry of Health (Kemenkes) in mid-June 2026.

## Official Announcement from Kemenkes

The closure was officially announced by **Aji Muhawarman**, Head of the Communications and Public Information Bureau at Kemenkes, on Tuesday, June 16, 2026. According to Muhawarman, the swift government action was designed to protect the public from dangerous medical practices conducted without proper permits or legally recognized competencies.

PRIME Skin Clinic was located in Badung Regency — Bali's most densely developed tourism zone, which has become a hub for premium beauty clinics targeting international tourists and expatriates.

## Three Serious Violations

A thorough inspection and investigation uncovered three critical violations:

### 1. Not Registered in the Kemenkes System
The facility was **entirely unregistered** in the Ministry of Health's Healthcare Facility Administration Information System (SIAF), meaning it had been operating completely outside the government's official oversight framework.

### 2. No Operating License
PRIME Skin Clinic had no official operating permit, yet continued to provide medical aesthetic services to members of the public — including injections, laser treatments, and other invasive procedures that carry direct risks to patient safety.

### 3. Foreign Medical Staff Without STR and SIP
The most serious violation: the clinic employed foreign medical and health workers from **Russia** and **Armenia** without the documentation required under Indonesian law. Regulations in Indonesia stipulate that medical services may only be provided by qualified healthcare professionals holding a **Registration Certificate (STR)** and a **Practice License (SIP)** issued by the relevant Indonesian authorities. None of the foreign staff met these requirements.

## Multi-Agency Enforcement Action

The enforcement action against PRIME Skin Clinic required coordination across multiple government ministries and agencies, led by Kemenkes in conjunction with:

- Ministry of Foreign Affairs
- Directorate General of Immigration
- Ministry of Manpower
- Ministry of Home Affairs
- State Intelligence Agency (BIN)
- Indonesian National Armed Forces (TNI)
- Bali Provincial Health Department

This whole-of-government response underscores how seriously Indonesian authorities treat cases of illegal healthcare facilities employing undocumented foreign workers — particularly in Bali, where the density of tourism creates conditions that can attract unlicensed operators.

## A Warning to Other Clinics in Bali

Kemenkes reaffirmed its commitment to protecting the public through rigorous healthcare quality monitoring, stating that all violations will be handled firmly under applicable law to preserve the integrity of the national health system.

The closure of PRIME Skin Clinic sends a clear message to other beauty clinics in Bali — especially those targeting premium tourists and digital nomads — that regulatory compliance is not optional. Bali's growing reputation as a global wellness tourism destination must be built on verified, safe, and properly licensed healthcare providers.

Members of the public who previously used PRIME Skin Clinic's services and have medical concerns are urged to consult a certified healthcare facility immediately for evaluation and appropriate follow-up care.
