In a show of unified governance, the **Badung Regency Government** and **Polres Badung** (Badung District Police) reaffirmed their joint commitment to maintaining public order and security (kamtibmas) across one of Indonesia's most internationally recognized tourist destinations—on the occasion of the **80th National Police Day (HUT Bhayangkara ke-80)**.

## High-Level Attendance at Ceremony

Badung Regency Secretary (Sekda) **Ida Bagus Surya Suamba** and Badung Regional Legislative Council (DPRD) Chairman **I Gusti Anom Gumanti** both attended the formal ceremony held by Polres Badung at Lapangan Mangupraja Mandala, the Badung Government Center (Puspem Badung), on Wednesday, July 1, 2026.

Their presence sent a clear institutional signal: the Badung regional government regards security not as a police matter alone, but as a shared responsibility that directly determines the sustainability of the region's tourism economy.

## Theme: 80 Years Serving the People

This year's national Police Day carries the theme **"80 Years of Service, Police for the People"**—reaffirming Indonesia's National Police (Polri) commitment to delivering professional, modern, and trustworthy law enforcement, protection, and public service.

In Badung—which encompasses globally recognized tourist zones such as Kuta, Seminyak, Canggu, Nusa Dua, and Jimbaran—maintaining security carries an unusually high economic stakes. Millions of domestic and international tourists pass through the regency each year, making safety a prerequisite for prosperity.

## DIJAGA: Community Surveillance Meets Local Wisdom

One standout innovation highlighted at the ceremony was the **Digital Jagabaya (DIJAGA) application**—a technology-driven surveillance system that blends modern infrastructure with Balinese communal values.

DIJAGA extends the CCTV network down to the **village (desa) and banjar (neighborhood council)** level—covering areas not yet reached by the government's existing camera infrastructure. Community members in each banjar install cameras at strategic points that feed into a centralized monitoring system, enabling real-time environmental surveillance.

The practical results have been significant: faster detection of suspicious activity, shorter police response times, and a measurable increase in the sense of security felt by both residents and tourists.

## Government CCTV Network: A Force Multiplier

Beyond DIJAGA, the Badung Regency's own institutional CCTV network has proven its worth in helping police investigate and solve cases across the regency's jurisdiction—including several high-profile incidents involving foreign nationals (WNA) that garnered national media attention in recent months.

The synergy between the regency's infrastructure and law enforcement has created a more effective security ecosystem, where technology amplifies the reach of human policing.

### Safe Tourism, Sustainable Economy

The Polres Badung Chief expressed hopes that this inter-agency collaboration would only deepen over time—keeping the regency's tourist corridors secure, predictable, and welcoming.

The message to investors, business owners, and travelers considering Bali is unambiguous: **Badung takes security seriously**. And in a global tourism landscape where safety is increasingly a deciding factor, that commitment is not just reassuring—it's a competitive advantage.
