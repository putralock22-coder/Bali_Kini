Bali's hospitality industry is entering what could be its most promising stretch of the year. The Indonesian Hotel and Restaurant Association (PHRI) Bali chapter is forecasting hotel occupancy rates across the Island of the Gods to climb between 10 and 12 percent during June and July 2026, compared to earlier months in the year.

## Foreign Tourists Leading the Surge

The occupancy boost is being driven largely by an influx of international visitors who are finding Bali increasingly affordable as the Indonesian rupiah weakens against the US dollar. With the exchange rate holding in the range of Rp17,000 to Rp18,000 per dollar, accommodation and day-to-day spending in Bali represents exceptional value for tourists from dollar-using countries.

The real-world effect is tangible: several foreign visitors have reportedly extended their stays in Bali, stretching their travel budgets further than originally planned. One Australian tourist noted being able to add two extra nights to their holiday — plus buy more souvenirs to bring home — thanks to the favorable exchange rate.

### Key Source Markets: Australia and India

Australia remains one of Bali's largest visitor markets, with average spending per person estimated at USD 1,500 to USD 2,000 per trip. India is also firmly on PHRI's radar as a growing market, especially as direct flight connectivity from major Indian cities to Bali continues to expand.

## A Perfect Storm: School Holidays and Galungan

The June-July spike is not solely a currency story. Two major domestic factors are also fueling demand:

1. **School holidays** — The mid-June to July period coincides with the Indonesian school break, drawing thousands of domestic families to Bali for leisure travel
2. **Galungan and Kuningan** — These major Hindu holy days in Bali fall in mid-June 2026, attracting visitors who want to witness Bali's spectacular religious ceremonies and temple processions firsthand

## Positive Trend Since Q1 2026

The current momentum builds on an already positive start to the year. In the first quarter of 2026, PHRI Bali member hotels recorded a 2.4 percent year-on-year increase in occupancy compared to Q1 2025 — a signal that the tourism sector's post-pandemic recovery is well on track.

### Room Rates: Stability Over Speculation

Despite surging demand, most PHRI Bali members have opted not to raise room rates. The prevailing strategy among hoteliers is to prioritize high occupancy over short-term revenue maximization — a deliberate move in a market where accommodation choices range from five-star resorts to boutique villas and budget guesthouses, keeping competition fierce.

## Broader Economic Ripple Effects

Rising hotel occupancy has a multiplier effect across Bali's wider tourism ecosystem. Restaurants, spas, vehicle rentals, tour guides, and souvenir vendors all stand to benefit from increased visitor traffic. Bank Indonesia's Bali representative office has projected that the province's economy will grow positively in Q2 2026, with the tourism sector identified as one of the primary growth engines.

With the peak season aligning with school holidays and the sacred Galungan celebration, Bali's tourism industry is cautiously optimistic that mid-2026 will rank among the sector's best performing periods in recent years.
