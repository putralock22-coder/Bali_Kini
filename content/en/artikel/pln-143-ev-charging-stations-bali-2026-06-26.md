PT PLN (Persero) Unit Induk Distribusi (UID) Bali has activated 143 public electric vehicle charging stations (SPKLU) distributed across 94 strategic locations throughout the island. The expanding network is designed to support the rapidly growing community of electric vehicle users on Bali — and to eliminate range anxiety as a barrier to EV adoption.

## Bali's Most Comprehensive EV Charging Network

As of June 2026, PLN and its partners have collectively deployed a total of 196 SPKLU units across Bali. Of these, 143 are operated directly by PLN, while the remaining 53 are managed by third-party partners. The flagship facility is a dedicated SPKLU hub on Jalan Hayam Wuruk, Denpasar — recognized as one of the most complete electric vehicle charging centers in eastern Indonesia, offering multiple fast-charging options.

With this coverage, EV users can now find a charging point on average every 30 kilometers across Bali's main road network — a density that makes island-wide electric travel practical for both residents and tourists.

## Over 14,000 Electric Vehicles on Bali's Roads

The infrastructure rollout reflects surging EV adoption on the island. Data shows that as of May 2026, Bali's electric vehicle population had reached 14,318 units — comprising more than 4,000 four-wheeled electric cars and nearly 12,000 electric motorcycles and scooters.

This growth has been fueled by government incentive programs, lower per-kilometer energy costs compared to fossil fuels, and rising environmental awareness among Balinese consumers. The tourism sector has also played a role: hotels, transport companies, and tour operators are increasingly shifting their fleets to electric to meet the sustainability expectations of international visitors.

## A Showcase for Indonesia's Clean Energy Transition

Bali's position as a global tourism destination makes it an ideal showcase for Indonesia's energy transition ambitions. Foreign visitors arriving from countries with mature EV markets — Australia, Europe, the United States, and Japan — bring expectations for accessible charging infrastructure.

PLN Bali has signaled plans to continue expanding the SPKLU network, with priority corridors including the tourist-heavy regions of Badung, Denpasar, Gianyar, and Karangasem, as well as cross-island transit routes. Integration with hotels, restaurants, tourist attractions, and rest areas is also a key part of the expansion strategy.

## Supporting the National EV Acceleration Program

The deployment is aligned with Indonesia's national program to accelerate electric vehicle adoption as a cornerstone of the country's energy transition and emissions reduction commitments. PLN's efforts in Bali serve as a model that can be replicated across other major Indonesian destinations.

For residents, the expanding charging network represents a concrete shift toward cleaner daily transportation. For visitors, it signals that Bali is serious about sustainable tourism — not just as a marketing message, but as an operational reality measurable in kilometers between charge points.

Bali's electric revolution is charging forward, one SPKLU at a time.
