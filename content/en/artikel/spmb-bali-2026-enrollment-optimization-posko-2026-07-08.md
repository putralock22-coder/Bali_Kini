The Bali Provincial Office of Education, Youth, and Sports (Disdikpora) has opened **capacity optimization posts** for the 2026 New Student Enrollment System (SPMB), targeting junior high graduates who were not accepted at their chosen schools in the regular selection rounds. The move aims to ensure that no graduating student is left without a place in a state senior high school or vocational school.

## How the Optimization Posts Work

Students who were not placed during the main selection can walk into two types of service points: the **Disdikpora Provincial Office** or the **nearest state school** to their address. Officers at the posts will cross-reference the student's registration number against available data in the SPMB application system and match them with schools that still have vacant seats.

The posts are especially important given the stark imbalance in applicant distribution: schools in city centers are heavily oversubscribed, while several state senior high schools in more rural or peripheral areas are operating well below their intake quota.

## 11 State Senior High Schools Filled Below 40 Percent

Results from the Phase I SPMB selection revealed that at least **11 state senior high schools in Bali** had filled fewer than 40 percent of their available spots. This highlights a mismatch between student preference — heavily skewed toward well-known schools in urban centers — and the actual distribution of available places across the island.

**Phase II SPMB results** are scheduled for announcement on **July 9, 2026**, and are expected to help redistribute students toward schools with remaining capacity.

### 94,599 Seats for 64,021 Projected Graduates

On paper, Bali has more than enough room for every junior high school graduate:

- State senior high school (SMA) seats: **42,236**
- State vocational school (SMK) seats: **52,363**
- **Total capacity**: 94,599 seats

The projected number of junior high school graduates entering the next level in 2026/2027 is **64,021** — meaning there is a theoretical surplus of over 30,000 seats. The challenge, therefore, is not one of capacity, but of **distribution and student preference**.

## Vocational Schools Gaining Ground

A notable trend in SPMB 2026 is rising interest in vocational schools (SMK). Families are increasingly viewing SMK as a pragmatic option, since graduates are equipped with job-ready skills and can enter the workforce directly — bypassing the need for a university degree.

This shift aligns well with Bali's labor market reality. The island's tourism industry is a perennial absorber of skilled technical workers in hospitality, culinary arts, food service, and information technology — areas where a strong SMK background provides a direct pathway to employment.

## Ombudsman Tightens Oversight

The Bali Ombudsman office has stepped up monitoring of SPMB 2026 processes to prevent recurring irregularities. The focus is on rooting out **informal name insertions** by influential figures and the **misuse of quota allocations** that bypass the proper admission lanes.

Stricter oversight is expected to uphold the integrity of the enrollment process — ensuring placement is determined by academic merit and zoning, not personal connections or back-channel influence.
