Bali has taken a landmark step in addressing one of its most persistent challenges. On Wednesday, July 8, 2026, state investment vehicle Danantara officially broke ground on the **PSEL Denpasar Raya** (Waste-to-Energy) facility in Pedungan Village, South Denpasar — a Rp 3 trillion project that promises to transform the island's approach to garbage management.

## A Rp 3 Trillion Answer to Bali's Waste Crisis

The PSEL Denpasar Raya facility, operated by Nusantara Bali New Energy under the Danantara umbrella, is designed to process up to **1,500 tons of waste per day** — exceeding the combined daily waste output of Denpasar City and Badung Regency, which stands at approximately 1,200 tons. On an annual basis, the plant targets processing **more than 500,000 tons of waste per year**, covering over 40 percent of Bali's total waste burden.

### European-Standard Technology

The plant will employ **moving grate incinerator** technology equipped with a multilayer **Air Pollution Control System (APCS)** that meets European emission standards. This ensures that the combustion process does not generate hazardous air pollutants for surrounding communities.

Key environmental metrics:
- Reduction of landfill (TPA) emissions by up to **80 percent**
- Carbon emission reduction of approximately **640,000 tons of CO₂ per year**

## High-Level Attendance, PLN Power Deal Signed

The groundbreaking ceremony drew prominent national figures including Coordinating Minister for Food Zulkifli Hasan, Environment Minister Mohammad Jumhur Hidayat, Bali Governor Wayan Koster, and Denpasar Mayor I Gusti Ngurah Jaya Negara. The commander of the Udayana Military Regional Command also attended, signaling broad institutional support for the national strategic project.

A **Power Purchase Agreement (PPA)** between Nusantara Bali New Energy and PT PLN (Persero) was signed during the ceremony, paving the way for electricity generated from waste to be fed directly into Bali's power grid.

## Tourism Image and 1,200 Jobs on the Line

Governor Wayan Koster emphasized that beyond its environmental significance, the PSEL project would directly strengthen Bali's reputation as a clean, sustainable destination — a narrative the island has struggled to maintain amid recurring media coverage of its waste problem.

> "This project is not just an environmental solution — it's an investment in the future of Bali's tourism," said Koster at the ceremony.

The project is also expected to generate approximately **1,200 jobs** during construction and operations, delivering meaningful economic impact for local communities.

### Target: Operational by End of 2027

Covering a 6-hectare site near the Benoa Port area, construction is targeted for completion by the **end of 2027**, with full operations expected by the first half of 2028. This marks the first PSEL project in the era of President Prabowo Subianto's administration, and the first high-technology waste management facility of its kind in Bali.

If successful, the project is expected to serve as a replicable model for other Indonesian cities grappling with similar urban waste challenges.
