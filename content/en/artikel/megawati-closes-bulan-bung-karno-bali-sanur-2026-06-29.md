PDI Perjuangan Chairperson **Megawati Soekarnoputri** presided over the closing ceremony of the **2026 Bulan Bung Karno** (Bung Karno Month) at the Bali Beach Convention Center, The Meru, Sanur, Denpasar, on Sunday (28/6/2026). The event marked the grand finale of a month-long series of commemorations held throughout June across Bali and Indonesia.

## Remarkable Village Participation Across Bali

Bali Governor **Wayan Koster** delivered a direct report to Megawati, announcing that **625 out of 636 villages** (desa) in Bali had successfully organized their own Bulan Bung Karno activities — a participation rate exceeding 98 percent of all villages on the island.

The total funds raised and utilized across all participating villages amounted to **Rp 10.5 billion**, drawn from village budget allocations (ADD) and community contributions. These funds financed a wide range of activities including cultural performances, national awareness seminars, social programs, and civic events.

## From Banten to Bali: A National Journey

The 2026 Bulan Bung Karno series was formally opened in early June at **Museum Multatuli, Lebak, Banten**, by PDI Perjuangan Secretary-General **Hasto Kristiyanto**. The choice of Lebak as the opening venue carried deep historical significance, rooted in the legacy of Indonesian nationalism that Bung Karno championed.

The closing ceremony was then centered in **Bali**, a province long recognized as one of PDI Perjuangan's strongest bases of support and the most active region in organizing the commemorations.

Megawati was accompanied by her son, **Prananda Prabowo**, along with senior party officials and cadres from across the country.

## The Message: Strength From the People

At the closing ceremony, PDI Perjuangan reaffirmed its core conviction: the strength of the party stems from the rakyat — the ordinary people — and not from political power alone. This message was woven throughout the Bulan Bung Karno 2026 theme, which emphasized mutual cooperation (*gotong royong*), village self-reliance, and the spirit of nationalism bequeathed by Indonesia's founding president.

The ceremony also featured regional cultural performances as a tribute to the rich diversity of the archipelago — in keeping with Bung Karno's vision of a unified but culturally vibrant Indonesia.

## A Month of Community Celebrations

Throughout June 2026, hundreds of villages across Bali organized activities in honor of Bulan Bung Karno. Events ranged from nationalism seminars, speech competitions, and wayang puppet performances to traditional arts, community service programs, and tree-planting drives. These were not simply political commemorations — they became genuine community-building occasions that brought villages together.

The high participation rate of 625 villages reflects how deeply the values associated with Bung Karno — independence, self-sufficiency, national pride — remain embedded in Balinese village life.

## Why Sanur?

The selection of Sanur as the closing venue was not without strategic meaning. This coastal district of Denpasar is home to the **Sanur Special Economic Zone (KEK) for Health** and serves as a key hub for tourism and economic development in Bali. Holding a nationally significant event here underscored the region's growing role as a center of cultural, economic, and civic activity.
