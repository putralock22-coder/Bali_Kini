Indonesian aviation has reached a significant milestone this week. TransNusa airline officially announced the launch of direct flights from Ngurah Rai International Airport in Denpasar, Bali, to Matahora Airport in Wakatobi, Southeast Sulawesi. This makes TransNusa the **first airline** to operate a direct connection between Bali and this world-class marine tourism destination.

## Flight Schedule and Frequency

The Denpasar–Wakatobi route is set to begin operations on **July 16, 2026**, with three flights per week. In addition, TransNusa is also launching a Denpasar–Waingapu route, which starts one day earlier on **July 15, 2026**, with four weekly flights.

Ticket sales for both routes opened on June 30, 2026. Travelers looking to explore these new destinations can book promo fares starting from **IDR 1,849,000** for the Bali–Wakatobi route. Tickets are available through TransNusa's official website and major online travel platforms.

## Why This Matters for Tourism

Previously, traveling from Bali to Wakatobi required transiting through Makassar or Kendari, making the total journey time well over six hours. The new direct route will dramatically reduce travel time and open up Wakatobi to a much wider audience, particularly international visitors who arrive via Bali and want to continue their Indonesian adventure.

### Wakatobi: A World-Class Diving Paradise

Wakatobi is widely recognized as one of the best diving destinations in the world. The name "Wakatobi" is an acronym derived from the four main islands in the archipelago: **Wangi-Wangi, Kaledupa, Tomia, and Binongko**. The entire area forms part of Wakatobi National Park, covering over 1.3 million hectares of ocean with some of the highest marine biodiversity on the planet.

International divers have long dreamed of visiting Wakatobi for its crystal-clear waters, pristine coral reefs, and hundreds of tropical fish species. With the new direct flight from Bali, access to this underwater paradise is now far more convenient.

## Part of TransNusa's Major Expansion

The Bali-Wakatobi and Bali-Waingapu routes are part of TransNusa's ambitious mid-2026 expansion strategy. In the same period, the airline also launched the international **Jakarta–Bangkok** route, marking a bold step into the Southeast Asian aviation market.

These moves align with Indonesia's broader policy goal of distributing tourist arrivals beyond Bali. Concerns about over-tourism on the island have been growing, and by creating direct air links to emerging destinations like Wakatobi and Waingapu, the government hopes to spread visitor traffic more evenly across the archipelago.

## Opportunity for Multi-Destination Itineraries

For travelers planning a trip to Indonesia with Bali as the entry point, these new routes open up exciting multi-destination itineraries. Visitors can enjoy Bali's culture, temples, and beaches, then fly directly to Wakatobi for world-class diving — all within a single, seamlessly connected trip.

The expansion of Bali's air connectivity also benefits the island's tourism ecosystem, as more travelers using Bali as a hub naturally generates positive economic ripple effects for local businesses, hotels, and service providers.
