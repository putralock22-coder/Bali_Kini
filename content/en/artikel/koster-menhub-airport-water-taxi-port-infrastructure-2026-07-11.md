Bali's chronic traffic problem may finally be getting a serious multi-pronged response. Minister of Transportation Dudy Purwagandhi and Bali Governor Wayan Koster held a strategic coordination meeting at the Kertha Sabha Meeting Room in Denpasar, reaching several key agreements to revamp the island's connectivity by air, sea, and land.

## Letkol Wisnu Airport: A Second Gateway for Bali

The meeting's headline outcome was the formal push to develop Letkol Wisnu Airport in the Gerokgak sub-district of Buleleng Regency. Governor Koster officially submitted the proposal to the Ministry of Transportation, requesting the airport be upgraded into a specialized aviation facility.

Once developed, Letkol Wisnu is intended to serve as an emergency landing option when the main I Gusti Ngurah Rai International Airport faces disruptions, while also accommodating private jets, charter flights, cargo logistics, and equipment transport. The Bali Provincial Government has already established a dedicated business entity and has opened investment channels for private sector participation.

Minister Dudy voiced his support and committed to following up through the ministry's regulatory channels.

## Water Taxi: Cut the Ngurah Rai–Canggu Journey to 30 Minutes

Among the most eye-catching proposals discussed was the Water Taxi program in Badung waters. This fast passenger boat service would connect the Ngurah Rai Airport area directly to Canggu — slashing what is currently a one-and-a-half to two-hour road journey down to just **30 minutes** on the water.

Minister Dudy set a target of **November 2026** for the water taxi facility to be operational, pending completion of coastal normalization work by the Ministry of Public Works. If realized on schedule, the service would be a game changer for tourists looking to bypass Bali's notoriously congested southern road network.

### Cross-Ministry Coordination Is Key

The water taxi project requires coordinated development of jetty facilities and coastal infrastructure. Smooth inter-ministerial coordination will be critical to hitting the November 2026 deadline.

## Celukan Bawang Port: North Bali's New Crossing Hub

On Bali's northern coast, Celukan Bawang Port was a major focus of the meeting. The Transport Minister instructed PT Pelindo to accelerate the port's revitalization so that it can handle ferry crossings from Jangkar Port (Situbondo) and Ketapang (Banyuwangi).

This move is part of a broader strategy to distribute tourist and vehicle flows more evenly, reducing the pressure on the heavily used Gilimanuk–Denpasar corridor, which is notorious for severe gridlock during peak holiday seasons.

### Other Port Developments on the Agenda

The meeting also addressed several other port projects across Bali:

- **Kusamba Port** (Klungkung) — improved ferry crossing facilities
- **Amed Port** (Karangasem) — to be offered to private investors as a logistics or tourism terminal
- **Sangsit Port** (Buleleng) — similar private-sector model as Amed

## A Vision for Distributed Connectivity

Bali has long been over-reliant on a single point of entry — Ngurah Rai Airport — and a land road network prone to congestion. These agreements mark a meaningful step toward a more distributed connectivity model that spreads the load across multiple airports, ports, and water routes.

With concrete targets on the table, including a water taxi expected to go live before the end of 2026, residents and tourism stakeholders now have reason to expect real, near-term change in how people move around the island.
