A total of 78 competitive swimmers representing **18 Asian nations** have arrived in Bali for the **12th Asian Open Water Swimming Championship 2026**. The three-day competition will take place from **June 13 to 15, 2026**, in the open waters of the InterContinental Bali Resort Beach in Jimbaran Bay, Badung Regency.

Today, Friday June 12, athletes are completing warm-up sessions and course trials in Jimbaran's open sea waters ahead of tomorrow's official competition start.

## Bahrain's Last-Minute Entry Makes It 18 Nations

The event was originally scheduled for 17 participating countries, but Bahrain confirmed its entry by sending one athlete alongside two officials, bringing the total to 18 nations. Combined with 49 officials from all delegations, the championship has drawn a total of **127 participants** to Bali.

Bahrain's participation signals the growing interest in open water swimming across West Asia and broadens the geographic reach of a championship traditionally dominated by East and Southeast Asian nations.

## Indonesia Fields Six Competitive Athletes

As the host nation, Indonesia enters the competition with **six athletes** — four men and four women. The national team has spent the past two days training in Jimbaran Bay, acclimatizing to local sea conditions including water temperature, visibility, and currents before the events begin.

Indonesian athletes will be competing against nations with long open water swimming traditions, including Japan, China, and South Korea.

## Three Disciplines on the Programme

The championship programme features three main disciplines:

- **5 kilometres** — men's and women's individual events
- **Mixed Relay 4 x 1,500 metres** — combined team event
- **10 kilometres** — men's and women's individual events

The 10-kilometre event is considered the flagship race and the most anticipated of the entire championship, demanding exceptional physical endurance and mental toughness across an open-sea course.

## Why Jimbaran Bay

The choice of Jimbaran Bay as the venue was deliberate. In June, the southern Bali coastline offers relatively calm swells, good water clarity, and a stunning backdrop of white sand beaches and lush hillsides — conditions that are both competition-ready and visually spectacular for international media coverage.

For visiting athletes and officials, competing in Bali also offers a rare cultural dimension: many are expected to extend their stay to experience Galungan celebrations on June 17 and the opening of the **Bali Arts Festival (PKB XLVIII)** on June 13.

## A Pillar of Bali's Sport Tourism Strategy

The Bali Provincial Government and Tourism Agency view the championship as a key component of the island's long-term **sport tourism** development strategy. By hosting events that attract hundreds of athletes and officials from across Asia, Bali is positioning itself not only as a leisure destination but also as a world-class venue for elite sporting competition.

This championship joins a packed 2026 calendar that includes the Bali & Beyond Travel Fair, the Red Bull Cliff Diving World Series, and the Bali Tourism Run in Jatiluwih — all designed to elevate Bali's profile as a sport and cultural tourism powerhouse.
