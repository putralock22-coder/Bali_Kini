## Day 11 of Bali's Grand Cultural Festival

The 48th Pesta Kesenian Bali (PKB) — Bali Arts Festival — continues its month-long run on Tuesday, June 23, 2026, with another full day of cultural performances and competitions. Now in its 11th day, the festival brings together thousands of artists from across the island, celebrating Bali's rich artistic traditions under the theme **"Atma Kerthi: Jiwa Sidha Parisudha"** — a Balinese phrase meaning the glorification and purification of the human soul toward perfect, clear, and sacred completion.

## Today's Program at a Glance

### 08:00 WITA — Pecukan Kite-Making Finals

The morning kicks off at **8 a.m. WITA** with the grand finale of the **Pacentokan (Competition) Membuat Layangan Pecukan** — a traditional Balinese kite-making contest. The Pecukan is a distinctive kite shaped like a cassava leaf, deeply rooted in Balinese folk tradition. Contestants from different regencies compete in both craftsmanship and flight technique, adhering strictly to ancestral guidelines.

This event represents one of the most charming corners of PKB, where everyday village games are elevated into cultural competitions.

### 11:00 WITA — Taman Penasar Drama Competition

At **11 a.m. WITA**, the stage shifts to **Wimbakara (Competition) Taman Penasar**, a dramatic arts contest that blends classical Balinese theatrical traditions with humor and moral storytelling. The Penasar characters — comedic court attendants in Balinese dance dramas — serve as witty translators between the archaic Kawi language and everyday Balinese, making the performances both entertaining and culturally instructive.

### Afternoon — Women's Gong Kebyar Parade

The day's highlight arrives in the afternoon: the **Parade Gong Kebyar Wanita**, or Women's Gong Kebyar Parade. All-female gamelan ensembles from across Bali take to the main stage, performing the dynamic and percussive Gong Kebyar style with precision and passion. The presence of women musicians in this traditionally male-dominated art form has become a celebrated symbol of gender equality and female empowerment in Balinese culture.

## About PKB XLVIII 2026

PKB XLVIII runs **June 13 to July 11, 2026**, spread across **16 performance venues** centered at the Taman Budaya Art Centre in Denpasar. This year's edition mobilizes over **23,000 Balinese artists** and, for the first time, features the **Bali World Culture Celebration (BWCC)** — an international showcase bringing together **10 art groups** and approximately **260 international performers** from the United States, China, Timor Leste, South Korea, Japan, India, and Hong Kong.

## Free for Everyone

All PKB XLVIII performances are **free and open to the public**. No tickets are required — simply show up and immerse yourself in one of Southeast Asia's largest traditional arts festivals. For the full daily schedule, visit the official website at **pestakesenianbali.id**.
