## Bali's Iconic "Gate of Heaven" Temple Suspends Tourism Activities

Thursday, June 18, 2026 — One of Bali's most beloved and photographed sites, **Pura Lempuyang Luhur** in Desa Adat Purwayu, Abang District, Karangasem Regency, has temporarily closed its doors to all tourists for five days — from Wednesday, June 17 through Sunday, June 21, 2026. The closure is in observance of the **pujawali** (piodalan), the sacred annual anniversary ceremony of the temple.

The peak of the ceremony falls today, Thursday, June 18 — coinciding with the ongoing Galungan-Umanis celebrations observed by Hindus across Bali. The convergence of these two sacred occasions gives the temple grounds a deeply spiritual and reverent atmosphere.

## Thousands of Worshippers Arrive from Across the Archipelago

Bendesa Adat (traditional village head) of Purwayu, **I Nyoman Jati**, confirmed that during the pujawali period, thousands of **pemedek** — devout Hindu worshippers — are expected to travel from across Bali and from other islands to pay their respects at Pura Lempuyang.

"This closure is to protect the comfort and sanctity of the worshippers during their prayers at Pura Lempuyang. We ask for understanding from all visitors," said Nyoman Jati, as reported by local media.

Pura Lempuyang is not just any temple. It holds the distinguished status as one of the **Sad Kahyangan** — Bali's six most sacred temples, believed to be the spiritual pillars of the island. Each pujawali here is a major religious event that draws pilgrims from far and wide.

## The Famous "Gate of Heaven" — Temporarily Off-Limits

Pura Lempuyang is world-famous for its breathtaking **"Gate of Heaven"** — a pair of symmetrical stone gates that frame a perfect view of Mount Agung against a blue sky. This iconic image has made the temple one of the most photographed spots in Southeast Asia, drawing visitors from every corner of the globe.

During the pujawali period, the entire temple complex — including the ascending pathway, the gate area, and the upper shrines at approximately 1,175 meters above sea level — is exclusively accessible to worshippers. No exceptions are made for tourists wishing to photograph or sightsee.

### Adjust Your Travel Plans

Visitors who had planned to visit Pura Lempuyang between June 17 and 21, 2026 are advised to reschedule their trip.

**The temple is scheduled to reopen for tourism on Monday, June 22, 2026.**

In the meantime, plenty of spectacular alternatives await in the Karangasem area:
- **Tirta Gangga** — a royal water garden with ornamental koi ponds
- **Taman Ujung** — a historic water palace built by the Kings of Karangasem
- **Virgin Beach (Pasir Putih)** — a secluded white-sand beach with calm turquoise water

## Respect for Tradition — The Heart of Bali's Tourism Model

The temporary closure of Pura Lempuyang reflects a principle deeply embedded in Balinese society: religious and cultural practices always take precedence over commercial activity. This approach is guided by the Balinese philosophy of **Tri Hita Karana** — the harmony of relationships between humans, nature, and God.

The Bali Provincial Government has been actively promoting quality tourism over mass tourism, and decisions like this — prioritizing the sanctity of sacred spaces — are central to that vision. Visitors who respect these boundaries ultimately contribute to a more sustainable and authentic Bali experience for everyone.

### Visiting Pura Lempuyang — Practical Tips

When the temple reopens on June 22:
- **Opening hours:** 07:00–17:00 WITA (local time)
- **Dress code:** Sarong and sash required (available for rent at the entrance)
- **Ascent:** Several hundred steps to reach the upper temple — wear comfortable shoes
- **Best time for photos:** Early morning, before crowds arrive and before midday haze

Confirm reopening with local guides or your hotel concierge before visiting.
