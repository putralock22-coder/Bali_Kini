The Indonesian government is accelerating its push to transform Bali from a world-renowned leisure destination into a global financial hub. Plans for the Indonesia Financial Centre (IFC) to be established as a standalone Special Economic Zone (KEK) in Bali are becoming increasingly concrete as regulators work to finalize its legal framework.

## A New KEK Standing on Its Own

Unlike Bali's two existing special economic zones—KEK Kura-Kura Bali on Serangan Island and KEK Sanur—the Indonesia Financial Centre will operate as an entirely separate KEK. This independence is intended to give the IFC a clear identity, a dedicated regulatory environment, and an unambiguous focus as a world-class international financial center.

The government is currently refining regulations that will serve as the legal foundation for the Financial Sector KEK, covering governance structures, investment facilities, and the full range of incentives needed to attract globally competitive financial institutions.

## Tax Incentives to Rival Dubai and Singapore

One of the IFC's headline offerings is its highly competitive tax incentive package. Potential tax rates could reach as low as 0 percent—on par with rates offered by globally recognized financial centers such as the Dubai International Financial Centre (DIFC) and Singapore.

These fiscal advantages are expected to attract international banks, global asset management firms, sovereign wealth funds, and fintech companies seeking a regional headquarters in Southeast Asia.

## A Boost for High-Value Business Tourism

Bank Indonesia's Bali Representative Office sees the IFC's arrival as a major catalyst for bleisure tourism—the growing segment of travel that blends business activities with leisure experiences.

This category of visitor is particularly attractive because they tend to spend more per day and stay longer. Business travelers attending conferences or investment meetings frequently extend their trips to explore Bali's beaches, culture, and cuisine, driving higher revenues across the hospitality and retail sectors.

## Bali's Infrastructure Already World-Ready

Bali is well-positioned to support international business mobility. Direct flight connections to dozens of global cities, world-class accommodation, international dining, and premium lifestyle amenities are already firmly in place and continuing to grow.

The track record of Bali's existing KEKs reinforces this confidence. KEK Sanur alone attracted 279,804 visits during the first quarter of 2026—clear evidence that the special economic zone concept in Bali is gaining strong traction in both domestic and international markets.

## Beyond Conventional Tourism

The IFC initiative is part of a broader strategy to diversify Bali's economic identity. Rather than relying solely on beaches and cultural attractions, Bali is positioning itself as a multi-functional destination that offers a competitive global business and investment ecosystem.

This ambition also addresses a long-standing concern about Bali's economic vulnerability—by reducing its dependence on tourism alone, the island's economy becomes more resilient against global shocks and market fluctuations.
