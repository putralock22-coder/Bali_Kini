Indonesia's Meteorology, Climatology, and Geophysics Agency (**BMKG**) has issued a formal **early warning for high waves** covering the period of **July 2–5, 2026**. The waters south of Bali are among the affected zones, with wave heights forecast to reach **2.5 to 4 meters** — conditions that pose genuine risks to fishing vessels, ferries, and coastal activities.

## Which Waters Are Affected?

The warning covers a wide arc of southern Indonesian waters, including:

- **The South Sea of East Java** and **Bali**
- **The southern waters of Bali Island**
- The Indian Ocean south of Lampung, Banten, West Java, Central Java, and Yogyakarta
- Southern waters of **West Nusa Tenggara (NTB)**

These are among the most active maritime corridors in the archipelago, carrying inter-island ferries, fishing fleets, and supporting some of Bali's most visited beach tourism zones.

## What Is Causing the Swells?

BMKG attributes the heightened sea state to strong wind patterns sweeping across the southern hemisphere. In the area south of the equator — which includes Bali — winds are blowing from the **east to southeast** at speeds of **5 to 25 knots**. In the northern hemisphere zone, winds are tracking from the southeast to southwest at 5–20 knots.

The combination of sustained wind speeds and direction is pushing ocean swells southward and creating dangerous conditions along exposed coastlines.

## Who Needs to Be Most Careful?

BMKG has issued specific advisories for:

- **Traditional fishermen** operating small vessels, urged not to put to sea during the warning period
- **Inter-island ferry operators**, particularly those running routes between Bali–Java (Ketapang-Gilimanuk) and Bali–Lombok (Padang Bai-Lembar)
- **Cargo and barge operators** transiting southern waters
- **Coastal communities** who should move away from the shoreline during peak swell periods

Fishermen are reminded to monitor warning signals and return to port immediately should conditions deteriorate.

## Advice for Tourists

Visitors planning water-based activities at Bali's popular beaches — including **Kuta, Seminyak, Sanur, Padang Bai, and Nusa Dua** — should check the latest weather updates before heading out. Activities such as surfing, snorkeling, diving, and island-hopping boat trips to Nusa Lembongan or Nusa Penida should be reconsidered during the July 2–5 window.

Safety authorities emphasize that even experienced swimmers can be overwhelmed by rip currents and swells during high-wave events.

## How to Monitor BMKG Updates

Real-time weather and maritime warnings are available through:

- **BMKG official website**: bmkg.go.id and maritim.bmkg.go.id
- **infoBMKG smartphone app** (available for Android and iOS)
- **BMKG Regional Office III in Denpasar**, which publishes updated maritime forecasts for Bali and surrounding waters

BMKG stresses that ocean conditions can change rapidly. Always consult the latest data before making any decision to venture out to sea.
