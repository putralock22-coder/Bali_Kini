Bali's Provincial Secretary (Sekda) Dewa Made Indra opened the Regional Poverty Reduction Coordination Team (TKPK) meeting on Tuesday, June 24, 2026, calling on all stakeholders to rally around two critical pillars: synergy and acceleration. The meeting, held in Denpasar, brought together poverty reduction teams from across the island's nine regencies and cities.

## A Province Ahead of the Curve

Bali currently records a poverty rate of just **3.42 percent** — one of the lowest among all Indonesian provinces and a marked improvement from 3.80 percent the previous year. Despite this achievement, Dewa Made Indra stressed that Bali should not rest on its progress, as cases of extreme poverty still demand urgent, coordinated intervention.

"These two keywords — strengthening synergy and acceleration — must become the focus of all stakeholders," the secretary said at the forum's opening.

## Coordination Across Districts and Cities

The TKPK meeting brought together provincial and district/city poverty reduction teams to evaluate progress, share strategies, and identify gaps requiring additional support from the provincial level. Each district was asked to present data on their local poverty interventions and flag bottlenecks slowing results.

## A Multi-Sector Challenge

Dewa Made Indra made clear that poverty alleviation cannot be handled by any single agency in isolation. Businesses, local adat institutions, and community organizations must all play active roles. The provincial government is working to align social assistance programs, vocational training, and UMKM (micro, small, and medium enterprise) financing access to lift those still below the poverty line.

### Key Strategies on the Table

Key approaches discussed at the coordination meeting included:

- **Data synchronization**: Ensuring poverty data used across all kabupaten and cities is consistent and cross-verified
- **Program collaboration**: Eliminating overlap in social assistance so support reaches those who need it most efficiently
- **Regular monitoring cycles**: Routine evaluations to detect and correct deviations early
- **Empowering traditional villages**: Desa adat (customary villages) positioned as frontline implementers of poverty programs

## Bali's Vision for Shared Prosperity

Bali's poverty reduction push reflects a broader provincial ambition: to ensure that tourism-driven economic growth reaches all corners of society — not just urban centers or the formal economy. Programs targeting rural households, women-led families, and workers in the informal sector are being expanded province-wide.

By strengthening coordination between provincial government, district administrations, and community institutions, Bali is working toward a future where prosperity is truly inclusive — and where its record-low poverty rate continues to fall.
