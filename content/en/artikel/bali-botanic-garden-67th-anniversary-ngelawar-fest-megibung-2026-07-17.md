Bali Botanic Garden (Kebun Raya Bali) in Candikuning village, Baturiti district, Tabanan regency, celebrated its 67th anniversary through a series of events held on July 11, 12, and 15, 2026. Under the theme "Ajegang Bhuana, Nguripang Budaya" — protecting nature while reviving culture — this year's celebration combined conservation, education, wellness, and culinary programming, with Ngelawar Fest as one of its centerpiece events.

## Ngelawar Fest, the First Community-Led Culinary Competition

Ngelawar Fest is the first lawar cooking competition and festival initiated by a non-government community, as similar events have typically been organized by local governments. The festival featured cooking competitions for various types of lawar, culinary demonstrations, and the Megibung tradition — communal dining that symbolizes togetherness in Balinese culture.

The event was designed as a collaborative space for cultural communities, small business owners, academics, and young people to preserve Bali's gastronomic heritage. Lawar — a traditional dish of minced vegetables and meat seasoned with distinctive Balinese spices — was chosen as the festival's focus for its status as one of the island's most identity-defining dishes, and for its vulnerability to being crowded out by a homogenizing food scene amid Bali's booming tourism industry.

The competition's winners included the Banjar Angin group, which took first place, followed by the Gejora group from Marga village, Tabanan, in second, and KSDM 1 from Penatih neighborhood, Denpasar, in third. KSDM 2, also from Penatih, was named the audience favorite.

## Tending 20,030 Plant Specimens Through Rehabilitation

Behind the culinary festivities, Bali Botanic Garden continues to fulfill its core mandate as a plant conservation institution. The garden now houses 20,030 plant specimens across roughly 3,000 species sourced from across Indonesia, including Papua, Maluku, Sulawesi, Java, and Sumatra — some of them rare or already extinct in their original habitats.

Garden managers apply strict standards, maintaining a minimum of five specimens for each plant type in the collection to ensure population continuity. Vegetation numbers are sustained through ongoing rehabilitation efforts, whether through seedling propagation or expanding existing collections, keeping the garden's role as a genetic bank for the archipelago's flora intact for future generations.

## Balancing Conservation and Culture

The 67th anniversary celebrations also included free admission on one of the event days, as a gesture of appreciation to the public that has long supported the conservation area. Garden management hopes the anniversary strengthens public awareness that Bali Botanic Garden is more than a nature tourism destination — it is also a bulwark for biodiversity preservation and a living space for Balinese culinary and social tradition.

This year's theme, "Ajegang Bhuana, Nguripang Budaya," reflects the garden's dual commitment: safeguarding nature while reviving cultural practices rooted in the relationship between Balinese people and their environment. Through Ngelawar Fest and the Megibung tradition, a culinary heritage steeped in communal values found a new stage amid the modern food culture increasingly dominating Bali as a global tourism destination.
