Bali's pristine coastal waters are set to become the stage for Asia's most prestigious open water swimming competition. From **June 13 to 15, 2026**, the **InterContinental Bali Resort beach in Jimbaran** will host the **12th Asian Open Water Swimming (OWS) Championship 2026**, drawing elite swimmers from across the continent to compete in the warm, clear waters of southern Bali.

## Asia Converges on Jimbaran

Organized by the Indonesian Aquatics Federation (PB Akuatik Indonesia), the championship will see **17 Asian nations** sending their finest open water athletes. A total of **128 competitors** will take part in the main championship, while the accompanying **A•STREAM OWS Series Bali 2026** will welcome an additional **300 participants**—making the combined event a celebration of open water swimming with well over 400 athletes from across the region.

The event marks a significant moment for Indonesian aquatics, as the country earns the trust of the Asian aquatics community to host the continent's premier open water competition for the 12th edition.

## Race Distances

Athletes will compete across two standard distances according to international regulations:

- **5,000 meters** (open water)
- **10,000 meters** (open water)

Both events demand far more than speed. Open water swimmers must navigate currents, waves, and unpredictable sea conditions that test strategy, endurance, and mental resilience in ways that pool swimming simply cannot replicate.

## Indonesia's Seven-Strong National Team

Indonesia will be represented by **seven national athletes** selected by PB Akuatik Indonesia. The men's contingent features five swimmers: **Sang Arka Jaladri**, **Andi Fauzan Permatani**, **Satria Dwi Candra**, **Alexander Adrian**, and **Mochamad Akbar Putra**.

The women's team includes two athletes: **Izzy Dwifaiva Hefrisyanthi**—who has just returned to Indonesia after a full year of intensive training at a top facility in France—and **Gusti Ayu Made Nadya Saraswati**, a Balinese athlete who will have the unique motivation of competing in her home waters.

Tournament director **Kiki Taher** expressed confidence in the Indonesian lineup, noting that Izzy's European training experience represents a meaningful upgrade that could put her in contention against Asia's best.

## Sport Tourism: Beyond Sand and Culture

This championship is a key piece of Bali's broader strategy to develop **sport tourism** as a growing segment of its tourism economy. Events like this one bring a different kind of visitor: athletes, coaches, officials, and their supporters from 17 nations who spend on accommodation, dining, transport, and local experiences throughout the Jimbaran area and beyond.

For Bali, it also reinforces the island's global image not just as a beach holiday destination or cultural retreat, but as a credible venue for world-class sporting events.

Jimbaran was selected for good reason. Its calm, clear waters are ideal for open sea competition; its five-star beachfront resort provides logistical convenience for international teams; and its natural beauty ensures that even the most competitive athletes will find the experience memorable.

As Bali continues building a richer, more diverse events calendar for 2026, the 12th Asian OWS Championship is yet another reason why this island remains Asia's most compelling destination.
