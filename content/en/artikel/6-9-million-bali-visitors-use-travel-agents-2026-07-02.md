Bali's travel agency sector continues to defy expectations in an era of online self-booking. The Association of Indonesian Tours and Travel Agencies (ASITA) Bali Province has revealed that **6.9 million tourists** who visited Bali in 2025 booked their trip through a travel agent — a new all-time record for the association.

## Consistent Growth Year After Year

ASITA Bali Chairman I Putu Winastra highlighted the consistent upward trajectory of travel agency usage since the post-pandemic recovery began:

| Year | Tourists via Travel Agent |
|------|---------------------------|
| 2022 | 2.1 million               |
| 2023 | 5.3 million               |
| 2024 | 6.3 million               |
| 2025 | 6.9 million               |

The growth from 2.1 million in 2022 to 6.9 million in 2025 represents more than a threefold increase in just three years — a remarkable recovery that reflects both restored traveller confidence and the enduring value that travel agents provide.

## Contributing 65% of International Arrivals

Perhaps even more striking is the scale of ASITA's contribution to Bali's international tourism figures. In 2025, travel agents under the ASITA Bali umbrella were responsible for **65 percent** of all international tourist arrivals to the island.

"The contribution of ASITA Bali reaches 65% of the total number of international tourist arrivals in 2025. This shows that trust in travel agents remains very high," said Winastra.

## 2026 Already at 2 Million

The strong momentum has carried into 2026. By **April 2026**, the number of tourists arriving in Bali through travel agents had already reached **2 million** — a pace that puts the sector on track to potentially surpass 2025's record.

## Why Tourists Still Choose Travel Agents

In an age when flights and hotels can be booked with a few taps on a smartphone, the persistence of travel agent dominance in the Bali market is a noteworthy phenomenon. Several factors help explain the trend:

- **Convenience and peace of mind:** Pre-organised itineraries eliminate logistical complexity, especially for first-time visitors navigating a multi-island destination.
- **Verified quality:** Travel agents provide guaranteed, vetted accommodations, transportation, and schedules.
- **Access to exclusive products:** Agents frequently offer packages unavailable through public channels, including tours of traditional villages and curated cultural experiences.
- **Emergency support:** Having a local point of contact provides reassurance in case of unexpected complications during the trip.

## Village Tourism: The New Product Frontier

ASITA Bali is also actively promoting village tourism as a flagship product to both domestic and international travellers. With **238 tourist villages** spread across the island, this strategy aims to strengthen Bali's product diversification and channel visitor flows to areas beyond the heavily trafficked southern region.

Culinary tourism and wellness travel are two emerging categories that ASITA Bali is specifically targeting — product types that tend to generate longer stays and higher per-visitor spending, aligning with the provincial government's quality-over-quantity tourism goals.
