Among the hundreds of performing groups at the 48th Pesta Kesenian Bali (PKB) 2026, **Sanggar Suara Kanti** from Gianyar Regency delivered a performance that stood out not only for its artistry but for its cultural mission — to introduce and protect the unique Angklung Kebyar style native to their homeland.

## Preserving Gianyar's Musical Identity

The group's choreographer and musical director, **I Gusti Ngurah Agung Giri Putra**, explained that the performance was conceived as more than a competition — it was a declaration of regional artistic identity.

"We want to highlight the character of Gianyar's Angklung Kebyar while preserving our regional identity amid the ever-evolving creative arts landscape," said Giri Putra.

According to him, what makes Gianyar's Angklung Kebyar truly distinctive is a combination of three elements: **dynamic tabuh rhythms**, the **melodic character** unique to the area's playing tradition, and the **seamless harmony between music, movement, and expression** maintained by every performer throughout the piece.

## A Call to the Next Generation

Behind the polished stage performance lies a deeper urgency. Giri Putra acknowledged that interest among younger generations in maintaining the Angklung Kebyar tradition has been declining — a trend driven by the pull of digital entertainment and shifting tastes among today's youth.

"PKB is the perfect stage to send a message to society, especially young people, that Angklung Kebyar is a heritage we must protect together," he said.

The performance served as a living reminder that Balinese traditional music is not a relic of the past but a living, breathing art form that requires active stewardship.

### What Is Angklung Kebyar?

Angklung Kebyar is a style of Balinese gamelan ensemble that emerged and flourished in the early 20th century. Unlike its sacred counterparts used in temple ceremonies, Angklung Kebyar embraces dynamic energy, spontaneity, and creative improvisation within its performances. Each of Bali's regencies has developed a distinct regional flavor — making the variety of Angklung Kebyar styles a vivid reflection of Bali's rich cultural mosaic.

## PKB as a Stage for Cultural Dialogue

The appearance of Sanggar Suara Kanti at PKB XLVIII 2026 underscores the festival's role as more than entertainment. PKB functions as a living cultural dialogue — between past and present, between tradition and innovation, and between the preservation of ancestral heritage and the impulse toward contemporary creativity.

The artists from Gianyar took home more than stage experience; they carried with them the hope that the music they played will echo in the ears of generations yet to come.

PKB XLVIII 2026 runs from June 13 to July 11 at the Bali Cultural Park (Art Center) in Denpasar.
