Away from the bustle of Bali's popular southern resorts, a small coastal village on the northern shore of the Island of the Gods is quietly preparing for a new chapter in its tourism journey. **Umeanyar Village** in Seririt District, Buleleng Regency, enters the June–July 2026 school holiday season with quiet confidence — armed with a thoughtfully designed integrated tourism package that promises authentic, unhurried experiences far removed from mass-market itineraries.

## A Village Full of Surprises

For most visitors, the name Umeanyar may not ring an immediate bell. And that's precisely the point. This village, strung along Bali's northern coastline, has long been celebrated as one of the island's **grape-growing centres** — a fruit that thrives in the dry, sun-drenched coastal climate. Visitors can pick fresh grapes directly from the vine, a rare agrotourism experience that's genuinely hard to find elsewhere on the island.

Beyond its vineyards, Umeanyar is surrounded by rich marine ecosystems. Its waters are home to wild **dolphins** that frequently surface in the early morning — a spectacle long associated with nearby Lovina, but equally enchanting from the Umeanyar coastline.

## Pokdarwis Crafts a Comprehensive Package

Ahead of the school holiday peak, **Umeanyar Village's Tourism Awareness Group (Pokdarwis)**, working alongside village administration and local entrepreneurs, is finalizing the concept for an integrated tourism package that weaves together multiple activities into a single meaningful journey. The package is being developed to target domestic tourists first — particularly families eager to give their children a holiday that is both enjoyable and genuinely enriching.

The integrated experience encompasses:

- **Baby Turtle Release (Tukik)** — Participate in releasing hatchling sea turtles into the ocean, part of Umeanyar's active community turtle conservation programme
- **Dolphin Watching** — An early morning boat excursion to the calm waters where wild dolphins frequently pass by
- **Grape Farm Visit** — A hands-on tour of the local vineyard: picking fruit straight from the vine and learning how it is processed into juice and other products
- **Local Cuisine** — Savour North Balinese specialities that differ distinctly from the flavours of South Bali
- **Melukat Ritual** — A spiritual purification ceremony conducted at a sacred freshwater spring, an experience increasingly sought by travellers who want a deeper, more contemplative dimension to their journey

## Ready for the Surge

Pokdarwis projects a **10–15 percent increase** in domestic visitor numbers during the school holidays compared to ordinary days — a figure that sounds modest, but is genuinely significant for a village economy that has long operated at a quiet, sustainable pace.

Holiday season surges in villages like Umeanyar tend to be most strongly felt in culinary and agrotourism segments — both of which happen to be Umeanyar's key strengths. To make the most of this opportunity, village tourism managers have expanded capacity, including community-owned homestays ready to host overnight guests who wish to immerse themselves more fully in village life.

## North Bali's Rising Star

The story of Umeanyar reflects a broader and deepening trend: today's travellers are no longer simply chasing scenic views or luxury amenities — they are seeking **authenticity**. Real experiences. Encounters that genuinely touch local life and take them beyond the well-worn tourist trail.

Buleleng, as Bali's largest regency and one still full of unexplored natural and cultural wealth, holds enormous potential as a world-class alternative tourism destination. And Umeanyar, with its distinctive constellation of offerings, is one of its brightest rising stars.
