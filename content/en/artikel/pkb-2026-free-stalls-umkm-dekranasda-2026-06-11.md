In a move that directly benefits Bali's grassroots entrepreneurs, the Regional Craft Council (Dekranasda) of Bali Province has confirmed that all exhibition stalls at the **48th Bali Arts Festival (PKB XLVIII) 2026** will be offered completely **free of charge** to local Small and Medium Enterprises (SMEs) and Small Industry players (IKM).

## A Break from the Old System

Historically, exhibition stalls at PKB were auctioned off at high prices, meaning only well-funded business groups could participate in Bali's largest cultural showcase. That system effectively locked out many small vendors and artisans who could have benefited most from the exposure.

Under the new policy championed by **Ny. Putri Suastini Koster**, Chair of Dekranasda Bali (and wife of Bali Governor Wayan Koster), two categories of free stalls are now available:

- **IKM Bali Bangkit stalls** — for small-scale industrial producers
- **UMKM Kuliner stalls** — for food and beverage micro-enterprises

## Zero Tolerance for Illegal Levies

While the stalls are free, Putri Koster has issued an equally firm directive: **no illegal levies (pungli) of any kind** will be tolerated. This includes unofficial charges for security, cleaning services, or any other pretexts that vendors may have encountered in previous years.

"There must be no levies in any form whatsoever — no exceptions," Putri Koster stated, signaling that the policy would be strictly enforced throughout the festival.

### Fair Pricing Required

In addition to the ban on pungli, stall holders are also expected to price their goods **reasonably and affordably**. By eliminating rental costs, the expectation is that vendors can pass the savings on to buyers, making local Balinese products more accessible to the public.

## Curation Ensures Quality Standards

Free access does not mean lower standards. All participants have undergone a **rigorous curation process** jointly conducted by the Bali Provincial Department of Industry and Trade, together with the Cooperative and SME Department. This ensures that only quality products — representative of Bali's creative heritage — reach the PKB stage.

## Green Standards at Every Stall

The free-stall policy comes with an environmental obligation. Every stall must provide **separate bins for organic and inorganic waste**, in line with Bali's commitment to a 100 percent waste sorting program. Putri Koster also urged all vendors to abandon single-use plastic bags entirely, replacing them with cloth bags or eco-friendly packaging.

## PKB XLVIII Opens June 13, 2026

The 48th Bali Arts Festival is scheduled to open on **June 13, 2026**, at the Taman Budaya Art Center in Denpasar. This year's theme is **"Atma Kerthi"** — a Balinese concept representing the purification of the soul through art and culture.

With free stalls, strong anti-pungli enforcement, and quality curation, PKB XLVIII 2026 is set to be one of the most inclusive and business-friendly editions of the festival in recent memory — a genuine win for Bali's creative economy.
