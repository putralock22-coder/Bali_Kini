Indonesia's Ministry of Tourism (Kemenpar) has formally designated **sport tourism** as a national strategic priority in its effort to drive regional economic growth across the archipelago. The policy goes beyond simply attracting sporting event spectators — it represents a systematic strategy to ensure every competition and athletic gathering produces meaningful multiplier effects for local SMEs, hotels, restaurants, and transportation providers.

The announcement was made by **Ni Komang Ayu Astiti**, Assistant Deputy for National Events at the Ministry of Tourism, during several high-level forums held throughout June 2026. Bali, with its world-class tourism infrastructure and unmatched natural-cultural appeal, stands as one of the most prepared regions to capitalize on this expansion.

## The Numbers Behind the Strategy

Kemenpar data reveals that Indonesia's sport tourism sector generated **Rp18.79 trillion (approximately USD 1.15 billion) in economic value in 2024** — encompassing the full economic impact of sporting events at all scales, from local tournaments to international world series competitions.

The ministry projects this figure to grow substantially through 2025 and 2026, as the government accelerates the placement of international sporting events in Indonesia, with Bali serving as a primary hub.

## Why Sport Tourism Works

**Ni Komang Ayu Astiti** articulated why the segment is so effective at stimulating local economies:

> *"Sport tourism drives community economic activity, including small and medium enterprises."*

Sport tourists have a distinct spending profile compared to conventional leisure travelers:

- **Higher per-capita spend** — they invest in quality accommodation, dining, and wellness services such as spa and massage therapy
- **Extended stays** — pre-event training camps and post-competition recovery extend the length of stay beyond what most leisure travelers maintain
- **Group travel patterns** — athletes travel with coaches, officials, and support staff; supporters often bring family or community groups
- **Broad economic distribution** — spending flows into markets, traditional restaurants, local transport operators, and community vendors — not just upscale resort enclaves

## Ministry of Youth Affairs and Sports: A Cross-Sector Alliance

This push does not belong to one ministry alone. The **Ministry of Youth Affairs and Sports (Kemenpora)** and Kemenpar are actively strengthening cross-sector coordination to accelerate sport tourism development in 2026. The goal is to ensure every nationally sanctioned sporting event doubles as a regional tourism promotion campaign.

The results of this coordination are already visible: when a regency or city is designated as an event host, teams from both ministries work in tandem to ensure economic benefits reach grassroots level — not merely the established players in the five-star hospitality sector.

## Bali as Sport Tourism's Showcase

Throughout 2026, Bali has hosted a series of world-class sporting events that demonstrate this policy in action:

### Asian Open Water Swimming Championship 2026 (June 13–15)
The 12th edition of the Asian Open Water Swimming Championship was held at the shores of the InterContinental Bali Resort in Jimbaran Bay, Badung Regency. **127 athletes and officials from 18 nations** competed, filling hotels across the Jimbaran–Uluwatu corridor and stimulating surrounding culinary and wellness businesses.

### Red Bull Cliff Diving World Series 2026 (May)
For the first time ever, the opening round of the prestigious Red Bull Cliff Diving World Series was staged at **Broken Beach, Nusa Penida**. The international media coverage placed Nusa Penida on the global adventure tourism map, generating promotional value worth far more than any conventional marketing campaign.

### Jatiluwih Festival VII — Bali Tourism Run 5K (June 20–21)
The festival at Jatiluwih's UNESCO-recognized subak landscape in Tabanan introduces Bali's first 5K trail run through terraced rice fields, targeting 2,000 participants — blending sport tourism with cultural heritage preservation.

## Karisma Event Nusantara: The National Roadmap

Kemenpar has structured its sport tourism agenda through the **Karisma Event Nusantara (KEN)** program. Events accepted into KEN receive ministry-backed promotion, access to national and international travel agent networks, and technical assistance from the ministry's event management team.

In Bali, several events have officially entered KEN 2026, including Jatiluwih Festival VII. Looking ahead, Kemenpar's target is to anchor at least one sport tourism event per month in every province — an ambitious commitment that, if realized, would fundamentally reshape the geography of Indonesia's tourism economy.

## Challenges Ahead

The optimism is grounded, but so are the challenges. Key obstacles for sport tourism development in Bali and across Indonesia include:

- **Supporting infrastructure** — transport access to event venues, parking capacity, and spectator facilities need significant upgrades in many regions
- **Safety and medical standards** — high-risk sporting events demand robust emergency response protocols
- **Ensuring equitable distribution** — making certain that economic benefits genuinely reach SMEs, not just established commercial interests

Despite these challenges, the trajectory is clear. **Sport tourism is no longer supplementary — it is becoming a core pillar** of Bali's and Indonesia's tourism strategy for 2026 and beyond.
