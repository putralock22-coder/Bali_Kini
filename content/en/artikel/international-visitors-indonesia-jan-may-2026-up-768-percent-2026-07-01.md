Indonesia's tourism recovery continues to gather momentum. Statistics Indonesia (BPS) has released new data showing that the number of international tourist (wisman) arrivals during **January to May 2026** reached **6.07 million visits** — a **7.68 percent increase** compared to the same period in 2025, which recorded 5.63 million arrivals.

## Highest Since 2021

The 6.07 million figure for the first five months of 2026 marks the **highest international arrival count since 2021**, officially signalling a full recovery of Indonesian tourism from the pandemic-era slump. This milestone reflects growing global confidence in Indonesia as an international travel destination.

## Month-by-Month Trends

BPS data shows fluctuating but broadly improving monthly arrival figures from January through May 2026:

- **January 2026:** 1.19 million arrivals
- **February 2026:** 1.16 million (minor dip, typical low season)
- **March 2026:** 1.09 million (lowest point in the period)
- **April 2026:** 1.25 million (recovery begins)
- **May 2026:** 1.38 million (highest point in the period)

The strong rebound in April and May aligns with improved international flight connectivity and the onset of the Northern Hemisphere summer travel season.

## Top Source Countries

In May 2026, the three largest source markets for international visitors to Indonesia were:

1. **Malaysia** — 298,210 arrivals (21.58% of total)
2. **Australia** — 155,030 arrivals (11.22%)
3. **Singapore** — 136,740 arrivals (9.89%)

Australian visitors — who are disproportionately Bali-focused — remain the most loyal international market, sustained by extensive direct flight connectivity from multiple Australian cities.

## Bali: Top Entry Point, Minor Adjustment

As Indonesia's principal international gateway, Bali recorded **2.5 million international arrivals** between January and May 2026. This represents a modest **1.77 percent decline** compared to the same period in 2025.

However, Bali's Provincial Tourism Office (Dispar) characterised this small correction as a natural and manageable fluctuation rather than a structural concern. Seasonal patterns and minor shifts in traveller destination preferences are normal variables in any dynamic tourism market.

## Domestic Tourists Also Growing

Parallel to the international data, domestic travel figures across Indonesia also performed positively. BPS recorded a total of **523.22 million domestic trips** nationwide during January to May 2026, up **2.86 percent** from 508.67 million in the same period last year.

For Bali specifically, domestic visitor numbers climbed **6.43 percent** from April to May 2026 — a trend expected to accelerate sharply as the national school holiday season kicked off in late June.

## Bali's 2026 Goal: Quality Over Quantity

Despite the positive headline numbers, Bali's provincial government has made clear that its 2026 tourism strategy is not about maximising visitor counts alone. The focus is on extending the **average length of stay**, increasing **tourist spending per visit**, and ensuring that growth in visitor numbers goes hand in hand with environmental sustainability and cultural preservation.

This philosophy aligns with global trends in responsible tourism, positioning Bali not just as a high-volume destination but as a model for high-value, low-impact travel in Southeast Asia.
