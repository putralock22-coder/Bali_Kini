The 48th Bali Arts Festival (PKB XLVIII) 2026 enters its 22nd day on Saturday, July 4, delivering another evening of extraordinary performances at the Taman Budaya Art Center in Denpasar. From the rare sounds of a traditional mandolin to Balinese dancers flown in from New York, today's schedule is a testament to the enduring global reach of Balinese culture.

## Theme: Atma Kerthi

PKB XLVIII carries the theme **"Atma Kerthi: Jiwa Sidha Parisudha"** — meaning the glorification and purification of the human soul toward perfect, clear, and sacred completeness. The festival runs for a full month, from June 13 to July 11, 2026, at the Taman Budaya Art Center, Jalan Nusa Indah, Denpasar.

This year's edition brings together **10 international art groups** with approximately 260 artists from the United States, China, Timor-Leste, South Korea, Japan, India, and Hong Kong. From across the Indonesian archipelago, 955 domestic participants represent cultural diversity from Jakarta, East Java, Central Java, West Java, Yogyakarta, Banten, and Central Kalimantan.

## Full Schedule — Saturday, July 4, 2026

### 3:00 PM WITA — Mandolin & Gamelan Penting

The afternoon opens with **Rekasadana Mandolin and Gamelan Penting** performed by Sanggar Seni Neo Nolin together with Komunitas Seni Wasesa Ananta from Karangasem Regency, staged at **Kalangan Angsoka**. The combination of Western mandolin and traditional Balinese gamelan is a rare and captivating pairing seldom seen on major stages.

### 4:00 PM WITA — BWCC: Barongsai

The **Bali World Culture Celebration (BWCC)** program presents a Barongsai (lion dance) performance at **Kalangan Madya Mandala**, celebrating cross-cultural exchange as part of the festival's international programming.

### 5:00 PM WITA — Balinese Dance from New York, USA

The evening's most anticipated international segment begins at **Gedung Ksirarnawa**: BWCC showcases **Balinese Dance and Gamelan Dharma Swara** from New York, USA. This performance by Balinese artists based in America is a powerful symbol of how far Bali's culture has traveled — taking root thousands of miles from its homeland.

### 7:00 PM WITA — Youth Janger Parade

As night falls, the **Utsawa (Parade) Janger Tradisi Remaja** takes the stage at **Kalangan Ayodya**. Sekaa Janger Banjar Pegok from Desa Adat Sesetan, South Denpasar, performs as the representative of Denpasar City. Janger is a traditional Balinese group dance emphasizing vocal and movement harmony — its youth format represents a conscious effort to pass the tradition to the next generation.

### 7:30 PM WITA — Wayang Cupak

Closing the night at **Depan Gedung Kriya**, **Rekasadana Wayang Cupak** is performed by Sanggar Wangked from Banjar Ked, Desa Taro, Tegallalang, Gianyar. Wayang Cupak is a genre of Balinese shadow puppet theatre famous for its comedic storytelling around the bumbling yet brave character of Cupak.

## Bali as a Global Cultural Stage

PKB XLVIII is more than a festival — it is Bali's annual declaration that its cultural identity will not bow to modernity. Every performance tonight, from the mandolin of Karangasem to the youth janger of Denpasar, flows from a single spirit: Atma Kerthi.

All PKB performances are open to the public. The Taman Budaya Art Center is located at Jalan Nusa Indah, Denpasar.
