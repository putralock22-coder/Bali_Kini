The three-day deadline handed down by the Bali Bergerak student alliance to the Bali Provincial Parliament (DPRD) expired on Monday, June 29, 2026. The DPRD has since confirmed it honored its commitment by forwarding all 16 demands to the central government in Jakarta — though no substantive response has yet come back from either the national parliament or the Ministry of Home Affairs.

## The Student Ultimatum

The Bali Bergerak alliance — a coalition of Udayana University (Unud) students, civil activists, and civic groups — descended on the Bali DPRD building on Monday, June 22, 2026. During the demonstration, the group submitted 16 demands covering five broad issue clusters: economic pressures on ordinary citizens, the state of democracy, protection of civil liberties, human rights enforcement, environmental degradation, and the quality of public education.

The Udayana University Student Executive Board (BEM Unud), one of the lead organizers, issued a firm warning: unless the DPRD forwarded all aspirations to DPR RI and the Ministry of Home Affairs within three working days, students would return with a larger and more disruptive protest.

## DPRD Says It Delivered

Komang Nova Sewi Putra, Third Deputy Speaker of the Bali DPRD, confirmed on Monday (29/6) that the parliament had acted within the prescribed timeframe. A formal letter was drafted after receiving the student demands and dispatched to Jakarta via two channels — email and physical post — within just two days of the June 22 demonstration.

Nova Sewi Putra stated that DPRD Bali had fulfilled its role as a conduit between Balinese constituents and the central government, and that documentation of the letter's dispatch was available as proof of the institution's compliance. He stressed that the provincial legislature had taken the students' concerns seriously and processed them through proper institutional channels.

## Silence from the Central Government

Despite the DPRD's assurances, the fact remains that neither DPR RI nor the Ministry of Home Affairs has issued any formal reply regarding the 16 demands as of the deadline. This silence carries the risk of reigniting frustration among the student movement, which has from the outset demanded concrete outcomes — not mere administrative compliance.

Representatives of BEM Unud expressed hope that the forwarded aspirations would be genuinely deliberated by policymakers in Jakarta, rather than filed away without further action. The students made clear they would continue monitoring the situation and have not ruled out escalating their protests if no meaningful follow-through materializes.

## The Issues at Stake

The five thematic areas underpinning the Bali Bergerak movement reflect a broader anxiety among Bali's younger generation about the direction of national policy. Economic grievances center on the rising cost of living and widening inequality. Democratic concerns relate to what activists describe as a shrinking civic space. Environmental demands focus on the mounting pressure that mass tourism and development impose on Bali's ecosystems. Education demands target uneven access to quality schooling across the island.

With the formal deadline now passed, the onus falls on the central government to demonstrate that regional aspirations — transmitted through legitimate institutional channels — receive genuine attention and a substantive policy response.
