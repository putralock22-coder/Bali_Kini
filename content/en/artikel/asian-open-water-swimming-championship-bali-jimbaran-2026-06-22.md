The crystal-clear waters of Jimbaran Bay, Bali, recently served as the prestigious arena for one of Asia's most coveted aquatics competitions. On **June 13–15, 2026**, Bali successfully hosted the **12th Asian Open Water Swimming Championship 2026**, held at the InterContinental Bali Resort in Jimbaran, Badung Regency.

## A Record-Breaking Field of 18 Nations

This year's championship brought together **78 competitive swimmers from 18 countries** across Asia — the largest field in the event's history. Asia's top open water swimming talent gathered in the calm, clear waters of Jimbaran Bay to compete across three distinct race formats:

- **June 13** — Asian Championship 5K Open Water Race
- **June 14** — Asian Championship Mixed Relay (4x1,500 meters)
- **June 15** — Asian Championship 10K Open Water Race

Alongside the elite competition, organizers also staged the **A.Stream OWS Series Bali 2026** — an inclusive companion event welcoming community swimmers, sports enthusiasts, and beginners. Approximately **300 age-group participants** took to the same waters as Asia's elite competitors, creating a rare and memorable shared experience.

## China Leads the Medal Table

On the competitive front, **China's swimmers dominated** the podium across the 5K, 10K, and mixed relay categories. Indonesia's contingent, fielded by the national aquatics federation (PB Akuatik), did not claim medals in any of the three championship events.

However, PB Akuatik made clear that **medal performance was not the primary objective** for Indonesia's team at this championship. The selected athletes were chosen specifically as part of a **long-term regeneration program** — gaining invaluable exposure to international competition standards on home turf, a priceless experience for athletes at the beginning of their elite careers.

## Bali as a Strategic Sport Tourism Hub

Beyond the lanes and stopwatches, the championship delivered significant tourism dividends for Bali. Indonesia's Minister of Tourism Widiyanti Putri Wardhana framed the event as far more than a sporting contest — calling it a **key moment to advance Indonesia's sport tourism ecosystem**, with Bali at the center.

Athletes and officials from 18 nations arrived as visitors, staying in local hotels, dining in local restaurants, and exploring Bali's diverse attractions. Support staff, families, and international media representatives multiplied the impact further, generating wide exposure for Jimbaran Bay and the broader Badung tourism area.

Globally, sport tourism now accounts for approximately **10 percent of total tourism spending** according to UN Tourism data, with projections pointing to a **17.5 percent growth rate** between 2023 and 2030. Indonesia — with Bali as its flagship destination — is positioning itself to capture a significant share of this expanding market.

## Why Jimbaran Bay?

The choice of Jimbaran Bay was deliberate. Known for its sheltered, gently sloping shoreline, the bay offers ideal open water swimming conditions — minimal currents, good visibility, and calm surface water that is rarely disrupted by rough weather at this time of year. The presence of the InterContinental Bali Resort as the event's partner venue ensured world-class facilities for athletes, officials, and spectators.

Bali's success in hosting this championship reinforces its reputation as one of Asia's premier sport tourism destinations, and signals strong potential to attract more international sporting events — from surf competitions and triathlete races to sailing regattas and beyond.
