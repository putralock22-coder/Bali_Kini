The open-air Ardha Candra stage at Taman Budaya Bali (Art Centre), Denpasar, erupted in thunderous applause on Thursday, June 25, as the delegation from Badung Regency brought the **Wimbakara (Competition) Tari Barong Ket** to a breathtaking conclusion at the 48th Bali Arts Festival (Pesta Kesenian Bali / PKB).

## Saving the Best for Last

Performing as the final competitor—often the most scrutinized slot in any competition—**Sekaa Gong Satya Winangun Budaya** from Tibubeneng Village, North Kuta, transformed the pressure into a performance that left thousands of spectators captivated. The combination of powerful dancer expression, precise movement synchronization, and the commanding resonance of the gamelan ensemble created an unforgettable spectacle.

The star of the show was the **Bapang Barong**—the segment where the Barong creature rises to its full majestic height—which drew the loudest response from the packed audience.

## Evolution Without Losing Tradition

What set Badung's performance apart was its approach to the choreography: evolving the signature **Badung Barong technique** without abandoning its traditional roots. Rather than introducing entirely new movements, the creative team refined existing techniques and reimagined the choreographic composition—resulting in a performance that felt both fresh and authentically rooted in Badung's Barong legacy.

The Badung style of Barong is recognized for its distinctively assertive, dynamic, and commanding character—qualities that were on full display Thursday evening.

### The Spiritual Significance of Barong

For those unfamiliar with Balinese tradition, Barong is one of the island's most sacred symbols: the embodiment of benevolent protective forces engaged in eternal cosmic struggle against evil. The **Barong Ket**—the lion-like creature most associated with Bali internationally—enacts the mythical battle between dharma (righteousness) and adharma (chaos). Every gesture, from the Bapang's fierce gaze to the sweeping movements of the Barong's elaborate costume, carries deep spiritual meaning rooted in Hindu Balinese cosmology.

## A Regent Who Shows Up

Badung Regent **I Wayan Adi Arnawa** attended the performance in person—a gesture that resonated deeply with the artists and the audience alike. His presence underscored the regency's ongoing commitment to supporting traditional arts.

"Badung's delegation was able to deliver the best possible performance. I am committed to continuing to support the arts and to being present at every performance at PKB as a gesture of support for our artists," he said.

## PKB XLVIII: A Festival of Global Proportions

The Barong Ket competition is just one event in a month-long cultural marathon. PKB XLVIII 2026, running from June 13 to July 11, carries the theme **"Atma Kerthi: Jiwa Sidha Parisudha"** (Honoring the Complete Soul), involving more than **20,000 artists** from across Bali performing across 16 venues.

This year's festival also features the **Bali World Culture Celebration (BWCC)**, bringing international artists to perform alongside Balinese masters—positioning PKB not just as a local cultural event but as a genuine world arts festival taking place on the Island of the Gods.
