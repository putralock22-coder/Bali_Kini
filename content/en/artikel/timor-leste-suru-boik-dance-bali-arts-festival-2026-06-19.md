The Ksirarnawa Hall at Bali's Art Centre in Denpasar recently resounded with thunderous applause. Fifty young performers from Timor Leste delivered a captivating performance at the Bali World Culture Celebration (BWCC), part of the 48th Bali Arts Festival (PKB XLVIII 2026), showcasing the traditional Suru Boik dance to thousands of enthusiastic spectators.

## What Is the Suru Boik Dance?

The Suru Boik is one of Timor Leste's most treasured cultural heritages, reflecting a unique fusion of indigenous Timorese traditions and the Portuguese cultural influence that took root during centuries of colonization. Its expressive movements and vibrant costumes offered Bali's audience an exotic yet deeply moving aesthetic experience, distinct from the island's own artistic traditions.

The fifty performers represented a combination of dance groups from Timor Leste and associations of Timorese students currently studying in Bali. They underwent weeks of intensive rehearsals to deliver a polished, memorable performance on one of Southeast Asia's most prestigious cultural stages.

## The Consulate's Meticulous Preparation

Jastho Pereira, a representative from the Timor Leste Consulate General in Bali, was the driving force behind the delegation's participation. For Pereira, this was his fourth time bringing Timorese culture to the PKB stage—but this year felt particularly special, as many of the young dancers were performing at the festival for the very first time.

"We prepared meticulously. We wanted to bring our very best to the people of Bali and its visitors," Pereira said.

## BWCC: A Global Cultural Bridge at PKB

The Bali World Culture Celebration serves as the international segment of PKB, inviting arts delegations from across the globe to share their traditions on Balinese soil. At PKB XLVIII, no fewer than **10 international arts groups** comprising approximately **260 artists** participated, representing countries including:

- **United States**
- **China**
- **South Korea**
- **Japan**
- **India**
- **Hong Kong**
- **Timor Leste**

This diversity transforms the BWCC into a genuine dialogue between civilizations, reinforcing Bali's identity not merely as a tourist hotspot, but as a true global centre of arts and culture.

## PKB XLVIII: The Theme of Purified Souls

The 48th edition of the Bali Arts Festival runs from June 13 to July 11, 2026, under the theme **'Atma Kerthi: Jiwa Siddha Parishudha'**—a philosophical concept of pure and perfected souls. Every performance, including those from international delegates like Timor Leste, is presented in the spirit of honouring and uniting the diversity of human civilization.

Beyond the BWCC, PKB XLVIII encompasses ten main types of activities, from the Peed Aya (cultural parade) and Rekasadana (performances) to Wimbakara (competitions) and Kriyaloka (workshops). Thousands of artists from across Bali and around the world participate in this annual celebration of Balinese cultural excellence.

## A Defining Moment for Young Artists

For the young Timorese dancers who took the stage for the first time at PKB, the experience was unforgettable. They had the rare opportunity to interact with Balinese artists, learn about the island's rich traditions, and introduce their own cultural identity to the world.

The presence of Timor Leste at PKB XLVIII is a powerful reminder that art transcends borders—bridging language, politics, and geography in ways that formal diplomacy rarely can.
