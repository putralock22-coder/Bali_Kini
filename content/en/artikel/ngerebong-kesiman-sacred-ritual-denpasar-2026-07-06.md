## An Ancient Tradition Alive at the Heart of Denpasar

In the midst of a bustling modern Denpasar, the traditional village of Kesiman has once again demonstrated why Bali's cultural heritage remains one of the most vibrant in the world. On Sunday, July 5, 2026, thousands of residents and visitors gathered at Pura Agung Petilan to witness and participate in the Ngerebong ceremony — one of the most distinctive and sacred Hindu-Balinese rituals exclusive to Kesiman village.

Ngerebong is far more than a religious ceremony. It is the living embodiment of *penyuda mala* — the purification of the universe from all forms of imbalance and impurity, encompassing both the *sekala* (visible, material world) and *niskala* (invisible, spiritual realm). What distinguishes Ngerebong at Kesiman from similar ceremonies elsewhere in Bali is its powerful visualization of Tantric teachings, where sacred movements and spiritual symbols serve as the primary medium for conveying profound philosophical meaning.

## A Procession Steeped in Sacred Symbolism

The peak of the 2026 ceremony began with **tabuh rah** — a ritual sacrifice that symbolically opens the channel of communication between the human world and the realm of ancestors. This was followed by performances of **tabuh lelambatan**, the slow and dignified gamelan compositions that prepare the minds and spirits of all participants for what lies ahead.

The resonant tones of Gong Gede and Sancaya Kanti then filled the entire temple courtyard, building an atmosphere of sacred anticipation before the most important phase of the ritual: **Nglungayang Ida Betara**. In this procession, sacred effigies (pratima) are carried in a circuit around all cardinal directions — known as *ngider buana* — symbolizing the restoration of harmony across the entire universe.

## Towering Penjor and Ngelawar Competition

Among the most visually stunning elements of the 2026 Ngerebong was the display of **32 penjor agung** — monumental bamboo poles crafted by youth groups (sekaa teruna) of Kesiman customary village. These spectacular poles, adorned with agricultural offerings and traditional Balinese ornaments, towered far above their everyday equivalents, representing gratitude and the abundance bestowed by the Creator.

The day before the peak ceremony, on Saturday, July 4, a **Ngelawar Cooking Competition** took center stage. Lawar is a traditional Balinese dish deeply tied to ceremonial life, and this competition was designed specifically to spark interest and ensure cultural transmission among younger generations. Participants competed to prepare the dish in the most authentic manner, keeping alive knowledge that might otherwise fade.

## A Living Heritage Since 1937

The Ngerebong ceremony has been held continuously in Kesiman since 1937, and has grown into one of Bali's most recognized authentic cultural traditions. Its unique integration of Tantric philosophy into ritual performance has attracted scholars, cultural researchers, and international visitors who recognize in Ngerebong something rare: a living tradition that has retained its spiritual authenticity while continuing to inspire and unite the community.

Alongside the main ritual, the 2026 event also featured an UMKM (small business) product exhibition and cultural performances, turning the ceremony into a celebration that honored both the spiritual and community dimensions of Balinese life.

Ngerebong is a powerful reminder that Bali's identity goes far deeper than its beaches and rice terraces. It is a civilization sustained by ritual, community, and an unbroken connection to the sacred — and Kesiman village continues to be one of its most devoted guardians.
