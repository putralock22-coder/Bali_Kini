Bali Governor Wayan Koster has renewed his call for urgent improvements to the transportation network surrounding Ngurah Rai International Airport, as the gateway to Bali now processes more than 16 million passengers per year — and the surrounding road network is struggling to keep pace.

## 16 Million Passengers: A Record That Reveals a Problem

The numbers are striking. In 2025, a total of 7.05 million international tourists entered Bali through Ngurah Rai Airport, while domestic passengers reached 9.2 million — bringing the combined total to over 16 million passengers for the year.

While this reflects the enormous popularity of Bali as a travel destination, it has also exposed a serious gap between passenger volume and the capacity of the island's ground transportation infrastructure. Roads leading to and from the airport — particularly in Badung and Denpasar — have become notorious for heavy congestion that frustrates travelers and undermines Bali's world-class tourism reputation.

## A Forum for Solutions

Governor Koster attended a Focus Group Discussion (FGD) on Transportation Accessibility to and from Ngurah Rai Airport held on Sunday (June 15, 2026). The gathering brought together stakeholders from transportation, tourism, and local government to map out integrated solutions.

At the forum, Koster was explicit: piecemeal approaches to the congestion problem will not work. What is needed is a comprehensive, integrated plan that covers all modes of transport and coordinates across jurisdictions.

### Traffic Jams Are a Tourism Threat

Koster stressed that the gridlock around Ngurah Rai has become a genuine threat to Bali's image as a premium destination. Visitors arriving exhausted after a long flight — only to spend hours in traffic — start their holiday on a sour note. The same experience awaits departing guests trying to make their flights.

"Bali as a world-class destination must deliver the best experience from the very first moment a tourist arrives," the governor said during the discussion.

## The Star Solution: Water Taxis

The headline proposal to emerge from the discussions is the development of a water taxi network linking key tourist areas directly to Ngurah Rai Airport. Bali's Provincial Government is working with the Ministry of Transportation to plan a series of dock points, including:

- **Nusa Dua** — Bali's premier resort enclave in the south
- **Canggu** — the hub for digital nomads and young international travelers
- **Other strategic points** — based on passenger distribution needs

The water taxi route would allow passengers to bypass congested roads entirely, cutting travel times to the airport significantly. The infrastructure is projected to be operational by **2027**.

### Airport Expansion Also on the Table

Koster also pushed the airport operator to move quickly on expanding terminal and parking facilities at Ngurah Rai. Current capacity, he argued, is no longer adequate for the volume of passengers the airport serves and will only grow more strained without intervention.

## Integration Is the Key

The governor underlined that solving Bali's traffic crisis — especially in the Denpasar and Badung corridor — requires seamless integration of all transport modes: roads, public ground transport, and maritime routes.

A maritime logistics corridor is also being explored to shift cargo flows off congested roads and into the water, which could reduce truck traffic on key arterial routes significantly.

The push to modernize airport access is part of Bali's broader economic strategy. With the provincial government targeting 6.1 percent economic growth in 2026, getting the infrastructure right is seen as essential to sustaining tourism numbers and the livelihoods that depend on them.
