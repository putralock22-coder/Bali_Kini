Bali has long been synonymous with spiritual retreats, traditional healing, and the art of slowing down. Now, Indonesia is making that reputation official. The Bali Wellness & Beauty Expo (BWB Expo) 2026, held from June 4–6 in Sanur, brought together over 140 leading brands from 90 national and international exhibitors to showcase what may be the island's most promising new identity: a world-class wellness destination with authentic cultural roots.

## A High-Level Push for Wellness Tourism

The expo was officially opened by Deputy Minister of Tourism Ni Luh Puspa, who underscored the strategic importance of the event for Indonesia's broader tourism ambitions. Her message was clear: wellness tourism is not a niche — it is the future. The Deputy Minister of SMEs also attended and publicly encouraged local entrepreneurs to seize this opportunity to strengthen Bali's position as a global wellness hub.

The event drew exhibitors spanning traditional Balinese spa treatments, holistic therapy practitioners, organic and functional nutrition brands, modern fitness equipment suppliers, and luxury wellness resort operators — creating a rich cross-section of Bali's layered wellness ecosystem.

## A $6.8 Trillion Global Industry

The timing of BWB Expo 2026 aligns with remarkable momentum in the global wellness sector. According to the Global Wellness Institute, the worldwide wellness economy is now valued at approximately USD 6.8 trillion and is projected to grow to USD 9.8 trillion by 2029. Much of this growth is being driven by post-pandemic behavioral shifts — travelers increasingly prioritize health, mindfulness, and meaningful experiences over conventional sightseeing.

### Indonesia Well-Positioned to Capture Growth

Indonesia, and Bali in particular, is uniquely positioned to capitalize on this trend. The island's cultural philosophy of Tri Hita Karana — which emphasizes harmonious balance between humans, nature, and the divine — aligns naturally with the core principles of wellness tourism. Hundreds of spas, yoga studios, meditation retreats, and traditional healing centers already operate across Bali, from the rice terraces of Ubud to the surf breaks of Canggu, serving an increasingly wellness-conscious international clientele.

## Quality Over Quantity Tourism Strategy

The push to position Bali as a wellness destination also fits neatly within the island's evolving tourism philosophy. Provincial tourism officials have consistently steered policy away from chasing sheer visitor numbers toward attracting higher-spending, longer-staying travelers who respect and engage with local culture and environment.

Wellness tourists embody this ideal profile almost perfectly. Research consistently shows that wellness travelers spend significantly more per trip than average tourists, stay longer, return more frequently, and tend to be more respectful of the destinations they visit.

## Collaboration Across Sectors

BWB Expo 2026 highlighted the power of cross-sector coordination in driving Bali's wellness ambitions. Support came from the Ministry of Tourism, the Ministry of SMEs, and the Bali Provincial Government — a coalition that created enabling conditions for local wellness businesses to access new markets, forge international partnerships, and raise their profiles on the global stage.

For the many small wellness entrepreneurs operating in Bali — traditional healers, boutique spa owners, herbal product makers — the expo represented a rare chance to connect with international buyers and distributors without needing to travel abroad.

With momentum building and government support deepening, Bali's bid for the title of Southeast Asia's premier wellness destination looks more credible than ever.
