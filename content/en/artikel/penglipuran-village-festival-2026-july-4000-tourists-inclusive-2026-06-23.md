## The World's Cleanest Village Hosts Its Annual Festival

Penglipuran Village in Bangli Regency — celebrated internationally as one of the cleanest and most beautifully preserved traditional villages on earth — is gearing up for the 13th edition of its signature annual event: **Penglipuran Village Festival 2026**. Scheduled for **July 9 to 11, 2026**, the three-day festival is timed to coincide with Indonesia's mid-year school holidays, when families across the country hit the road in search of meaningful cultural experiences.

## Bold Targets: 4,000 Visitors Per Day

Village managers are setting ambitious goals for this year's festival. They're aiming to welcome **3,000 to 4,000 visitors daily** across the three days of the event, with a total transaction target of approximately **IDR 3 billion (about USD 180,000)** — matching the strong commercial performance of the previous year's festival.

## Theme: Harmony Towards Sustainable and Inclusive Tourism

This year's festival carries a thoughtful and forward-looking theme: **"Samskerti Bhumi Jana: Harmony Towards Sustainable and Inclusive Tourism."** The theme reflects Penglipuran's growing commitment to ensuring that tourism growth doesn't come at the expense of environmental integrity or social equity.

The phrase signals a deliberate choice: Penglipuran isn't chasing visitor numbers alone — it's building a model of tourism that works for the ecosystem, the local community, and travelers of all abilities.

## Three Days, Three Dimensions

Each day of the festival is designed around a distinct theme:

### Day 1 (July 9) — Culture and Inclusion
The opening day features an **arts and cultural parade**, highlighted by special performances from **community members with disabilities**. This inclusion isn't symbolic — it's a practical demonstration of Penglipuran's commitment to accessible tourism for all.

### Day 2 (July 10) — Environment and Nature
Day two centers on **environmental awareness activities** and green campaigns, inviting visitors to engage with the ecological values that have made Penglipuran famous. The village's spotless lanes and harmonious architecture have long set the standard for sustainable community tourism in Bali.

### Day 3 (July 11) — Health and Wellness
The final day brings **wellness activities** to the forefront, including sessions of **laughter yoga** — a practice that combines yogic breathing techniques with spontaneous laughter to promote physical and emotional wellbeing. It's an unexpected but fitting capstone for a festival rooted in harmony.

## Going Digital: QRIS and Homestay Push

The 2026 festival will also accelerate Penglipuran's digital transformation. Organizers are pushing for **100% QRIS (QR Code cashless payment)** adoption for all transactions during the event, reducing cash dependency and modernizing the visitor experience.

Simultaneously, the village is promoting its **100% homestay program**, encouraging visitors to stay overnight in traditional Balinese family homes — an immersive option that generates income directly for villagers while offering guests an authenticity no hotel can replicate.

## Local SMEs Take Center Stage

The festival provides a vital commercial platform for local small and medium enterprises (SMEs) from Bangli and surrounding areas. Visitors can browse and purchase a range of regional specialty products, including locally grown rice, signature Bangli coffee, handcrafted goods, and traditional culinary specialties.

## Why Penglipuran Stands Apart

Penglipuran isn't a reconstructed cultural theme park — it's a living village where ancient customs govern daily life. Its orderly traditional architecture, sacred bamboo forest, immaculate streets, and deep-rooted adat (customary law) have earned it global recognition as one of the world's most remarkable village tourism destinations.

The annual festival bridges that living heritage with responsible modern tourism — making it one of the most genuine cultural experiences available in Bali, particularly for families visiting during the school holiday season.
