The 2026 school holiday season has officially arrived, and Bali is once again gearing up to welcome millions of domestic tourists eager to enjoy a family getaway. Among the most sought-after destinations, **Waterbom Bali** in Kuta stands out with increasingly impressive credentials: the water park has earned a coveted place on the **World's Top 10 Best Water Parks of 2026** list.

## Deputy Tourism Minister Makes a Personal Inspection

The government's commitment to ensuring quality tourism during the school holiday peak was made clear when **Deputy Minister of Tourism Ni Luh Puspa** conducted a hands-on working visit to Waterbom Bali in early June 2026. During the inspection, the Deputy Minister reviewed facilities in detail, assessed safety standards, and evaluated visitor capacity management protocols for the upcoming surge.

Ni Luh Puspa stressed that family-oriented destinations must meet the highest safety standards — particularly when visitor volumes spike dramatically. A single incident during peak season can have outsized consequences for Bali's overall tourism reputation.

## World-Class Safety and Operations

Waterbom Bali CEO Sayan Gulino confirmed that the facility had undergone intensive preparations ahead of the high season. All rides and supporting facilities have been put through more rigorous routine inspections than usual, with additional technicians and safety personnel deployed to ensure every operation runs to international standards.

Waterbom Bali is well known for its *slow tourism* approach — deliberately capping daily visitor numbers to preserve comfort and safety rather than chasing the highest possible attendance figures. This philosophy aligns closely with Bali's provincial government policy shift toward quality tourism over sheer quantity.

## School Holidays Expected to Drive 80-100% Surge

The June–July 2026 school holiday period is projected to push family tourism visits across Bali up by **80–100 percent** compared to normal days. The largest increases are anticipated at family-focused destinations: amusement parks, water parks, conservation-based zoos, and family dining and recreation centres.

The Ministry of Tourism also urged all tourism destinations not to focus solely on ticket sales volume, but to prioritize visitor experience management. Overcrowding can lead to poor experiences that ultimately damage a destination's long-term reputation far more than any short-term revenue loss.

## Bali Remains the Top Family Choice

Despite the emergence of new holiday destinations — from Batam to Phu Quoc in Vietnam — Bali remains the first choice for many Indonesian families during school breaks. The combination of modern attractions like Waterbom, authentic cultural experiences, stunning beaches, and rich local cuisine makes Bali an unrivalled complete package.

Beach areas in Sanur, including Pantai Segara and Pantai Mertasari, are the most popular with domestic family tourists, while Pantai Semawang tends to attract more international visitors. Tourism zone operators have prepared shuttle services, reinforced beach cleanliness, and strengthened security through traditional pecalang community guards and Jagabaya village security teams.

## Holistic Readiness

Kemenpar emphasized that destination readiness extends beyond physical infrastructure — it also encompasses human resource quality, environmental management, and emergency response capabilities. Deputy Minister Ni Luh Puspa urged every destination to embody the values of quality tourism: satisfying visitors today while safeguarding sustainability for the generations to come.

With an ever-strengthening international reputation and proactive policy support, Bali enters the 2026 school holiday season with greater optimism and readiness than ever before.
