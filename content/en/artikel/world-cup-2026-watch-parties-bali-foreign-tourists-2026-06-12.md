The **FIFA World Cup 2026** officially began today, Friday June 12, 2026. The opening match pairs Mexico against South Africa in Group A, kicking off at **3:00 AM WITA** from Estadio Azteca in Mexico City. In Bali, the buzz around the world's biggest football tournament is already palpable — bars, sport cafes, and entertainment venues across Denpasar and Badung have geared up with giant screens and themed events to welcome both local supporters and the island's large international tourist community.

## Bali's Unique World Cup Advantage

Bali stands out among Indonesian cities when it comes to World Cup enthusiasm, and for a clear reason: the island's year-round international tourism ecosystem. While other cities draw mostly local audiences for watch parties, Bali's hotels, hostels, and streets are continuously filled with visitors from the very nations competing in the tournament.

**De Berry Beerhouse in Denpasar** exemplified this strategy by openly targeting foreign tourists from countries like **the Netherlands, Brazil, and Australia** — three of Bali's largest source markets. Rather than a standard domestic watch party format, the venue has positioned its World Cup screenings as a full international football experience.

"We've set up large screens both indoors and outdoors. This is about creating an international football atmosphere right here in Bali," the management was quoted as saying by Tribun Bali.

## Free-to-Air Coverage Across All of Bali

Access to the tournament is guaranteed for everyone. **TVRI** has confirmed free broadcast of all FIFA World Cup 2026 matches across the entire province of Bali — critical for tourists staying at smaller guesthouses and homestays without premium sports subscriptions.

**Telkomsel and TVRI** have also partnered to expand digital access through the **MAXStream TV** streaming platform, allowing fans to catch every match on their phones or tablets regardless of where they are on the island.

## A Golden Month for Bali's F&B and Hospitality Sector

For Bali's food, beverage, and hospitality industry, the World Cup arrives at the most commercially promising time of the year. June 2026 sees an extraordinary convergence of major events:

- **FIFA World Cup 2026** running through June and July
- **Galungan** Hindu festival on June 17
- **Bali Arts Festival (PKB XLVIII)** opening June 13 and running through July 11
- **School holidays** driving a surge in domestic family travel

This combination creates ideal commercial conditions for bars, restaurants, and villas in Legian, Seminyak, Kuta, and central Denpasar to generate significantly above-average revenue throughout the month.

## Planning Your World Cup Viewing in Bali

Because most matches are broadcast in the early morning or late night hours in Bali (WITA) due to the time difference with North America — the host continent — venues have tailored their watch party schedules accordingly, with dedicated late-night and dawn sessions for key matches.

For tourists looking to combine football with the Bali experience, recommended spots include:
- **Legian** for internationally themed bars with lively atmosphere
- **Seminyak** for upscale pool bars with quality screens
- **Sanur** for a more relaxed café setting that still delivers every match

With the Galungan and school holiday period starting this weekend, tourism activity across South Bali is expected to surge further — making this one of the most energetic World Cup atmospheres in Southeast Asia outside of the host nations themselves.
