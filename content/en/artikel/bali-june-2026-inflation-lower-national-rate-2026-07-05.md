Bali's year-on-year inflation rate in June 2026 came in at 3.27 percent, Bank Indonesia's Bali Representative Office has confirmed. The figure remains below the national inflation rate of 3.34 percent and sits within Indonesia's official inflation target range of 2.5±1 percent. BI Bali described the situation as under control, while noting the need for continued vigilance in the months ahead.

## A Step Up from May

Although still tracking below the national average, Bali's June inflation marked an increase from the 2.99 percent recorded in May 2026. The uptick was driven by a combination of seasonal factors and policy adjustments that took effect during the month.

## Two Key Drivers

### Non-Subsidized Fuel Price Increases

Adjustments to non-subsidized fuel (BBM non-subsidi) prices were among the primary contributors to June's inflation. The policy directly raised transportation and logistics costs, which in turn pushed up the price of consumer goods across multiple sectors.

### Galungan Holiday Demand

The Balinese Hindu celebration of Galungan, which fell in mid-June, fueled a seasonal surge in demand for various commodities—particularly food, beverages, and tobacco. The elevated need for ritual offerings and ceremonial ingredients is a recurring seasonal factor that consistently influences Bali's annual inflation figures.

## The 4K Strategy for Price Stability

Bank Indonesia and Bali's Regional Inflation Control Team (TPID) have deployed a coordinated response through the so-called "4K" strategy:
- **Ketersediaan (Availability)**: ensuring adequate supply of essential goods
- **Keterjangkauan (Affordability)**: keeping prices within reach of local purchasing power
- **Kelancaran (Distribution)**: smoothing logistics and supply chain bottlenecks
- **Komunikasi (Communication)**: delivering transparent price and supply data to the public

This multi-agency approach is expected to keep inflation anchored, even as external pressures continue to build.

## July Outlook: Pressure May Rise

BI projects that inflationary pressure could intensify in July 2026. The General Price Expectation Index (IEH) for July is forecast at 200, up significantly from June's reading of 192.

The jump signals that both businesses and consumers anticipate further price increases in July—likely driven by school holiday travel demand, peak tourist season activity, and the associated surge in goods and services consumption.

## Bali Remains a Relative Bright Spot

Bali's consistent performance below or near the national inflation average reflects the underlying strength of an economy underpinned by a thriving tourism sector. The steady inflow of foreign exchange from millions of international visitors helps buffer local purchasing power and maintain economic equilibrium.

BI Bali has pledged to continue monitoring price movements closely and to strengthen coordination with TPID to secure adequate supplies of essential goods—particularly as July and August usher in the peak of Bali's international tourist season.
