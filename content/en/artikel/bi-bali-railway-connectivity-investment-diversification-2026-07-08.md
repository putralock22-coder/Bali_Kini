Bank Indonesia's Bali representative office has called for rail-based mass transit as a critical prerequisite for achieving broader and more sustainable investment distribution across the island. The statement came from BI Bali Head **Achris Sarwani** on the sidelines of an investment and infrastructure forum held in Denpasar on Wednesday, July 8, 2026.

## Investment Locked in Three Areas

BI data paints a stark geographic imbalance. A full **64 percent** of Bali's total investment flows into Badung Regency alone, with another **15 percent** going to Gianyar and **10 percent** to Denpasar City. That means nearly **90 percent** of all investment on the island is concentrated in just three of Bali's nine districts and cities, while the remaining six share an increasingly thin slice.

Sectorally, the imbalance is just as pronounced: **98.56 percent** of investment remains in the tertiary sector — hotels, restaurants, housing, industrial estates, and offices — all closely tied to tourism. Diversification into productive sectors such as manufacturing, agriculture, and technology has barely materialized.

*"We see connectivity as an unfinished homework item if we want growth to be more broadly shared,"* said Achris Sarwani.

## Q1 2026 Realization: Rp 13.31 Trillion of Rp 48 Trillion Target

Through the first quarter of 2026, Bali's investment realization reached **Rp 13.31 trillion** against an annual target of nearly **Rp 48 trillion**. Domestic investment (PMDN) accounted for **Rp 9.04 trillion**, while foreign direct investment (FDI) contributed **Rp 4.27 trillion**.

At roughly 27.7 percent of target in just one quarter, there remains considerable ground to cover. BI and the Bali Provincial Government have jointly compiled an **investment catalogue** featuring 22 strategic infrastructure projects pitched to global investors through the 2026 Bali Jagadhita Investment Forum.

### Rail Plans Exist on Paper — Not Yet on the Ground

Mass transit development for Bali is not a new idea. It is already enshrined in **Regional Regulation No. 2 of 2023** on Bali's Provincial Spatial Plan for 2023–2043. Yet as of mid-2026, construction has yet to begin, and **financing** remains the biggest obstacle. Infrastructure of this scale demands long-term committed capital, making innovative funding structures and private-sector participation essential.

## Bali Urban Rail: A Rp 325 Trillion Vision

The provincial government has already selected a strategic investor for the **Bali Urban Rail** project, with total investment across all phases estimated at **USD 20 billion** (approximately **Rp 325 trillion**), making it potentially the largest infrastructure project in Bali's history.

Phase one of the project covers a **16-kilometer corridor**: Ngurah Rai International Airport – Kuta Central Parking – Seminyak – Berawa – Cemagi. If realized, it would directly alleviate the chronic congestion that plagues South Bali's tourism belt and provide an alternative to the private car that has long been the only viable option for getting around.

## Q2 2026 Outlook Positive

BI projects Bali's economy to expand positively through the second quarter of 2026, underpinned by three main drivers: the **school holiday season** boosting tourist arrivals, a **rice and horticultural harvest** cycle supporting agriculture, and **government construction projects** in tourism and strategic infrastructure.

With adequate mass transit connectivity, BI is confident that investment flows can gradually be redirected away from the saturated south and steered toward Buleleng in the north, Jembrana in the west, and other regencies that have long been bypassed by Bali's economic boom.
