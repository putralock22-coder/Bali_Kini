The Denpasar City Office of Education, Youth, and Sports (Disdikpora) became the flashpoint of public frustration on Monday, June 29, 2026, when hundreds of parents descended on the building to protest the results of the **Sistem Penerimaan Murid Baru (SPMB 2026)** — the new national school enrollment system — for elementary school (SD) placements.

Their grievance was straightforward but deeply felt: their children had been assigned to schools far from where they live, appearing to contradict the very purpose of the zoning-based enrollment system, which is supposed to prioritize proximity to home.

## Real Cases, Real Frustration

Several parents shared their experiences directly. **Ketut Ratminiasih**, who joined the protest, recounted that her child had been placed at SD Negeri 28 Dangin Puri — despite the family's home being in the Peguyangan area, a considerable distance away.

A similar case involved a family residing in the **Cekomaria** neighborhood, whose child was assigned not to a nearby school but to one in **Kesiman** — a completely different part of the city. These mismatches left families feeling confused and misled, particularly since they had followed all the registration procedures correctly and in good faith.

For working parents with limited transportation options, the prospect of daily commutes across Denpasar for a young child is not just inconvenient — it poses real practical and financial challenges.

## How the SPMB 2026 System Works

Disdikpora Denpasar officials explained the underlying mechanics of the system. SPMB 2026 operates through an **automated algorithm** that assigns students based on two core variables:

1. **Available remaining capacity** at each eligible school
2. **Shortest distance** between a student's registered home address and schools that still have open slots

The problem emerges when schools nearest to a student's home are already at full capacity. In those cases, the system automatically redirects the student to the next available school — which may be significantly further away.

### The Family Registration Card (KK) Factor

An additional complication involves household registration documents. **The majority of protesting parents held family registration cards (Kartu Keluarga / KK) issued outside Denpasar City**. Under the zoning pathway, priority is given to students with Denpasar-issued KK cards, meaning families with out-of-city cards often receive only leftover placements at more distant schools.

This situation is common in a city like Denpasar, where population mobility is high and many residents live in the city without having updated their official registration. It creates a systemic disadvantage that leaves a significant portion of the population with fewer school choices.

## SPMB 2026: New System, Unresolved Tensions

SPMB 2026 introduced an updated enrollment framework with three pathways: **the zoning (domicile) pathway**, **the affirmation pathway** (for underprivileged students), and **the parental relocation pathway**. The zoning pathway carries the largest quota and is intended to be the most equitable option for most families.

However, the protests in Denpasar illustrate that automated systems can produce outcomes that feel unjust to the people they are meant to serve — especially in densely populated urban areas where school capacity is tight and residential mobility is high.

How Disdikpora Denpasar resolves these individual cases will be closely watched as a test of the government's responsiveness in education policy. If systematic issues are identified, the outcome of this protest could also inform adjustments to how SPMB is implemented in future enrollment cycles.
