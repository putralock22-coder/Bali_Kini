**Jakarta/Bali** – Indonesia is pushing ahead with its most ambitious economic project yet: transforming Bali into a world-class international financial hub. The government is finalizing regulations to establish the **Indonesia International Financial Centre (IIFC)** at the **KEK Kura Kura Bali** Special Economic Zone — a flagship initiative identified as a top priority by President Prabowo Subianto in his broader economic transformation agenda.

## Airlangga Leads Preparations

**Coordinating Minister for Economic Affairs Airlangga Hartarto** personally inspected KEK Kura Kura Bali to assess its readiness as the primary candidate location for the IIFC. Following the site visit, Airlangga confirmed that the government is finalizing a comprehensive legal and regulatory framework to underpin the new financial zone.

"We are refining the various incentives that will be provided to this area," Airlangga stated, noting that cross-ministry discussions on incentive schemes and institutional arrangements are still ongoing as of late June 2026.

## The Dubai Blueprint: Common Law and Zero-Tax Incentives

One of the most ambitious elements of the plan is the adoption of a **common law legal system** within the 100-hectare KEK Kura Kura zone. This legal framework, widely used at international financial centers such as the Dubai International Financial Centre (DIFC) and Abu Dhabi Global Market (ADGM), is considered more predictable and familiar for foreign institutional investors than Indonesia's existing civil law system.

The government is also evaluating the application of **zero-percent tax incentives** for businesses operating within the IIFC. Such incentives could serve as a powerful draw for global banks, asset management firms, hedge funds, and international financial institutions looking for a Southeast Asian base outside of Singapore.

## Why KEK Kura Kura Bali?

The selection of KEK Kura Kura Bali is driven by several compelling strategic advantages:

- **Premium location** at the heart of Bali's globally recognized tourism corridor
- **Direct international connectivity** via I Gusti Ngurah Rai International Airport, one of the busiest in Southeast Asia
- **Purpose-built modern infrastructure** already in place within the special economic zone
- **Bali's global brand** — already embedded in the consciousness of international investors and business travelers

The zone is situated near Serangan Island, just minutes from central Denpasar, placing it within easy reach of Bali's existing hospitality and business infrastructure.

## Eyes on Expansion Beyond Bali

While the initial development is centered on Bali, Airlangga did not rule out future expansion of the IIFC concept to other Indonesian locations. However, Bali's unparalleled international name recognition makes it the most logical and compelling starting point for attracting the attention of global financial institutions.

## Redefining Bali's Role on the World Stage

The IIFC project signals a profound shift in how Indonesia envisions Bali's global role — not just as a tropical vacation destination, but as a **world-class financial and investment hub** capable of competing with Singapore, Dubai, and Hong Kong.

If fully realized, the project promises to create high-quality employment, substantially increase foreign direct investment (FDI) inflows, and cement Indonesia's position as a rising player in the global financial architecture. For Bali, it could mean a new chapter entirely: from paradise island to financial powerhouse.
