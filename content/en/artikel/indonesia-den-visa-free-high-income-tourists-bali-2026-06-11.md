**Jakarta/Bali** — Indonesia's National Economic Council (Dewan Ekonomi Nasional/DEN) has proposed a selective visa-free policy for tourists from high-income nations. The proposal was presented directly in a meeting with President Prabowo Subianto at the State Palace in Jakarta on Tuesday (9/6/2026), as a strategic response to the sustained weakening of the Indonesian rupiah against the US dollar in 2026.

If implemented, the policy could deliver a transformative impact on Bali's tourism sector, which serves as the primary gateway for international visitors entering Indonesia.

## A Weaker Rupiah as a Tourism Opportunity

The depreciation of the rupiah against the dollar and other major currencies has inadvertently made Indonesia — and Bali in particular — an increasingly affordable destination for foreign visitors. DEN believes this window of opportunity must be seized by eliminating entry barriers for tourists from high-income countries who have the spending power to meaningfully boost foreign exchange reserves.

Currently, visa processing requirements remain a deterrent for some potential visitors, prompting them to choose competing destinations offering simpler visa-on-arrival or visa-free arrangements — countries such as Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia that have already been gaining ground in the premium tourism market.

## A Different Approach from Existing Visa Policies

DEN's proposal differs fundamentally from Indonesia's existing bilateral visa-free agreements, which target specific countries through diplomatic negotiations. The new proposed scheme is income-category based rather than country-specific.

Under this framework, citizens of any high-income country — regardless of whether a bilateral visa agreement exists — could potentially gain facilitated entry. This approach is considered more flexible and capable of adapting quickly to shifting geopolitical dynamics and evolving global economic conditions.

## Projected Impact on Bali Tourism

For Bali, this policy holds significant potential to expand the premium tourist market. Visitors from high-income nations tend to spend considerably more per trip, favor higher-quality accommodation, dine at fine restaurants, and engage with premium experiences — all translating to greater foreign exchange contributions per tourist.

The Bali Provincial Government has been actively advancing the concept of quality tourism — a strategic shift away from maximizing raw visitor numbers towards attracting visitors with higher spending power and longer stays. A visa-free policy for high-income country tourists aligns directly with this philosophy.

## Supporting Regulations Already in Place

Separately, the Bali Provincial Government had already been preparing a proof of funds requirement for foreign visitors entering Bali in 2026. Visitors would need to present bank statements covering at least three months to demonstrate sufficient financial capacity during their stay on the island.

The combination of facilitated visa entry and a proof of funds requirement creates a coherent policy framework: lower the barrier for genuine high-spending tourists while filtering out those misusing tourist visas for informal employment or unauthorized long-term residence.

## Decision Awaits President's Approval

The DEN proposal currently awaits an official decision from President Prabowo Subianto. If approved, implementation would likely proceed in phases, with periodic evaluation of economic benefits and national security considerations.

Indonesia is closely studying the experiences of regional peers like Thailand and Vietnam, which implemented more open visa policies in recent years and recorded dramatic surges in tourist arrivals. With Bali's unmatched combination of cultural richness, natural beauty, and improving infrastructure, officials are confident the island is ready to capture a similar wave of premium international tourism.
