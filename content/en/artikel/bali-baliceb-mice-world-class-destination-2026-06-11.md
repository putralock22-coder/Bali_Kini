**Denpasar** — Bali is doubling down on its ambition to become a world-class destination for Meetings, Incentives, Conventions, and Exhibitions (MICE). The inauguration of the Bali Convention and Exhibition Bureau (BaliCEB) board for the 2026–2031 period took place at The Meru, Sanur, on Friday (5/6/2026), marking a decisive step in the island's long-term MICE strategy.

Deputy Minister of Tourism Ni Luh Puspa affirmed that Bali possesses unique competitive advantages that few destinations worldwide can match — outstanding natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and the legendary warmth of its local community.

## Bali's Strong Case for Global MICE

According to Deputy Minister Ni Luh Puspa, the MICE industry delivers far broader economic benefits than leisure tourism alone. Delegates attending international congresses or incentive programs typically spend three to five times more than ordinary tourists, stay longer, and generate sustainable business networks for the local economy.

"Bali's nature and culture are priceless assets for competing on the international MICE stage," said the Deputy Minister at the BaliCEB inauguration ceremony in Sanur.

Bali's MICE infrastructure has also been growing steadily, with five-star ballrooms in Nusa Dua and Kuta alongside dedicated convention centers capable of accommodating thousands of international delegates. The island's strategic geographic location at the heart of the Asia-Pacific region adds a further competitive edge.

## BaliCEB's Five Priority Agendas

BaliCEB Chairman Ketut Jaman outlined five core agendas designed to elevate Bali's standing on the global MICE map:

1. **Strengthening competitive positioning** based on cultural values and sustainability in international markets
2. **Expanding global networks** through intensive collaboration with world associations and industry bodies
3. **Designing more aggressive bidding strategies** for prestigious international events and congresses
4. **Elevating human resource quality** through internationally recognized certifications and training
5. **Enhancing service standards** using cutting-edge digital technology with high-level security measures

These five agendas are designed to respond to challenges while seizing opportunities as global event organizers show growing interest in Southeast Asian destinations.

## Challenges That Must Be Addressed

Ketut Jaman also candidly acknowledged the obstacles ahead. Infrastructure quality, environmental management, waste handling, and a range of supporting factors all require urgent attention for Bali to genuinely compete with the world's top MICE destinations.

"We cannot rely on reputation alone. We must continually improve so that Bali's service standards are genuinely on par with the best MICE destinations in the world," he stated firmly.

The Bali Provincial Government fully supports BaliCEB's direction as part of a broader vision to transform Bali — not just as a leisure destination, but as a world-class, sustainable meetings and conventions hub that delivers lasting economic value.

## The MICE Multiplier Effect

The MICE industry is well known for its significant economic multiplier effect. Every international congress delegate is estimated to spend three to five times more than a regular tourist. With thousands of MICE events potentially winnable each year, Bali has a major opportunity to substantially boost foreign exchange earnings.

BaliCEB's strategic push aligns with government policy actively promoting quality tourism — prioritizing visitor spending power and sustainable cultural impact over raw visitor numbers.
