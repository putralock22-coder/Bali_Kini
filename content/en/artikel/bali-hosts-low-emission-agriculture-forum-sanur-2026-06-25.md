Bali has once again been chosen as the venue for an international dialogue on sustainability and food security. From June 24 to 26, 2026, the Sanur area of Denpasar is hosting the Regional Workshop titled **"Knowledge Exchange on Frontier Technologies for Low-Emission Rice and Livestock Systems: Advancing Farmer-Centred Innovation and Catalysing Climate Finance."**

## A Global Forum for the Future of Agriculture

This workshop brings together representatives from across Asia and Africa, including researchers, policymakers, farmer organizations, private sector players, development institutions, and climate finance providers. The core objective is to accelerate the transformation of rice cultivation and livestock production systems into models that are more productive, climate-resilient, and low in greenhouse gas emissions.

The forum serves as a platform for knowledge exchange on cutting-edge technologies to reduce agricultural emissions — a sector that remains a significant contributor to global greenhouse gas output, particularly from paddy farming and livestock rearing.

## Farmers at the Center of Change

Indonesia's Coordinating Ministry for Food Affairs emphasized the importance of positioning farmers as primary actors — not passive recipients — in the low-emission agricultural transition. The forum's agenda includes in-depth discussion on strengthening farmer capacity, access to appropriate technology, and easier pathways to climate finance.

This farmer-centred approach is widely recognized as the key to achieving lasting, inclusive transformation in sustainable agriculture.

## Bali's Subak System: A Global Model

Among the forum's highlights was the spotlight placed on Bali's Subak irrigation system by the National Research and Innovation Agency (BRIN). Subak — recognized by UNESCO as a World Heritage cultural landscape — is presented as a global example of low-emission agricultural management.

The Subak system relies on community cooperation to regulate rice field irrigation, minimizing resource waste and maintaining ecological balance. This centuries-old philosophy closely mirrors the principles of sustainable agriculture now being championed at the global level — proving that indigenous wisdom can inform modern solutions.

### Artificial Intelligence and Technology for Green Farming

BRIN also presented initiatives to maximize the use of digital technology and Artificial Intelligence in the agricultural sector. These innovations aim to boost production efficiency while reducing emissions — for instance, through real-time paddy field monitoring, fertilizer optimization, and weather forecasting to support farmer decision-making.

## Bali as a Living Laboratory for Sustainability

The choice of Bali as the forum's location is deliberate. Bali uniquely faces the challenge of balancing mass tourism development with the preservation of its agricultural culture and natural environment. This makes Bali a real-world laboratory for integrated development models that harmonize economic growth, ecological health, and socio-cultural preservation.

The three-day forum in Sanur is expected to produce concrete recommendations applicable across participating nations, while strengthening Bali's — and Indonesia's — standing as a global leader on sustainable agriculture and climate action.
