Bali's General Elections Commission (KPU) officially ratified the Continuous Voter List (DPB) for the First Semester of 2026 during an open plenary session held in Denpasar on Monday (6/7/2026). A total of **3,377,285 registered voters** were recorded, spread across 57 sub-districts and 716 villages and urban neighborhoods throughout Bali.

## Voter Breakdown

Of the total registered voters, **1,671,956 are male** and **1,705,329 are female**, meaning female voters slightly outnumber their male counterparts — a reflection of Bali's current demographic composition.

This figure represents a **0.84 percent increase** compared to the Semester II 2025 DPB recapitulation, which previously stood at 3,349,303 voters. The additions stem from newly eligible voters who have reached voting age, address changes, and administrative data updates.

## Data Synchronization Challenges

Despite the official ratification, Bali's Election Supervisory Body (Bawaslu) noted that **13 corrective recommendations** have yet to be fully addressed by KPU, particularly regarding voter data synchronization. Bawaslu is urging KPU to strengthen coordination with the military (TNI) and police (Polri) to ensure data accuracy — especially for voters who have passed away, relocated, or enlisted in the armed forces and thus lost their voting rights.

### The Importance of Continuous Updates

Regular voter data updates are mandated by regulation to ensure every eligible citizen is properly registered. The process is conducted twice a year and involves multiple government agencies, from the Population and Civil Registration Service (Dukcapil) to village officials.

KPU Bali emphasized that the ratified DPB will serve as the basis for compiling the Permanent Voter List (DPT) should any upcoming elections be scheduled. Citizens who believe their data is missing or inaccurate are encouraged to report to their local KPU office for correction.

## Commitment to Data Accuracy

KPU Bali has pledged to continually improve the quality of Bali's voter data. Ongoing synchronization with various state institutions is expected to minimize data errors that have historically drawn scrutiny from election observers.

With the First Semester 2026 DPB now established, Bali has a more up-to-date and accurate voter database as the foundation for democratic participation on the Island of the Gods.
