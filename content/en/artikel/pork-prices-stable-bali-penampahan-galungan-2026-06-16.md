Bali's traditional markets are buzzing with activity on Penampahan Galungan, Tuesday, June 16, 2026 — the sacred day preceding the Galungan holiday. Tens of thousands of Balinese Hindu families have been flooding market stalls since early morning, seeking pork for the Penampahan ritual: the ceremonial slaughter of pigs that symbolizes the rejection of negative forces before Galungan's spiritual peak tomorrow.

Despite this dramatic surge in demand, pork prices across Bali's traditional markets have remained remarkably steady — a reassuring sign that the local supply chain is holding strong.

## Pork Prices: A Slight Rise, But Well Within Reason

At Pasar Badung and traditional markets across Denpasar, **cleaned pork meat is currently priced at Rp90,000 to Rp95,000 per kilogram**. At the farm level, live pigs are being sold for Rp38,000 to Rp40,000 per kilogram.

Compared to normal days, there has been a price uptick ahead of Penampahan Galungan — but only a modest one. The average increase is just **Rp5,000 to Rp10,000 per kilogram**, which falls well within acceptable limits. Surplus pig supply and a well-maintained distribution chain are the primary reasons prices have not spiraled beyond control.

### Badung Government Confirms Supply is Secure

The Badung District Government conducted direct inspections at local markets in the days leading up to Galungan, checking both prices and food availability. The findings were reassuring: supply conditions are described as **surplus and under control**, with no significant shortages despite the spike in demand.

## Disperindag Bali Monitors 10 Strategic Commodities

Bali's Department of Industry and Trade (Disperindag) had already taken proactive steps well before Galungan arrived. The agency mapped out commodities with the highest potential to drive inflation during the Galungan–Kuningan period (June 17–27, 2026). Ten strategic commodities are under close watch:

1. **Rice** — the non-negotiable staple
2. **Cooking oil** — essential for Galungan cooking
3. **Refined sugar** — key ingredient for traditional Balinese cakes (jaja)
4. **Chili peppers** — frequently volatile in price
5. **Red and white onions** — kitchen essentials
6. **Chicken eggs** — daily protein source
7. **Broiler chicken** — a pork alternative
8. **Pork** — the central Penampahan commodity
9. **Religious ceremony supplies** — palm leaves, flowers, incense, and offerings

Monitoring is conducted regularly across traditional markets, people's markets, and trading centers throughout Bali's regencies and cities. Government intervention covers not just prices but also stock volumes at distributors, agents, and retailers — ensuring no artificial shortages emerge.

### Subsidized Markets Ease the Burden

As a tangible relief measure, the government has organized **subsidized market events** (pasar murah) at several strategic locations. One venue is in the Kesiman Petilan area of Denpasar, offering rice, cooking oil, sugar, eggs, and ceremonial supplies at below-market prices to help ease the financial load on families preparing for Galungan.

## Galungan and Bali's Economic Stability

Every Galungan cycle, household consumption in Bali rises sharply. The demand is not limited to pork — it extends across a wide range of goods including ceremony supplies, traditional Balinese cakes, fresh produce, and ritual offerings. Managing this demand surge without triggering price spikes is a real logistical challenge.

The relative stability of pork prices during Penampahan Galungan 2026 reflects a well-coordinated effort between local government, market operators, and Bali's livestock farmers. With affordable prices and secure supplies, Balinese Hindu families can carry out the Penampahan tradition in peace — and welcome **Hari Raya Galungan on June 17, 2026** with hearts full of joy and gratitude.
