Karangasem Festival 2026 has wrapped up with an impressive economic impact. Over four days, from June 19 to 22, the cultural and economic festival in Karangasem Regency generated a total economic turnover of **Rp1.445 billion** (approximately USD 90,000), proving that community-based tourism and cultural preservation can go hand-in-hand.

## Local Vendors Power the Festival Economy

The economic breakdown tells a compelling story. **32 UMKM (small business) vendors** recorded combined transactions worth **Rp358 million**, while **85 culinary businesses** brought in an impressive **Rp1.087 billion** in sales. Food and beverage emerged as the clear economic driver of the festival.

These results exceeded organizers' expectations, particularly given that the festival was held during the dry season—a period that typically sees lower tourist activity in eastern Bali. Strong community participation and solid government backing made the difference.

## Vice Governor Closes the Festival

Bali's **Vice Governor Giri Prasta** attended personally to close Karangasem Festival 2026, signaling the provincial government's recognition of the event's significance. The Karangasem Regent reaffirmed the government's commitment to continuing the festival as a cornerstone of community-based tourism development.

## Showcasing East Bali's Unique Heritage

Beyond the economic numbers, Karangasem Festival served as a powerful showcase for one of Bali's most culturally rich regencies. Known as the most traditionally Balinese of all the island's districts, Karangasem offered visitors an authentic window into the island's living heritage—from traditional dance performances and intricate wood carvings to organic agricultural products from the slopes of Mount Agung.

Local weavers displayed their *endek* fabric creations, artisans demonstrated traditional craftsmanship, and culinary vendors served specialties that rarely reach tourist restaurants in the south. For many small producers, the festival represented a rare opportunity to access direct consumer markets.

## A Blueprint for Sustainable Tourism

Karangasem Regency has announced plans to expand future editions of the festival—longer duration, more UMKM participants, and deeper partnerships with the banking sector to support small business financing. The success of the 2026 edition positions Karangasem Festival as a model for other regencies seeking to balance cultural authenticity with economic development.

With Bali's tourism increasingly shifting toward quality over quantity, festivals like this offer a path forward: generating genuine economic benefits for local communities while preserving the cultural identity that makes Bali worth visiting in the first place.
