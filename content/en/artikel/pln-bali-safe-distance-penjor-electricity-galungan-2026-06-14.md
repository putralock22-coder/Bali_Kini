## Galungan Approaches — PLN Issues Critical Safety Warning

With Hari Raya Galungan falling on **Wednesday, June 17, 2026**, Hindu communities across Bali are preparing to erect penjor — tall decorative bamboo poles that symbolize the triumph of dharma (righteousness) over adharma (evil). These graceful arching bamboo structures, adorned with coconut leaves, fruits, and woven decorations, line every road and home during the festival period.

But amid the spiritual preparations, PT PLN (Persero) Unit Induk Distribusi (UID) Bali is issuing an urgent reminder about a safety risk that recurs every Galungan: **the danger of penjor placed too close to electrical power lines**.

This is not a routine caution. Each year, electrical disruptions and near-miss incidents occur when penjor — especially when wet from rain — come into contact with or fall against live power cables.

## Safety Forum Engages Traditional Village Council

To proactively address this risk, PLN UID Bali organized a **Forum Group Discussion (FGD)** titled *"Collaboration of Cultural Acculturation and Technology in Improving PLN Bali's Service and Electrical Safety"* at PLN UID Bali's headquarters in Denpasar on Thursday, June 11, 2026.

Crucially, the session brought together the **Majelis Desa Adat (MDA) Bali Province** — the governing body of Bali's traditional village councils — alongside media representatives. By engaging MDA, PLN ensures its safety message reaches rural communities through trusted local channels that extend far beyond conventional media.

## What Is the Required Safe Distance?

PLN's safety guidelines specify the following minimum clearances for penjor installation near electrical infrastructure:

- **At least 2.5 meters** of clearance from all sides (left, right, above, and below) of any power cable
- **3 meters recommended** as the preferred buffer, particularly for tall penjor or locations with frequent strong winds

These requirements apply to all distribution network cables, including low-voltage lines that run along residential streets.

## The Hidden Risk: Wet Bamboo Conducts Electricity

One key hazard that many residents may not be aware of is the change in bamboo's electrical properties when wet. While dry bamboo is generally a good insulator, **freshly cut or rain-soaked bamboo can become electrically conductive** — capable of carrying current if it makes contact with a live cable.

The chain of potential dangers includes:

- **Short circuits** that knock out power for surrounding neighborhoods
- **Electrical sparks** at the point of cable contact with wet bamboo
- **Electrocution risk** for people standing near fallen or leaning penjor during rain
- **Fire hazard** if arcing occurs near flammable materials on or around the penjor

These risks are further amplified during strong winds, which can cause even firmly planted penjor to sway and make contact with cables they would otherwise avoid.

## MDA Bali: The Bridge to Every Village

The inclusion of Majelis Desa Adat (MDA) as a partner in this effort is strategically significant. MDA oversees and advises hundreds of traditional village assemblies (desa pakraman) throughout Bali, giving it unparalleled reach into communities that may not consume mainstream media.

Through this partnership, PLN's safety message can be communicated in the cultural language and context that local communities respond to best — framed not as a government regulation, but as a shared responsibility for the wellbeing of the community during a sacred celebration.

Both PLN and MDA agree: **electrical safety awareness must become part of Balinese cultural practice**, not merely an external technical mandate.

## Safety Checklist for Installing Penjor This Galungan

Before putting up your penjor, keep these steps in mind:

1. **Scout your location** — identify all overhead power cables near the installation site
2. **Measure the height** — ensure the tip of the penjor stays more than 3 meters from any cable
3. **Choose appropriate bamboo** — avoid very fresh or waterlogged bamboo
4. **Secure the base firmly** — prevent the penjor from toppling in windy conditions
5. **Call PLN at 123** if you notice any damaged or sagging cables near your property

The Galungan period (June 17) is followed by **Kuningan on Saturday, June 27, 2026**. PLN also advises residents to take down penjor carefully after the celebrations conclude to avoid the same risks in reverse.
