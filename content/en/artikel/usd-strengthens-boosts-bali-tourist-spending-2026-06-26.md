The sustained strengthening of the US dollar against the Indonesian rupiah in 2026 is having a dual effect on Bali's tourism economy. On the upside, a weaker rupiah makes Bali increasingly affordable for foreign visitors — boosting nominal spending and improving the island's competitive position globally. On the downside, rising import costs are squeezing margins for tourism businesses.

According to data from Bali's Central Statistics Agency (BPS), the **average nightly spending by foreign tourists in Bali has risen to Rp2.11 million** — an increase of Rp60,000 compared to 2024's figure of Rp2.05 million per night.

## Dollar Hits Rp17,805

The US dollar exchange rate reached **Rp17,805 as of May 27, 2026**, continuing a trend of rupiah depreciation driven by global geopolitical tensions and capital outflows from emerging markets. For visitors carrying dollars, euros, Australian dollars, or British pounds, this means their currency stretches significantly further when converted to rupiah.

A practical illustration: an Australian tourist bringing AUD 1,000 today receives more rupiah than they would have a year ago — effectively making accommodation, dining, spa treatments, and activities in Bali cheaper in their home currency terms. This dynamic encourages visitors to spend more freely, upgrade their accommodation, or extend their stay.

## Bali's 2025 Tourism Foreign Exchange: Rp176 Trillion

BPS data also reveals that the average total expenditure per foreign tourist visit to Bali in 2025 reached approximately **USD 1,522.44** per trip. When aggregated across all international arrivals, Bali's tourism sector generated **Rp176 trillion in foreign exchange earnings** throughout 2025.

This positions Bali as Indonesia's single largest earner of tourism foreign exchange — a title the island has held for years. With the dollar remaining strong into 2026, nominal devisa figures are expected to climb further even if raw visitor numbers remain flat.

## Bali Gains Competitive Edge Over Regional Rivals

One of the most significant macro-level consequences of rupiah weakness is that **Bali becomes comparatively cheaper** than other Asian tourist destinations where local currencies have held firmer — such as Thailand, Malaysia, or Japan (depending on the period).

For a budget-conscious traveler choosing between Bali, Phuket, and Kuala Lumpur, the current exchange rate dynamics increasingly favor Bali. Tourism officials and private industry leaders are actively leveraging this advantage to court premium travelers who might not have previously considered Bali as a value proposition.

"Rupiah depreciation indirectly makes Bali more accessible to foreign tourists. We need to capitalize on this to attract quality visitors who spend more," a Bali Tourism Office official stated.

## The Two Sides of the Exchange Rate Coin

Despite the headline benefits, BPS cautions that higher nominal spending figures should not be interpreted as a straightforward surge in real consumption. A significant portion of the increase reflects **currency conversion effects** — the same amount of foreign currency now buys more rupiah, inflating rupiah-denominated spending figures without necessarily indicating that tourists are purchasing more goods or services.

More critically, rupiah depreciation carries real costs for Bali's tourism operators:

### Benefits
- Foreign visitors have greater purchasing power in rupiah terms
- Bali appears more affordable on international comparison platforms
- Nominal foreign exchange earnings increase
- Visitors face stronger incentives to extend stays and upgrade experiences

### Risks and Challenges
- **Import cost pressures**: processed food ingredients, beverages (especially imported spirits), hotel equipment, and supplies priced in foreign currencies become more expensive
- **Operational margin squeeze**: smaller hotels, restaurants, and tour operators with high import exposure may see profits erode
- **Potential price inflation**: to maintain margins, businesses may raise prices — potentially undermining Bali's affordability advantage over time
- **Cost-of-living impact**: local residents face rising prices for imported consumer goods

## Policy Response

The Bali Provincial Government has advised tourism industry players to use the current exchange rate window strategically — by investing in quality improvements rather than simply relying on short-term currency gains. A pivot toward locally sourced ingredients and materials is also being encouraged to reduce vulnerability to import cost fluctuations.

Bank Indonesia's Bali representative office has emphasized the need for macro-economic policy coordination to protect Bali's tourism sector from excessive exchange rate volatility. Expanding digital payment infrastructure is also seen as a strategic tool to improve revenue transparency and efficiency across the hospitality sector.

## Second-Half 2026 Outlook

With peak tourist season running from June through September and the dollar expected to remain elevated, Bali is poised to record stronger foreign exchange earnings in the second half of 2026. Officials are cautiously optimistic that a combination of sustained dollar strength, quality tourism initiatives, and Bali's enduring cultural appeal will make 2026 one of the island's strongest economic years in recent memory.
