East Bali is celebrating in grand style. The Karangasem Festival 2026 was officially opened by Regent I Gusti Putu Parwata — widely known as Gus Par — at the Tanah Aron Field on Friday, June 19, 2026. Running through June 22, this four-day festival commemorates the 386th anniversary of Amlapura City and showcases the vibrant culture and creative spirit of the Karangasem Regency.

This year's edition carries the theme **"Tidak Mudah, Tapi Harus Bisa"** — loosely translated as *"Not Easy, But We Must Make It Happen"* — a rallying call that reflects the community's determination to continue developing the region despite the many challenges it faces.

## A Parade of 1,200 Artists

The festival's opening ceremony was highlighted by a spectacular cultural parade featuring around **1,200 local artists** from across Karangasem. Participants filled the main streets of Amlapura in colorful traditional costumes, performing regional dances and showcasing arts that have been passed down through generations.

A particularly memorable moment unfolded when **139 dancers** from the Dharma Wanita Persatuan of Karangasem Regency performed the Karangasem Mascot Dance and the Sekar Tunjung Dance. In a striking visual display, the performers formed the number **386** with their bodies — a living tribute to Amlapura's nearly four centuries of history.

## Ogoh-Ogoh Mask Competition

One of the most anticipated competitions at the festival is the **Tapel Ogoh-Ogoh Contest**, open across three categories: elementary school, junior high school, and general public. The event encourages young Karangasem residents to engage with and preserve the traditional Balinese art of mask-making and ogoh-ogoh sculpture.

Additional activities include performances from motorcycle communities, participation by the Amlapura Running Club, and appearances from Jegeg Bagus Karangasem — the regency's youth tourism ambassador competition.

## National Artists Take the Stage

The Karangasem Festival 2026 isn't only about tradition. The festival's closing night will be headlined by **Donnie Sibarani**, the former lead vocalist of iconic Indonesian band Ada Band. His appearance promises to draw large crowds and give the event a modern, crowd-pleasing dimension alongside its cultural programming.

Bringing national artists to a district-level festival reflects Karangasem's ambition to position itself as an entertainment and cultural destination that can rival more prominent tourist areas in Bali.

## Why Karangasem Matters

Despite being home to some of Bali's most iconic sites — including **Mount Agung**, **Pura Besakih** (the Mother Temple of Bali), **Taman Ujung Water Palace**, **Tirta Gangga**, and the diving paradise of **Amed** — Karangasem attracts far fewer visitors than the island's southern regions.

Through the Karangasem Festival, the local government aims to raise the profile of East Bali and attract both domestic and international tourists who are looking for an authentic, less commercialized experience.

## Regent's Message

In his opening address, Regent Gus Par emphasized that the theme "Not Easy, But We Must Make It Happen" is more than a slogan — it is a collective commitment from all elements of Karangasem society to push forward together. The festival, he said, is living proof that culture and community can be powerful engines of regional development.
