Indonesia's Directorate General of Immigration has issued a firm reminder to foreign nationals visiting Bali: creating commercial content on a tourist visa is illegal. The crackdown is targeting influencers, bloggers, photographers, and digital content creators who conduct paid work — or work in exchange for benefits — while on a visitor's visa.

## What Is Now Prohibited Under a Tourist Visa

The rules make clear that **any commercially motivated content creation is off-limits on a tourist visa**. The prohibited activities include:

- **Paid brand endorsements** and promotional posts on social media
- **Professional photography or videography** in exchange for payment
- **Paid collaborations** with villas, restaurants, or local businesses
- **Other professional services** with economic value, such as makeup artistry or consulting
- **Barter arrangements**: receiving free accommodation, meals, or discounts in exchange for creating promotional content

The barter clause is particularly notable. Immigration officials have clarified that **non-cash benefits still constitute economic value** and therefore count as unauthorized work under a tourist visa.

## Content Posted After Leaving Bali Can Still Result in Violations

One of the most surprising aspects of the enforcement is its temporal reach. Indonesian immigration authorities have stated that **content created in Indonesia but published after a traveler has left the country can still be treated as a violation**, particularly if the content is demonstrably commercial and was produced during the period of stay in Bali.

This means creators cannot avoid liability simply by scheduling posts or waiting until they land back home to upload their Bali-based branded content.

## The Dharma Dewata Immigration Patrol Task Force

In April 2026, the Directorate General of Immigration established the **Dharma Dewata Immigration Patrol Task Force**, a specialized unit operating across Bali's most popular tourist areas including Canggu, Seminyak, Ubud, and Kuta. The task force combines physical patrols with **active social media monitoring** of foreign nationals residing in Indonesia.

Digital surveillance allows officers to identify accounts showing signs of commercial activity — such as sponsored posts, brand tags, or affiliate links — and cross-check the account holder's visa status. Those found operating outside their visa's permitted scope may be subject to immediate investigation.

## Penalties: Detention, Deportation, and Entry Bans

For those found to be in violation, the consequences are serious:

1. **Detention** by immigration authorities
2. **Forced deportation** to the country of origin
3. **Permanent or temporary ban** on re-entering Indonesia

Bali has already carried out numerous high-profile deportations of foreign nationals working illegally on the island, several of which went viral on social media and drew significant international attention.

## Legal Alternatives for Commercial Creators

Foreign content creators who wish to work commercially in Bali do have legal options. The key is securing the **right type of visa before entering Indonesia**:

- **Work Visa (ITAS/KITAS)**: Required for long-term commercial activities
- **Second Home Visa**: Allows stays of up to 10 years for those meeting specific financial requirements
- **Consult a qualified immigration agent**: Given the complexity of Indonesia's visa categories, professional advice is strongly recommended

Immigration authorities urge all foreign nationals planning commercially motivated content creation in Bali to clarify their visa category well in advance of their trip, to avoid disruption — or worse — during their stay.
