Benoa Port in Denpasar is getting busier with luxury international cruise ships throughout 2026. PT Pelabuhan Indonesia (Pelindo) Bali–Nusa Tenggara Sub-Region reports that 76 cruise ships are now registered to dock this year — up from an earlier projection of 73 — as the Bali Maritime Tourism Hub (BMTH) expansion continues to add dock and terminal capacity.

## A Steady Growth Trend

Pelindo data shows a positive trend from the start of the year. In just the first 13 days of January 2026, Benoa Port welcomed six luxury cruise ships carrying a combined 5,500 passengers and 2,802 crew members from various countries. That momentum carried through the first quarter, with 35 cruise ships from multiple flags calling at the port.

Overall, cruise tourist traffic through Benoa Port in Q1 2026 reached 68,035 people, up 5.58 percent from the same period in 2025, which recorded 64,436 visitors. The figures reinforce Bali's status as one of the favored ports of call along luxury cruise routes in the Asia-Pacific region, amid stiff competition from other Southeast Asian ports.

## Bali Maritime Tourism Hub Expansion

This growth is underpinned by infrastructure investment Pelindo has been pushing through the BMTH project. Benoa Port's eastern dock, originally just 360 meters, has been extended to 500 meters, allowing the international terminal to now handle disembarkation and embarkation for up to 3,000 passengers at once.

But the expansion isn't finished. Pelindo plans to extend the dock further to 1,300 meters — a major leap that would allow four cruise ships to dock simultaneously, compared to just two jumbo vessels currently. The BMTH area is set to be organized into several development zones going forward, including tourism, marina, entertainment, park, and multipurpose areas, turning Benoa into more than just a port of call — an integrated maritime tourism destination.

## Economic Impact for Bali

Every cruise ship docking at Benoa brings thousands of tourists who shop immediately, join shore excursions to destinations across southern Bali, sample local cuisine, and use tourist transport services. This multiplier effect is felt directly by small businesses, drivers, tour guides, and operators of cultural attractions along cruise passenger routes.

The sector's growth aligns with the government's strategy to develop Bali as a leading maritime tourism hub in the Asia-Pacific, while also diversifying tourism products so the island isn't entirely dependent on land-based tourism density in popular areas like Kuta, Seminyak, and Ubud. With dock capacity continuing to expand, Benoa Port is projected to accommodate even more jumbo cruise ships in the coming years, further cementing Bali's position as an important hub along global luxury cruise routes.

Looking ahead, Pelindo aims for the completion of the next phase of the BMTH project to help Benoa move up in class to become one of Indonesia's leading cruise homeports — not merely a transit port, but a departure and return point for cruise voyages, which could generate significantly greater economic value for Bali.
