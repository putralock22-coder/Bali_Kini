Investment inequality in Bali is coming into sharp focus. Fresh data reveals that **88 percent** of all investment realised in the province flows into just three areas — Badung Regency, Denpasar City, and Gianyar Regency — while the remaining six regencies share a meagre 12 percent of the total.

## Badung Towers Above the Rest

According to Statistics Indonesia (BPS) data for Bali Province, total foreign investment realisation reached **Rp25.6 trillion**. The breakdown tells a stark story:

- **Badung Regency**: Rp16 trillion — by far the largest share
- **Denpasar City**: Rp3.6 trillion
- **Gianyar Regency**: Rp2.9 trillion

These three areas form what is locally known as **Sarbagita** (Denpasar-Badung-Gianyar-Tabanan) — Bali's primary tourism and commercial belt. Their dominance in the investment landscape mirrors their dominance on the tourist map.

## Services Sector Reigns Supreme

Compounding the geographic imbalance is a sectoral one: **97 percent** of investment entering Bali goes into the tertiary sector — encompassing tourism, property, retail, and services. Primary sectors like agriculture and fisheries, as well as secondary manufacturing industries, attract almost no outside capital.

This over-reliance on the services sector makes Bali's economy structurally fragile. The COVID-19 pandemic provided a brutal demonstration of what happens when a mono-sector economy takes a direct hit.

## Push for a More Balanced Playing Field

Bank Indonesia (BI) Bali and the Provincial Government have both committed to redistributing investment more evenly. Measures being explored include:

- Mapping the locally-specific economic potential of each regency
- Developing **industry clusters outside Sarbagita** that leverage regional competitive advantages
- A collaboration between **Udayana Hindu University (UNHI)** and the government to produce detailed economic potential studies for underserved regencies
- Accelerating infrastructure — roads, ports, and public transport — to make outer regencies more accessible to investors

## Jagadhita Forum Offers a Glimpse of the Future

At the **Bali Jagadhita Investment Forum (BJIF) 2026**, 22 strategic projects spanning Bali, West Nusa Tenggara, and East Nusa Tenggara were pitched to more than 35 domestic and international investors. The forum — initiated by Bank Indonesia alongside the Provincial Government through the Kerthi Bali Sadhana Investment Centre (PIKBS) — signalled a concerted effort to draw capital toward new economic frontiers beyond South Bali.

Projects on offer ranged from the Sanur Health Special Economic Zone (KEK) and solar-powered street lighting to electric vehicle procurement — all aimed at building a more diversified and sustainable economic base.

## A Long Road Ahead

Despite the stated commitments, shifting entrenched investment patterns takes time. As long as infrastructure gaps persist and investor confidence in outer regions remains low, capital will continue to gravitate toward the safe bets of Badung and Gianyar. The real test will be whether infrastructure projects are accelerated and whether newly mapped economic clusters translate into actual deals on the ground.
