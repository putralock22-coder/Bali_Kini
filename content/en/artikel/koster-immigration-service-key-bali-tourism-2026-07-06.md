## World-Class Service for a World-Class Destination

Bali Governor Wayan Koster delivered an unambiguous message to public servants across the island on Saturday, July 4, 2026: poor service to foreign visitors is not just an inconvenience — it is a direct threat to Bali's standing as one of the world's premier tourist destinations.

The statement came during the opening of the **2026 Public Service Bazaar**, organized by the Bali High Prosecutor's Office (Kejati Bali) at the Baruna Disaster Shelter on Kuta Beach, Badung Regency. The event brought together dozens of government agencies, law enforcement bodies, state-owned enterprises, banking institutions, and healthcare providers under one roof to offer integrated services to the public.

## Immigration: The First Impression That Lasts

Governor Koster singled out immigration services as the single most critical touchpoint in any international visitor's experience of Bali. As the first formal interaction that foreign tourists have with Bali's administrative systems upon arrival, the quality of immigration services sets the tone for the entire visit.

"We cannot be negligent. Poor, slow, or unfriendly service at the point of arrival can immediately create a negative impression that spreads rapidly in the age of social media," Koster emphasized in his remarks.

The urgency of this message is backed by numbers. Throughout 2025, international tourist arrivals in Bali approached **7 million visits** — a milestone that places the burden on Bali's immigration infrastructure as one of the highest in all of Indonesia. This volume demands speed, accuracy, and professionalism that cannot be compromised.

## One Location, Dozens of Services

The 2026 Public Service Bazaar at Kuta Beach went well beyond ceremonial significance. Bringing together services from multiple institutions in a single, accessible location, the event allowed residents and visitors to address a wide range of administrative needs in one place, including:

- **Civil registration** services (ID cards, family certificates, birth records)
- **Immigration information** and passport assistance
- **Tax consultation** and financial guidance
- **Free legal consultation** and return of evidentiary items
- **Traffic fine payments** and vehicle-related services
- **Healthcare services** and BPJS health insurance enrollment
- **Banking services** and financial products

In addition to administrative services, the event featured a **Kuta Beach cleanup** and a **sea turtle hatchling release** — symbolizing a shared commitment to protecting Bali's fragile coastal environment. The integration of environmental advocacy into a public service event reflects Bali's broader approach to governance, where tourism, public welfare, and ecological stewardship are treated as interconnected responsibilities.

## Setting International Standards

Koster made clear that public service standards in Bali must not merely be "adequate" — they must match international expectations. This aligns with the overarching vision for Bali's tourism in 2026: a shift from chasing visitor numbers to delivering a consistently high-quality experience throughout a traveler's journey on the island.

This paradigm shift is significant. Bali is no longer simply aiming to maximize tourist headcounts. Instead, the provincial government is focused on building an ecosystem of safety, comfort, and cultural authenticity — from the moment a visitor steps off a plane at I Gusti Ngurah Rai International Airport to the moment they depart with lasting memories.

With nearly 7 million foreign arrivals in 2025 and even higher targets for 2026, upgrading the capacity and quality of immigration services is no longer optional. It is an absolute necessity for maintaining Bali's position as Asia's top travel destination.
