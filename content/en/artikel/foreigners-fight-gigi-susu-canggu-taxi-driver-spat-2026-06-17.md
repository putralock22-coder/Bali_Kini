A violent altercation involving three foreign nationals and a local taxi driver went viral on social media after the incident unfolded in broad daylight outside Gigi Susu, one of Canggu's most popular cafes on Jalan Pantai Batu Bolong, North Kuta, Badung Regency, Bali.

## It Started With a Spit

The confrontation began on Monday (June 15, 2026) at approximately 3:35 PM local time. A taxi driver was traveling south to north along Jalan Pantai Batu Bolong when one of three foreign nationals riding two large automatic motorcycles — identified as Yamaha NMax units — suddenly spat at him.

Refusing to let the incident go, the taxi driver gave chase. The pursuit ended when both parties stopped in front of the Gigi Susu establishment.

## Smashed Window, Heated Exchange

CCTV footage reviewed by police showed that after vehicles stopped, one of the foreigners — dressed in white — immediately stepped off his motorcycle and struck the rear window of the taxi. This prompted the driver to get out of his car, leading to a heated verbal confrontation.

The situation appeared to de-escalate briefly when the foreigners attempted to leave. However, the taxi driver suddenly threw a punch at one of the foreigners in black who was still on a motorcycle.

### Ojol and Street Drivers Join the Fray

The foreigners retaliated, and a full-scale brawl broke out on the street. The commotion drew the attention of nearby online motorcycle taxi (ojol) and traditional motorcycle taxi (opang) drivers, who gathered at the scene and escalated the chaos further.

## Security Team Steps In

Security staff from Gigi Susu — identified as Kanis, Donis, and Kris — intervened to break up the fighting. Their quick action helped bring the situation under control, and all parties eventually dispersed from the location.

Police quickly arrived to gather statements and collect CCTV evidence to reconstruct the full timeline of events.

## Settled Peacefully, but the Internet Remembers

Reports indicate both sides have since agreed to settle the matter amicably without pursuing formal legal action. However, video footage of the brawl had already spread widely across social media, reigniting public debate about tourist behavior in Bali.

The incident highlights ongoing concerns about disrespectful conduct by some foreign visitors in Bali. Authorities and the Bali Provincial Government have repeatedly called on tourists to respect local norms, laws, and the people of Bali during their stay on the Island of the Gods.
