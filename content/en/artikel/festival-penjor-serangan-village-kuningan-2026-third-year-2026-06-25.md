## A Festival Growing Year by Year

The Penjor Festival at Serangan Village in Denpasar has returned for its third consecutive year, establishing itself as a beloved cultural fixture in the local calendar. Held at the sacred grounds of Pura Dalem Sakenan, the festival coincides with the Puja Wali ceremony of the temple and the Balinese holy days of Galungan (June 17, 2026) and Kuningan (June 27, 2026).

This year marks a milestone: all six traditional banjar (village administrative units) of Serangan participate as competitors — Banjar Dukuh, Peken, Ponjok, Kawan, Tengah, and one additional unit — making it the most inclusive edition of the festival to date.

## Months of Work, Millions of Rupiah, One Magnificent Penjor

Creating a penjor worthy of this festival is no small undertaking. Each towering bamboo pole takes months to prepare and costs millions of rupiah to complete. A traditional penjor is an arched bamboo structure adorned with young coconut leaves (janur), agricultural produce, and intricately crafted ornaments that carry deep spiritual and symbolic meaning in Balinese Hinduism.

Five giant penjor decorated the Pura Dalem Sakenan complex during the festival, creating a breathtaking visual landscape that drew visitors from across Denpasar and beyond. The installation and judging period ran from June 22 to 25, 2026.

## Youth at the Heart of the Festival

The festival is entirely driven by the yowana — Bali's Hindu youth communities. For them, participation goes beyond competition: it is an expression of ngayah, the Balinese concept of selfless devotion to community and ancestors.

A panel of expert judges from Kesiman and Ubud, along with representatives from the Denpasar City Culture Department, assessed each banjar's creation based on aesthetic beauty, spiritual depth, and adherence to traditional Balinese penjor-making conventions.

### The Spiritual Meaning of Penjor

In Balinese Hindu belief, the penjor symbolizes Mount Agung — the island's most sacred peak. The flowing decorations represent prosperity and gratitude for the earth's bounty. The raising of penjor during Galungan and Kuningan celebrates the victory of Dharma (righteousness) over Adharma (evil), a theme central to these holy days.

## Corporate Meets Community

PT Bali Turtle Island Development (BTID), which manages development in the Serangan Island area, has been a consistent sponsor and technical supporter of the festival. The partnership between a private developer and a traditional village community offers a replicable model for corporate involvement in cultural preservation.

As the festival grows in scale and reputation, the Penjor Festival of Serangan Village is increasingly seen as a potential draw for cultural tourism in Denpasar — an authentic Balinese experience that visitors can witness during the Galungan-Kuningan season.
