With **Galungan** — one of Bali's most sacred Hindu celebrations — arriving on **Wednesday, June 17, 2026**, Balinese families across the island are preparing **penjor**: tall, curved bamboo poles adorned with coconut leaves, fruits, woven ornaments, and traditional offerings, traditionally erected in front of homes as expressions of gratitude to Ida Sang Hyang Widhi Wasa.

While this centuries-old tradition is central to Balinese cultural and spiritual life, **PT PLN (Persero) Unit Pelaksana Pengatur Distribusi (UP2D) Bali** has issued an important public safety reminder ahead of the installation day on **Penampahan Galungan, Tuesday June 16**.

## The Hidden Electrical Hazard

PLN warns that penjor installed too close to overhead electrical poles and cables can create two categories of serious risk:

**Electrical supply disruptions** — Bamboo poles or decorative elements making contact with distribution cables can cause electrical faults, trips, or outages affecting entire neighborhoods without warning.

**Electrocution and fire hazards** — Direct contact between a penjor or its metallic ornaments and energized overhead cables poses real dangers of electric shock, short circuits, and fires that could endanger lives and property.

These risks are most acute during the physical installation process and during strong winds, which can cause tall penjor to sway unpredictably into nearby cable lines.

## Safety Guidelines for Penjor Installation

PLN recommends all Bali residents follow these steps before and during penjor installation:

1. **Survey overhead cables** in your immediate area before selecting a spot for the penjor
2. **Check the height** of the penjor against nearby cable lines — the tip should remain safely below overhead distribution cables
3. **Avoid metallic or moisture-prone decorations** in portions of the penjor near cable lines, as these materials can conduct electricity
4. **Reposition the penjor** if placing it in front of a pole proves unavoidable — move it to a side where there is more clearance from cables
5. **Call PLN emergency line 123** immediately if any electrical incident, visible cable damage, or power disruption occurs related to penjor placement

## Double Celebration: Galungan and PKB XLVIII

This year's Galungan period coincides with the opening weeks of the **48th Balinese Arts Festival (Pesta Kesenian Bali / PKB XLVIII)**, which runs from June 13 to July 11, 2026 at Bajra Sandhi Monument, Denpasar. The overlap of both celebrations is expected to draw record visitor numbers to the island throughout June.

PLN has confirmed that Bali's power supply system is fully prepared and reliable for the entire duration of both the Galungan–Kuningan cycle and the PKB XLVIII festival, ensuring that residents and tourists alike experience no disruptions during this festive and culturally significant period.

Celebrate Galungan with pride — and install your penjor safely.
