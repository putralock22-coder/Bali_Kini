For many art studios across Bali, performance opportunities have long been limited to one window per year: the Bali Arts Festival (Pesta Kesenian Bali / PKB) held at the Taman Budaya Cultural Park in Denpasar. Outside that brief annual showcase, artists wait — with no guaranteed stage to perform on. Badung Regent I Wayan Adi Arnawa has spoken out directly against this situation and made a firm commitment to change it.

## The Regent's Pledge: A Regular Stage for Badung Artists

When Regent Adi Arnawa attended two performances by Badung Regency representatives at the 48th Bali Arts Festival on Wednesday, July 1, 2026, he publicly announced his intention to provide a dedicated venue where Badung's art studios can perform on a regular, scheduled basis.

"We are thinking about how to give art studios in Badung the opportunity to perform regularly — not just during PKB. Hopefully we can prepare a special place so they can continue their work while entertaining tourists," Adi Arnawa was quoted as saying by Antara News Bali.

This is not empty rhetoric. Badung Regency, according to the Regent, is planning to set up a permanent performance space that Badung art studios can use on a regular schedule — turning Balinese performing arts into a tourism attraction available year-round, rather than dependent on a single festival calendar.

## Culture as the Foundation of Badung's Tourism

Badung is the regency with the highest tourism revenue in Bali — and arguably in all of Indonesia. Areas like Kuta, Seminyak, Canggu, Nusa Dua, and Jimbaran all fall within Badung and collectively attract millions of visitors annually. Yet Regent Adi Arnawa makes clear that this dominance cannot rest on beaches and accommodation alone.

"Badung lives from tourism. That is why art and culture as pillars supporting cultural tourism must continue to be preserved," he said.

His vision: Badung should not only be a destination for natural beauty and luxury hospitality, but a living centre of Balinese culture — where visitors can experience authentic traditional performances, gamelan music, barong dance, and other art forms that simply cannot be found anywhere else in the world.

## Badung Shines at PKB XLVIII 2026

The Regent's attendance at PKB XLVIII was also an act of direct appreciation for the artists of Badung who trained intensively to perform at Bali's largest arts festival. Under the theme **"Atma Kerthi: Jiwa Sidha Parisudha"** (Honouring the Complete Soul), Badung delivered high-quality performances, including a Bapang Barong presentation that drew enthusiastic applause from audiences.

### Thousands of Young Artists Involved

PKB XLVIII 2026 demonstrated Badung's commitment to engaging the younger generation in preserving traditional arts. Thousands of young artists from studios across the regency participated, proving that the succession of Balinese artists is alive and well in this corner of the island.

## What This Means for Tourism and Local Income

Should this plan materialise, the impact could be significant. Tourists staying in Badung would gain far more options for authentic cultural entertainment — without having to wait for PKB or other periodic festivals. This directly supports the goal of increasing **length of stay**, one of the key indicators the Bali Provincial Government has been actively pushing to improve.

For the art studios themselves, a regular performance schedule translates into more stable income. Musicians, dancers, choreographers, and studio managers could potentially earn a sustainable living from their craft — reducing their economic dependence on a single annual event.

Regent Adi Arnawa's initiative aligns squarely with Governor Koster's broader policy direction, which champions art and culture as the authentic backbone of Bali's tourism industry — positioning the Island of the Gods not merely as a beach destination, but as a living cultural capital of Asia.
