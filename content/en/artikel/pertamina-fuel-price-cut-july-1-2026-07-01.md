Effective Tuesday, July 1, 2026, PT Pertamina Patra Niaga has officially reduced the retail prices of three non-subsidized fuel products. The price cuts apply simultaneously across all of Indonesia, including Bali, and arrive as the island enters its busiest tourism season of the year — the July–August peak.

## Three Non-Subsidized Fuels Now Cheaper

### Pertamax Turbo (RON 98)
Pertamax Turbo — Indonesia's premium gasoline — dropped by **Rp1,450 per liter** (approximately 7 percent). In regions with a 5 percent regional fuel tax (PBBKB) such as Jakarta, the price fell from Rp20,750 to **Rp19,300 per liter**. Bali applies a slightly different PBBKB rate, so prices will vary marginally.

### Dexlite
This non-subsidized diesel fuel recorded one of the sharpest cuts: **Rp3,300 per liter** or roughly 14 percent — falling from Rp23,000 to **Rp19,700 per liter** (Jakarta reference). Dexlite is widely used in tourism buses, delivery trucks, and fishing vessels throughout Bali.

### Pertamina Dex
The largest single drop belongs to Pertamina Dex, which plunged by **Rp3,650 per liter** or 15 percent — from Rp24,800 to **Rp21,150 per liter**. This high-performance diesel is used by modern diesel-engine vehicles and heavy equipment.

Pertamina also lowered the price of **Avtur** (aviation turbine fuel), which could help ease airline operating costs and put modest downward pressure on airfares to and from Bali.

## Fuels That Remain Unchanged

Not all products followed suit. The following prices held steady:
- **Pertalite** (subsidized regular gasoline) — unchanged
- **Biosolar** (subsidized biodiesel) — unchanged
- **Pertamax** (RON 92) — unchanged
- **Pertamax Green 95** — unchanged

Subsidized fuels are priced by the government and do not fluctuate with global oil markets on a monthly basis.

## Why Did Prices Drop?

The reductions reflect **falling global crude oil prices** over recent weeks. Both Brent crude and West Texas Intermediate (WTI) have traded at lower levels compared to the previous quarter, giving Pertamina room to pass savings on to consumers.

Under Indonesian regulations, the Ministry of Energy and Mineral Resources (ESDM) reviews non-subsidized fuel prices monthly based on the **Mean of Platts Singapore (MOPS)** benchmark and the prevailing rupiah exchange rate against the US dollar.

## What This Means for Bali

### Transport and Logistics
The drop in Dexlite and Pertamina Dex pricing directly benefits transport operators in Bali. Tourism shuttle buses, intercity cargo trucks, and the ferry services on the Gilimanuk–Ketapang crossing all stand to reduce their operating costs, potentially stabilizing fares for travelers.

### Tourism Sector
With Bali entering **peak season in July**, when millions of domestic and international tourists arrive, lower fuel costs may help prevent the sharp hike in transportation fares that sometimes accompanies the busy period.

### SMEs and Fishing Communities
Small and medium enterprises (SMEs) that rely on diesel-powered generators or distribution vehicles, along with fishing communities across Buleleng, Jembrana, and Karangasem, may see tangible relief from this cut.

## How to Check Current Fuel Prices in Bali

Non-subsidized fuel prices differ by province due to varying regional fuel tax rates. To find exact current prices in Bali:
- Download the **MyPertamina app** (available on Google Play and the App Store)
- Visit the **official Pertamina Patra Niaga website**
- Check the price board at any **SPBU (Pertamina gas station)** — posting prices publicly is mandatory

## What Comes Next?

The next monthly review will take place on **August 1, 2026**, based on crude oil movements and the rupiah exchange rate through the end of July. Whether prices hold, drop further, or reverse depends on global energy markets in the weeks ahead.

For now, the July 1 price cuts deliver a positive signal ahead of Bali's high season — providing small but meaningful relief to the businesses, workers, and travelers who keep the island's economy moving.
