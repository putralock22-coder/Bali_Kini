The 48th Pesta Kesenian Bali (PKB) — Bali Arts Festival — continues its magnificent run on Friday, June 26, 2026. Entering its 14th day, the Taman Budaya Art Center in Denpasar serves as the stage for an evening packed with traditional performances, from the iconic Topeng Bebadungan mask dance of Badung Regency to a dazzling Children's Gong Kebyar Parade under the open Balinese sky.

## Festival of the Soul

PKB XLVIII 2026 is staged under the theme **"Atma Kerthi: Jiwa Sidha Parisudha"** — a Sanskrit-rooted phrase meaning the purification and honoring of the human soul toward perfect, clear, and sacred completeness. Running from June 13 to July 11, 2026, this prestigious month-long celebration of Balinese performing arts draws audiences from across Bali, Indonesia, and the world.

## Tonight's Performance Schedule

### 5:00 PM WITA – Kalangan Ratna Kanda
**Rekasadana (Performance): Topeng Bebadungan**
Sanggar Seni Wayang Waduk from Banjar Umakepuh, Buduk Village, Mengwi District, represents Badung Regency with this distinctive mask dance tradition. Topeng Bebadungan blends comedic storytelling with deep philosophical underpinnings, portraying characters through expressive wooden masks in a tradition passed down across generations.

### 5:00 PM WITA – Gedung Ksirarnawa
**Rekasadana: A Tribute to Ida Bagus Blangsinga**
Sanggar Griya Tetamian of Gianyar Regency presents a special homage to the late Balinese arts master Ida Bagus Blangsinga. This tribute performance offers a rare opportunity for younger artists and audiences to encounter the artistic legacy of one of Bali's most revered artistic figures.

### 6:30 PM WITA – Kalangan Ayodya
**Utsawa (Parade): Classical Arja**
Sekaa Arja Giri Nata Kusuma from Denpasar City takes the stage with Arja, Bali's traditional opera form that weaves together refined tembang singing, stylized dance movements, and dramatic storytelling. Mastering Arja demands years of dedicated training in both vocal technique and body expression.

### 7:30 PM WITA – Panggung Terbuka Ardha Candra (Open Stage)
**Utsawa (Parade): Children's Gong Kebyar**
Two of Bali's most talented youth ensembles compete on the open stage:
- **Sanggar Tari dan Tabuh Rajapala** – representing Badung Regency
- **Sekaa Gong Kumara Sandhi** – representing Gianyar Regency

The Children's Gong Kebyar Parade consistently draws some of the largest crowds at PKB, as young performers — many just primary school age — demonstrate breathtaking precision and energy in commanding the full gamelan orchestra.

## A Truly International Festival

PKB 2026 extends its reach far beyond Balinese borders. Through the **Bali World Culture Celebration (BWCC)**, approximately 260 artists from 10 international groups representing the United States, China, Timor-Leste, South Korea, Japan, India, and Hong Kong have been confirmed to participate. On the national side, 955 artists from provinces including Jakarta, East Java, Central Java, West Java, Yogyakarta, Banten, and Central Kalimantan also join the celebrations.

## Visiting the Art Center

Taman Budaya Art Center is conveniently located in the heart of Denpasar and can be easily reached by car, motorbike, or taxi. Visitors are encouraged to arrive before 5:00 PM WITA to secure good viewing spots before the evening performances begin.

PKB XLVIII 2026 continues through July 11 — there are still many evenings of extraordinary Balinese and world arts ahead.
