Buleleng Regency in northern Bali has once again proven its role as a passionate guardian of Balinese cultural heritage. The annual **Pesona Seririt 2026 Festival** (Fesona Seririt) was officially launched with a breathtaking mass performance featuring **425 dancers**, captivating thousands of residents who packed the Lapangan Umum Seririt open field.

## A Spectacle of 425 Dancers

The opening ceremony of Fesona Seririt 2026 was nothing short of spectacular. Hundreds of dancers from various arts groups across the Seririt area performed in perfect unison, bringing to life the beauty and diversity of northern Bali's cultural traditions. The synchronized mass choreography drew sustained applause and admiration from the packed crowd.

The sheer scale of the performance demonstrated just how deeply ingrained the love of traditional art remains in this corner of Bali — especially among the younger generation, who made up a significant portion of the performers.

## Theme: Abhinaya Kumala

Fesona Seririt 2026 is themed **"Abhinaya Kumala"** — meaning *Expression of Beauty*. The theme reflects the belief that traditional Balinese arts are not merely performances, but expressions of a community's soul. From the intricate costumes and graceful hand gestures (*mudra*) of the dancers to the ornamental displays and traditional music, every element of the festival was chosen to embody this idea of beauty as identity.

Beyond the headline dance performance, the festival also featured regional ornament exhibitions, traditional arts showcases from various community groups, and a dedicated space for local small businesses to display and sell their products.

## Regent Sutjidra: Culture Is the Foundation of Development

Buleleng Regent **I Nyoman Sutjidra** attended the opening in person and delivered a message that went beyond ceremony.

> "Fesona Seririt is not merely an arts performance. It is a space for communities to showcase regional potential, strengthen unity, and drive economic growth through local businesses and tourism," said Sutjidra.

The regent made a particular point of addressing the younger generation, urging them to become active participants in cultural preservation rather than passive spectators. In his view, safeguarding cultural heritage means living it — not just admiring it from a distance.

## North Bali's Biggest Cultural Festival

Over the years, Fesona Seririt has grown into the largest arts and culture festival in Buleleng Regency. It consistently draws visitors from across Bali and beyond, shining a spotlight on the cultural richness of northern Bali — a region that tends to receive less tourist attention than the island's popular southern corridor.

The Buleleng Regency Government has made support for arts and cultural activities a central pillar of its local development strategy, framing them as expressions of regional identity that are inseparable from sustainable tourism growth.

## Positive Impact on the Local Economy

Fesona Seririt's cultural value goes hand in hand with its economic impact. The flood of visitors during the festival brought a surge in business for local merchants, from food stalls and street vendors to artisans selling traditional handicrafts and souvenirs. Locally made textiles, Balinese snacks, and handcrafted ornaments were in particularly high demand.

The local government hopes that the festival will continue to grow as a tourist magnet, drawing more visitors to explore Buleleng's temples, beaches, and mountains — and spreading the economic benefits of tourism more evenly across Bali.
