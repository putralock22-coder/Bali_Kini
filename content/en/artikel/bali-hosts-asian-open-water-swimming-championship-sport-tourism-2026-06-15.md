The shimmering waters of Jimbaran Bay became the arena for elite Asian aquatic competition as Bali hosted the **12th Asian Open Water Swimming Championship 2026** from June 13 to 15, 2026. Set against the stunning backdrop of the InterContinental Bali Resort's beachfront, the three-day event drew athletes from across Asia while putting Indonesia's sport tourism strategy squarely in the spotlight.

## 18 Nations Compete in Jimbaran's Open Waters

The championship brought together **78 athletes and 49 officials** — a total of **127 delegates from 18 countries** across the Asian continent. Competitors tested their endurance in two grueling open-water disciplines: individual races of **5 and 10 kilometers**, and a **4×1.5 kilometer relay** — all in the open sea off Jimbaran's coast.

The choice of Jimbaran Bay was deliberate. Its relatively calm, clear waters and world-class resort infrastructure made it an ideal setting for a high-level international competition. Athletes from across Asia arrived to find not only a demanding course but also an extraordinary natural setting.

## A Stream OWS Series: Open to Everyone

Running alongside the elite championship was the **A Stream OWS Series** — an open-water swimming event designed for recreational participants. Approximately **300 participants** including beginner swimmers, sports community members, and members of the general public joined in, transforming the event into an inclusive sports celebration rather than an elite-only affair.

## Sport Tourism as an Economic Engine

The Jimbaran championship reflects a deliberate national policy shift. Indonesia's **Ministry of Tourism (Kemenpar)** has officially designated **sport tourism** — including marine and water sports — as a strategic priority for stimulating regional economies across the country.

The economic logic is compelling: international sports events attract athletes, officials, coaches, families, and fans who typically spend significantly more than average leisure tourists. They fill hotels, dine at local restaurants, purchase equipment and souvenirs, and often extend their stays to explore the surrounding region. These are exactly the type of high-spending, quality visitors that destination managers across the globe covet.

### Direct Local Economic Impact

The presence of 127 delegates from 18 countries in Jimbaran over three days generated immediate economic activity for local businesses — from premium resort accommodations to local restaurants, transport operators, and street vendors. The accompanying media coverage also amplified Bali's profile as an international sports destination at no additional marketing cost.

## Bali's Sport Tourism Future

Bali has long been celebrated as a cultural and natural paradise. Events like the Asian Open Water Swimming Championship are opening a new chapter: Bali as a competitive **sport tourism hub** in Southeast Asia.

With its world-class beaches, rapidly improving infrastructure, and strong governmental support, Bali is well-positioned to attract more international sporting events. From triathlon and surfing to marathon and water polo — the island has the natural settings and the ambition to compete with established sport tourism destinations across the region.
