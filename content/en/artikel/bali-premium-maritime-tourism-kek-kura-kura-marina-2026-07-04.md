Bali is engineering a significant shift in its tourism strategy. The Island of the Gods is no longer relying solely on crowded beaches and conventional cultural attractions — premium maritime tourism is now emerging as a powerful new pillar that promises high economic value while advancing the quality tourism agenda the government has long championed.

## KEK Kura Kura Bali: The New Maritime Tourism Hub

At the heart of Bali's maritime ambition lies Serangan Island in Denpasar — home to the Kura Kura Bali Special Economic Zone (KEK). This 498-hectare zone, developed by PT BTID and established under Government Regulation No. 23 of 2023, sits just 10 minutes from Ngurah Rai International Airport.

Within this KEK, the **Kura Kura International Marina** is under construction — the most ambitious integrated international marina in Southeast Asia. The facility is designed to accommodate up to **146 luxury yachts and superyachts** in a protected lagoon, paired with world-class hospitality, retail, lifestyle, and marine tourism infrastructure.

Underwater construction work commenced in October 2025, with full operations targeted for 2028. When complete, KEK Kura Kura Bali is projected to attract an additional **1.6 million tourists annually** and generate approximately 50,000 jobs.

## Private Charters and Curated Experiences on the Rise

Beyond the large-scale marina, Bali's premium maritime tourism trend is also reflected in growing demand for exclusive packages that can't be found in conventional tourism:

- **Private yacht charters** — personalized sailing experiences on a rented yacht
- **Exclusive island hopping** — exploring Bali's surrounding islands aboard private vessels
- **Premium sunset cruises** — watching Bali's legendary sunsets from the deck of a luxury boat
- **Private diving and snorkeling** — exclusive dive packages to top sites like Menjangan Island and Nusa Penida

Tourists in this high-value segment increasingly prioritize privacy, flexibility, and personally curated experiences — a stark contrast to the mass-market packages that have long dominated Bali's tourism landscape.

## Aligned with the Quality Tourism Strategy

This push for premium maritime tourism directly supports Bali's broader policy shift from quantity to quality. The Bali Provincial Government and Bank Indonesia's Bali office have mapped **maritime tourism** as a priority development focus for West Bali — particularly around Negara and Gilimanuk, where vast marine potential remains largely untapped.

KEK Kura Kura Bali offers a compelling package of investment incentives to attract global investors:
- Income tax holidays of up to 25 years
- Import duty exemptions
- VAT exemptions on construction materials and equipment
- Simplified PT PMA (foreign company) registration

### Benefits for the Local Tourism Ecosystem

Premium tourism development is not exclusively for large investors. Local fishermen and boat operators around Serangan and Benoa are expected to form partnerships and access new opportunities within the growing maritime tourism ecosystem. The government is encouraging micro and small enterprises to integrate into the supply chain — from guiding and catering to supplying locally made products.

With world-class marina infrastructure, rising demand for private charters, and strong government backing, Bali's premium maritime tourism is poised to become a defining edge that sets the Island of the Gods apart from other destinations across Southeast Asia.
