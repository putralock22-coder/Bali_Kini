In what he described as a first in his career, Bali Governor **Wayan Koster** attended the inauguration of a law firm — and he was quick to explain why he made an exception. The firm, **Lawyerindo Law Partnership**, officially opened its doors at Jimbaran Hub, South Kuta, Badung, on Wednesday, July 1, 2026, offering specialized legal services tailored specifically to the needs of **foreign nationals (WNA)** in Bali.

## Why Koster Broke Protocol

Koster typically does not attend law firm openings. But Lawyerindo's focus on serving foreign nationals living, visiting, or investing in Bali made this inauguration strategically significant in his eyes — and worth his personal presence.

In his address at the opening ceremony, Koster framed the firm's arrival not merely as a business milestone but as a meaningful contribution to **Bali's broader infrastructure as a world-class destination**. He stressed that Bali's development cannot be measured in roads and buildings alone; the quality of professional services available to residents and visitors matters just as much.

## The Gap That Lawyerindo Fills

Bali receives millions of international visitors annually. A significant and growing number of them choose to stay longer — as digital nomads, retirees, entrepreneurs, or foreign direct investors. Inevitably, this creates a wide range of legal needs: property transactions, business licensing, immigration and residency permits, employment contracts, and dispute resolution.

For years, a meaningful gap has existed between these needs and the availability of legal professionals who combine **deep expertise in Indonesian law** with the ability to advise clients in English and other foreign languages. Navigating Indonesian legal processes without the right guidance has led to costly mistakes, prolonged disputes, and — in some cases — reputational damage for Bali as an investment destination.

### Legal Certainty Drives Investor Confidence

Koster made the link explicit: **legal certainty equals investor confidence**. When foreign nationals know they have access to competent legal representation that understands both Indonesian law and international business norms, they are more likely to commit capital, stay longer, and recommend Bali to their networks. The reverse is also true — unresolved legal ambiguity discourages serious investment.

## What Lawyerindo Offers

Located in Jimbaran Hub — a strategic location accessible to the large expatriate community in southern Bali — Lawyerindo Law Partnership covers a broad spectrum of legal services relevant to foreigners, including:

- **Immigration and residency law** (KITAS, KITAP, business visas)
- **Property and land transactions** for foreign buyers and long-term lease arrangements
- **Business entity establishment** (PT PMA and other foreign investment vehicles)
- **Labor law** for foreign workers and the companies that employ them
- **Commercial dispute resolution** involving international parties

## Protecting Bali's Reputation

Koster closed his remarks by returning to a theme central to his administration: **protecting Bali's image on the world stage**. When foreigners in Bali face legal problems without proper support, stories of negative experiences tend to travel fast on social media and international news platforms. These narratives — whether accurate or sensationalized — can undermine the carefully built reputation that Bali has cultivated over decades.

By ensuring that quality legal services are available to every foreigner who needs them, Bali takes a proactive step toward preventing such narratives from taking hold in the first place.

Lawyerindo Law Partnership's opening marks a small but meaningful step in building the kind of comprehensive, trustworthy ecosystem that Bali needs to sustain its position as one of the world's most sought-after destinations for tourism, living, and investment alike.
