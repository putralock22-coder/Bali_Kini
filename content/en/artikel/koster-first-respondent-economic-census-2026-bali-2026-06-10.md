Bali Governor I Wayan Koster has officially become the first resident of Bali to be recorded in the 2026 Economic Census (SE2026). The data collection took place at the governor's official residence, Jayasabha, in Denpasar on Monday (8/6/2026), witnessed by members of the Bali Provincial Government and the leadership of BPS (Statistics Indonesia) Bali Province.

## A Historic Milestone for Bali's Economy

Head of BPS Bali, Agus Gede Hendrayana Hermawan, described the event as a formal marker for the start of census activities at the provincial level. "This first data collection marks an important milestone in the launch of the 2026 Economic Census in Bali," he said in an official statement.

Governor Koster expressed enthusiasm for his role as the first respondent, emphasizing that data gathered from the census would serve as a critical foundation for Bali's economic development. He urged all Balinese residents — from business owners to households — to provide honest and accurate information to BPS field officers.

## Scale and Scope of the Census

The 2026 Economic Census in Bali runs from May 1 to July 31, 2026. BPS Bali has deployed **3,200 field officers** across all regencies and cities on the island. Areas with the highest officer deployment include Badung Regency, Buleleng Regency, and Denpasar City — the three regions with the most intensive economic activity.

In terms of coverage, the census targets not only households but also **more than 640,000 business operators** across Bali, ranging from micro-enterprises to large corporations. Field officers come from diverse backgrounds, including village administrators and specially trained university students.

## Data to Drive Sustainable Development

### Strategic Purpose of the Economic Census

The Economic Census is a large-scale survey conducted by BPS every 10 years, covering all business units throughout Indonesia. It collects data on business types, operational scale, workforce size, and production or service capacity.

The results of SE2026 will be used to:
- **Formulate evidence-based economic policies**
- **Plan investment development** across sectors
- **Generate targeted employment** opportunities
- **Evaluate development programs** that have been implemented

The census results are expected to be fully processed at the national level approximately one year after data collection ends.

### Bali as a National Economic Barometer

Bali occupies a strategic position in Indonesia's economic landscape, particularly due to its dominant tourism sector. Data from SE2026 is expected to provide a more precise picture of Bali's economic resilience and dynamics following multiple global challenges, from currency fluctuations to disruptions in international air connectivity.

## Koster's Call to All Balinese

Governor Koster specifically urged the people of Bali not to hesitate when census officers come knocking. He stressed that providing accurate data is not just a statistical obligation, but a tangible form of community participation in regional development.

"Correct and accurate data will produce the right policies for Bali," Koster affirmed.

For businesses that have not yet been visited by field officers, BPS Bali has opened a self-reporting channel to ensure no business unit is missed in the data collection.
