Denpasar has done it again. **Porjar Bali 2026** — the Bali Student Sports and Arts Week — officially closed on Wednesday (10/6/2026), with Kota Denpasar retaining the overall championship title for the **fourth consecutive year**, cementing its reputation as Bali's dominant force in youth sports.

## A Clean Sweep at the Top

Denpasar's contingent amassed **345 gold medals, 264 silver medals, and 197 bronze medals**, a total that placed them comfortably ahead of all other regencies and cities. Across the 40 sports disciplines contested, Denpasar secured **12 overall discipline championships**.

The two standout performances came from:

- **Wushu**: 23 gold, 23 silver, 16 bronze — a stunning display that reflects deep investment in martial arts development among young athletes
- **Dancesport**: 20 gold, 16 silver, 7 bronze — proof of Denpasar's strength in movement arts

## Battle for Second Place

While Denpasar's dominance was never seriously challenged, the competition for second and third proved fierce. **Badung Regency** secured the runner-up position with **268 gold, 237 silver, and 223 bronze medals**, posting one of their strongest-ever results.

**Buleleng Regency** claimed third place with 152 gold, 153 silver, and 194 bronze medals, maintaining their consistent presence at the top of Porjar's leaderboard.

## 8,500+ Participants, 40 Sports

The scale of Porjar Bali 2026 was significant. A total of **8,585 participants** competed across six days, comprising:

- **7,291 athletes** from all nine regencies and cities of Bali
- **817 coaches** guiding their young charges
- **477 officials** managing competition logistics

The 40 sports ranged from traditional track-and-field and swimming to modern disciplines including e-sports, dancesport, and wushu — a lineup that reflects the evolving landscape of youth sport in Bali.

## What the Numbers Mean

Four straight titles is not an accident. Sports analysts and observers in Bali point to Denpasar's **structured athlete development pipeline** as the key differentiator: consistent access to training facilities, high-caliber coaching, and strong municipal government support have created an environment where young talent can be identified and cultivated systematically.

For other regions, Porjar's results serve as an important benchmark. The gap between first and second remains sizable, and kabupaten sports bodies across Bali are already expected to ramp up programs ahead of the next edition.

Porjar Bali stands as one of the island's most important youth competitions — not just for medals, but as a proving ground that feeds into Bali's national and international sporting ambitions.
