**Singaraja, Bali** – The northern Bali city of Singaraja is preparing once again to host one of the island's most intellectually vibrant events. The Singaraja Literary Festival (SLF) 2026 will take place July 3–5 at the Buleleng Regency Office of Culture and Tourism, marking the fourth edition of this annual celebration of Balinese literary heritage.

## Theme: The Energy of Women in Ancient Texts

This year's festival carries the theme **"Stri Sasana: Energi Keseimbangan Semesta"** (Women's Realm: Cosmic Balance Energy). Drawn from a lontar manuscript housed at Gedong Kirtya — Indonesia's oldest lontar library — the theme invites a fresh reading of Bali's ancient texts, particularly those concerning women's roles within traditional knowledge systems.

The *Stri Sasana* lontar contains teachings on women's wisdom, responsibilities, and cosmological position in Balinese society, making it both historically significant and remarkably relevant to contemporary discourse on gender and cultural heritage.

## Gedong Kirtya: Guardian of Living Knowledge

At the heart of SLF lies Gedong Kirtya, a historic library established during the Dutch colonial era in Singaraja. Its collection of over 1,000 lontar manuscripts, inscriptions, and ancient texts spans fields from literature and history to traditional medicine, law, and cosmology.

Once a center of intellectual and spiritual life in the archipelago, Gedong Kirtya and its collections represent a bridge between Bali's ancient wisdom and the modern world — a bridge that SLF seeks to reinforce year after year through public programming and community engagement.

## Distinguished Speakers and Diverse Programs

SLF 2026 will welcome dozens of writers, academics, philologists, and cultural practitioners from across Indonesia. Confirmed participants include celebrated philologist **Sugi Lanus**, award-winning author **Oka Rusmini**, philosopher **Romo A. Setyo Wibowo**, and novelist **Ratih Kumala**.

The three-day program includes:

- Cultural discussions and seminars on ancient manuscripts and oral traditions
- Book reviews and author talks featuring local and national writers
- Creative writing workshops for young participants
- Lontar manuscript exhibitions from Gedong Kirtya's collection
- Traditional Balinese arts performances
- Community spaces for writers, literary activists, and cultural organizations

### Singaraja as a Living Literary Laboratory

Through SLF, Singaraja positions itself not merely as an administrative center, but as a living literary laboratory — a place where ancient texts enter into dialogue with contemporary creativity. The active involvement of local communities, schools, and customary villages (desa adat) gives the festival deep roots in Buleleng's social fabric.

## Cultural Tourism Potential

Beyond its intellectual significance, SLF plays a growing role in Buleleng's cultural tourism landscape. While southern Bali dominates the headlines with its beaches, surf breaks, and luxury resorts, Singaraja and northern Bali offer a distinctly different proposition: intellectual and spiritual tourism rooted in centuries of written tradition.

As SLF grows in scope and reputation, Singaraja is positioning itself as one of Indonesia's foremost destinations for literary and manuscript tourism — demonstrating that Bali is not just a paradise of natural beauty, but also a living archive of human knowledge and creative thought.
