A clear trend is emerging across Indonesia's 2026 school holiday season: **short-haul travel is surging**, as families prioritise destinations close to home in response to rising living costs and a more deliberate approach to holiday budgeting. The shift signals a meaningful recalibration in how Indonesians choose to travel — and Bali remains firmly at the centre of that conversation.

## The Data Behind the Trend

According to national travel data from 2025, inter-regency and inter-city travel within the same province already accounted for **67.7 percent** of all domestic journeys — a figure that tourism analysts expect to rise further in 2026. The pattern reflects a broader global movement toward "near tourism," where travellers discover that proximity often unlocks more authentic, flexible, and economical experiences.

For many Indonesian families, a short drive or domestic flight to a nearby destination offers all the novelty of a holiday without the logistical complexity and expense of long-haul travel. This mindset is now driving holiday decisions across all income brackets.

## Bali Holds Its Ground

Despite the momentum behind short-haul travel, **Bali remains the top domestic holiday destination** for Indonesian families — particularly those from Java, where the island is considered a manageable journey offering extraordinary value for the experience.

The island's combination of affordable flight connections, an unrivalled activity offering (from temple tours to surf lessons), excellent family infrastructure, and globally recognised hospitality keeps Bali relevant even as travellers become more cost-conscious. For many, a trip to Bali still represents the pinnacle of domestic travel aspiration.

## Smart Ways to Explore Bali on a Budget

For families heading to Bali this school holiday, several practical strategies can make the most of every rupiah:

### Getting Around Affordably
- **Trans Metro Dewata (TMD)** – Bali's public bus network charges a flat fare of just **Rp4,400 per journey** using contactless payment, with routes covering Denpasar and Badung
- **Motorbike rental** – available from **Rp50,000 per day**, the most flexible way to navigate Bali's scenic backroads
- **Car rental** – from **Rp150,000 per day**, ideal for families needing more space and comfort

### Free and Low-Cost Attractions
Bali offers a remarkable number of high-quality experiences at zero or minimal cost:
- **Free beaches**: Kuta, Sanur, Padang Padang, and Nusa Dua — all accessible without entry fees
- **Pura Taman Ayun** in Mengwi and **Pura Tanah Lot** in Tabanan — iconic cultural landmarks with modest entrance fees
- **Local markets** – Pasar Badung and Pasar Kumbasari in Denpasar offer authentic Balinese goods and street food at local prices

### Eating Local
Warungs (traditional Balinese eateries) serve generous portions of nasi campur, babi guling, or lawar from just **Rp15,000 to Rp35,000** — a fraction of what resort restaurants charge for similar dishes.

## Government Support for Domestic Tourism

The Indonesian government is actively backing the short-haul travel trend. The Ministry of Tourism's *"Liburan Cara Baru #DiIndonesiaAja"* campaign promotes nearby domestic destinations as the smart holiday choice. Alongside 95 affordable tourism packages priced from Rp10,000, the government has also arranged discounts on train and ferry tickets to reduce the cost of inter-island journeys.

These measures collectively make it easier and cheaper for Indonesian families to holiday at home — supporting local economies while keeping money circulating within the country.

## The Outlook for Bali's Peak Season

Industry players across Bali are optimistic. Hotel occupancy rates are forecast to climb to **70–80 percent** when the school holiday season reaches its peak in July 2026, representing a healthy rebound from the low-season dip in the first quarter.

The rise of budget-conscious, short-haul travel doesn't threaten Bali — it strengthens it. By attracting more domestically focused visitors alongside its international base, Bali's tourism ecosystem becomes more resilient, more evenly paced, and better positioned for the long term.
