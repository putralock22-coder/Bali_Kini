Bali's push toward a cleaner, greener transportation ecosystem is gaining serious momentum. As of June 2026, PT PLN (Persero) — Indonesia's state electricity company — through its Bali Distribution Unit (UID Bali), together with private partners, has deployed a total of **196 public electric vehicle (EV) charging stations (SPKLU)** across the island.

The milestone represents a significant leap in Bali's EV infrastructure and underscores the island's commitment to accelerating its energy transition while supporting a growing community of electric vehicle users.

## A Network Spanning the Entire Island

Of the 196 total charging stations, **143 units are operated directly by PLN** across **94 locations**, covering every regency and city in Bali — from Jembrana in the west to Karangasem in the east. The remaining **53 units** are managed by private sector partners who have joined forces with PLN to build out the charging network.

The result is a remarkably accessible infrastructure for a tropical island destination. On average, EV drivers in Bali can find a charging point every **30 kilometers** along their route — a coverage density that makes range anxiety a thing of the past for most daily commuters and even longer inter-regency journeys.

## Keeping Pace with a Booming EV Market

The rapid expansion of charging infrastructure is a direct response to Bali's fast-growing electric vehicle market. Currently, more than **4,000 four-wheeled electric vehicles** are registered and operating across the island, while the number of **two-wheeled electric vehicles is approaching 12,000 units** — and both figures continue to climb month by month.

In total, Bali now hosts over **16,000 electric vehicles** on its roads — a number that would have seemed unlikely just a few years ago. The surge reflects shifting consumer awareness, more competitive EV pricing in the Indonesian market, and government incentives that have made switching to electric vehicles financially attractive for both residents and businesses.

## PLN's Expansion Strategy

PLN UID Bali is not stopping at 196 units. The utility is pursuing an ongoing rollout of new charging stations, combining direct investment with partnership agreements with private operators. Priority locations for new SPKLU installations include shopping centers, hotels, restaurants, public parking areas, and rest stops along major inter-regency corridors.

This strategic placement ensures that both residents going about their daily lives and tourists renting EVs during their stay can find convenient charging options wherever they are on the island.

## Bali as a National Model

Bali's progress in building out its EV charging ecosystem has attracted attention from other Indonesian provinces looking to replicate the model. The combination of strong tourism traffic, supportive local government policies, and PLN's sustained infrastructure commitment has created a virtuous cycle: more chargers encourage more EV adoption, which creates demand for even more chargers.

For Bali specifically, the push toward electrified transport aligns neatly with the island's broader identity as a sustainable tourism destination. International visitors — many of whom regularly drive EVs at home — increasingly expect the same convenience when traveling abroad, and Bali is delivering.

## A Greener Bali in the Making

With 196 public charging stations already in place and more on the way, Bali is laying the foundation for a transportation future that is cleaner, quieter, and more aligned with the natural beauty the island is famous for. The 30-kilometer coverage interval and the expanding private EV fleet signal that this is no longer a pilot program — it is a transformation already underway.

As Bali continues to position itself as a global leader in responsible, high-quality tourism, its investment in clean energy infrastructure sends a clear message to the world: the Island of the Gods is serious about protecting the environment that makes it extraordinary.
