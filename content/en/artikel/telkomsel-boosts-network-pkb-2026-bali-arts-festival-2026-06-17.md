The **Pesta Kesenian Bali (PKB) XLVIII 2026** — Bali's largest annual arts and cultural festival — is drawing massive crowds to **Taman Werdhi Budaya Art Center in Denpasar** from June 13 to July 11, 2026. Behind the scenes of the traditional dances, gamelan performances, and artisan exhibitions, a different kind of infrastructure has quietly been put in place: **Telkomsel** has deployed a Combat Mobile BTS unit to ensure visitors stay digitally connected throughout the month-long event.

## Combat Mobile BTS: Instant Network Boost

To handle the surge in network traffic caused by tens of thousands of daily visitors, Telkomsel has stationed one **Combat (Compact Mobile BTS)** unit at the Art Center complex. A Combat unit is a portable, rapid-deployment mobile base station that can be quickly positioned at any location to temporarily boost network capacity and signal quality.

This is not Telkomsel's first time using this technology at major Balinese events. The company has previously deployed Combat units during IBTK ceremonies at Pura Besakih and other large-scale religious and cultural gatherings across the island.

At PKB 2026, the Combat unit ensures visitors — from local schoolchildren attending school field trips to international tourists streaming performances live — can enjoy seamless connectivity. Telkomsel has also carried out **network optimization** at the Bajra Sandhi Monument in Renon, Denpasar, which served as the PKB 2026 opening ceremony location.

## On-Site Booth and Exclusive Promotions

Beyond infrastructure, Telkomsel has opened a dedicated **service booth** within the PKB event area. Visitors can:

- Explore **Telkomsel's featured products and services** with in-person assistance from customer service representatives
- Participate in an **exclusive event promotion**: every purchase of Telkomsel products worth a minimum of **IDR 50,000** enters customers into a roulette-style game for a chance to win attractive prizes
- Access **consultation services** for data plans, digital services, and more

This approach — combining infrastructure with direct customer engagement — positions Telkomsel as an active participant in Bali's cultural events rather than just a background utility provider.

## PKB XLVIII 2026: Where Art Meets the Digital Age

PKB XLVIII 2026 runs for 29 days, showcasing hundreds of performances spanning traditional dance, gamelan music, creative arts competitions, exhibitions, and cultural seminars representing all regencies and cities across Bali.

The schedule for Wednesday, June 17, 2026 — which coincides with Hari Raya Galungan — includes performances such as **Joged Bumbung Tradisi**, a **Rekasadana** piece featuring compositions by Maestro Wayan Berata, and the **Gong Kebyar Wanita** parade competition. These performances draw enthusiastic audiences and generate significant social media content.

For international visitors, PKB is an unparalleled window into the depth and diversity of Balinese artistic tradition. And with reliable connectivity, every awe-inspiring moment can be shared instantly across the world — organically amplifying Bali's global cultural appeal.

## Telkomsel's Ongoing Commitment to Bali

Telkomsel's support for PKB 2026 is part of a long-standing pattern of backing Bali's major cultural and religious events. The company has consistently positioned itself as a digital partner for Balinese society — reinforcing networks during Galungan, major temple ceremonies, and now Bali's most prestigious cultural festival.

As Bali continues to position itself as a world-class cultural destination, the intersection of traditional arts and modern connectivity becomes increasingly important. Telkomsel's infrastructure ensures that authenticity and accessibility go hand in hand at PKB 2026.
