The world of Balinese *karawitan* — traditional ensemble music — never stops evolving. Among the hundreds of gamelan varieties that thrive across the Island of the Gods, one name has emerged as a remarkable phenomenon: **Gamelan Pesel**. What began as a college thesis project has grown into one of the most talked-about innovations in Balinese musical heritage — and this year, it took the stage at the prestigious Pesta Kesenian Bali (PKB) XLVIII 2026.

## Born in an ISI Denpasar Classroom

The story of Gamelan Pesel traces back to around 2010–2011, when a young student named **I Wayan Arik Wirawan** was pursuing his studies at Institut Seni Indonesia (ISI) Denpasar — Bali's foremost arts institute. His final project was anything but conventional: Arik set out to design an entirely new gamelan ensemble, one that had never existed before.

Drawing on his deep knowledge of Balinese musical traditions, he built an early prototype using basic metal key instruments. Before his academic examination even took place, the ensemble had already performed publicly at the Bali Arts Festival (Pesta Kesenian Bali), representing Denpasar in the music development category. The warm reception from audiences was an early sign that this creation held far greater potential than a campus assignment.

## The Name "Pesel": A Fusion of Two Traditions

The name **Pesel** is not arbitrary — it carries layered meaning:

- **Pe** derives from *Semar Pegulingan*, a refined and lyrical Balinese gamelan tradition associated with courtly music and gentle tonality
- **Sel** draws from *Selonding*, a sacred iron-keyed gamelan closely tied to ancient Balinese ritual and ceremonial life

Beyond this etymological fusion, "pesel" in everyday Balinese language means **"to unite"** or "brought together as one" — a fitting metaphor for an ensemble that merges two distinct musical lineages into a single, harmonious voice.

Gamelan Pesel was born and continues to be rooted in **Banjar Kehen, Kesiman Village, Denpasar** — a community with a long tradition of nurturing Balinese arts.

## Thirteen Years of Remarkable Consistency

What makes Gamelan Pesel especially compelling is not just its musicality, but the extraordinary loyalty of its members. Since 2016, the ensemble has maintained the exact same core group — no one has joined, no one has left. By 2026, these founding members, most of whom are now married with careers and families of their own, continue to carve out time for rehearsals and performances whenever artistic projects arise.

Over thirteen years since the gamelan was first conceived, Gamelan Pesel has proven that academic work need not gather dust on a library shelf. With community support, creative spirit, and a genuine love for the art form, Arik's creation has outgrown the walls of academia and carved its own permanent place in the living tradition of Balinese karawitan.

## On Stage at PKB XLVIII 2026

The appearance of Gamelan Pesel at PKB XLVIII 2026 further solidifies its status as a recognized innovation in Balinese music. PKB 2026 carries the theme **"Atma Kerthi: Jiwa Sidha Parisudha"** — a concept centered on the glorification and purification of the human soul through art. In this context, Gamelan Pesel's performance is deeply resonant: it represents a creative soul that has been nurtured with devotion and allowed to blossom into something that speaks to both tradition and the modern era.

### Innovation Without Losing Its Roots

Gamelan Pesel offers a sound that feels fresh without abandoning its cultural soul. The blending of the soft, flowing register of Semar Pegulingan with the ancient resonance of Selonding produces a distinctive sonic identity — capable of moving listeners while still honoring the aesthetic principles that define Balinese musical tradition.

Scholars and artists across Bali point to Gamelan Pesel as living evidence that tradition and innovation are not opposites. From their dialogue, works emerge that can introduce Bali's musical heritage to new generations without ever letting go of its roots.

The story of Gamelan Pesel offers a simple but powerful lesson for young Balinese artists: creative work born from genuine love for one's tradition, when nurtured with consistency, can survive and flourish far beyond the limits of time and place.
