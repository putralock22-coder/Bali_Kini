For most travelers, "Bali" means Seminyak, Kuta, Ubud, or Sanur. The island's southern corridor has long dominated tourist flows, while North Bali, West Bali, and neighboring Banyuwangi remain largely undiscovered. Indonesia's Ministry of Tourism (Kemenpar) is now taking concrete steps to change that imbalance through the Forum Table Top Bangga Berwisata di Indonesia (BBWI), focused on the strategic tourism corridor known as **Destinasi 3B: Buleleng, Jembrana (West Bali), and Banyuwangi**.

The forum brought together tourism business operators from the 3B region with travel agents and tour operators from across the country, creating a direct marketplace for tourism products that are otherwise rarely included in mainstream Bali travel packages.

## Why the 3B Region?

Each of the three destinations in this corridor offers something genuinely distinct:

- **Buleleng (North Bali)**: Pristine blue coastlines at Lovina Beach, ancient temples, cool highland villages like Munduk and Bedugul, and a relaxed, unhurried pace of life
- **Jembrana (West Bali)**: Home to the unique *Mekepung* water buffalo racing tradition, dense mangrove forests, and the waters of West Bali National Park
- **Banyuwangi**: A gateway to East Java's cultural wonders, including the celebrated Kawah Ijen volcano with its mesmerizing blue fire phenomenon, and just a short ferry crossing from Gilimanuk

These destinations have long existed in the shadow of South Bali's mega-resorts and internationally known attractions. The BBWI 3B Forum aims to change that narrative by building commercial bridges between local producers and national distributors.

## Targeting 1.2 Billion Domestic Trips

The Forum is part of Kemenpar's broader **#DiIndonesiaAja** ("Travel in Indonesia") campaign, which aims to stimulate domestic travel and reduce outbound tourism. The ministry has set an ambitious national target of **1.2 billion domestic tourist trips** in 2026 — a goal that requires diversifying destinations beyond already-saturated hotspots.

The forum serves as a business transaction platform where sellers from the 3B region can negotiate directly with tour operators who have the distribution capacity to sell their packages nationwide. The goal is not simply more visitors, but better-quality tourism that is economically meaningful for local communities.

## School Holidays as the Key Opportunity

The timing of the forum — just ahead of the June–July school holiday season — is strategic. School holidays typically trigger the single largest surge in domestic travel each year, with families from Java and other islands looking for accessible, affordable, and enriching destinations. The 3B corridor, with its natural landscapes, cultural heritage, and relative affordability, is well-positioned to capture this demand.

Kemenpar is encouraging tourism businesses in the region to prepare competitive packages combining nature, culture, and adventure to appeal to family travelers.

## Empowering Village Tourism

Beyond the economics of visitor numbers, the BBWI 3B initiative aims to integrate **village tourism (desa wisata)** into the national tourism supply chain. Villages in Buleleng — such as Munduk and Pemuteran, known for its coral reef restoration projects — as well as communities in Jembrana and Banyuwangi, are being positioned as authentic, community-based alternatives to Bali's more commercialized offerings.

## The Broader Vision: All of Bali, Not Just the South

The clearest message coming out of the BBWI 3B Forum is this: Bali is an entire island, not just its southern tip. By redistributing tourist flows, the Ministry of Tourism hopes to spread the economic benefits of Indonesia's most-visited island more equitably — ensuring that families in Buleleng, farmers near Jembrana's coastline, and communities across Banyuwangi can all participate in the prosperity that tourism brings.
