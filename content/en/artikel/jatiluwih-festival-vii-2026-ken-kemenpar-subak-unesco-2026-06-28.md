A world-class village destination in the heart of Tabanan Regency has once again captured national attention. The 7th Jatiluwih Festival was officially opened on Saturday, June 20, 2026, marking a historic milestone: for the first time, the event successfully earned a spot in the prestigious Karisma Event Nusantara (KEN) calendar — a national event recognition program managed directly by Indonesia's Ministry of Tourism. This achievement underlines the growing importance of Jatiluwih as a model destination for sustainable cultural and nature-based tourism.

## Theme: In Balance with Nature, Inspired by Tradition

The two-day festival (June 20–21, 2026) adopted the theme **"In Balance with Nature, Inspired by Tradition"**, reflecting the community's commitment to maintaining harmony between nature, ancestral customs, and responsible tourism development. Regent of Tabanan I Komang Gede Sanjaya, who officially opened the event, emphasized that the festival is designed not merely as entertainment, but as a genuine vehicle for cultural promotion and a driver of grassroots economic activity.

He noted that successfully passing KEN's rigorous curation process signals national recognition of Jatiluwih Festival's consistency in delivering authentic, high-quality cultural and nature-based experiences.

## Activities: From Traditional Harvest to Fun Run Through UNESCO Heritage

Jatiluwih Festival VII offered a diverse lineup of activities blending cultural tourism with sports:

- **Traditional farming activities** involving more than 50 local farmers, including hands-on traditional rice harvesting demonstrations
- **UMKM (small business) exhibitions** showcasing local culinary products, handicrafts, and community produce
- **A fun run through the subak terraces** — the newest highlight of the festival, inviting participants to jog through the UNESCO-recognized rice terrace landscape while experiencing authentic Balinese rural life
- **Traditional performing arts** from Tabanan, including Balinese dances and gamelan music

The organizers targeted up to 2,000 participants for the fun run. The response exceeded expectations, with accommodation establishments in and around the Jatiluwih area recording a **100 percent occupancy rate** throughout the festival.

## Subak: A Living UNESCO World Heritage

Jatiluwih is more than a picturesque landscape. It is home to one of Bali's four subak areas that were collectively inscribed by UNESCO in 2012 as a World Cultural Heritage, under the designation *Cultural Landscape of Bali Province: the Subak System as a Manifestation of the Tri Hita Karana Philosophy*. The subak irrigation system — over a thousand years old — embodies the Balinese philosophy of balance between humans, nature, and the divine.

This UNESCO status elevates the Jatiluwih Festival beyond a typical village fair. Visitors are not just passive spectators of the scenery; they are actively engaged in activities that keep ancestral farming traditions alive and meaningful.

## A Milestone in Bali's 100 Years of Tourism

The 7th Jatiluwih Festival was held against the backdrop of the **100th Anniversary of Bali Tourism**, a celebratory milestone that reinforces Bali's position as an internationally recognized destination that continues to innovate. The festival's entry into the KEN calendar sends a clear signal that tourism potential outside Bali's crowded southern coast is vast and ready to be developed.

Tourism stakeholders hope the success of Jatiluwih Festival VII will inspire more travelers to venture into Bali's rural heartland — rich in culture and natural beauty — while helping to distribute the economic benefits of tourism more equitably across the island.
