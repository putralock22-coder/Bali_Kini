Ten Bali residents belonging to the Coalition for Environmental Movement and Sustainability — known as **Koalisi Pulihkan Bali** — have formally filed a *citizen lawsuit* at the Denpasar District Court. The case, registered under docket number **1024/Pdt.G/2026/PN Dps**, was submitted on July 2, 2026, and targets 14 government officials ranging from local district heads all the way up to the President of Indonesia.

## The September 2025 Floods That Triggered the Case

The lawsuit stems from catastrophic flash floods that struck the Sarbagita metropolitan area — covering Denpasar, Badung, Gianyar, and Tabanan — on **September 10, 2025**. The disaster left at least **18 people dead**, 295 residents displaced, and **6,309 households affected**.

Infrastructure damage was severe: 520 public facilities were damaged, three bridges collapsed, 23 road sections were destroyed, 82 retaining walls failed, and 194 homes were damaged. The coalition argues that the scale of destruction was not purely an act of nature but the direct result of government failure in environmental management, spatial planning, and disaster mitigation.

The 10 plaintiffs are supported by **19 legal advocates** from various civil society and legal aid organizations.

## The 14 Defendants

The lawsuit names 14 officials across multiple levels of government:

- **President Prabowo Subianto**
- Minister of Finance
- Minister of Energy and Mineral Resources
- Minister of Investment and Downstream Processing/BKPM
- Minister of Environment
- Minister of Forestry
- Minister of Public Works
- Minister of Agrarian Affairs and Spatial Planning
- **Bali Governor Wayan Koster**
- Bali Regional Legislature (DPRD)
- **Denpasar Mayor IGN Jaya Negara**
- **Badung Regent Wayan Adi Arnawa**
- Gianyar Regent I Made Mahayastra
- Tabanan Regent I Komang Gede Sanjaya

## Alarming Land Conversion Data

The lawsuit is backed by striking land-conversion figures from the National Land Agency (BPN) covering 2019–2024: the Sarbagita area lost no fewer than **6,522 hectares of rice fields** in just five years.

Denpasar City recorded the steepest proportional decline, losing **38.3 percent** of its rice-field area. Gianyar Regency lost the most in absolute terms at **1,745 hectares**, followed by Badung at **771 hectares**.

The remaining forest cover in the Ayung River watershed stands at roughly **1,500 hectares**, while Denpasar's urban green space reaches only **3.2 percent** of city area — far below the statutory minimum of 30 percent.

### Prior Warning Ignored

Before filing the lawsuit, the coalition sent a formal notice to the relevant officials on **November 12, 2025**. When no substantive response came from the government, the coalition proceeded to court.

## Demands: Climate Justice Policies, Not Financial Damages

Crucially, the plaintiffs are not seeking monetary compensation. Instead, they are asking the court to order the government to enact a series of concrete policies:

- Draft a national **Climate Justice Bill**
- Issue a **Bali Climate Justice Regional Regulation**
- Conduct a comprehensive spatial audit of the Sarbagita area
- Impose a moratorium on business permits that risk environmental harm
- Rehabilitate the Ayung River watershed and expand urban green space
- Build a flood early warning system
- Upgrade urban drainage infrastructure
- Accelerate solid waste management programs

This citizen lawsuit represents one of the most comprehensive environmental legal actions ever brought in Bali, and signals a growing awareness among citizens of their constitutional right to a healthy environment.
