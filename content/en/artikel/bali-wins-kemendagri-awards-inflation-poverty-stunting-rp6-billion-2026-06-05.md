## Bali Takes Home Two Top Prizes from the Ministry of Home Affairs

The Bali Provincial Government made headlines on **June 4, 2026**, walking away with two of the most prestigious awards at the Ministry of Home Affairs (*Kemendagri*) Best Regional Government Night 2026 for the Java-Bali Region. The gala event took place at the Marriott Hotel in Yogyakarta and brought together senior officials from regional governments across Java and Bali.

Governor Wayan Koster personally received both awards on behalf of the Bali Provincial Government. The province claimed **first place** in two separate categories simultaneously — a remarkable achievement that established Bali as the highest-performing province in both economic stability and human development within the Java-Bali region.

## Two Back-to-Back National Honors

### 1st Place: Provincial Inflation Control

The first award recognized Bali's exceptional performance in **controlling inflation** at the provincial level. The trophy and certificate were presented by Titiek Soeharto, Chairwoman of Commission IV of the House of Representatives (DPR RI), in front of all attending delegates.

Keeping inflation in check is no small feat, particularly against the backdrop of global commodity price pressures and post-pandemic economic adjustments. Bali's success in this category reflects strong coordination between the provincial government, Bank Indonesia's Bali Representative Office, the Regional Inflation Control Team (TPID), and key stakeholders in food supply and distribution.

### 1st Place: Poverty and Stunting Reduction

Bali's second award was equally significant. The province was named **national first-place winner** in the category of **Poverty Alleviation and Stunting Reduction** at the provincial level. This honor was presented by Maruarar Sirait, Minister of Housing and Settlements.

Stunting — a condition of chronic undernutrition leading to impaired growth and development in children — and poverty reduction are two of the most critical indicators of human development quality in any region. Bali's achievement in both areas speaks to well-targeted, data-driven social programs that are reaching those who need them most.

## Rp6 Billion in Fiscal Incentives: A Tangible Reward

Beyond the trophies, Bali's double win came with a very concrete benefit: **Rp6 billion in fiscal incentives** from the central government.

Kemendagri's incentive framework is structured as follows:
- **3rd Place:** Rp1 billion
- **2nd Place:** Rp2 billion
- **1st Place:** Rp3 billion

Since Bali claimed first place in both categories, the total fiscal incentive amounts to **Rp3 billion × 2 = Rp6 billion**. These funds will strengthen Bali's provincial budget capacity, enabling continued investment in the programs that delivered these results.

## The Policies Behind the Prizes

Bali's success was built over years of consistent policy implementation. The key strategies that contributed to both awards include:

### Inflation Management
- Regular coordination meetings of the Regional Inflation Control Team (TPID)
- Subsidized market operations (*operasi pasar murah*) across all regencies and cities
- Strict monitoring of essential commodity distribution chains
- Collaboration with regional state-owned enterprises and market traders to maintain stable stock levels

### Poverty and Stunting Reduction
- Free nutritious meal programs for toddlers and pregnant mothers
- Intensification of community health posts (*Posyandu*) across rural Bali
- Targeted social assistance based on integrated data systems
- Skills training and empowerment programs for low-income households

## Bali Reinforces Its Reputation as a Governance Benchmark

These national awards reaffirm Bali's standing as one of Indonesia's best-governed provinces. In a period marked by global inflation pressures, a rapidly changing tourism landscape, and the continuing need to lift living standards, Bali has demonstrated that economic stability and human welfare can advance hand in hand.

Governor Koster underlined that the recognition belongs to every citizen of Bali, and serves as motivation to further elevate the quality of public services and evidence-based development programs across the island.
