The Central Statistics Agency (BPS) of Bali Province has released strong economic data for the first quarter of 2026. Bali's economy expanded by 5.58 percent year-on-year (y-o-y) in Q1 2026 — outpacing the national growth average and reaffirming the island's status as one of Indonesia's most economically resilient provinces.

## GRDP Hits Rp85 Trillion

Bali's Gross Regional Domestic Product (GRDP) at current prices reached Rp85.05 trillion in Q1 2026. At constant 2010 prices, the figure stood at Rp46.75 trillion. These numbers reflect an economy that continues to draw strength from its deep roots in tourism and hospitality services.

## Three Engines of Growth

### 1. Religious Holiday Spending

Two major religious celebrations fell within Q1 2026: Nyepi (the Balinese Hindu New Year) and Eid al-Fitr (Idulfitri). The overlap of these two significant holidays from different faith traditions created a compound boost to household consumption, domestic travel, and demand across the hospitality sector.

### 2. Accommodation and Food & Beverage

The Accommodation and Food & Beverage sector remained Bali's single largest economic contributor, adding 1.20 percentage points to overall year-on-year growth. Peak-season demand during the holiday period drove hotel occupancy and restaurant revenue to elevated levels, sustaining the sector's outsized role in the provincial economy.

### 3. Broader Tourism Activity

Tourism-linked spending across transport, retail, entertainment, and attraction services provided broad support throughout the quarter. The growth of visitor markets from India and Australia — two of Bali's fastest-growing source countries — contributed meaningfully to the sector's performance.

## Seasonal Dip Quarter-on-Quarter

Despite the impressive annual growth, Bali's economy contracted 4.57 percent on a quarter-on-quarter (q-to-q) basis. This is a well-established seasonal pattern rather than a structural concern: January and February see a relative lull in tourist arrivals compared to the prior December peak, before numbers recover from March onward.

## Positive Outlook for Q2 2026

Bank Indonesia's Bali Representative Office projects even stronger performance in Q2 2026. Key tailwinds include:

- **Peak international tourist season** for India and Australia arrivals in June–July
- **Domestic school holidays** driving family travel to Bali
- **Harvest season** benefiting the agricultural and trade sectors
- **Government infrastructure projects** worth a combined Rp788 billion progressing toward completion

The priority infrastructure works include a parking complex at Pura Ulun Danu Batur, the Tukad Unda raw water reservoir, the Nusa Ceningan–Nusa Lembongan Bridge, and the Jimbaran underpass. These investments are expected to add construction sector momentum throughout Q2 and into the second half of the year.

## Resilience in a Turbulent World

Against a backdrop of global geopolitical tensions and a weakening rupiah, Bali's Q1 2026 economic performance stands out as a testament to the island's structural strengths. Its world-class tourism brand provides a natural buffer against external shocks, while a softer rupiah inadvertently makes Bali more competitive as an international destination — a tailwind that could further lift inbound visitor numbers and spending in the quarters ahead.
