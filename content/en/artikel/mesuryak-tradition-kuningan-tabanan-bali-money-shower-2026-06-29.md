Thousands of locals and visitors gathered in **Desa Bongan, Tabanan**, Bali on Saturday (27/6/2026) to witness one of the island's most unique and joyful traditions: **Mesuryak**. As Hari Raya Kuningan arrived, villagers threw money and rice into the air in a spirited celebration believed to send their ancestors back to the heavens in peace and with sacred provisions.

## What Is Mesuryak?

The word *mesuryak* comes from the Balinese word meaning to shout or cheer with joy. This ancient ritual takes place every six months, always on **Hari Raya Kuningan** — the day that falls ten days after Galungan. According to Balinese Hindu belief, ancestral spirits descend to earth during Galungan to visit their descendants, then return to the spirit realm on Kuningan.

To honor this return journey, families and communities gather in open courtyards and simultaneously toss coins, paper money, and rice into the air while cheering loudly. These offerings are believed to serve as spiritual provisions for the ancestors as they make their way back to *Suarga Loka* — the divine realm.

## A Ceremony of Joy and Remembrance

What makes Mesuryak stand out among Bali's many sacred traditions is its atmosphere of **unrestrained happiness**. Unlike solemn rituals, this ceremony is filled with laughter, shouts of joy, and the scrambling of children and adults alike to collect the money that rains down from above.

The ceremony typically runs from **9:00 AM to noon WITA**, bringing together the entire community regardless of age or social standing. This collective spirit reflects one of Bali's core values: the deep, joyful bond between the living and those who have passed on.

## Nationally Recognized Heritage

In 2025, Mesuryak was officially listed as **National Intangible Cultural Heritage (WBTB)** by the Indonesian government. This recognition gives the tradition lasting protection and places it alongside hundreds of other Indonesian cultural practices deemed worthy of preservation for future generations.

For Tabanan residents, the recognition is a source of immense pride — a formal acknowledgment that their centuries-old custom holds national significance.

## A Must-See for Visitors

Every Kuningan celebration, Mesuryak attracts a growing number of domestic and international tourists who come specifically to witness the spectacle. The sight of money filling the air amid joyous shouts is unlike anything most visitors have ever seen, making it one of Bali's most Instagram-worthy and authentic cultural experiences.

**Practical information:** Desa Bongan is located in Tabanan district, approximately **20 minutes by car from Tanah Lot** — one of Bali's most iconic temples. This makes it easy to combine a Mesuryak visit with other popular attractions in the area.

## Kuningan 2026 in Full Swing

Kuningan 2026 fell on June 27, bringing the entire island alive with ceremonies, prayers, and community gatherings. The streets of Bali were lined with *penjor* — tall bamboo poles decorated with offerings — and the air was thick with incense and the sound of gamelan music.

For travelers visiting Bali in late June, witnessing Mesuryak or any of the Kuningan celebrations offers a rare window into the soul of Balinese culture: deeply spiritual, communal, and overflowing with life.
