Penglipuran Tourism Village in Bangli Regency, Bali, is experiencing a remarkable surge in visitors during the 2026 school holiday season. An average of **3,500 tourists** are arriving each day — nearly double the usual daily figure of around 2,000 visitors. The trend is expected to accelerate further as Penglipuran Village Festival XIII approaches, running **July 9–11, 2026**.

## School Holiday Boom

Village managers have warmly welcomed the flood of visitors, the majority of whom are domestic tourists from across Indonesia. Throughout the school holiday period — stretching from late June into early July 2026 — there has barely been a quiet day in this village, renowned for its perfectly uniform traditional houses and dense bamboo forest.

Even so, managers are urging all guests to help preserve the village's integrity: disposing of waste properly, reducing single-use plastics, respecting local customs and culture, and supporting the local economy by purchasing products from small and micro businesses (UMKM) and sampling Penglipuran's traditional cuisine.

## Festival XIII: Harmony for Regenerative Tourism

The Penglipuran Village Festival XIII 2026 carries the theme **"Harmoni Bhumi Penglipuran: Towards Inclusive, Sustainable, and Regenerative Tourism"**. Organizers are targeting up to **4,000 visitors per day** during the festival itself — even higher than the already-impressive school holiday figures.

The three-day program is packed with cultural events:

### Thursday, July 9 — Grand Opening
The opening day features a colossal dance performance, a spectacular **Gebogan Parade** (elaborate offerings of fruit and flowers balanced on the head), welcoming dances, the official opening ceremony, and a Palegongan dance performance titled "Raksan Gumi." Visitors can also enjoy bondres (traditional Balinese comedic performance) and watch the gebogan and penjor (bamboo pole decoration) competitions judged by village elders.

### Friday, July 10 — Education and Creativity
Day two focuses on environmental workshops, a vintage fashion competition celebrating Bali's past, and an evening of local music performances.

### Saturday, July 11 — Closing in Joy
The festival closes with a communal Laughter Yoga session and local music acts — a celebration of health, togetherness, and the positive spirit of Penglipuran's community.

## The 4S Experience: Beyond Just Photos

Penglipuran Village Festival XIII uses a **4S concept**: *Something to Do, Something to See, Something to Buy, and Something to Learn*. This framework ensures visitors leave with far more than photographs — they can participate in creative activities, watch live cultural performances, shop for local handicrafts, and gain genuine insight into Bali's traditional village life and environmental philosophy.

Festival organizers are also encouraging cashless payments via QRIS and promoting 100 percent homestay-based accommodation to ensure economic benefits flow directly to village residents.

## World-Class Recognition

Penglipuran is not just a local gem. In 2024, the village received the prestigious **Best Tourism Village** award from UN Tourism (the United Nations' World Tourism Organization), placing it among the world's finest villages for its commitment to cultural preservation, environmental stewardship, and community empowerment.

With the school holiday visitor surge and the imminent opening of Festival XIII, Penglipuran once again proves why it is Bali's most compelling cultural tourism destination — offering not just visual beauty, but a profound lesson in how communities can thrive in harmony with nature and tradition.
