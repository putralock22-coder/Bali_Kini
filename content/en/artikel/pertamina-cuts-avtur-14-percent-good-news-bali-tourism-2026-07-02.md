Positive news for Bali's aviation and tourism sectors arrived on July 1, 2026, as PT Pertamina Patra Niaga officially slashed aviation fuel (avtur) prices by 14 percent. The adjustment, effective at Soekarno-Hatta International Airport, has been welcomed by industry stakeholders and tourism players on the Island of the Gods alike.

## A Significant Drop in Fuel Costs

According to Pertamina Patra Niaga's official announcement, the price of domestic aviation fuel at Soekarno-Hatta Airport fell from **Rp22,190 per liter** in June 2026 to **Rp19,190 per liter** starting July 1 — a reduction of Rp3,000 per liter, or approximately 14 percent.

The adjustment follows the dynamics of global oil markets and was implemented in accordance with applicable regulations and pricing mechanisms. This is actually the second consecutive monthly cut: in June 2026, Pertamina had already reduced avtur prices by 10 percent. The cumulative effect of two successive cuts represents meaningful relief for airlines operating in Indonesia.

## When Will Airfares Drop?

The question on most travelers' minds is: when will lower fuel costs translate into cheaper flight tickets? According to aviation observers, the answer is not immediate.

Airlines typically need around **1 to 2 days** to update their ticketing systems to reflect new operating cost structures. Aviation fuel accounts for approximately **50 percent** of total operating costs at low-cost carriers, while Indonesian flag carriers generally allocate around **40 percent** of their total costs to fuel. These proportions underscore why fuel price changes carry significant weight in the industry.

That said, lower avtur prices do not automatically trigger lower surcharges or fares — ticket pricing depends on many additional variables, including route demand, competition, and each airline's hedging strategy.

## Impact on Bali's Tourism Industry

Bali is one of Indonesia's premier tourism destinations, with exceptionally high air connectivity. Dozens of domestic and international flights serve I Gusti Ngurah Rai International Airport in Denpasar every day.

The mid-2026 period coincides with the peak school holiday season and the high season for international tourist arrivals. A significant reduction in aviation fuel costs could create room for airlines to offer more competitive pricing, ultimately boosting visitor numbers to Bali.

### Reliable Supply Guaranteed

Pertamina's Vice President for Corporate Communication reaffirmed that smooth flight operations remain a company priority, with avtur supply at all major airports secured and uninterrupted. The company views reliable fuel supply as part of its broader commitment to supporting public mobility and national economic growth.

For Bali's tourism industry — which has been steadily recovering and growing in the post-pandemic era — stable and falling aviation fuel prices represent a structural tailwind. More affordable airfares open up Bali to a broader pool of domestic travelers from across the Indonesian archipelago.

## Pertamax Turbo Also Falls

Beyond aviation fuel, Pertamina also reduced Pertamax Turbo prices by 7 percent from July 1, 2026 — from Rp20,750 per liter to Rp19,300 per liter. This dual price reduction sends an encouraging signal for Indonesia's transportation sector as a whole.

With energy prices trending downward, tourism stakeholders in Bali remain optimistic that greater numbers of both domestic and international visitors will choose the island as their holiday destination for the second half of 2026.
