## Where Tradition Meets Technology

As Bali prepares for Galungan on June 17 and Kuningan on June 27, 2026, PT PLN (Persero) Unit Induk Distribusi (UID) Bali took an unconventional step to promote electrical safety: partnering with **Majelis Desa Adat (MDA) Bali Province** — the highest traditional village authority in the region. On Wednesday, June 11, 2026, PLN hosted a Forum Group Discussion (FGD) in Denpasar under the theme **"Cultural-Technological Acculturation Collaboration for Improving Electrical Services and Safety in PLN Bali."**

The event was more than a technical briefing. It represented PLN's deliberate strategy to embed electrical safety messaging within Bali's deeply rooted adat (customary) system — reaching communities through the channels they trust most.

## The Hidden Hazard in a Sacred Tradition

Galungan is visually defined by the **penjor** — tall, arching bamboo poles adorned with coconut leaves, flowers, fruits, and sacred offerings, standing before every Hindu household in Bali. These graceful bamboo installations are one of the most iconic symbols of the festival.

But they also carry an underappreciated risk. When penjor are installed without adequate awareness of their surroundings, **tips and decorations can come dangerously close to overhead power cables**. A penjor that touches or grazes a live wire can cause short circuits, fires, or even fatal electric shocks.

PLN UID Bali reminded residents of several key safety guidelines for penjor installation:

- **Maintain a minimum clearance of 2.5 meters** from the nearest electrical cable
- Ensure the **bamboo tip does not lean or sway toward distribution or transmission lines**
- Avoid placing penjor **directly beneath or adjacent to utility poles**
- Check the home's internal wiring and electrical connections before the holiday

## Local Wisdom as a Safety Channel

The power of this collaboration lies in its cultural intelligence. PLN recognizes that technical directives issued in a corporate voice may not resonate deeply in traditional Balinese communities. But when the same messages are delivered through **Bendesa Adat** (customary village chiefs) and MDA-affiliated leaders — figures who hold moral and ceremonial authority in their communities — the reception is entirely different.

Village elders and adat leaders are being mobilized as **grassroots safety ambassadors**, communicating PLN's guidance in the language, tone, and context their communities understand. Local media partners were also included in the FGD to extend the reach of safety information across the island.

## Reliable Power Guaranteed for the Celebrations

On the technical side, PLN UID Bali has confirmed that the **electrical supply will remain stable and reliable** throughout the Galungan and Kuningan period. Dedicated standby crews have been deployed to respond rapidly to any network disruptions, ensuring that prayers, temple lighting, and community activities proceed without interruption.

This operational preparedness is especially important because Galungan celebrations extend into the evening, with ceremonies and offerings requiring consistent, uninterrupted lighting across thousands of household shrines and village temples simultaneously.

## Building a Culture of Safety Through Cultural Values

The FGD between PLN UID Bali and MDA exemplifies how **modern infrastructure and local tradition can reinforce rather than conflict with each other**. Electrical safety is not purely an engineering challenge — it is also a social and cultural one, requiring community buy-in that cannot be achieved through signage and leaflets alone.

By framing electrical safety within the values of adat and mutual care for the community, PLN Bali is working toward something more durable than compliance: a genuine culture of electrical safety awareness — one that fits naturally within the fabric of Balinese life.

As Galungan approaches, this partnership between the national electricity company and Bali's traditional village institutions stands as a model for how contemporary public safety efforts can be made more effective by respecting and working through the values that communities already hold dear.
