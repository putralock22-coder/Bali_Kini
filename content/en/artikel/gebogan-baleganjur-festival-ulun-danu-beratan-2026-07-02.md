The Bedugul highland area in Baturiti District, Tabanan Regency, is alive with the sights and sounds of Balinese culture. A spectacular Gebogan and Baleganjur Festival — running for over a month from June 21 to July 9, 2026 — has brought together no fewer than **1,500 young artists** from 20 traditional villages across Tabanan. The event stands as one of the most significant cultural celebrations in central Bali's calendar for the 2026 high season.

## Two Iconic Venues, One Cultural Stage

The festival takes place simultaneously at two of Bedugul's most celebrated tourist attractions: the **Ulun Danu Beratan temple complex** and **The Blooms Garden**. These venues were chosen not only for their natural beauty but also for their deep cultural and spiritual significance — particularly Pura Ulun Danu Beratan, the iconic water temple perched at the edge of Lake Beratan, widely regarded as one of Bali's most photographed landmarks.

The dramatic backdrop of mist-wrapped mountains and a calm volcanic lake, combined with the color and dynamism of traditional performances, creates an immersive experience that lingers long in the memory of every visitor.

## The Gebogan Parade: An Offering Carried with Grace

The centerpiece attraction of this festival is the **Gebogan Parade**, a cherished Balinese tradition in which women carry elaborately arranged ceremonial offerings — composed of fruits, flowers, cakes, and natural produce — balanced skillfully atop their heads. Gebogan is far more than a decorative act; it is a living expression of Bali's aesthetic philosophy, gratitude to the divine, and the harmony between humanity, nature, and God — the very essence of the island's guiding principle of Tri Hita Karana.

The parade is accompanied by the thunderous beats of **Baleganjur**, Bali's distinctive percussion gamelan ensemble known for its energetic, driving rhythm. The sound of interlocking gongs, cymbals, and drums forms the heartbeat of the festival, energizing performers and captivating audiences in equal measure.

## The Theme: Dharma Santi Mahotsava

The festival carries the theme **"Dharma Santi Mahotsava"** — a Sanskrit-rooted phrase meaning "a great celebration of peace grounded in virtue (dharma)." The theme reflects a spirit of unity among participating villages, where young artists from diverse traditional communities gather to collectively celebrate their shared cultural heritage.

The Regent of Tabanan personally inaugurated the festival, signaling strong local government commitment to arts and culture preservation. Each participating traditional village (desa adat) received a **Rp30 million funding grant** to support their involvement and ensure the quality of their presentations.

## Empowering the Next Generation of Balinese Culture

What makes this festival particularly meaningful is the central role assigned to **young people**. The vast majority of the 1,500 participants are youths who are involved from the very start — preparing gebogan offerings, training in baleganjur ensembles, and ultimately performing before thousands of visitors.

This is authentic cultural regeneration in action. By placing young people at the heart of the festival, organizers ensure that Bali's traditional arts do not remain frozen in the past, but continue to evolve and thrive in the hands of future generations.

### Boosting Tourism in the Bedugul Highlands

Organizers expect the festival to significantly boost visitor numbers to The Blooms Garden and Ulun Danu Beratan during the mid-year high season. Travelers who make the journey to Bedugul during this period gain something rare: not just the natural splendor of the highland lake, but a front-row seat to living Balinese culture as it has been practiced for generations.

For those who haven't yet visited, the festival continues through **Sunday, July 9, 2026**. Performance schedules are available at the venues directly or through the Tabanan Tourism Office's official channels.
