Southeast Asia's largest B2B travel trade show has closed its doors for another year. The **Bali & Beyond Travel Fair (BBTF) 2026** concluded with a total transaction value of **Rp6.9 trillion (approximately USD 425 million)** after three intense days of business meetings and deal-making at **The Westin Resort Nusa Dua, Badung**, from May 28 to 30, 2026.

## 407 Buyers, 286 Sellers, 44 Countries

The scale of BBTF 2026 underscores Bali's standing as the region's premier tourism hub. The fair brought together **407 tour operators (buyers)** from **44 countries** with **286 exhibitors (sellers)** from across Indonesia's travel industry. The three-day event created a concentrated marketplace where international travel companies booked packages, accommodations, and experiences directly with Indonesian providers.

The B2B format remains BBTF's defining strength — cutting out intermediaries and allowing for direct, lasting business relationships between Indonesian hospitality providers and global travel distributors.

## Results Fall Short of Target, But Context Matters

The Rp6.9 trillion result fell below the **Rp7.6 trillion target** and represented approximately a **12 percent decline** from BBTF 2025's Rp7.84 trillion. The primary reason: a reduced turnout from **European tour operators**, many of whom could not attend due to the ongoing impact of Middle East geopolitical conflicts on travel routes and operational costs.

Industry observers caution against reading too much into the single-year dip. The disruption was external — a consequence of global instability, not a reflection of declining interest in Bali as a destination.

### NTB Scores a Win

Even as the headline number disappointed, there were bright spots. Neighboring **Nusa Tenggara Barat (NTB)** province pulled in nearly **Rp20 billion in tourism package transactions** — a result that demonstrates growing international appetite for destinations beyond Bali's core circuit.

## A Bold Vision: Bali Nusra Travel Fair 2027

The most significant announcement came from **Bali Governor Wayan Koster**, who proposed transforming BBTF 2027 into the **Bali Nusra Travel Fair** — expanding the fair's geographic scope to formally include three provinces: **Bali, Nusa Tenggara Barat (NTB), and Nusa Tenggara Timur (NTT)**.

This isn't simply a rebranding exercise. It represents a strategic vision for an integrated regional tourism ecosystem where Bali serves as the gateway, while Lombok, Flores, Labuan Bajo, and the broader Nusra region offer complementary wonders that turn a single-destination trip into a multi-island journey. For international buyers, a bundled Bali-NTB-NTT package could be an irresistible proposition.

**BBTF 2027** — or Bali Nusra Travel Fair — is scheduled for **June 9–11, 2027**, giving organizers a year to lay the groundwork for a significantly expanded fair.

## Quality Over Quantity

BBTF 2026 reinforces a broader lesson for Bali's tourism industry: transaction volume matters, but so does visitor quality. Industry analysts emphasize that *length of stay*, *spending per capita*, and the *quality of visitor experience* are ultimately more meaningful indicators of sustainable tourism success than raw arrival numbers.

With BBTF's B2B model connecting Bali's best properties and operators with the world's top travel distributors, the fair remains one of the most effective tools for ensuring that visitors who choose Bali do so with high expectations — and the spending power to match them.
