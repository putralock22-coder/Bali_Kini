Thursday, July 2, marked a distinctive day at the **48th Bali Arts Festival (PKB XLVIII) 2026** — one that balanced the joyful nostalgia of traditional folk games in the morning with a deep dive into ancient gamelan craftsmanship at midday, before culminating in one of Bali's most beloved performing art forms: **Drama Gong**.

## Morning: Traditional Games Come Alive

### 7:30 AM — Kelik-kelikan Demonstration
The open field east of the Bajra Sandhi Monument (Monumen Perjuangan Rakyat Bali) was transformed into an arena for **Kelik-kelikan** — a traditional Balinese game of agility and reflexes that has grown increasingly rare in everyday life. As part of the festival's **Jantra Tradisi Bali** programme, the demonstration brought this endangered form of play back into public view, inviting audiences of all ages to reconnect with a childhood activity that older generations still remember with fondness.

### 8:00 AM — Traditional Sports Competition
The same venue then hosted a lively contest across three traditional athletic disciplines:
- **Metajog Putra** — a traditional men's running race
- **Terompah Putri** — a women's team relay race using wooden clogs (*bakiak*)
- **Hadang Putra** — a men's team tag game similar to the nationally popular *gobak sodor*

Far from being mere nostalgia, the competitions drew competitive spirit from participants and genuine enthusiasm from spectators, proving that traditional physical games retain their capacity to entertain and unite communities.

## Midday: A Workshop on a Sacred Gamelan

### 10:00 AM — Gong Luang Karawitan Workshop
Inside Kalangan Angsoka, a rare opportunity was offered to music enthusiasts: a hands-on workshop (*Kriyaloka*) on **Gong Luang** — one of Bali's oldest and most sacred gamelan ensembles. Unlike the more commonly heard Gong Kebyar, the Gong Luang is traditionally reserved for specific religious ceremonies and is seldom performed in public contexts. The workshop gave participants direct access to its history, tuning system, and playing techniques — a form of living cultural transmission.

## Afternoon: Cross-Cultural Exchange

### 11:00 AM — Art from Indramayu and Cirebon
PKB has always embraced the breadth of Indonesian cultural expression. **Sanggar Seni Nusantara from Indramayu Regency, West Java**, took the Kalangan Ayodya stage to present music and dance traditions from the Indramayu and Cirebon coastal regions — styles characterised by their vibrant rhythms, elaborate costuming, and seafaring cultural roots. The performance served as a living reminder that Indonesia's cultural richness extends far beyond any single island.

## Evening: The Grand Drama Gong

### 7:30 PM — "Grenggeng Maya" at Ardha Candra
The night's centrepiece was staged at the iconic **Panggung Terbuka Ardha Candra** — PKB's largest and most prestigious outdoor venue. **Paguyuban Peduli Seni Drama Gong Lawas Padsmagol** from Denpasar City performed the full-length production of **"Grenggeng Maya"**.

Drama Gong is one of Bali's most distinctive theatrical traditions — a genre blending dramatic storytelling, classical dance, gamelan accompaniment, and sharp comedic interludes delivered in the Balinese language. The form has deep roots in village entertainment culture and can hold audiences spellbound for several hours.

The Padsmagol collective focuses specifically on preserving the *lawas* (old) style of Drama Gong — performance traditions from earlier decades that are in danger of being forgotten as newer, faster-paced entertainment formats dominate. Their appearance at PKB was both a cultural statement and a call to younger generations to look back before leaping forward.

## A Festival That Embraces All of Bali

The July 2 programme illustrated the full range of what PKB aspires to be: not merely a showcase for elite artistic achievement, but a living cultural ecosystem that encompasses children's games, sacred music, regional folk art, and grand theatrical performance alike. With the festival closing on **July 11**, each remaining day represents a chance to experience something irreplaceable.
