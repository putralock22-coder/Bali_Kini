Badung Police officially named the former head of the Village Credit Institution (LPD) of Mambal Customary Village, identified by initials **I.W.A.W. (56)**, as a suspect in an alleged corruption case. The announcement was made by the Badung Police Chief at a press conference held on Monday (6/7/2026), with estimated financial losses to the LPD reaching **Rp33.67 billion**.

## How the Case Unfolded

The suspect was formally charged following a case conference conducted on June 25, 2026, based on a thorough investigation backed by a special audit carried out by the public accounting firm Dony N and Partners. The investigation involved the examination of no fewer than **111 witnesses**, including LPD management, staff members, supervisory board members, loan debtors, savings and deposit customers, and four expert witnesses.

The experts consulted comprised two independent auditors, one state economics expert, and one criminal law expert.

## The Fraud Schemes

### Loans Issued Under His Own Name

The suspect allegedly abused his authority as LPD head by disbursing loans using his own name, family members, and close associates as fictitious borrowers. The funds released were believed to have been used for personal gain.

### Concealing Non-Performing Loans

The second scheme involved **repeatedly restructuring** loans that had already entered non-performing status — without the knowledge of the borrowers whose names were used. This maneuver was designed to keep troubled loans classified as performing, allowing the fraud to evade detection by internal and external auditors for an extended period.

## Charges and Penalties

The suspect faces charges under Articles 2(1), 3, and 9 in conjunction with Article 18 of Law No. 31 of 1999 on the Eradication of Corruption Crimes. If convicted, he could face a maximum sentence of **20 years in prison** and a maximum fine of Rp1 billion.

## What Is an LPD?

LPD (Lembaga Perkreditan Desa) is a micro-financial institution unique to Bali, operating under the governance of a customary village (desa adat or pakraman). These institutions serve the savings and lending needs of local customary communities and provide funding for village socio-cultural activities. A corruption case of this magnitude not only inflicts direct financial damage on the institution but also erodes the trust and stability of the affected customary village.

Badung Police confirmed that the investigation is ongoing and that the possibility of the case being expanded to involve other parties has not been ruled out.
