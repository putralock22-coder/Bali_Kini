Bali has lost its crown as the top island in the Asia-Pacific region. The prestigious **Travel + Leisure Luxury Awards Asia Pacific 2026** has placed Koh Samui, Thailand at the top of this year's rankings, with Bali slipping to third place — behind Vietnam's Phu Quoc.

## The Full Rankings

This year's Travel + Leisure Luxury Awards Asia Pacific best islands list reads:

1. **Koh Samui** — Thailand
2. **Phu Quoc** — Vietnam
3. **Bali** — Indonesia
4. **Palawan** — Philippines
5. **Penang** — Malaysia

The shift has surprised many in the travel industry, as Bali had consistently topped various global destination rankings for years.

## Why Koh Samui Took the Crown

Koh Samui's surge to the top is driven by several factors. The Gulf of Thailand island offers a compelling blend of stunning natural beauty, white-sand beaches, and an authentically relaxed atmosphere.

The biggest catalyst, however, is the global fame brought by **HBO's "The White Lotus" Season 3**, which was filmed on location in Koh Samui. The critically acclaimed series exposed the island to millions of viewers worldwide, sparking a wave of new interest from travelers eager to experience the destination firsthand.

Additionally, Koh Samui has managed to retain its traditional fishing village communities — a genuine draw for travelers seeking authentic cultural experiences away from over-commercialized tourist zones.

## The Challenges Facing Bali

Despite retaining a top-three global ranking, Bali faces a set of serious structural challenges. **Mass tourism** that exceeds the carrying capacity of iconic areas such as Seminyak, Kuta, and Ubud has drawn repeated criticism. Overcrowding, traffic congestion, and environmental pressure are among the most frequently cited concerns.

Beyond visitor numbers, Bali has also struggled with **visa violations** by foreigners working illegally on the island, and a series of incidents involving tourists disrespecting local cultural values and sacred sites — all of which have dented the island's international image.

### Bali's Policy Response

The Bali Provincial Government and the Ministry of Tourism have been actively responding. **Quality tourism** — a strategy focused on increasing per-visitor spending rather than chasing raw visitor numbers — has been adopted as the guiding policy direction.

Stricter enforcement of visitor visa regulations and behavioral rules at sacred sites continues to be rolled out. Governor Wayan Koster recently attended the **London Climate Action Week 2026**, reinforcing Bali's positioning as a responsible, low-carbon, and environmentally conscious destination.

## Phu Quoc: Vietnam's Rising Star

Equally notable is Phu Quoc's climb to second place, overtaking Bali for the first time in a major international ranking. The Vietnamese island has undergone a dramatic transformation in recent years, backed by massive infrastructure investment — including world-class resorts, a modern international airport, and competitive tourism packages.

Phu Quoc's rise reflects a broader regional trend: Southeast Asia's destination competition has intensified sharply, and Bali can no longer rely solely on its longstanding reputation.

## A Moment for Reflection

Rather than viewing this ranking shift purely as a setback, Bali's stakeholders — government, tourism operators, and local communities — can treat it as a valuable signal. The island's authentic competitive advantages remain unmatched: a living Hindu cultural heritage, breathtaking landscapes, and the legendary warmth of its people.

With smarter governance, a commitment to sustainable growth, and authentic product development, Bali has every potential to reclaim its position — not just as the most popular island, but as the best-managed and most sustainable one.
