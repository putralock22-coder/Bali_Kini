The **Meteorology, Climatology, and Geophysics Agency (BMKG) Region III Denpasar** has issued an early warning for high waves in southern Bali's waters, effective from **July 2 to 6, 2026**. Wave heights are forecast to reach **2.5 to 4 meters** — classified as *high* to *very high* — posing a significant hazard for shipping activities and marine tourism in the area.

## Affected Waters

The warning covers several strategic maritime zones around Bali:

- **Badung Strait** — the channel separating Bali and Nusa Penida Island
- **Southern Bali Strait** — an active crossing route to Java
- **Waters south of Bali Island** — from Kuta's coastline to the Bukit Peninsula cliffs
- **Southern part of Lombok Strait** — between Bali and Lombok Island

The conditions are driven by strong wind patterns across Indonesian waters. In the southern equatorial region, winds are blowing from east to southeast at speeds of **5–25 knots**, consistently generating high swells over the coming days.

## Vessel Risk Categories

BMKG has outlined risk levels by type of vessel operating in Bali's waters:

- **Traditional fishing boats**: high risk when wind speeds reach 15 knots with 1.25-meter waves
- **Passenger ferries**: risk rises sharply at 21-knot winds with 2.5-meter swells
- **Cargo ships**: caution advised at 16-knot winds and 1.5-meter waves

With waves forecast to hit 4 meters, all vessel categories fall within the danger zone during the active warning period.

## Warnings for Fishermen and Tourists

BMKG urges all **fishermen** who regularly work in southern Bali's waters to temporarily postpone their fishing trips until conditions return to safe levels. Traditional fishing boats are extremely vulnerable in wave conditions of this height.

**Tourists** planning marine activities — such as diving or snorkeling to Nusa Penida, Nusa Lembongan, or Amed — are advised to check the latest weather information before departing. Many boat tour operators are likely to delay or cancel scheduled trips during this warning period.

**Ferry operators** running the Gilimanuk–Ketapang (Bali–Java) route and other sea crossings are requested to heighten vigilance and adjust departure schedules according to actual conditions at sea.

### Why Southern Bali Waters Can Be Rough at This Time of Year

Southern Bali's waters are known for their dynamic conditions, particularly during the **east monsoon season (May–October)**. During this period, above-average waves are more frequent. The powerful Indian Ocean current, combined with seasonal wind patterns, makes the sea south of Bali considerably rougher than the calmer northern coast.

This situation serves as a reminder to divers and travelers that Bali's breathtaking underwater landscapes are best enjoyed when the forces of nature are carefully respected. Safety must always come first.

## Stay Updated Through BMKG's Official Channels

BMKG confirms that maritime weather conditions will be continuously monitored and updates issued regularly. Fishermen, boat operators, and coastal communities are advised to access the latest weather information through BMKG's official channels — including maritim.bmkg.go.id, the Info BMKG app, and BMKG Bali's official social media accounts.
