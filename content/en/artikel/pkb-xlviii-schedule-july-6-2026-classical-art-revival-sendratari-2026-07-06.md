The 48th Pesta Kesenian Bali (PKB), or Bali Arts Festival 2026, continues its month-long celebration with two standout performances on Monday, July 6, 2026. The day's program features a Classical Art Revival in the afternoon and a large-scale Sendratari — Balinese dance drama — in the evening.

## Classical Art Revival from Badung Regency

At 3:00 PM local time, the Kalangan Angsoka stage hosts Komunitas Seni Grahasta Bawera, an arts community from Banjar Pengayehan, Desa Adat Munggu, Mengwi District, Badung Regency. Their performance is part of the Classical Art Revival programme, a dedicated segment of PKB designed to resurrect traditional Balinese art forms that are in danger of fading from public life.

The community's dedication to preserving village-rooted classical arts makes them a natural fit for this programme, which serves as both an artistic showcase and a cultural conservation effort.

## Grand Sendratari by Sanggar Paripurna, Desa Bona

The evening's centrepiece begins at 7:30 PM on the Open-Air Stage at Ardha Candra. Sanggar Paripurna from Desa Bona, Blahbatuh, Gianyar, takes the stage with a Kolosal Sendratari — a large-format Balinese dance drama involving hundreds of performers. Desa Bona holds a special place in Bali's cultural history as one of the earliest villages to perform the Kecak Dance, and Sanggar Paripurna carries that legacy forward with its sweeping storytelling through movement, gamelan music, and elaborate costume.

A Kolosal Sendratari typically draws from Balinese mythology, the Mahabharata or Ramayana epics, and local history, presenting them in a theatrical spectacle that can fill an entire outdoor stage.

## Theme: Atma Kerthi — Purification of the Soul

PKB XLVIII 2026 is held under the theme **"Atma Kerthi: Jiwa Sidha Parisudha"**, which can be understood as the elevation and purification of the human soul toward ultimate perfection, clarity, and holiness. The theme draws on Balinese Hindu philosophy and frames the entire festival as a spiritual as well as artistic journey.

### International Dimension: Bali World Culture Celebration

Alongside the local and national performances, the Bali World Culture Celebration (BWCC) adds an international dimension to this year's festival. Ten foreign arts groups, comprising approximately 260 artists, have confirmed their participation. They represent countries including the United States (New York), China, Timor-Leste, South Korea, Japan, India, and Hong Kong.

This international participation transforms PKB from a regional arts event into a genuine global cultural gathering, facilitating meaningful exchange between Balinese traditions and artistic expressions from across the world.

## Final Days of PKB 2026

The 48th Bali Arts Festival runs from June 13 to July 11, 2026. With only a few days remaining, visitors and residents are encouraged to attend the closing performances, which promise to be among the most impressive of the entire run. All performances at Taman Budaya (Art Center) in Denpasar are free and open to the public.
