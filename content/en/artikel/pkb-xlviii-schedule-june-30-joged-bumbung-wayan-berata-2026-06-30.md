## PKB XLVIII 2026: Glorifying the Soul Through Art

The 48th Pesta Kesenian Bali (PKB) — Bali Arts Festival — themed **"Atma Kerthi: Jiwa Sidha Parisudha"** (The Glorification of the Perfected, Pure, and Sacred Soul) continues with a rich lineup of performances on Monday, June 30, 2026.

Bali's premier cultural festival runs for a full month, from **June 13 to July 11, 2026**, timed to coincide with the school holiday season. All performances are held at the Taman Budaya Bali (Art Center) in Denpasar and are **completely free** for the public and visiting tourists alike.

## Three Main Performances on June 30, 2026

### 5:00 PM WITA — Traditional Joged Bumbung Parade

The evening opens at **5:00 PM WITA** at **Kalangan Madya Mandala**, featuring a Traditional Joged Bumbung Parade by Sanggar Tabuh and Tari Pada Girang from **Gianyar Regency**.

Joged Bumbung is a joyful and energetic Balinese folk dance accompanied by a bamboo gamelan orchestra (bumbung). This social dance has existed for centuries and authentically captures the communal spirit and joy of Balinese society. Joged Bumbung performers famously invite audience participation, creating the warm and vibrant atmosphere that has made the tradition beloved for generations.

### 6:30 PM WITA — Works of Maestro I Wayan Berata

The most anticipated moment of the evening is the **Performance of Works by Maestro I Wayan Berata**, beginning at **6:30 PM WITA** at **Kalangan Ayodya**, presented by Sekaa Gong Gita Bandana Praja from **Denpasar City**.

I Wayan Berata is one of Bali's most influential music maestros. Over decades of creation, he has composed tabuh and kidung pieces that now serve as primary references in contemporary Balinese musical arts. His works have gained international recognition, and this performance offers a rare opportunity to experience the maestro's musical legacy performed live by an elite ensemble.

### 7:30 PM WITA — Women's Gong Kebyar

Closing the evening from **7:30 PM WITA** at the **Panggung Terbuka Ardha Candra** open-air stage, the Women's Gong Kebyar Parade features two outstanding performing groups:

- **Sanggar Sakura Dewata** from Bangli Regency
- **Sekaa Gong Remaja Tri Yowana Sandhi** from Tabanan Regency

Women's Gong Kebyar is one of PKB's most spectacular categories, showcasing female musicians' extraordinary mastery of gamelan performance — combining precision, unity, and high artistic expression. This category consistently draws large, enthusiastic crowds to the Art Center every evening during the festival.

## PKB: A Living Legacy of Art

PKB XLVIII 2026 features not only Balinese arts but also invites artists from across Indonesia and internationally through the **Bali World Culture Celebration (BWCC)**. More than **23,000 artists** from across Bali participate in this festival, which has been held annually since 1979.

This year's "Atma Kerthi" theme carries a profound philosophical message: amid modernization and contemporary challenges, Bali remains anchored in the profound cultural values that form the very soul of its community. PKB is not merely an art performance — it is a collective space for reflection and cultural affirmation for all of Bali's people.

For tourists visiting Bali, PKB is a golden opportunity — free of charge and deeply authentic — to immerse in Balinese cultural richness at one of Southeast Asia's largest cultural festivals.
