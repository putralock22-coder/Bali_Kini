## A Quiet Revolution in Bali's Tourism Landscape

Something remarkable is happening across Bali. Between the rice paddies of Canggu, the clifftop vistas of Uluwatu, and the beachside elegance of Seminyak, world-class **padel and tennis facilities** are rising — and drawing thousands of sports enthusiasts from across Asia and beyond.

In 2026, Bali has firmly established itself as one of Asia's leading **sport tourism** destinations for racket sports, joining the ranks of Thailand, Vietnam, Singapore, and Australia in attracting a new wave of active, high-spending visitors.

## New Courts Across Bali's Key Tourism Corridors

Investment in racket sports infrastructure is flowing into virtually every major tourism zone on the island:

### Uluwatu — Bali's Fastest-Growing Sports Hub
Uluwatu has transformed from a surf-only destination into a multi-sport lifestyle enclave. **Island Padel Uluwatu** offers four panoramic padel courts with spectacular views of limestone cliffs and the Indian Ocean, complemented by locker rooms, an outdoor café, and a pro shop. Playing padel here is less a sport and more an experience.

### Canggu — The Sport Lifestyle Capital
**Canggu Padel Centre** stands as the island's most comprehensive racket sports venue, boasting **seven professional courts** including a central show court with seating for 200 spectators. Alongside competitive play, the facility offers a full academy program, sauna, ice baths, and a children's playground — making it a genuine family destination.

**Island Sports Club** in Canggu goes even further, weaving together padel with a state-of-the-art fitness center, Bali's largest indoor golf experience, and a recovery center complete with sauna, jacuzzi, ice bath, and rooftop pool.

### Seminyak, Sanur, and Nusa Dua
These established tourism zones have developed **integrated lifestyle venues** that pair sport with gastronomy, wellness, and social experience. Operators have recognized that the most successful model is not a standalone sports facility, but a destination that keeps visitors engaged — and spending — for hours.

## A Strategic Fit with Indonesia's Tourism Vision

The padel and tennis boom in Bali aligns with the **Indonesian Ministry of Tourism's sport tourism strategy**, which identifies active travel as a key driver of high-quality visitor arrivals. Sport tourists typically stay longer, spend more on premium accommodation and services, and seek authentic local experiences that go beyond the beach.

Padel itself is the fastest-growing sport in the world, and Bali has moved ahead of most Asian destinations in embracing it. The island's established community of digital nomads, expatriates, and premium travelers from Europe and Australia — many of whom play regularly at home — find world-class facilities waiting for them in Bali.

## Local Economy Benefits

The benefits flow beyond the courts. Integrated sport venues generate traffic for surrounding restaurants, guesthouses, transport providers, and wellness businesses. Communities near major facilities in Canggu and Uluwatu are already seeing new economic activity stimulated by the growing stream of sports-motivated visitors.

As Bali continues to position itself as a destination for high-value travel, sport tourism built around padel and tennis is becoming one of its most exciting — and fastest-growing — growth stories.
