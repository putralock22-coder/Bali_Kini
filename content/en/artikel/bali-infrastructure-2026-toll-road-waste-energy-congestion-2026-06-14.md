Indonesia's central government and the Bali Provincial Government are jointly accelerating a sweeping infrastructure overhaul in 2026, targeting three of the island's most pressing challenges at once: chronic traffic congestion, a mounting waste crisis, and energy reliability. Several strategic projects worth trillions of rupiah are being fast-tracked to preserve Bali's competitiveness as a world-class tourism destination.

## Gilimanuk–Mengwi Toll Road: 96.84 km Awaiting Construction

The single most anticipated project is the **Gilimanuk–Mengwi Toll Road**, stretching **96.84 kilometers** from Bali's main western ferry port to the Mengwi area in central Bali. The highway is designed to break the severe traffic bottleneck on the island's main arterial road, which is routinely choked by freight logistics flowing from Java through the Ketapang–Gilimanuk ferry crossing.

Governor Wayan Koster has been actively pressing the central government to kick-start construction, noting that land designation was completed more than two years ago with no shovels yet in the ground. The government has pledged to begin phased construction from **2026 through 2027**.

### Parliament Demands Faster Action

Indonesia's House of Representatives Commission V has weighed in decisively, demanding the Ministry of Public Works (Kementerian PU) accelerate the toll road while simultaneously addressing coastal erosion that continues to eat away at Bali's shoreline. Lawmakers have warned that stalled infrastructure progress risks undermining Bali's contribution to the national economy.

## Waste-to-Energy Plant: Danantara to Process 120 Tons of Waste Daily

On the waste management front, Indonesia's sovereign investment platform **Danantara** has designated **Bali as the priority site** for one of the country's first *waste-to-energy* (WtE) facilities. The plant is engineered to process **120 tonnes of waste per day**, converting refuse into electricity and replacing the open-dumping system that has plagued the island for years.

The contract was awarded to Chinese firm **Zhejiang Weiming Environment Protection** for a **30-year operating period**. Construction is scheduled to commence in **June 2026**, with the facility targeted for completion by late 2027. The Bali project is part of a broader national program aiming to build **30 waste incinerators** across Indonesia's major cities by 2027.

## Rp1.27 Trillion Budget from Ministry of Public Works

The Ministry of Public Works has earmarked a **Rp1.27 trillion budget** specifically for integrated infrastructure development in Bali in 2026. The funding is focused on sustainable tourism-supporting infrastructure, including road improvements, coastal erosion management, and drainage and sanitation upgrades in key tourist zones.

## Southern Bali Traffic: Water Taxis and Green Transit

While long-term land infrastructure solutions remain in progress, short-term alternatives are already being rolled out. A **water taxi service** connecting Ngurah Rai International Airport to the Canggu area is being trialled with a target travel time of just **30 minutes** — a dramatic improvement over the 1–2 hour drive during peak congestion periods.

For the longer term, the government is preparing **electric-based public transit** options and evaluating **MRT/LRT corridors** along major southern Bali tourism routes. However, investor interest in mass transit projects remains low, meaning full implementation is still several years away.

## Bali's Net-Zero 2045 Roadmap

All these infrastructure initiatives form part of **Bali's green development roadmap** toward achieving net-zero emissions by 2045. By combining investments in renewable energy, waste management, and eco-friendly transportation, Bali aims to demonstrate that economic growth and environmental sustainability can move forward hand in hand.
