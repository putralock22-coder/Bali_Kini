Despite global geopolitical uncertainties rattling investment markets worldwide, Bali is sending a confident signal to the international investment community. The **Bank Indonesia (BI) Representative Office for Bali Province** and the **Bali Provincial Government**, through the Kerthi Bali Sadhana Investment Center (PIKBS), hosted the **Bali Jagadhita Investment Forum (BJIF) 2026** — a high-level forum showcasing **22 strategic investment projects** from Bali, Nusa Tenggara Barat (NTB), and Nusa Tenggara Timur (NTT) to over **35 domestic and international investors**.

## 22 Projects Ready for Investment

The BJIF 2026 investment catalog presents a carefully curated portfolio of shovel-ready projects spanning infrastructure, health, and sustainable development. Three flagship projects drew the most investor interest:

- **KEK Kesehatan Sanur (Sanur Health Special Economic Zone)** — Indonesia's first health-focused Special Economic Zone, offering premium medical services and wellness facilities for international patients
- **Street Lighting Infrastructure** — fundamental urban infrastructure enhancing livability and tourist experience
- **Electric Vehicle (EV) Fleet Project** — a forward-looking initiative aligning with Bali's green economy agenda

All 22 projects were presented in a *ready-to-offer* format, complete with feasibility data, projected returns, and regulatory support frameworks — removing the guesswork that often deters foreign investors.

## Ambassadors from Six Nations Attend

The forum's one-on-one business meetings between project owners and prospective investors were attended by senior diplomatic representatives, including ambassadors from:

- Bulgaria
- The Kingdom of Bahrain
- The Sultanate of Oman
- The Republic of Armenia
- The Islamic Republic of Pakistan
- Romania

The participation of ambassadors from six nations underscores the growing international interest in Bali as a serious investment destination, not just a leisure one.

## Site Visit to Sanur Health SEZ

A highlight of BJIF 2026 was an on-site visit to the **KEK Kesehatan Sanur** — Indonesia's first designated health Special Economic Zone. Investors had the rare opportunity to walk the grounds, inspect completed infrastructure, and assess the development potential firsthand. For many international investors, seeing is believing — and Sanur's health SEZ delivered on that expectation.

## Bali Remains Competitive Amid Global Uncertainty

BI Bali made a clear case: Bali's investment appeal is structural, not cyclical. Two fundamental strengths insulate the island from geopolitical turbulence — consistent and competitive **government incentives** and the enduring global brand of Bali as a **world-class tourism destination** that continues to draw millions of visitors annually.

### Economic Fundamentals Support the Pitch

The numbers back the argument. Bali's economy expanded by **5.58 percent in Q1 2026**, according to BPS Bali (Bali Statistics Agency). Bank Indonesia projects full-year growth of **5.4 to 5.9 percent for 2026** — a resilient performance in the context of a slowing global economy.

By combining a structured, professionally prepared investment catalog with high-level diplomatic engagement and hands-on site visits, the Bali Jagadhita Investment Forum 2026 demonstrated that Bali is not just open for business — it's actively competing for the world's investment capital.
