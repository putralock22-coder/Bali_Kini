The Central Statistics Agency (BPS) of Bali Province has revealed that the appreciation of the US dollar against the Indonesian rupiah has played a key role in inflating the nominal value of foreign tourist (wisman) spending on the island. According to the latest data, the average nightly expenditure per international visitor rose to **Rp2.11 million in 2025**, up from Rp2.05 million in 2024 — an increase of approximately Rp60,190 per night.

## The Exchange Rate Effect

The increase cannot be read in isolation from currency movements. Bank Indonesia's reference rate (JISDOR) recorded an average of **Rp15,850 per US dollar in 2024**, rising to **Rp16,478 per US dollar in 2025** — a difference of nearly Rp628 per dollar.

This means that even if international visitors spend a similar amount in their home currencies, the rupiah equivalent of that spending naturally rises as the rupiah weakens. BPS highlighted that the exchange rate factor is a significant variable influencing the fluctuation of foreign visitor spending figures in Bali.

## Breakdown of Tourist Spending

The full picture of how international visitors spend during their time in Bali reveals clear priorities:

| Category | Average Spending |
|---|---|
| Accommodation | Rp10.18 million |
| Food & Beverage | Rp4.92 million |
| Shopping & Souvenirs | Rp2.92 million |
| Entertainment | Rp2.36 million |
| Local Transportation | Rp1.3 million |
| Tour Packages | Rp830,000 |
| Vehicle Rental | Rp710,000 |
| Domestic Flights | Rp560,000 |
| Health & Beauty | Rp480,000 |

**Accommodation** remains the single largest expense category, a reflection of Bali's wide range of lodging options from budget guesthouses to ultra-luxury villas. Meanwhile, **entertainment spending** registered the most notable year-on-year growth — rising from Rp2.26 million in 2024 to Rp2.36 million in 2025 — suggesting that visitors are increasingly seeking out experiences beyond their hotel rooms.

## Bali's Tourism Dominance

The BPS data arrives against a backdrop of continued growth in inbound tourism. National inbound tourist arrivals grew 8.62 percent in the first quarter of 2026, with Bali maintaining its position as Indonesia's primary entry point for international visitors. Bali's tourism sector is estimated to contribute up to 55 percent of total national tourism revenue in 2025 — an extraordinary concentration for a single island.

## Looking Ahead: Peak Season Boost

The June–July 2026 peak season is expected to push hotel occupancy rates in Bali up by as much as 12 percent compared to the same period last year. For industry players, this represents a crucial window to maximize spending per visitor — particularly in the entertainment, dining, and experiential categories where growth momentum is strongest.

However, BPS also cautioned against over-reliance on currency effects to drive spending growth. For sustainable gains, Bali's tourism industry must continue developing diverse, high-quality products that genuinely compel visitors to spend more — regardless of what the exchange rate does.
