Thousands of residents from **Desa Adat (Traditional Village) Munggu**, Mengwi District, Badung Regency, Bali, once again performed the sacred **Mekotek** ritual to mark **Hari Raya Kuningan** on Saturday, June 27, 2026. This age-old tradition is more than a cultural spectacle — it is a deeply rooted spiritual obligation that the Munggu community believes is essential to maintaining the safety and harmony of their village.

## What Is the Mekotek Tradition?

Mekotek is a unique customary ceremony indigenous to Munggu Village, in which hundreds to thousands of village men carry **long wooden staffs (pulet)** measuring two to three meters in length. These staffs are brought together to form tall, cone-shaped formations while participants push and shove one another, accompanied by resounding cheers and the thunderous beat of gamelan music.

What appears to be chaotic movement from the outside is, in reality, a highly organized ritual infused with deep spiritual meaning. Every villager understands their role in the ceremony from childhood, as Mekotek is passed down through generations.

## Spiritual and Historical Significance

Mekotek is far more than a cultural performance. The people of Munggu Village believe it symbolizes the **triumph of Dharma (goodness) over Adharma (evil)** and serves as an expression of gratitude for the safety and harmony the village has enjoyed throughout the year.

Historically, the tradition is believed to date back to the **Mengwi Kingdom in the 17th century**, originally performed to welcome home victorious troops returning from battle. Over the centuries, it evolved into a spiritual ceremony observed every Hari Raya Kuningan.

The community holds a firm belief that **failure to perform the ritual would invite disaster or disease upon the village**, making Mekotek not a choice but a sacred duty for every resident of Munggu.

## Kuningan 2026: A Day of Ancestral Honor

Hari Raya Kuningan, which fell on Saturday, June 27, 2026, marks the culmination of the Galungan celebration cycle. Across Bali, it is observed as the day when ancestors who have descended to earth during Galungan return to the heavens, leaving behind blessings and protection for their descendants. In villages throughout the island, unique traditions are performed as acts of reverence.

In Munggu, Mekotek serves as the community's distinctive way of honoring Kuningan while reinforcing **social solidarity** among villagers. Men of all ages gather, stand united, and move together in a display that embodies communal strength and spiritual alignment.

## A Draw for Visitors

The Mekotek tradition has long attracted both domestic and foreign visitors to Bali during Hari Raya Kuningan. The ritual offers an authentic cultural experience that is rare and deeply moving. Visitors wishing to witness Mekotek should be mindful that it is a religious ceremony — respectful behavior and appropriate attire are required.

Munggu Village is strategically located along the route between the Seminyak/Canggu tourism area and Tanah Lot, making it easily accessible from the major tourism centers of South Badung and Denpasar.

## A Living Heritage Worth Preserving

The Mekotek tradition reflects the extraordinary richness of Bali's cultural heritage. Even as modernization and globalization accelerate, Bali's traditional village communities are doubling down on their cultural identity through the preservation of rituals like this one. The Badung Regency Government fully supports the continuation of Mekotek as a vital part of the region's intangible cultural heritage, to be safeguarded and passed on to future generations.
