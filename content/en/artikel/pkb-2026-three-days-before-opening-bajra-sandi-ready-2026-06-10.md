Final preparations for the 48th Pesta Kesenian Bali (PKB) — Bali Arts Festival — are in full swing. Just three days before the official opening on Saturday, June 13, 2026, the honor stage at the Monument of the Balinese People's Struggle (MPRB), Bajra Sandi, Niti Mandala Renon, Denpasar, has been fully erected and is ready to welcome dignitaries.

## Honor Stage Draped in Red and White

The grand stage that will serve as the focal point of the *Peed Aya* (cultural parade) has been completed and wrapped in red and white cloth — the colors of the Indonesian national flag — symbolizing national pride. Spectator stands on both the east and west sides of the Renon area have also been installed to accommodate the thousands of residents expected to line the streets and watch the parade.

The head of the Taman Budaya (Cultural Park) UPTD of Bali Province confirmed that overall venue readiness has reached approximately 95 percent. The main venue, Taman Werdhi Budaya Art Centre in Denpasar, is at 85 percent and crews are working continuously to complete the remaining preparations.

## Theme: Glorifying the Soul

PKB XLVIII 2026 carries the theme **"Atma Kerthi: Jiwa Sidha Parisudha"** — loosely translated as "glorification and purification of the human soul toward perfect wholeness, clarity, and sanctity." The theme is aligned with the Bali provincial government's vision of *Nangun Sat Kerthi Loka Bali* — a development philosophy rooted in Balinese spiritual values.

## 20,929 Artists from 673 Arts Organizations

This year's festival involves an extraordinary **20,929 artists** representing 673 arts organizations and foundations from across all nine regencies and one city in Bali. For the *Peed Aya* parade alone, **3,430 artists** will march through the Renon area before the formal opening ceremony takes place at the Art Centre in the evening.

The parade will open with a performance of the sacred **Siwa Nataraja Dance**, followed by cultural contingents representing each region of Bali, including invited performers from abroad.

## 16 Venues, Free Entry for All

PKB 2026 runs for a full month — from **June 13 to July 11, 2026** — across **16 venues** centered on the Taman Budaya complex and surrounding areas. All performances are open to the public at no charge, staying true to the festival's spirit of cultural accessibility.

The total budget for this year's festival is set at **Rp10.5 billion**, with emphasis on improving artist welfare and elevating the quality of performances.

## President Prabowo Invited to Open the Festival

Governor of Bali, Wayan Koster, has formally invited President Prabowo Subianto to officiate the opening of PKB ke-48. The Bali Provincial Government is still awaiting confirmation from the presidential palace. Traditionally, the president or a high-ranking representative attends the *Peed Aya* parade before formally opening the festival at Taman Budaya in the evening.

The Pesta Kesenian Bali has been held annually since 1979, making it one of Indonesia's longest-running and most celebrated cultural festivals. As it enters its 48th edition, PKB continues to affirm Bali's standing as a global center of living arts and culture.
