Crossing to Bali by sea just got more affordable. State-owned ferry operator PT ASDP Indonesia Ferry has officially launched a **fare stimulus program** for the **Ketapang (Banyuwangi) – Gilimanuk (Bali)** crossing route, offering significant savings for travelers heading to or returning from Bali during the mid-year school holiday period.

## How the Discount Works

ASDP's stimulus scheme operates by waiving **100 percent of port service fees** — a component embedded in the total ticket price that passengers typically pay without distinguishing it from the base fare. On average, this port service component amounts to **21.9 percent of the total ticket price**, making the effective discount a substantial saving for travelers, especially those traveling with vehicles.

The actual savings in rupiah will vary depending on the vehicle category, but all eligible passengers and vehicle classes receive the same proportional benefit.

## Who Is Eligible

The discount applies to:

- **Walk-on passengers** (foot passengers without a vehicle)
- **Vehicle classes II through IVA** — covering motorcycles, private cars, and light commercial vehicles

This covers the majority of travelers using the Ketapang–Gilimanuk crossing for holiday purposes.

## Validity Period

The fare discount is valid for departures between **June 20 and July 5, 2026**, covering the peak mid-year school holiday window. This period traditionally sees a sharp spike in crossings as families from Java travel to Bali for holidays, and Balinese residents return home or travel outward.

## Online Booking is Mandatory

There is an important condition travelers must be aware of: **all discounted tickets must be purchased online**. ASDP does not allow on-site ticket purchases at the port for this program. The two approved booking channels are:

1. **Ferizy mobile app** (available on the App Store and Google Play)
2. **Website**: trip.ferizy.com

This requirement is part of ASDP's broader strategy to reduce congestion at terminal areas, improve passenger flow management, and ensure a smoother experience for everyone.

## Book Early to Avoid Rush

ASDP strongly advises travelers to **plan ahead and purchase tickets well in advance**. Online booking for the holiday period opened on **June 6, 2026** — two weeks before the discount period started — giving travelers ample lead time.

During peak holiday periods, demand for ferry crossings on this route is extremely high. Without a pre-booked ticket, travelers risk being turned away or facing very long waits at the port. Early booking not only secures a place on the desired crossing but also allows travelers to choose the most convenient departure time.

## Why Ketapang–Gilimanuk Matters

The Ketapang–Gilimanuk crossing is the **primary land access point into Bali from Java**. Thousands of vehicles and passengers traverse the Bali Strait on this route every day, making it one of the busiest ferry routes in Indonesia. For travelers driving from Java or other parts of the archipelago, this short sea crossing is the final leg of their journey before setting foot on the Island of the Gods.

The discount program reflects ASDP's role not just as a transport operator, but as an active supporter of **domestic tourism mobility**. By reducing the cost of reaching Bali, the program aims to encourage more Indonesian families to plan holiday trips to the island — supporting the recovery and strengthening of Bali's domestic tourism economy.
