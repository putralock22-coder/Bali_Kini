Residents and tourism operators on the Nusa Islands — particularly Nusa Lembongan and Nusa Ceningan — are set to benefit from a major infrastructure breakthrough this year. The long-awaited Lembongan–Ceningan Bridge project is officially scheduled to break ground in 2026, backed by national budget (APBN) funding channelled through the Ministry of Public Works (Kementerian PU).

## Rp112 Billion Total Project Value

The full scope of the project carries a price tag of **Rp112 billion**. The budget is not limited to the bridge structure itself — it also covers the widening and construction of access roads stretching all the way to **Bias Munjul Port**, which was completed in 2023 but has since faced utilization constraints due to poor road connectivity.

The Ministry of Public Works has approved an initial allocation of **Rp60 billion** — approximately half of the total requirement. Bali Governor Wayan Koster has been actively lobbying for the full budget to be secured, ensuring the project can be delivered in its entirety.

## More Than Just a Bridge

Governor Koster has framed the project as critical infrastructure with strategic significance beyond transportation alone. The Lembongan–Ceningan Bridge is intended to serve as the **backbone of the Bias Munjul Port**, which has been unable to reach its full potential without adequate road access from the neighboring island.

Once the bridge is in place, **four-wheeled vehicles** will be able to cross between Ceningan and Lembongan — a major upgrade from the existing narrow crossing that currently accommodates only motorcycles and pedestrians.

### Tourism Impact on the Nusa Islands

The Nusa Islands — comprising Nusa Lembongan, Nusa Ceningan, and Nusa Penida — are among Bali's most sought-after marine tourism destinations. Thousands of domestic and international visitors arrive each year for world-class snorkeling, diving, and pristine coastal scenery.

The new bridge is expected to:
- Significantly improve tourist mobility between the two islands
- Ease the distribution of essential goods and supplies for local communities
- Unlock new tourism development potential on Nusa Ceningan
- Stimulate growth in accommodation and tourism businesses in the area

## Part of Bali's Broader Infrastructure Push

The Lembongan–Ceningan Bridge is one component of a larger strategic infrastructure package for Bali. Governor Koster previously secured ministerial approval for a total infrastructure investment exceeding **Rp1.5 trillion**, which encompasses a range of projects including the Singaraja–Mengwitani shortcut road and an underpass in the Jimbaran area.

All of these initiatives are designed to support the island's vision of high-quality, sustainable tourism while ensuring equitable economic development across Bali — including its more remote island territories. Construction beginning in 2026 brings this long-standing aspiration one step closer to reality.
