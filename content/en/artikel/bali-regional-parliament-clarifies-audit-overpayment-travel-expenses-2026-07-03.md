Reports of alleged overpayments in the official travel expenses of **Bali Provincial Parliament (DPRD)** members resurfaced on Friday (3/7/2026), prompting an immediate response from the institution. **I Ketut Nayaka, Secretary of the DPRD (Sekwan)**, moved quickly to clarify the issue to the public, emphasizing that the matter has already been addressed.

According to Nayaka, the audit findings by the **Supreme Audit Agency (BPK)** related to excess payments in legislative travel expenditures — findings that re-emerged in recent online circulation — date from a **prior audit period** and have long since been resolved through official procedures.

## What the BPK Found

The BPK identified **overpayments in travel expense reimbursements** for DPRD members, with the total discrepancy reaching into the **billions of rupiah**. The findings pointed to a gap between actual travel costs and the amounts billed by travel agencies managing ticketing, accommodation, and transportation for official parliamentary trips.

In standard government procurement, travel agencies handle bookings on behalf of public institutions. Anomalies arise when billed amounts exceed market rates or actual purchase prices — the discrepancy between the two constituting the overpayment flagged in the audit.

## Parliament: Matter Is Closed

Sekwan Nayaka stated categorically that all overpayments identified in the BPK audit **have been returned** by the relevant travel agencies to the regional treasury. The follow-up process was carried out in accordance with applicable regulations and reported back to BPK.

"This is an old issue that has been followed up and resolved," Nayaka said, as quoted in Bali Post's Friday (3/7/2026) edition.

The clarification was necessary because circulation of the original audit report on social media and news platforms had created the impression that the overpayment issue was ongoing — when in fact the institutional response had already been completed.

## Governance and Accountability in Focus

The episode highlights the persistent challenge of **transparency in official travel expenditure management** at regional legislative bodies across Indonesia. Travel expenses are among the more scrutinized budget lines in government audits, given their susceptibility to manipulation through inflated ticket prices, hotel cost markups, or claims for trips that never took place.

In many regions, BPK audits have uncovered similar patterns: collusion between officials and travel agencies resulting in losses to public funds. The audit-and-remedy mechanism remains the key institutional tool for detecting and recovering such losses.

## Lessons and Next Steps

Beyond the resolution of this particular case, the episode underscores the value of robust internal controls in travel expense management — including regular market-price verification for tickets and accommodation, e-procurement transparency, and alignment with the Ministry of Finance's standardized government travel cost guidelines.

Proactive publication of travel expenditure realizations through official channels — DPRD websites or regional transparency portals — would further strengthen public trust in Bali's legislative body.

Strong governance is not a peripheral concern for Bali. The island's reputation as a world-class destination rests not just on its temples and terraced rice fields, but also on the perceived quality of its institutions. Investors evaluating Bali projects, international organizations considering the island as a summit venue, and discerning travelers all factor governance quality into their assessment of Bali as a reliable partner and destination.

Accountability in the use of public funds — even for something as routine as official travel — contributes to that broader reputational picture.
