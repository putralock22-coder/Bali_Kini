The iconic terraced rice fields of Jatiluwih in Tabanan Regency are once again the setting for a unique blend of culture, sport, and community celebration. Jatiluwih Festival VII is officially underway from June 20 to 21, 2026, hosted in Jatiluwih Tourism Village, Penebel District, Tabanan. This year carries special significance: for the first time in the festival's history, it has been included in the **Karisma Event Nusantara (KEN) 2026** — Indonesia's national calendar of distinguished tourism events recognized by the Ministry of Tourism.

## Day One: Harvest Season Meets Festival Spirit

On the first day, Friday June 20, the festival grounds come alive with cultural performances and hands-on activities that invite visitors to step into the world of Balinese rice farmers. The timing could not be better — the festival coincides with the local harvest season, offering guests the rare opportunity to participate in traditional paddy harvesting directly in the Subak fields.

The Subak system — a cooperative water management and farming philosophy in Bali recognized as a UNESCO Cultural Heritage of Humanity — forms both the backdrop and the soul of this festival. Visitors gain direct experience of the values of harmony between humans, nature, and spiritual life that underpin Balinese agrarian culture.

## The 5K Bali Tourism Run: Racing Through a World Heritage Site

The festival's centerpiece event is the **Bali Tourism Run 2026**, a 5-kilometer fun run scheduled for Sunday, June 21. The route winds through Jatiluwih's stunning terraced Subak landscape, offering participants an unforgettable running experience through one of the world's most picturesque UNESCO heritage sites.

With more than **1,500 registrations already confirmed** against a target of 2,000 participants, the run has generated strong interest. Notably, the event has attracted dozens of foreign participants, including runners from:
- **Russia**
- **Malaysia**
- **Japan**
- **China**

International participants cited the unique appeal of running through lush, stepped rice paddies in Tabanan's cool highland air as a major draw — an experience that combines physical activity with cultural immersion in a way few events worldwide can match.

## Boosting Local Economies Through Sport Tourism

The festival is not just about sport and culture — it is designed as a deliberate economic engine for the surrounding community. More than **40 local SMEs from Tabanan** have set up stalls showcasing and selling products that represent the region's agricultural bounty, including:
- **Jatiluwih red rice** — an organically grown variety cultivated using traditional Subak irrigation
- **Jatiluwih coffee** — a locally grown coffee with the distinct character of Tabanan's highland terroir
- Handicrafts, traditional snacks, and other locally produced goods

By encouraging festival visitors to stay overnight in village accommodations and purchase local products, organizers aim to maximize the economic trickle-down effect for residents of Jatiluwih and surrounding communities.

## Quality Tourism: The Bigger Picture

The combination of a cultural festival with a sport event reflects Bali's evolving tourism philosophy. Rather than simply competing on visitor numbers, Bali is increasingly designing experiences that encourage longer stays, deeper engagement with local culture, and higher per-visitor spending.

Jatiluwih Festival VII's inclusion in KEN 2026 elevates its national profile, making it eligible for wider promotion through official Ministry of Tourism channels. This should translate into greater visibility among both domestic and international travelers looking for authentic, experience-driven travel in Indonesia.

The Subak landscape of Jatiluwih — already inscribed on the UNESCO World Heritage list since 2012 as part of the Cultural Landscape of Bali Province — continues to prove its value not only as an agricultural marvel, but as a living tourism attraction capable of competing on the world stage.
