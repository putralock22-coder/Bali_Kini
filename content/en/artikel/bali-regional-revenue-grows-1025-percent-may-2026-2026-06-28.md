## Bali's Fiscal Position Remains Strong Amid Global Uncertainty

Bali's combined regional own-source revenue — known as PAD (Pendapatan Asli Daerah) — across all nine regencies, cities, and the provincial government reached **Rp7.59 trillion** by the end of May 2026. This represents a growth of **10.25 percent** compared to the same period last year, when the figure stood at Rp6.78 trillion.

The growth is particularly notable given the ongoing turbulence in the global economy, including the geopolitical tensions in the Middle East that have weighed on several economic sectors across the region.

## Tourism Taxes: The Engine of Growth

The primary driver of this revenue surge is **regional taxation**, dominated by sectors directly tied to tourism. Accommodation taxes and the PBJT (pajak barang dan jasa tertentu) — covering food and beverage consumption — continue to account for the largest share of PAD collection across Bali's regencies.

This underscores a familiar reality: Bali's finances remain deeply intertwined with the health of its tourism industry. Rising international arrivals and robust hotel and restaurant occupancy rates have translated directly into healthier regional coffers.

### Progress Toward the 2026 Annual Target

The Rp7.59 trillion collected through May represents **35 percent** of Bali's total 2026 PAD target of **Rp21.6 trillion**. With approximately seven months of the fiscal year now elapsed, regional governments will need to accelerate collection in the second half — a period that typically benefits from school holiday peaks and year-end travel surges.

## A Signal of Fiscal Resilience

The double-digit PAD growth sends a positive signal about Bali's fiscal resilience. This is particularly significant as some other Indonesian regions are experiencing revenue pressures due to economic slowdowns and currency volatility.

The broader fiscal picture is also encouraging. The national budget (APBN) recorded a **surplus of Rp679.98 billion** in the Bali region through May 2026, indicating that central government spending in the area is also being managed efficiently.

## The Risk of Over-Reliance on Tourism

Despite the positive trend, Bali's heavy dependence on tourism revenue remains a structural concern. External shocks — whether travel advisories from key source markets, natural disasters, or global health events — can rapidly translate into revenue shortfalls.

Policymakers are increasingly aware of the need to **diversify Bali's revenue base** beyond tourism. Initiatives such as village-level cooperative development (KDMP), digital economy promotion, and support for craft-based manufacturing are being positioned as long-term strategies to reduce fiscal vulnerability.

### Which Regencies Are Leading?

Denpasar and Badung regency — home to the majority of Bali's hotels, restaurants, and tourist attractions — are expected to be the top PAD contributors. Tabanan and Gianyar, with their strong cultural and natural tourism appeal, also show consistent revenue growth.

---

Bali's 10.25% PAD growth through May 2026 reflects genuine economic dynamism. But with only 35% of the annual target achieved, the real test lies in the months ahead — and in whether the island can sustain this momentum while reducing its long-term dependence on a single engine of growth.
