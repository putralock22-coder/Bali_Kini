A daughter of Bali brought pride to Indonesia once again on the international stage. **Desak Made Rita Kusuma Dewi**, Indonesia's top women's speed climbing athlete, claimed the gold medal in the women's individual speed event at the **World Climbing Series Krakow 2026** in Poland on Saturday, July 4, 2026 — and she did so in historic fashion.

## The Four-Lane Era Begins in Krakow

This competition marked a significant turning point in the history of the World Climbing Series. For the first time ever, the final was held in a **four-lane format** — four athletes competing simultaneously on four parallel routes. The system replaces the traditional head-to-head duel format and is designed to make finals more dynamic, unpredictable, and thrilling for spectators.

Desak thrived in this new setting. She recorded a blistering time of **6.54 seconds** to claim the top spot on the podium ahead of three world-class rivals.

## Women's Final Results

| Place | Athlete | Country | Time |
|-------|---------|---------|------|
| Gold | Desak Made Rita | Indonesia | 6.54s |
| Silver | Natalia Kalucka | Poland | 6.62s |
| Bronze | Emma Hunt | USA | 11.37s |
| 4th | Aleksandra Miroslaw | Poland | — |

Kalucka — one of the fastest speed climbers in the world — was just 0.08 seconds behind Desak. It was a razor-thin margin that underscored just how competitive the final was, and how impressive Desak's performance truly was.

## Additional Indonesian Medals

Desak's gold was not the only success story for Indonesia at Krakow. Paired with Antasyafi Robby Al Hilmi, she also secured a **silver medal in the mixed relay** event. Meanwhile, male speed specialist Raharjati Nursamsa added a **bronze medal** to Indonesia's tally.

### Indonesia's Seven-Athlete Squad

**Men:** Raharjati Nursamsa, Aditya Tri Syahria, Veddriq Leonardo, Antasyafi Robby Al Hilmi
**Women:** Desak Made Rita, Rajiah, Kadek Asih

All three Indonesian women — Desak, Rajiah, and Kadek Asih — advanced through the qualification rounds to reach the finals at this prestigious event, which ran from July 3 to 5, 2026.

## Bali's Rising Star on the World Stage

Desak Made Rita is more than just Indonesia's best speed climber — she represents Bali's growing footprint in world-class athletics. Her performance in Krakow cements her status as one of the best active female speed climbers in the world, and positions Indonesia's climbing program as a formidable force heading into future international competitions.

Her achievement also adds a new chapter to the story of Bali as a breeding ground for exceptional athletes — a narrative that continues to grow alongside the island's reputation as a world-class travel destination.
