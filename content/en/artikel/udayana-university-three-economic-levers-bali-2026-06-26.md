As Bali navigates a slightly slower pace of economic growth amid persistent structural challenges, academics are stepping forward with concrete policy prescriptions. **Prof. Dr. Ni Putu Wiwin Setyari**, an economics professor at Universitas Udayana (Unud), has proposed that the Bali provincial government focus on strengthening three strategic sectors that she describes as the key "economic levers" for the island's sustainable development.

## Bali's Economy: Growing, but Slowing Slightly

To understand the urgency behind these recommendations, consider Bali's recent economic trajectory. According to the Central Statistics Agency (BPS), Bali's economy grew **5.58 percent in the first quarter of 2026** — marginally lower than the 5.59 percent recorded in the same period of 2025. While the difference is minimal, the slight deceleration signals that Bali needs to consciously diversify and strengthen its economic base rather than rely solely on continued tourism momentum.

## First Lever: Infrastructure and Digital Foundation

The first group of priority sectors identified by Prof. Wiwin comprises what she calls the **foundational pillars** of the economy:

- **Electricity and gas supply**
- **Information and communication technology (ICT)**
- **Transportation and warehousing**

According to her analysis, these sectors are uniquely positioned because they exhibit both strong upstream-downstream linkages with other industries **and** the **highest multiplier effects** in the economy. When these infrastructure sectors are strengthened, the benefits cascade across the entire economic ecosystem — from micro-businesses to large corporations.

Investing in digital connectivity, for example, directly reduces operational costs for small businesses, enables digital tourism products, and opens new markets for local producers. Reliable energy and improved transportation networks similarly lower production costs and raise Bali's overall competitiveness as a destination for both tourists and investors.

## Second Lever: Labor-Intensive Sectors

The second strategic group consists of sectors with the **highest capacity for job creation**:

- **Accommodation and food and beverage (F&B) services**
- **Wholesale and retail trade**

These sub-sectors form the backbone of Bali's tourism-driven economy while simultaneously supporting inclusive economic participation. They employ a large proportion of Bali's workforce — from hotel staff and restaurant workers to market vendors and tour guides — and ensure that economic benefits are distributed across communities, not just concentrated in high-end establishments.

Prof. Wiwin emphasizes that strengthening these sectors is not simply about adding more hotels or shops, but about improving the **quality, formalization, and digital readiness** of existing businesses so they can be more productive and competitive in an evolving market.

## Third Lever: Diversification and Creative Economy

Beyond the two primary pillars, Prof. Wiwin advocates for systematic **economic diversification** to reduce Bali's structural over-dependence on conventional tourism. Developing the creative economy — encompassing crafts, fashion, film, music, digital platforms, and other creative industries — is seen as essential to providing an economic buffer when tourism faces external shocks such as pandemics, geopolitical disruptions, or global travel trend shifts.

## Alignment with Provincial Policy Direction

These academic recommendations align closely with the direction set by Bali's Governor, who has previously emphasized the importance of reducing excessive reliance on international tourism. The Governor has pointed to the need for stronger digital and creative economy development as a long-term strategy to make Bali's economy more resilient and self-sustaining.

By reinforcing infrastructure as a foundation, optimizing labor-intensive sectors as employment engines, and cultivating the creative economy as a long-term pillar, Bali aims to build a growth model that is **more equitable, more resilient, and more sustainable** for its people.
