Kedonganan Beach in Badung once again became the backdrop for an international open water swimming competition. Oceanman Bali Indonesia 2026 officially ran from June 19 to 21, 2026, marking the return of this prestigious event after a two-year hiatus. Under the theme **"Waves of Change"**, this edition set a new record as the largest Oceanman event ever held in Indonesia.

## Over 900 Swimmers from 27 Countries

Enthusiasm for Oceanman Bali 2026 far exceeded the organizers' expectations. More than **900 participants** registered from **27 countries**, representing an increase of approximately **30 percent** compared to the 2023 edition which attracted around 700 swimmers.

Participating nations included Indonesia, Singapore, Malaysia, Vietnam, the Philippines, Australia, India, Russia, Japan, Thailand, the USA, UAE, Hong Kong, Italy, Mexico, Colombia, the UK, Ireland, Iceland, Hungary, Qatar, South Korea, Maldives, Taiwan, Ukraine, Azerbaijan, and Belarus.

This extraordinary global participation reinforces Kedonganan Beach — and Bali as a whole — as a premier international sport tourism destination.

## Six Competitive Categories

Oceanman Bali 2026 offered six race categories suited to varying ages and skill levels:

- **Oceankids 500 meters** — for junior participants
- **Oceanteams 3x500 meters** — relay team category
- **Oceanfins 2 kilometers** — swimming with fins
- **Ocean Sprint 2 kilometers** — standard speed category
- **Half Oceanman 5 kilometers** — intermediate distance
- **Oceanman 10 kilometers** — the flagship full-distance race

On the second competition day, athletes contested the Oceanfins 2KM, Oceankids 500M, and Oceanteams 3x500M events. Favorable sea conditions throughout the three days ensured the competition ran safely and comfortably for all participants.

## Back on the World Stage After Two Years

Oceanman Bali 2026 forms part of the official **Oceanman World Series**, reintroducing Indonesia into the global open water swimming calendar. Co-Founders Felix Sutanto and Albert Sutanto of Oceanman Indonesia ensured that safety remained the top priority throughout the event.

"The competition ran with quite favorable sea conditions because the safety and comfort of participants remain our top priority," said Felix Sutanto.

To back this commitment, organizers deployed dedicated medical teams, lifeguards, rescue teams, and continuous monitoring systems for both weather and water conditions across all three days of competition.

## Indonesian Athletes in the Spotlight

Among the hundreds of participants, two Indonesians drew particular attention: **Joe Aditya**, a promising young Indonesian swimmer, and **Desmond Sutanto**, a prominent healthy-lifestyle advocate. Their participation is expected to inspire more Indonesians to embrace open-water sports as part of an active lifestyle.

Swimmer registrations opened as early as October 2025, and the high early uptake signaled that Oceanman Bali has already built a loyal global following since its last edition.

## Boosting Bali's Sport Tourism Economy

Oceanman Bali 2026 is far more than a competition — it is a testament to the growing momentum of sport tourism in Bali. The presence of hundreds of international athletes directly stimulated the local economy, from accommodation and restaurants to transportation and the broader hospitality industry in and around Kedonganan and Jimbaran.

Bali continues to position itself as a premium sport tourism hub, joining a growing roster of international events including the Bali Marathon, the Asian Open Water Swimming Championship, and Red Bull Cliff Diving — all held on the Island of the Gods in 2026.
