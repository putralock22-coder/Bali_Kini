On Saturday, June 20, 2026, the verdant terraced rice fields of Jatiluwih in Tabanan, Bali, came alive with celebration. Tabanan Regent Komang Gede Sanjaya officially opened **Jatiluwih Festival VII 2026**, an annual gathering that has grown into one of Bali's most prestigious cultural tourism events.

## A Theme Rooted in Balance

This year's festival carries the theme **"In Balance with Nature, Inspired by Tradition"** — a statement of shared commitment from local communities, regional government, and tourism managers to preserve the delicate equilibrium between nature, culture, and human society as the foundation of sustainable tourism development.

Regent Sanjaya emphasized that the festival serves a dual purpose: it is both an effective tourism promotion platform and a direct economic driver for local UMKM vendors, artisans, and subak farmers who participate in the event.

## Selected for Indonesia's Karisma Event Nusantara

The festival's most significant achievement this year is its selection as a featured event in the **Karisma Event Nusantara (KEN) 2026**, the Indonesian Ministry of Tourism's prestigious national calendar of events, chosen through a rigorous competitive process from thousands of cultural and tourism events across the archipelago.

Being listed on KEN 2026 positions Jatiluwih not just as a local pride of Tabanan and Bali, but as a destination representing the best of Indonesian tourism on the world stage.

## UNESCO Recognition and Global Awards

Jatiluwih is no ordinary destination. Its iconic rice terraces form part of Bali's **Subak System**, recognized by UNESCO as a World Cultural Heritage site since 2012. The subak represents a sophisticated traditional water management system that equitably distributes irrigation water from upstream to downstream — a living embodiment of Tri Hita Karana, Bali's philosophy of harmony between humans, nature, and the divine.

Beyond UNESCO recognition, Jatiluwih has accumulated an impressive list of international accolades:
- **Best Tourism Village** by UN Tourism (2024)
- **Top 100 Green Destination** (2025)
- **Leading UNESCO Culture Landscape Tourism Destination** at the Asian Tourism and Hospitality Awards, Kuala Lumpur (2026)

### Bali Tourism Run 2026

The festival is paired with the **Bali Tourism Run 2026**, a sport tourism running event that takes participants along the breathtaking terraced rice field trails of Jatiluwih. The event draws participants from across Indonesia and abroad, with local economic circulation estimated at billions of rupiah.

## Festival Highlights

On opening day, visitors were treated to a rich program of traditional Balinese performing arts — from sacred dances to captivating gamelan performances. A local culinary fair showcased authentic Tabanan flavors, while UMKM booths displayed handcrafted goods and local agricultural products.

The festival's standout experience is one found nowhere else: **harvesting rice directly in the paddies**. Visitors are invited to roll up their sleeves and experience firsthand how Balinese farmers cultivate their land within the centuries-old subak system — a tangible connection between modern tourism and living heritage.

## Jatiluwih as a Model for Responsible Tourism

Through this festival, Jatiluwih demonstrates that tourism and cultural preservation are not competing forces. When managed thoughtfully, tourism becomes the most powerful instrument for keeping traditions alive, strengthening local economies, and protecting natural environments for generations to come.

Jatiluwih Festival is proof that Bali can rise to the challenges of modern tourism without losing its soul.
