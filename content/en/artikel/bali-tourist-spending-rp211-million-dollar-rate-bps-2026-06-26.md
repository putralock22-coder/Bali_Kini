Bali's statistics office has revealed a nuanced picture behind the apparent surge in foreign tourist spending. While headline figures show a clear increase, the Central Statistics Agency (BPS) of Bali Province explains that the strengthening US dollar against the Indonesian rupiah played a major role in inflating the nominal numbers.

## Spending Figures Above National Average

According to BPS data, the average daily expenditure of foreign visitors staying in Bali reached **Rp2.11 million per night in 2025** — up approximately Rp60,000 compared to 2024's figure of Rp2.05 million per night.

Notably, Bali's per-night spending figure surpasses the **national average of Rp2.02 million**, reaffirming Bali's position as Indonesia's premium tourism destination where foreign visitors consistently spend more.

## The Currency Factor

The head of BPS Bali was careful to clarify that the nominal increase does not automatically translate into higher real consumption. The agency calculates tourist spending using the **JISDOR exchange rate** (Jakarta Interbank Spot Dollar Rate) published daily by Bank Indonesia.

In 2024, the average JISDOR rate stood at **Rp15,850 per US dollar**. By 2025, it had weakened further to **Rp16,478 per US dollar** — a depreciation of roughly 4 percent. As a result, even if a tourist spent the same amount of dollars, the converted rupiah value would automatically appear higher.

The rupiah weakness continued into 2026, with the US dollar briefly reaching around **Rp17,805 in May 2026**, its highest level in recent history amid global economic volatility.

## Real Benefits for Bali's Tourism Sector

Despite the technical nuance around currency conversion, the stronger dollar does produce genuine economic benefits for Bali. Foreign visitors who bring dollars or similarly strong currencies receive more rupiah when exchanging money, effectively boosting their purchasing power during their stay.

This dynamic has had a positive ripple effect across multiple tourism sub-sectors:

- **Accommodation**: Hotels and villas report higher spending by foreign guests on premium rooms and additional services
- **Food and beverage**: Restaurants and cafes in tourist areas register larger per-visit transactions from international visitors
- **Spa and wellness**: One of Bali's most iconic offerings has seen growing demand as foreign visitors capitalize on favorable exchange rates
- **Local crafts and souvenirs**: Sales of handmade goods, traditional textiles, and creative economy products have been buoyed by increased foreign spending power

## The Other Side: Rising Import Costs

BPS also cautioned that the weaker rupiah carries a downside. Industries in Bali that rely on imported inputs — including alcoholic beverages, kitchen equipment, hotel linens, and imported food ingredients — face rising operational costs. This pressure could compress profit margins, particularly for businesses heavily dependent on imported goods.

Over time, if operators pass these costs on to consumers, domestic tourists — who do not benefit from the dollar's strength — may feel the pinch through higher prices across the island.

## Context: Bali's Foreign Exchange Contribution

Strong foreign tourist spending is a critical driver of Bali's broader economic output. In 2025, the island's tourism sector reportedly contributed around **Rp176 trillion in foreign exchange earnings** to the national economy, underlining Bali's role as Indonesia's primary foreign exchange earner from tourism.

BPS advised industry stakeholders not to be misled by the apparent spending increase, and instead to focus on improving service quality and product differentiation — ensuring Bali remains genuinely competitive on the global tourism stage beyond the temporary boost of currency fluctuations.
