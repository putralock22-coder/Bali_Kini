The village of Jatiluwih in Tabanan Regency just wrapped up **Jatiluwih Festival VII 2026**, a two-day celebration held on Saturday and Sunday, June 20–21, 2026. This year marks a historic milestone: for the first time, the festival was officially listed in Indonesia's prestigious **Kharisma Event Nusantara (KEN) 2026** calendar by the Ministry of Tourism — a recognition that positions Jatiluwih as one of the country's premier cultural tourism destinations.

## A Festival Rooted in Balance

Under the theme *"In Balance with Nature, Inspired by Tradition"*, the festival reaffirms its core commitment to sustainable tourism that honors the balance between nature, local culture, and community livelihood. Tabanan Regent I Komang Gede Sanjaya opened the event and emphasized that the festival is more than entertainment — it's a strategic vehicle for promoting Jatiluwih globally while driving the local economy.

"This festival is our way of introducing Jatiluwih to the world and moving the local economy at the same time," said Regent Sanjaya.

## Bali Tourism Run: Racing Through a UNESCO World Heritage Site

The highlight of this year's edition was the **Bali Tourism Run 2026**, a leisurely run threading through the 227-hectare terraced rice fields of Jatiluwih's subak irrigation system — a UNESCO World Cultural Heritage site since 2012. Nearly **2,000 participants** joined the run, setting a new attendance record for the festival.

Runners didn't just compete — they experienced the living landscape of Bali's ancient subak system up close, with views of layered green terraces stretching toward the slopes of Mount Batukaru. The Tabanan Regional Secretary announced plans to rotate similar running events across other iconic Bali destinations, with the sacred Besakih area slated as the next host.

## Rice Harvesting, Local Products, and Fully Booked Accommodation

Beyond the run, visitors could participate in **hands-on rice harvesting** with local farmers — a genuine agrotourism experience that connects guests with the living agricultural traditions of the village. An **UMKM (small business) bazaar** offered local culinary specialties and artisanal products from Jatiluwih's small entrepreneurs.

The economic ripple effect was immediate and measurable. Hotels, homestays, and guesthouses in the Jatiluwih area reached near **100% occupancy** during the two festival days — a dramatic uptick that demonstrates the event's growing appeal among both domestic and international visitors.

## A Portfolio of International Awards

Jatiluwih's global reputation is well-established. Beyond its UNESCO heritage status, the village earned the **UN Tourism Best Tourism Village** award in 2024, entered the **Top 100 Green Destinations** in 2025, and most recently received the *Leading UNESCO Culture Landscape Tourism Destination* title at the Asian Tourism and Hospitality Awards in Kuala Lumpur in 2026. The KEN listing adds significant domestic recognition to this growing international portfolio.

## What's Next: Besakih on the Horizon

With Jatiluwih Festival VII setting a new high-water mark, organizers plan to expand the model to other iconic destinations across Bali. The **Besakih Temple complex** — often called the "Mother Temple of Bali" — has been named as the leading candidate for the next Bali Tourism Run.

The festival proves that Bali's tourism appeal extends far beyond its beaches. Its rice fields, living subak traditions, and agricultural heritage offer world-class experiences that are sustainable, culturally resonant, and deeply authentic.
