**The 48th Bali Arts Festival (Pesta Kesenian Bali / PKB XLVIII)** rolls on through Thursday, June 18, 2026, with a packed schedule stretching from midday to late night at Taman Budaya Art Centre in Denpasar. Today's programme spans traditional court literature, folk dance competitions, classical dance drama, and a showcase of gamelan performance at its most electrifying.

The 2026 festival runs from **June 13 to July 11** — coinciding with Indonesia's school holiday season and drawing families, cultural enthusiasts, and international visitors from across the globe.

## Today's Full Programme — Thursday, June 18, 2026

### 11:00 AM WITA — Wimbakara Taman Penasar (Competition)
**Venue: Kalangan Ratna Kanda**

The afternoon opens with a **Taman Penasar competition** featuring **Sanggar Seni Semar Pegulingan Sekar Tunjung Biru** representing Badung Regency. Taman Penasar is a sophisticated Balinese performing art form rooted in classical *geguritan* (literary poetry), accompanied by the Semar Pegulingan gamelan — an ancient court orchestra known for its ethereal, five-tone melodies. This is one of the rarer, more cerebral art forms at PKB, and worth seeking out for serious enthusiasts of Balinese court culture.

### 5:00 PM WITA — Utsawa Joged Bumbung Tradisi (Parade)
**Venue: Kalangan Madya Mandala**

As the afternoon cools, the open-air **Kalangan Madya Mandala** stage hosts a **Joged Bumbung Tradisi parade** — one of the liveliest events on the PKB calendar. Joged Bumbung is Bali's premier social dance, characterised by a female lead dancer (*penjoged*) who invites audience members — often unsuspecting tourists — to dance alongside her to the syncopated rhythm of bamboo instruments.

Two groups go head-to-head:
- **Sekeha Joged Akah Luki** (Badung Regency representative)
- **Sekaa Joged Bumbung Duta Suara** (Jembrana Regency representative)

Expect laughter, good-natured competition, and a crowd that quickly forgets it's watching a formal cultural event.

### 6:30 PM WITA — Rekasadana Palegongan Dharmapatni
**Venue: Kalangan Ayodya**

The evening's more refined offering comes from **Yayasan Bali Kuna Santi** of Karangasem Regency, presenting **Palegongan Dharmapatni** — a classical Balinese dance that centres on the portrayal of noble female characters from Hindu mythology and literary tradition. Palegongan is known for the extraordinary precision of its hand and eye movements, and the emotional depth its performers bring to ancient stories of devotion, strength, and sacrifice.

### 7:30 PM WITA — Rekasadana Sendratari Ciptoning Mintaraga
**Venue: Gedung Ksirarnawa**

One of tonight's most anticipated performances comes from an unexpected direction: **the Department of Dance Education, Yogyakarta State University (UNY)**, presenting **Sendratari Ciptoning Mintaraga**. Sendratari is a Javanese-influenced ballet-drama form that tells mythological stories through choreographed movement and gamelan — without spoken dialogue. The presence of a Yogyakarta ensemble at PKB underscores the festival's role as a platform for inter-island cultural exchange, not just a Balinese showcase.

### 7:30 PM WITA — Wimbakara Baleganjur Remaja (Youth Competition)
**Venue: Panggung Terbuka Ardha Candra (Open-Air Stage)**

The unmissable **highlight of the night**: the **Youth Baleganjur Competition** at Ardha Candra Open Stage. Baleganjur is Bali's most dynamically theatrical gamelan form — a procession-based ensemble built for maximum sonic impact, with clashing cymbals, rolling drums, and a pulsing tempo that seems to accelerate the heartbeat. When performed by teenage competitors fighting for their regency's honour, the energy reaches a whole different level.

Multiple youth delegations from across Bali's regencies will compete. The crowd at Ardha Candra on Baleganjur nights is legendary for its enthusiasm. Arrive well before 7:30 PM to secure a good spot.

## Practical Information

All PKB performances at Taman Budaya Art Centre are generally **free and open to the public**. The complex is walkable from central Denpasar and easily accessible by ride-hailing apps. For high-demand evening shows like Baleganjur Remaja, plan to arrive **30–45 minutes early**. Parking fills quickly after 6:00 PM.
