The Badung Regency Government (Pemkab Badung) is putting the finishing touches on preparations for the **Simultaneous Village Head Election (Pilkel Serentak) 2026**. The electoral process will be held across three villages within **Mengwi District**, with an official schedule now taking shape.

## Three Villages in Mengwi to Hold Elections

The three villages slated to hold their village head elections this year are **Sobangan**, **Baha**, and **Munggu** — all located within Mengwi District, Badung Regency. Elections are being called in these villages because the current terms of their respective village heads (*perbekel*) are set to expire this year.

## Key Dates in the Election Timeline

Pemkab Badung has outlined the following schedule for the Pilkel Serentak:

- **Candidate Registration Period**: July 20–28, 2026
- **Election Day (Voting)**: November 17, 2026

Prospective candidates will have an eight-day window to formally register during the late-July period. Following administrative screening and campaign stages, voting will take place on the designated November date.

## New Rule: Single Candidate Can Face an Empty Box

The 2026 Pilkel Serentak introduces a notable regulatory change. Under **Law Number 3 of 2024** and **Government Regulation Number 16 of 2026**, the procedure for cases where only a single candidate registers has been updated.

If, after two extensions of the registration period, only one candidate is available, the election process may still proceed — with the sole candidate running against a **kotak kosong** (empty box). This mechanism preserves voters' right to reject the only available candidate, offering a more democratic recourse than earlier rules which could have left villages without a functioning electoral choice.

## Preparations Underway

Pemkab Badung is urging village election committees to wrap up their administrative groundwork well ahead of the registration opening. All technical requirements and regulatory prerequisites must be in place before July 20 to ensure a smooth and compliant process.

Security arrangements are also being coordinated to maintain a peaceful atmosphere throughout all stages — from candidate registration through to the November vote count.

## The Role of the Perbekel in Bali

The position of perbekel — the administrative village head — carries significant weight in Balinese society. A perbekel manages village finances (including the national village fund allocation, which has grown considerably in recent years), coordinates with the traditional Balinese village (*desa pakraman*) on adat (customary) matters, and serves as the primary point of contact between central government programmes and the local community.

For the residents of Sobangan, Baha, and Munggu, this election is an opportunity to choose a leader who will shape their village's direction for the next six years. With registration just weeks away, community engagement in the process is expected to grow rapidly.
