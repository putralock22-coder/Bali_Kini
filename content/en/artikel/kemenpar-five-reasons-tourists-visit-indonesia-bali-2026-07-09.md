Indonesia's Ministry of Tourism (Kemenpar) has released key findings from an international survey covering **15.6 million foreign visitors** to the country. The results identify five primary factors that motivate international tourists to choose Indonesia — particularly Bali — as their holiday destination. These findings are now shaping the national tourism promotion strategy for the coming years.

## The Five Top Draws for International Visitors

### 1. Culture: The Number One Reason

Perhaps surprisingly, **culture — not natural scenery** — tops the list of reasons foreign tourists visit Indonesia. Traditional ceremonies, religious rituals, performing arts, temple architecture, and local craftsmanship are among the top things international visitors want to experience firsthand.

Bali serves as the strongest example of this cultural magnetism. The island's thousands of annual Hindu ceremonies, its living artistic traditions, the Pesta Kesenian Bali (Bali Arts Festival), and the deeply spiritual rhythm of daily life make Bali distinct from anywhere else in Southeast Asia.

### 2. Culinary Heritage

Indonesian cuisine ranks second. Dishes like nasi goreng, sate, babi guling (suckling pig), bebek betutu (slow-cooked duck), lawar, and countless regional specialties have built a global following. Tourists don't just want to eat Indonesian food — many actively seek out cooking classes and culinary tours to learn how to make it themselves.

Bali's food scene, which ranges from humble warungs to award-winning fine dining restaurants, further cements the island's position as a world-class culinary destination.

### 3. Quality Tourism Events

Tourism events rank as the third-biggest draw. Cultural festivals and international sporting events have proven effective at generating "destination-specific" travel — visitors who come *because* of a particular event.

Bali has an exceptionally rich events calendar: the PKB XLVIII (Bali Arts Festival), Penglipuran Village Festival, Jatiluwih Festival, international concerts, surfing competitions, and more. The Bali Provincial Government has programmed **56 events** in the 2026 Calendar of Events (CoE) to ensure the island remains vibrant year-round.

### 4. Effective Destination Promotion

Strategic and well-targeted promotion is the fourth factor. Kemenpar currently focuses on **15 key international markets**, including Australia, China, India, the United States, and several European nations. Digital content campaigns, collaboration with international travel media, and presence at global tourism fairs all contribute to keeping Indonesia top-of-mind for potential visitors.

### 5. Natural Beauty

Natural beauty comes in fifth — still irreplaceable, but no longer the dominant motivator it was once assumed to be. Bali's beaches, Jatiluwih rice terraces, Mount Agung, and Ubud's lush rainforests continue to captivate visitors from around the world. Combined with the four factors above, however, nature alone is no longer sufficient to compete in today's global tourism market.

## Kemenpar's Strategy: Empower Regional Events

Based on these findings, Kemenpar is doubling down on **strengthening regional events** as a core tourism attraction strategy. Events with strong local identity and authentic cultural roots are seen as the most powerful organic marketing tools in the social media era — when visitors document and share their experiences, the reach extends far beyond traditional advertising.

The ministry is also actively encouraging destinations outside of Bali to develop compelling event offerings, with the long-term goal of distributing international visitors more evenly across the Indonesian archipelago.

## Bali Remains Indonesia's Tourism Powerhouse

Taken together, the data confirms what many already suspected: Bali continues to outperform every other Indonesian destination in terms of international visitor arrivals. The island's unique ability to deliver world-class culture, cuisine, events, and natural beauty — all in one place — gives it a competitive edge that is genuinely difficult to replicate.

From January to May 2026, international arrivals through Ngurah Rai Airport grew significantly compared to the same period last year, reaffirming Bali's position as TripAdvisor's Best Destination in the World for 2026.
