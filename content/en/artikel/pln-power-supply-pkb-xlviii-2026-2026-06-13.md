PT PLN (Persero) Unit Pelaksana Pengatur Distribusi (UP2D) Bali has confirmed the readiness of the island's electrical distribution system to support the smooth running of the 48th Pesta Kesenian Bali (PKB XLVIII 2026), Indonesia's largest cultural festival, which runs from June 13 to July 11, 2026 across 16 venues in Denpasar.

## Comprehensive Distribution Security Scheme

PLN UP2D Bali Manager **Petrus Irwan Ichwansaputra** stated that the company has prepared a comprehensive distribution security scheme with a reliable network configuration and network maneuver scenarios to handle potential disruptions in any part of the system. This ensures that power supply to PKB venues can be quickly restored without interrupting ongoing activities.

Through intensive network security measures and distribution monitoring, PLN is ensuring that both local communities and tourists can enjoy the entire series of cultural events safely and comfortably throughout the festival.

## 24-Hour Distribution Control

As the unit operating Bali's **Distribution Control Center (DCC)**, PLN UP2D conducts round-the-clock monitoring of the distribution system for the full duration of PKB. Field personnel are stationed at strategic points to rapidly respond to any potential disruptions.

This is critical given that PKB XLVIII 2026 involves over 20,000 artists and is expected to draw tens of thousands of visitors daily. Reliable electricity underpins everything from large-scale stage performances to UMKM market stalls and public facilities.

## Power for 16 Venues

The festival spans **16 venues** centered on the Taman Budaya Art Center complex. PLN's power supply scope covers stage lighting systems, sound equipment, the Kandarupa exhibition area, UMKM booths, and general visitor facilities.

PLN has committed to zero blackouts during PKB 2026, recognizing the festival's status as a nationally and internationally watched world-class cultural event.

## Early Coordination with PKB Organizing Committee

PLN UP2D Bali coordinated with the Bali Provincial Culture Office and the PKB 2026 organizing committee well in advance of the festival's opening, mapping power requirements at each venue, identifying peak loads, and preparing emergency backup capacity if needed.

This proactive approach is part of PLN's broader commitment to supporting major national events while delivering a top-quality experience for the hundreds of thousands of attendees expected over the festival's month-long run.
