After months of intense preparation, **Bali Arts Festival XLVIII (Pesta Kesenian Bali XLVIII)** is set to open its doors tomorrow, **Saturday, 13 June 2026** — launching a month-long celebration of art, culture, and spirituality that will draw over 20,000 artists and performers to the heart of Denpasar.

## Theme: The Glorification of the Soul

PKB XLVIII carries the theme **"Atma Kerthi: Jiwa Sidha Parisudha"** — a Sanskrit-rooted phrase that translates roughly to *the glorification of the soul and the perfection of the human spirit*. The theme aligns with Bali's overarching development philosophy, **Nangun Sat Kerthi Loka Bali**, which places the harmony of people, nature, and culture at the centre of governance.

For this year's festival, the theme invites artists and audiences alike to reflect on the deeper purpose of art: not mere entertainment, but a vehicle for spiritual elevation and the affirmation of shared human values.

## Opening Ceremony: Peed Aya at Bajra Sandhi

The festival kicks off on Saturday afternoon with **Peed Aya** — a grand cultural procession — beginning at **3:00 PM WITA** in front of the **Bajra Sandhi Monument (Monumen Perjuangan Rakyat Bali)**, Niti Mandala, Renon, Denpasar.

Peed Aya is arguably the most spectacular element of PKB's opening: thousands of dancers, musicians, and performers in elaborate traditional costumes parade along the boulevard in front of the monument, showcasing the artistic wealth of all nine regencies and cities in Bali, alongside delegations from outside the province and international guests.

### ISI Bali's Contribution: Mahamredangga Kalpa

The **Institut Seni Indonesia (ISI) Bali** — Bali's premier arts university — has been assigned to anchor this year's Peed Aya with its creation titled **"Mahamredangga Kalpa"**. The performance is a large-scale choreographic composition incorporating hundreds of dancers, traditional percussion, and intricately designed costumes. ISI Bali has been rehearsing intensively in recent weeks to ensure a flawless execution of this centrepiece work.

## Evening Gala: Sendratari Lubdaka

Following the afternoon parade, the opening night continues with a **Sendratari (dramatic dance) performance** at the main stage of **Taman Werdhi Budaya Art Centre** in Denpasar. ISI Bali will present the story of **Lubdaka** — a classical Balinese tale of a hunter who, through an accidental act of devotion, achieves spiritual liberation.

The Lubdaka story resonates powerfully with this year's theme: it is ultimately a narrative about the soul's journey toward purity and perfection, making it an ideal artistic centrepiece for a festival dedicated to the glorification of the human spirit.

## 16 Venues, Free Admission, One Month

PKB XLVIII runs from **13 June to 11 July 2026** — nearly four weeks of daily performances across **16 different venues** in and around Denpasar. All events are **free and open to the public**, continuing the festival's tradition of making Bali's highest art forms accessible to everyone regardless of income.

The main hub remains **Taman Werdhi Budaya (Art Centre)**, but the expansion to 16 venues reflects the sheer scale of this year's programme. Performances will cover an extraordinary range of forms: sacred temple arts, classical dance, gamelan compositions, contemporary works, and experimental cross-cultural collaborations with artists from abroad.

## President Prabowo Invited to Open the Festival

Governor Wayan Koster has formally extended an invitation to **President Prabowo Subianto** to personally open PKB XLVIII. The Bali Cultural Affairs Office has been coordinating closely with the Presidential Palace to confirm his attendance — a detail that, if confirmed, would add significant national weight to the occasion.

Whether or not the President attends, PKB XLVIII is already shaping up to be a landmark edition of what is widely regarded as one of Southeast Asia's most important cultural festivals — a living testament to Bali's extraordinary artistic heritage and its enduring commitment to preserving it for future generations.
