Bank Indonesia's (BI) Bali Representative Office has taken a strategic step toward promoting more balanced economic growth across the island. By mapping tourism clusters according to each region's natural strengths, BI aims to reduce the widening gap between Bali's overcrowded southern areas and its largely untapped northern, eastern, and western regions.

## Southern Bali Has Reached Its Limits

For decades, tourism development in Bali has been overwhelmingly concentrated in the south—Badung, Denpasar, and parts of Gianyar. This has resulted in over capacity in infrastructure, water resources, and accommodation. BI Bali believes this lopsided pattern must change so that economic benefits from tourism can be shared more equitably across the island.

## Three Regions, Three Distinct Tourism Identities

### West Bali: Maritime Tourism and Marine Ecosystems

Jembrana Regency in western Bali is seen as a prime candidate for maritime tourism development. Its relatively pristine marine ecosystems make it ideal for snorkeling, diving, and eco-conscious marine conservation experiences that increasingly attract environmentally aware travelers.

### North Bali: Wellness and Agro-Spiritual Tourism

Buleleng Regency in the north is mapped for wellness tourism and agro-spiritual retreats. Its cooler climate, tranquil natural settings, and natural hot springs offer a compelling competitive advantage over the bustling south, appealing to travelers seeking rest, rejuvenation, and spiritual experiences.

### Klungkung-Bangli: Cultural Heritage and Culinary Tourism

The eastern regencies of Klungkung and Bangli are directed toward heritage and culinary tourism. Rich in age-old adat traditions and distinctive local cuisines, these areas hold enormous untapped potential for international visitors seeking authentic Balinese cultural encounters.

## Tourism: The Backbone of Bali's Economy

Bali's Tourism Office reports that the tourism sector contributed 66 percent to Bali's economic growth based on 2024 data. At the national level, Bali generated Rp176 trillion in foreign exchange earnings—equivalent to 55 percent of Indonesia's total tourism-related foreign exchange of Rp319.9 trillion.

International tourist arrivals also continue to climb. In 2025, Bali welcomed 7.05 million foreign visitors, up significantly from 6.33 million in 2024.

## Diversification as the Key to Sustainability

The tourism cluster mapping initiative is part of a broader, long-term strategy to diversify Bali's tourism products and destinations. By distributing visitor flows more evenly across the island, planners hope to ease pressure on southern Bali while unlocking real economic opportunities for communities in regions that have long been left behind by the tourism boom.

BI Bali is also pushing for improved infrastructure and human resource capacity-building in all three clusters, ensuring they are ready to host visitors in a sustainable, responsible, and community-benefiting manner.
