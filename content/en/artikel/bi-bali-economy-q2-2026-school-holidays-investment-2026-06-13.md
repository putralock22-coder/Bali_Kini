Following a robust performance in the first quarter, Bali's economy is expected to maintain strong positive momentum through **Q2-2026**. **Bank Indonesia's Bali Representative Office** has projected continued growth driven by a favorable convergence of peak tourism season, agricultural harvests, religious celebrations, and significant government investment projects.

## Strong Foundation from Q1

The first quarter provided a solid base to build on. According to the **Bali Provincial Statistics Agency (BPS)**, Bali's economy grew by **5.58 percent** in Q1-2026 — a commendable result achieved against a backdrop of ongoing global geopolitical tensions that have constrained growth in many economies around the world.

This resilience reflects Bali's structural strengths: a deep and diversified tourism sector, strong domestic consumption, and an agricultural base that continues to provide stability.

## Three Engines Driving Q2 Growth

Bank Indonesia identifies three primary factors set to power Bali's economy through April–June 2026:

### Peak Tourism and School Holidays

June and July represent **peak international tourism season** in Bali. Visitors from India are particularly prominent during this period, while Indonesia's national school holiday break simultaneously delivers a surge of domestic tourists to the island's beaches, cultural sites, and mountain resorts.

The convergence of international peak season and domestic holiday traffic creates one of the most commercially significant months on Bali's tourism calendar.

### Rice and Horticulture Harvest

The agricultural sector is adding meaningful momentum. **Rice and horticulture harvests** across multiple Bali regencies are boosting farmer incomes and stimulating trade activity at local markets. The increased agricultural output also has positive knock-on effects for the food processing industry and local supply chains.

### Galungan and Kuningan Religious Celebrations

Bali's sacred calendar is contributing a powerful consumption boost. **Galungan (June 17, 2026)** and **Kuningan (June 27, 2026)** — two of the most important Hindu celebrations in the Balinese year — drive significant household spending on ceremonial supplies, traditional food ingredients, adat materials, and ritual preparations. Local markets and artisan communities see some of their strongest sales of the year in the lead-up to these celebrations.

## Rp788 Billion in Priority Infrastructure

Beyond the seasonal drivers, government investment projects provide a robust structural underpinning to Q2 growth. Bali has earmarked several **priority infrastructure projects** in 2026 with a combined estimated value of **Rp788 billion**:

- **Pura Ulun Danu Batur parking facility** in Kintamani — supporting visitor management at one of Bali's most sacred and visited temples
- **Tukad Unda Reservoir water supply system** — ensuring reliable clean water for agricultural and household use
- **Nusa Ceningan–Nusa Lembongan bridge** — connecting two rapidly growing tourist island destinations
- **Jimbaran underpass** — easing chronic traffic congestion in one of Bali's busiest southern corridors

These projects stimulate the construction sector, generate employment across skill levels, and strengthen the infrastructure backbone that makes Bali competitive as a global tourism destination over the long term.

## Risks Remain on the Horizon

Despite the positive trajectory, Bank Indonesia flags several risks requiring careful monitoring. Ongoing global geopolitical uncertainty, rupiah exchange rate volatility, and potential demand slowdowns in key tourist-origin markets could affect the tourism sector unexpectedly.

Sustained coordination between the provincial government, the business community, and local communities will be essential to translate Q2's considerable growth potential into broad-based economic benefits for all layers of Balinese society.
