## A Small Village With a National-Award Winning Story

Far removed from Bali's bustling southern resort corridor, **Desa Les** in Buleleng Regency, North Bali, has quietly built one of the island's most compelling community tourism success stories. This unassuming coastal village has demonstrated that economic development and environmental preservation are not competing goals — they can reinforce each other, creating lasting prosperity without destroying the very things that make a place worth visiting.

The proof is in the accolades: Desa Les took home the **Grand Prize at the Indonesian Tourism Village Award (ADWI) 2024**, the nation's highest recognition for village-level tourism excellence, awarded by the Ministry of Tourism of Indonesia.

## A Village Blessed with Diverse Natural Riches

Desa Les occupies a unique geographic position that spans multiple ecological zones. The village stretches from fertile green hillsides and agricultural land in the interior to a productive coastal stretch where fishing communities and traditional **salt producers** have sustained their livelihoods for generations.

This natural diversity has been thoughtfully developed into a range of visitor experiences:

- **Coastal and snorkeling tourism** in North Bali's clear, relatively uncrowded waters
- **Waterfall and trekking experiences** in the verdant inland hills
- **Traditional salt-making tours** — a rare opportunity to witness an ancient artisanal practice still alive in Bali's north
- **Community agrotourism** centered on organic and traditional farming
- **Village homestay programs** that immerse guests in the everyday rhythms of Balinese rural life

## The Desa Sejahtera Astra Program: Four Pillars of Change

The transformation of Desa Les accelerated markedly after the village joined the **Desa Sejahtera Astra (DSA)** program in 2024. The DSA program, backed by the Astra Group corporate foundation, takes a holistic approach to community development organized around four interconnected pillars:

1. **Education** — Tourism management training, language skills, and digital literacy for village youth
2. **Health** — Community health initiatives, sanitation improvements, and wellness programs
3. **Environment** — Waste management systems, reforestation efforts, and coastal ecosystem restoration
4. **Entrepreneurship** — UMKM development, market access expansion, and product branding support

Together, these four pillars have reached more than **800 Desa Les residents** — a substantial proportion of the village's total population.

## Tangible Economic Results

The program has not merely generated abstract improvements in community well-being. The economic impacts are concrete and measurable:

- **Average community income has risen by 25 percent** since program initiation
- **Dozens of new jobs** have been created in tourism, hospitality, and local enterprise
- The market reach of Desa Les locally-made products has expanded by **100 percent**, with village goods now finding buyers far beyond the immediate area
- Tourist arrivals to Desa Les have grown consistently year on year

These figures position Desa Les as a model for rural tourism development in Bali — particularly for villages outside the island's heavily trafficked southern zone.

## Cultural Identity at the Core

What distinguishes Desa Les from many other tourism villages is its unwavering commitment to **preserving authentic local identity**. The community has resisted the temptation to package its culture as mere spectacle for tourist consumption. Instead, traditions, language, customs, and ways of life remain living practices that tourists are invited to observe and, where appropriate, participate in.

This authenticity is arguably Desa Les's greatest competitive advantage. Visitors who make the journey to North Bali don't find a curated tourism product — they encounter a genuinely functioning community, with all the texture, color, and depth that entails.

## A Model for Bali's Tourism Future

The story of Desa Les arrives at a pivotal moment in Bali's tourism evolution. The island is increasingly grappling with the consequences of concentrated, mass tourism in its southern areas — traffic congestion, environmental stress, cultural commodification, and economic benefits that largely bypass local communities.

Desa Les offers an alternative blueprint: distribute tourism more widely across the island, anchor it in community ownership, and ensure that the natural and cultural assets driving visitor interest are actively protected — not gradually depleted.

Both the Bali Provincial Government and national tourism authorities have pointed to Desa Les as a reference case, encouraging other villages across the island to explore similar community-driven development models.
