Bali has once again earned the trust of the international sports community. The **12th Asian Open Water Swimming Championship 2026** is officially underway from **June 13–15, 2026** in the waters off **InterContinental Bali Resort in Jimbaran Bay, Badung Regency**. The event brings together **78 swimmers from 18 Asian nations**, making it one of the largest open water swimming competitions ever hosted in Indonesia.

Including officials and support staff, a total of **127 people** from across Asia are participating in the championship, underscoring the event's true international scale.

## Indonesia Fields Six Athletes

As the host nation, Indonesia is not just organizing the event — it is competing in it. The national team sent **six athletes** — four men and two women — to face off against Asia's best open water swimmers on their home waters.

Competing on familiar turf carries psychological advantages. The Indonesian contingent heads into the championship with home crowd support and intimate knowledge of Jimbaran's conditions, which could prove decisive in the grueling distances ahead.

One last-minute addition elevated the participation count: **Bahrain** sent one athlete and two officials shortly before the event, bringing the total to **18 competing nations**, up from the original 17.

## Three Distances, Three Challenges

Athletes will compete across three disciplines that test endurance, strategy, and raw speed in open water:

- **5 kilometers** — individual events for men and women
- **10 kilometers** — the marathon of open water swimming, widely regarded as the discipline's pinnacle
- **4×1.5-kilometer relay** — a team event demanding coordination as much as individual strength

Open water swimming bears little resemblance to pool racing. Athletes must navigate natural variables — currents, chop, water temperature, and visibility — that make every race unpredictable and every finish hard-earned.

## Jimbaran Bay: Where Sport Meets Paradise

The selection of Jimbaran Bay as the competition venue speaks for itself. Known for its calm, clear waters framed by green hills on one side and the Indian Ocean on the other, Jimbaran offers near-ideal conditions for open water competition while delivering a backdrop of extraordinary natural beauty.

Athletes from across Asia have voiced their admiration for Jimbaran. For many, the championship is as much a journey to an iconic Balinese destination as it is a sporting event. Several participants have already extended their stays to explore more of the island — directly benefiting local businesses and the broader Balinese tourism economy.

## Sport Tourism: A Government Priority

This championship fits squarely within **Indonesia's Ministry of Tourism's strategic shift** toward positioning **sport tourism** as a core pillar of national tourism development.

Unlike conventional leisure tourism, sport events generate a multiplier effect: athletes, officials, families, media crews, and supporters all require accommodation, transport, food, and other services. The financial flows reach hotels, restaurants, taxis, and small businesses alike.

Alongside the main Asian championship, the event also hosts the **A Stream OWS Series 2026** — an open entry series welcoming amateur swimmers, sports communities, and the general public to compete in the same iconic waters. Around **300 additional participants** are expected to join this parallel event.

## Cementing Bali as a World-Class Sports Destination

Hosting the Asian Open Water Swimming Championship adds another credential to Bali's growing resume as an international sporting venue. Beyond beaches and temples, Bali is increasingly recognized as a place capable of organizing world-class events with the infrastructure, hospitality, and natural settings that athletes and event organizers demand.

Every successful international event is an investment — building reputation, broadening the tourism market, and proving that Bali's facilities can rise to any occasion.
