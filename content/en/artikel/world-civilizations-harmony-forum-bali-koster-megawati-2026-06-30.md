Bali once again took center stage as a venue for international civilizational dialogue. The **3rd World Civilizations Harmony Forum** was held at Hotel Renaissance Bali Uluwatu, Badung, on Monday, June 29, 2026. Under the theme *"Harmony in Diversity, Human Fraternity,"* the prestigious forum gathered world leaders, academics, religious figures, and cultural luminaries to explore solutions to pressing global challenges.

Bali Governor Wayan Koster delivered an address on behalf of Indonesia's fifth President, Megawati Soekarnoputri. Her message—channeled through Koster—drew immediate attention: a forceful call for humanity to reject the dominance of the *"law of the jungle"* in global affairs, where might overrides justice.

## Bali as a Model of Harmony

In her address, Megawati described Bali as *"a true reflection of harmony between nature, spirituality, culture, and community hospitality, representing Indonesian civilization on the world stage."* The choice of Bali as the forum's location was deliberate and deeply symbolic.

The island's governing philosophy of **Tri Hita Karana**—which teaches balance in three sacred relationships: between humans and God, among humans, and between humans and nature—was cited as directly relevant to the forum's theme of cross-civilizational harmony. For centuries, this philosophy has shaped how Balinese Hindu society organizes itself, and at this forum it was offered as a moral framework for building fairer international relationships.

## A Call for a Just World Civilization

The forum served as a strategic space to advance dialogue among nations at a time of acute global tensions—from armed conflicts and economic inequality to threats against fundamental human values.

Megawati's core message was unambiguous: building a just world civilization is not merely an idealistic aspiration, but an imperative for humanity's survival. *"Do not lead the world with the law of the jungle,"* she declared through Governor Koster. The statement resonated powerfully, arriving at a moment when powerful nations continue to exert disproportionate influence over international decision-making.

### Indonesia as a Bridge Between Civilizations

As the world's largest Muslim-majority nation and a keeper of profound multicultural traditions, Indonesia occupies a unique position in global affairs. Through Bali, it offers a living demonstration that diversity is not a source of division but of strength. The forum reaffirmed Bali's role as a bridge between nations and civilizations—a place where differences are celebrated rather than contested.

## The 3rd World Civilizations Harmony Forum

This was the third edition of the annual forum. By selecting Bali as its venue—a place internationally recognized as both a tourism destination and a center of world culture—the event drew significant attention from the global community. Delegates from numerous countries gathered not only to deliberate, but to experience firsthand Bali's atmosphere of harmony and spiritual depth.

The hosting of such forums adds another dimension to Bali's growing reputation as a world-class MICE destination (Meetings, Incentives, Conferences, and Exhibitions). Alongside its vibrant cultural tourism sector, Bali is increasingly positioning itself not merely as a holiday paradise, but as a venue for serious intellectual and diplomatic engagement that shapes global discourse.

The forum's success reinforces the trust placed in Bali by the international community and aligns with the Bali Provincial Government's vision of establishing the island as a global center of culture, spirituality, and intercultural understanding.
