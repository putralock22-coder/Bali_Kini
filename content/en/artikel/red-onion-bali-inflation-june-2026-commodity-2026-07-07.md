## Bali June 2026 Inflation: Food and Fuel Drive Price Rises

Bali's Statistics Agency (BPS) has published inflation data for June 2026, showing the province recorded year-on-year inflation of 3.27 percent. The Consumer Price Index (CPI) rose from 109.71 in June 2025 to 113.30 in June 2026, reflecting sustained pressure from both food commodities and fuel prices.

Despite the uptick, Bank Indonesia's Bali representative confirmed that the figure remains within the national inflation target range of 2.5 ± 1 percent. Bali's inflation also came in slightly below the national average of 3.34 percent, demonstrating relatively effective price management in the province.

## Red Onions Emerge as a Key Price Pressure

Among the many commodities that pushed prices higher in June, red onions (bawang merah) stood out as one of the most significant contributors. The commodity recorded a monthly price increase of 14.38 percent, contributing 0.10 percentage points to overall inflation.

The price surge in red onions is linked to limited supply from key production regions outside Bali, at a time when demand from the culinary sector and households has been elevated due to school holidays and a busy calendar of cultural festivals throughout June 2026.

### Other Notable Commodity Drivers

In addition to red onions, several other commodities pushed inflation higher:

- **Petrol (gasoline)** — the most dominant contributor following a government fuel price adjustment that effectively displaced pork as the leading inflationary force, a role pork typically plays during Balinese religious holidays like Galungan
- **Garlic (bawang putih)** — experienced sharp price increases alongside red onions
- **Carrots and green beans** — fresh vegetables with restricted supply from highland farming areas
- **Gold jewelry** — continued rising in line with elevated global gold prices
- **Cooking oil** — prices remain volatile at traditional markets
- **Rent and tap water (PAM) tariffs** — service-sector components adding to cost-of-living pressures

## Fuel Price Adjustment Reshapes Inflation Landscape

A notable development in June 2026 is how the government's fuel price adjustment displaced the typical seasonal pattern. In previous years, pork prices would surge dramatically during the Galungan and Kuningan religious celebrations — a period that fell in May and June 2026 — making it the top inflationary commodity.

This year, however, the fuel price hike sent ripple effects across the economy: transportation costs rose, distribution chains became more expensive, and the price of most perishable goods followed suit. The result was a structural shift in Bali's inflation composition.

## Factors That Eased Price Pressure

Not everything moved upward. The government's Free Nutritious Meals (MBG) program had a measurable dampening effect on demand for chicken and eggs from certain institutional consumers, helping keep prices of those staples stable.

Additionally, the shift in household spending toward tourism and entertainment during the school holiday period — rather than food purchases — helped moderate demand for several categories of food products.

## Bank Indonesia Maintains Cautious Watch

The Head of Bank Indonesia's Bali representative office acknowledged the price pressures while maintaining an overall positive outlook. The Regional Inflation Control Team (TPID), which coordinates between Bank Indonesia and local government agencies, will continue monitoring commodity prices and supply chains throughout the second half of 2026.

The central bank is particularly focused on managing risks from external supply disruptions and potential further fuel price adjustments that could push headline inflation beyond the target range.
