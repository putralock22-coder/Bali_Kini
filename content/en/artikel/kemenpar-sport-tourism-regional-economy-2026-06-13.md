Indonesia's Ministry of Tourism has formally elevated sports tourism — **sport tourism** — to a top government priority for stimulating regional economies across the country, including Bali. The policy announcement coincides with the **Asian Open Water Swimming Championship (AOWSC) 2026**, held at InterContinental Beach, Jimbaran Bay, June 13–15, 2026.

## Real Impact on Local MSMEs

Deputy Assistant for National Events at the Ministry of Tourism, **Ni Komang Ayu Astiti**, explained that sport tourism effectively mobilizes the economy for local communities, including micro, small, and medium enterprises (MSMEs). Sports events combined with tourism play a critical role in attracting high-quality foreign tourists who spend more during their visits.

"The quality of tourists is reflected in their ability to spend on accommodation, transportation, food and beverages," said Ni Komang Ayu Astiti.

Sport tourism is also closely linked to spa, health, and wellness services, meaning it not only boosts tourist spending but also expands opportunities in the local labor market at tourism destinations.

## Asian Open Water Swimming Championship 2026 in Jimbaran

As a flagship example of this policy in action, Bali is hosting the **Asian Open Water Swimming Championship 2026** at Jimbaran Bay. The event draws **78 athletes** and **49 officials** from **18 countries** across Asia.

Beyond the elite competition, the event also includes the **A Stream OWS Series** for beginner-level swimmers, attracting approximately **300 participants** from sports communities and the general public. This combination of professional competition and public participation amplifies the economic impact on local businesses throughout the Jimbaran area.

## Joint Strategy from Two Ministries

The Ministry of Youth and Sports (Kemenpora) and the Ministry of Tourism are working together to strengthen Indonesia's national and international sports event calendar for 2026. Their collaboration is projected as one of the main drivers of economic growth, particularly in tourism destinations with natural potential and sports infrastructure.

## Bali's Edge as a Sport Tourism Hub

Bali holds a natural advantage as a sport tourism destination thanks to its world-class beaches, year-round tropical climate, and mature tourism infrastructure. International events ranging from triathlons and marathons to surfing competitions and open water swimming continue to cement Bali's position as Southeast Asia's leading sport tourism hub.

By making sport tourism a national priority, the government aims to attract visitors with higher spending profiles while diversifying local economic income streams beyond conventional mass tourism.
