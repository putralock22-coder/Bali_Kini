Bali faced a multi-front natural disaster emergency on Thursday evening (June 12, 2026). Extreme weather struck several regions of the island almost simultaneously — flash floods inundated residential areas in Buleleng Regency with fatal consequences, while a whirlwind tore through a densely populated area of West Denpasar. The Bali Provincial Disaster Management Agency (BPBD) issued a situational report at 6:00 PM local time (WITA).

## Fatal Flash Flood in Baktiseraga

The deadliest incident unfolded in Baktiseraga Village, Buleleng District, Buleleng Regency. A torrential surge of floodwater swept through the BTN Geriya Mahadewa housing complex, Block A4, in Banjar Dinas Tista, sweeping away a local resident in the current.

A joint search-and-rescue team consisting of BPBD personnel, SAR units, police, military, and volunteers was immediately deployed to the area. After an intensive search operation, the missing resident — identified as **Ricardo Razaq Alghivier** — was found deceased, having been carried away by the floodwaters. A second resident, **Nyoman Gede Suara**, was pulled from the scene with injuries and received medical treatment.

### Floods Spread Across Denpasar and Badung

The flooding was not confined to Buleleng. Reports of inundation emerged from Denpasar and Badung as well, with multiple neighborhoods reporting standing water, fallen trees, and localized landslides at various points across the island. BPBD noted that disaster incidents were recorded simultaneously across multiple regencies, underscoring the widespread and severe nature of the weather event that struck Bali on that evening.

## Whirlwind Strikes West Denpasar

Compounding the flood crisis, a whirlwind touched down in the area of Imam Bonjol Street, Pemecutan Klod Village, West Denpasar District, Denpasar City. The incident occurred during roughly the same timeframe as the flooding in Buleleng. While no fatalities were reported from the whirlwind, structural damage to buildings and surrounding infrastructure was noted by local authorities who rushed to assess the aftermath.

## Emergency Response and Coordination

The Head of BPBD Bali Province coordinated emergency response teams across the affected areas. Evacuation, damage assessment, and victim assistance operations ran through the night. The coordinated response drew on resources from government agencies, security forces, and community volunteer groups working together to address the scale of the emergency.

Authorities confirmed that despite the chaos, Bali's key transport infrastructure remained operational. Road access to Ngurah Rai International Airport was temporarily slowed by flooding, causing some passengers to arrive late, but no flights were cancelled or significantly delayed as the airport itself remained unaffected.

### Prior BMKG Warning and Safety Advice

The Meteorology, Climatology and Geophysics Agency (BMKG) had issued an advance alert warning of potential heavy rain in several Bali regions, including Badung, Denpasar, Gianyar, and Klungkung. Residents are advised to stay alert to BMKG forecasts and avoid activity near river channels or landslide-prone slopes during periods of adverse weather.

The cascading disasters of June 12 serve as a stark reminder of Bali's vulnerability to extreme weather events during seasonal weather transitions — and of the critical importance of disaster preparedness at both the community and government level.
