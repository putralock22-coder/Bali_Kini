**Badung** — Bank Indonesia's (BI) Bali Representative Office leveraged the Bali Jagadhita VII 2026 event as a strategic investment promotion platform, connecting 21 Bali and Nusa Tenggara project owners with six foreign ambassadors and 35 potential investors. The event ran from June 5 to 7, 2026, at Bali Galeria Mall in Kuta, Badung.

The centerpiece of the investment pitch was the Kawasan Ekonomi Khusus (KEK) Sanur — Indonesia's first and only Health Special Economic Zone, which began operations in 2025 and has been expanding rapidly throughout 2026.

## KEK Sanur: Where Healthcare Meets Premium Tourism

KEK Sanur is designed as an integrated zone combining world-class healthcare services with premium tourism offerings. Spread across 41.26 hectares in the coastal Sanur area of Denpasar, the zone will feature hospitals, specialist clinics, luxury hotels, a MICE center, an ethnomedicinal botanic garden, and a modern commercial complex.

The planned total investment value stands at Rp10.2 trillion, targeting the creation of 43,647 jobs when fully operational. By Q1 2026, realized investment had already reached Rp5.37 trillion with 5,444 workers employed — progress rated as highly positive by Indonesia's Coordinating Ministry of Economic Affairs.

## Bali International Hospital Has Served 14,950 Patients

KEK Sanur's flagship healthcare facility, Bali International Hospital (BIH), has been fully operational since April 2025. By Q1 2026, the hospital had already served 14,950 patients, with a breakdown of 60 percent foreign nationals and 40 percent Indonesian citizens — demonstrating strong demand from the medical tourism market.

In 2026, KEK Sanur will also welcome the opening of The Solitaire Clinic, offering cosmetic surgery, medical aesthetics, and body contouring services — segments in exceptionally high demand from medical tourists from Europe, Australia, and East Asia.

## Special Pathways for Foreign Medical Professionals

To uphold international service standards, the Indonesian government has opened a dedicated pathway for foreign medical professionals to practice within KEK Sanur. Practice permits (SIP) for foreign doctors and medical staff are valid for five years and renewable.

Additionally, hospitals within KEK Sanur are permitted to access medications not yet approved by Indonesia's National Drug Agency (BPOM) through a Special Access Scheme (SAS) — allowing KEK Sanur facilities to offer cutting-edge treatments unavailable elsewhere in the country.

## Six Ambassadors Respond Positively

The six foreign ambassadors present at the dedicated investment session during Bali Jagadhita responded positively to the opportunities presented. Bank Indonesia's Bali office laid out strategic investment projects spanning tourism, infrastructure, and healthcare — all seeking international investment partners.

Governor Wayan Koster has previously emphasized that Bali is positioning itself as an international financial and investment hub, not merely a tourism destination. Plans are even underway to develop Bali as an International Financial Center capable of competing with Singapore and Hong Kong in the longer term.

With KEK Sanur as the locomotive, the government is confident that Bali will increasingly feature on the global investment map for health and premium tourism — delivering a dual benefit of foreign exchange earnings and access to world-class healthcare services for Indonesian citizens.
