Karangasem Festival 2026 officially opened today, Friday (19/6/2026), at Taman Budaya Candra Bhuana in Amlapura, East Bali. The four-day annual cultural festival, running through June 22, is held in celebration of the **386th anniversary of Amlapura City** and serves as a major showcase for the arts and cultural heritage of Karangasem Regency.

## Theme and Meaning

This year's festival carries the theme **"Tidak Mudah, Tapi Harus Bisa"** — translated as *"Not Easy, But We Must Be Able To"* — a rallying message that reflects Karangasem's collective spirit to keep building the region despite ongoing challenges. The theme underscores the need for collaboration and determination from all segments of society.

The main stage concept draws inspiration from **Nakula-Sadewa**, two complementary warrior figures from the Mahabharata epic, symbolizing balanced leadership between the region's administrators and its people.

A special festival jingle titled **"Mentari Timur Menari"** (*"The Eastern Sun is Dancing"*) has been composed to represent the spirit of unity and optimism for Bali's eastern heartland.

## 1,200 Local Artists Take the Stage

The centerpiece of Karangasem Festival 2026 is a grand **cultural parade** featuring approximately **1,200 local artists** from traditional villages across Karangasem. The parade showcases the authentic arts of East Bali, including:

- **Legong Keraton** — a refined and sacred court dance
- **Joged Bungbung** — a lively social dance accompanied by bamboo percussion
- Various traditional rituals and customary ceremonies unique to Karangasem

These performances offer visitors a rare, immersive window into living cultural traditions that have been preserved across generations.

## National Artist Headlines the Event

Beyond traditional arts, the festival also features a performance by national artist **Donnie Sibarani**, former lead vocalist of the beloved Indonesian band Ada Band, who is scheduled to perform at the festival's closing night. His appearance is expected to attract visitors from outside Karangasem and beyond Bali.

## Competitions and Community Events

The festival hosts a wide range of competitive and community-based activities, including:

- **Tapel Ogoh-Ogoh Competition** — mask-making contest for elementary school, junior high school, and open categories
- Motorcycle community showcases
- Participation from the Amlapura Running Club
- **Jegeg Bagus Karangasem** pageant

This diversity of programming makes Karangasem Festival an inclusive event that engages all generations — from schoolchildren to adults.

## Boosting Karangasem Tourism

Karangasem Festival is one of the flagship events on the **Karangasem Calendar of Event 2026**, designed to drive visitor arrivals to this often-underexplored corner of Bali. Located close to major attractions including Pura Besakih (Bali's "Mother Temple"), Tirta Gangga Royal Water Palace, and Amed's black-sand beaches, the festival is positioned to stimulate local economic growth, particularly for SMEs and the hospitality sector.

Karangasem — long celebrated as "Bali at its most Balinese" — is steadily cementing its identity as an authentic cultural tourism destination with growing appeal for discerning travelers.
