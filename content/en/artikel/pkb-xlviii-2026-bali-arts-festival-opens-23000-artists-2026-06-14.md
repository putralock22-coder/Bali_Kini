Bali Governor Wayan Koster officially opened the 48th Pesta Kesenian Bali (PKB) — the Bali Arts Festival — on Saturday, June 13, at the Bajra Sandhi Monument area, Niti Mandala Renon, Denpasar. This annual celebration of Balinese culture carries the theme **"Atma Kerthi: Jiwa Sidha Parisudha"**, which translates as *Honoring the Perfect Soul* — a call to embrace art as a path toward spiritual completeness and the preservation of civilization.

## A Grand Cultural Parade Opens the Festival

The festival kicked off with a spectacular cultural procession (*peed aya*) that wound through the Renon area following the *purwa daksina* (clockwise) direction. The parade began at the intersection of Jl. Ir. H. Juanda and Jl. Raya Puputan, concluding in front of the Bali Regional Office of the Ministry of Finance.

The city of Denpasar alone contributed around **700 artists** to the procession, one of the largest delegations in the opening parade. Thousands of both domestic and international tourists lined the streets to witness the dazzling display of Balinese heritage.

## Over 23,000 Artists, 307 Cultural Groups

PKB XLVIII stands as one of the most expansive editions in the festival's history. More than **23,000 artists** will perform across the full run of the event, drawn from **307 sekehe** (performing groups), sanggar (studios), and yayasan (cultural foundations) — representing local Balinese, national, and international arts communities.

### 10 Main Activity Categories Across 16 Venues

All performances and exhibitions are organized into **10 main categories**, held across **16 venues** spread throughout Denpasar. These span traditional performing arts, craft exhibitions, literary arts, and competitive events that have been the backbone of the PKB since its founding.

## Free Participation for Local SMEs

A notable initiative this year sees **small and medium enterprises (SMEs and IKM)** offered free participation in PKB exhibitions. The policy is designed to boost the local creative economy and give Bali's artisans and small business owners greater access to an international stage through the festival.

## National Recognition: Karisma Event Nusantara

At the opening ceremony, PKB XLVIII 2026 was awarded the **Karisma Event Nusantara (KEN) 2026 Certificate**, officially placing it among Indonesia's top 125 premier national events. The certificate was presented directly by Deputy Minister of Tourism **Ni Luh Enik Ermawati** to Governor Koster, recognizing the PKB's cultural significance and nationwide impact.

Governor Koster underscored that PKB XLVIII is more than a festival — it is a declaration that **"Bali never tires of protecting its cultural civilization."** He also framed the event as a platform for artistic regeneration, encouraging young artists to perform alongside seasoned masters.

## Running Through July 11, 2026

The 48th Bali Arts Festival runs from **June 13 to July 11, 2026**, spanning nearly a full month of performances, competitions, workshops, and exhibitions across Denpasar. The public and visitors are welcome to attend free of charge, making it one of the most accessible cultural events in Southeast Asia.
