Indonesia's Meteorology, Climatology, and Geophysics Agency (BMKG) has issued a maritime weather advisory for the waters surrounding Bali on Tuesday, June 16, 2026 — a day that coincides with Penampahan Galungan, the day of preparation before the Hindu holy day. Wave heights in parts of southern Bali are forecast to reach **up to 3 meters**, raising safety concerns for marine tourism activities and local fishermen.

## Wave Forecast for Today

According to the BMKG maritime weather report issued by the Regional Office III in Denpasar, wave conditions are split sharply between Bali's northern and southern coasts:

- **Northern Bali Waters**: 0.5 – 1.0 meters (calm)
- **Southern Bali Waters**: 0.75 – 3.0 meters (potentially dangerous)
- **South Bali Strait, Badung Strait, South Lombok Strait**: waves exceeding 2 meters

This pattern is consistent with prevailing seasonal winds blowing across the Indian Ocean south of Bali, which is known for generating much larger swells than the calmer northern coast.

## Areas Most Affected

### Nusa Penida and Nusa Lembongan
Both islands — among Bali's most popular day-trip destinations — are accessed via crossings through the Badung Strait and southern waters currently under the advisory. Ferries from Sanur and Kusamba operate in these waters and may face rougher-than-usual conditions.

### West Buleleng Coastline
The western stretch of Buleleng regency, which faces the Bali Strait, may also experience elevated swell. Traditional fishing boats based along this coastline are advised to assess conditions before setting out.

### Uluwatu and Southern Surf Beaches
Surf spots along Bali's southern coast — including Uluwatu, Padang-Padang, and Balangan — will see more energetic conditions than usual. While this may appeal to experienced surfers, casual beachgoers and swimmers should exercise caution.

## Land Weather: Mostly Clear

On the bright side, conditions on Bali's mainland are forecast to be largely sunny to partly cloudy today. Only Bangli Regency is expected to see light rain during the afternoon hours. This favorable land weather is welcome news for Balinese families who are busy erecting penjor bamboo poles and preparing offerings (banten) ahead of tomorrow's Galungan celebration.

## Safety Advice from BMKG

The BMKG Regional Office III Denpasar has issued the following advisories:

1. **Tourists** planning snorkeling, diving, or island-hopping excursions should check the latest weather updates before departing.
2. **Fishermen and boat operators** should not force a voyage if sea conditions exceed the safe limit for their vessel type.
3. **Marine tourism operators** should proactively inform guests about today's conditions and offer flexibility in rescheduling if needed.

## Context: Penampahan During Peak Tourist Season

Today's Penampahan Galungan falls during one of Bali's busiest tourism periods. International visitors have increasingly chosen to time their trips around Galungan, drawn by the spectacle of towering penjor lining every village road and the deeply authentic spiritual atmosphere that pervades the island.

The wave advisory serves as a timely reminder that while Bali's inland and cultural offerings are safely accessible, those planning water-based excursions — particularly to Nusa Penida, one of Bali's fastest-growing attractions — should factor in current sea conditions before departure. Checking BMKG's official real-time forecasts is recommended for the most accurate information.
