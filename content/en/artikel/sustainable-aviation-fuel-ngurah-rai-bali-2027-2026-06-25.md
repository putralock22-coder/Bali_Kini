## Bali's Airport Among First to Go Green in 2027

Indonesia has officially announced it will implement Sustainable Aviation Fuel (SAF) beginning in 2027, in a landmark step toward decarbonizing the country's aviation sector. The announcement was made by Coordinating Minister for Infrastructure and Regional Development, Agus Harimurti Yudhoyono (AHY), on June 25, 2026.

I Gusti Ngurah Rai International Airport in Bali and Soekarno-Hatta International Airport in Tangerang have been designated as the first two airports where SAF will be introduced. Both airports were selected due to their status as Indonesia's busiest international aviation hubs.

## What Is SAF and Why Does It Matter for Bali?

Sustainable Aviation Fuel is jet fuel derived from renewable or waste organic sources rather than conventional fossil fuels. Across its full lifecycle, SAF can cut aviation carbon emissions by up to 80 percent compared to standard jet fuel — a significant reduction for an industry that accounts for roughly 2.5 percent of global CO₂ emissions.

For Bali, the policy carries particular strategic weight. Tourism — which contributes more than 40 percent to the island's regional GDP — depends almost entirely on international air connectivity. With millions of international passengers transiting through Ngurah Rai each year, SAF adoption positions Bali as a global tourism destination committed to environmental sustainability: a quality increasingly prioritized by today's environmentally conscious travelers.

## Starting Small: 1 Percent of International Flights

In the first phase of implementation in 2027, SAF will be blended into fuel for 1 percent of international departing flights from both Ngurah Rai and Soekarno-Hatta airports. While this figure may seem modest, the government has emphasized that it represents the beginning of a carefully staged rollout.

### SAF Implementation Roadmap

The government has outlined a phased expansion plan:
- **2027 (Phase 1):** 1% SAF mandate for international flights departing from Ngurah Rai and Soetta
- **Medium term:** Expansion to additional international routes
- **Long term:** Integration into domestic flight routes across Indonesia

All SAF used must comply with the sustainability and quality standards set by the International Civil Aviation Organization (ICAO), the United Nations body that governs global civil aviation standards.

## Building a Domestic SAF Supply Chain

AHY stressed that Indonesia's ambition goes beyond simply using imported SAF — the government intends to develop the entire SAF production chain domestically, from feedstock cultivation to refining and airport distribution. Pertamina Group has already signaled its commitment to SAF development as part of Indonesia's broader energy transition.

A domestic SAF industry is expected to generate new jobs in renewable energy and agriculture, particularly from non-food feedstocks such as certain palm products, sugarcane derivatives, and waste materials.

## What This Means for Bali Tourism

The introduction of SAF at Ngurah Rai may lead to modest increases in airline operating costs, which could potentially affect international airfare to Bali. However, the government remains optimistic that with the right incentives and economies of scale, cost increases can be kept at a level that does not meaningfully erode Bali's tourism competitiveness.

More broadly, the move reinforces Bali's positioning as a sustainable tourism destination — a brand increasingly valuable in a global travel market where green credentials influence destination choice.
