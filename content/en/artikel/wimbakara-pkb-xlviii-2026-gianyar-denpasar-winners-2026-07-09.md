The 48th Bali Arts Festival (Pesta Kesenian Bali / PKB XLVIII) reached its competitive climax on Wednesday, July 8, 2026, with the official announcement of Wimbakara winners — the prestigious arts competition that forms the heart of this month-long cultural celebration. Gianyar Regency emerged as the dominant force, while Denpasar City also claimed a top prize in one of the festival's most celebrated categories.

## Gianyar Captures Baleganjur Remaja Crown

In the coveted Wimbakara Baleganjur Remaja (Youth Baleganjur) category, Gianyar Regency secured the championship through the remarkable performance of Sekaa Gong Batur Mahaswara from Desa Batuan, Sukawati District. Their mastery of the dynamic gamelan percussion ensemble earned them the top position in one of PKB's most fiercely contested competitions.

Karangasem Regency's Sekaa Baleganjur Lengka Swara claimed second place, with Badung Regency's Sekaa Gong Cakradhara rounding out the podium in third. The tight competition among these three groups reflected the remarkably high standard of Balinese gamelan artistry among the younger generation.

## Denpasar Claims Taman Penasar Title

Denpasar City had reason to celebrate as well. Sekaa Taman Penasar Gambelan Sundaram from Ubung Kaja Village took first place in the Wimbakara Taman Penasar category, with Gianyar's Sanggar Cudamani finishing as runner-up. The performance of these two regions across multiple categories underlines their status as Bali's leading centers for traditional performing arts.

## Legendary Comedy Trio Hosts the Announcement

The Wimbakara results announcement was broadcast live and hosted by Clekontong Mas, Bali's legendary comedy trio. Their lively presentation added a festive energy to the proceedings, keeping thousands of artists and fans engaged throughout the reveal.

### A Festival of 20,000 Artists

PKB XLVIII, which runs from June 13 to July 11, 2026, has brought together an extraordinary 20,929 artists from 673 sekaa (arts communities) across Bali. The festival carries the theme **Atma Kerthi: Jiwa Sidha Parisudha** — a philosophical concept meaning the purification and glorification of the human soul toward perfection.

Throughout its nearly month-long run, the Art Centre Denpasar has hosted a rich tapestry of programs including Peed Aya (Opening Parade), Kandarupa (Exhibitions), Rekasadana (Performances), Kriyaloka (Workshops), Jantra Tradisi Bali (Traditional Arts Week), and the Bali World Culture Celebration (BWCC), which drew international artists from dozens of countries.

## Grand Closing Set for July 11

PKB XLVIII will conclude with a grand closing ceremony on Saturday, July 11, 2026, at the Ardha Candra Open Stage at the Bali Art Centre in Denpasar. The evening program begins at 7:00 PM local time and will feature the presentation of first-place Wimbakara trophies, the Adhi Sewaka Nugraha awards honoring lifetime contributions to Balinese arts, and the official launch of the PKB XLIX 2027 theme.

Earlier in the afternoon at 4:00 PM, second and third place winners — as well as honorable mentions across all categories — will receive their awards before the main closing event.

### Living Heritage Through Competition

The Wimbakara competitions serve as both celebration and incubator for Bali's living cultural heritage. The participation of youth ensembles like the Baleganjur Remaja groups demonstrates that the next generation of Balinese artists is not only maintaining tradition but pushing it forward with creativity and skill. Gianyar and Denpasar's strong showing in 2026 sets a high bar for all regions heading into the 49th edition of this iconic festival.
