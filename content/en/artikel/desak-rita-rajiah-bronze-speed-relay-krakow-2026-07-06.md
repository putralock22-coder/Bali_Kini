## Indonesia's Climbing Team Shines in Poland

The city of Krakow, Poland became the stage for an extraordinary Indonesian climbing achievement from July 3–5, 2026. At the **World Climbing Series Krakow 2026**, Indonesia's national team delivered a historic performance across multiple disciplines, with Bali's own Desak Made Rita Kusuma Dewi once again proving herself to be among the very best in the world.

On Sunday, July 5, Desak Made Rita and her relay partner Rajiah Sallsabillah stepped onto the podium to collect a **bronze medal** in the **women's speed relay** event. Competing as INA 1, the pair recorded a time of **13.14 seconds** in the bronze medal race, defeating Spain's Leslie Adriana Romero Perez and Carrla Martinez Vidal, who posted 13.52 seconds.

## The Race for Bronze

The relay final played out at high intensity with top nations from across the globe. The gold medal went to China 1, represented by Yefei Zhoe and Lijuan Deng, who delivered a blistering run of 12.89 seconds in the final to claim the top step of the podium. Silver went to the home crowd's Polandia 2 team with a time of 14.00 seconds, while Indonesia's Desak/Rajiah secured bronze with their 13.14-second effort — a performance that demonstrated both technical precision and mental composure under pressure.

The victory over Spain was anything but straightforward. Competing in a packed international arena with partisan support for the home team, Desak and Rajiah executed their baton exchange and individual climbs with the kind of controlled aggression that separates champions from competitors.

## A Historic Three-Medal Haul from Desak Made Rita

The bronze in the relay added another chapter to what was already a legendary performance by Desak Made Rita at Krakow 2026. In the days preceding the relay final, she had already captured an **individual gold medal** in the women's speed climbing event — one of the defining moments of her already outstanding career. Together with male partner Robby, she had also secured a **silver medal** in the mixed relay.

By the time the competition concluded on July 5, Desak Made Rita had collected three medals from a single World Series event: gold, silver, and bronze. It is a feat that underscores her dominance in speed climbing and her growing reputation as one of Asia's finest athletes in any discipline.
