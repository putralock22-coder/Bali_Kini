The fuel price hike that took effect on June 10, 2026 is now rippling through Bali's maritime transport sector. Speed boat operators on the busy **Kusamba–Nusa Penida** crossing route say they are feeling intense pressure from soaring operational costs, and a difficult choice looms: raise fares or absorb the losses.

## Pertamax Price Jump: From Rp12,300 to Rp16,250 per Liter

State energy company PT Pertamina officially adjusted non-subsidized fuel prices on June 10, 2026. Pertamax (RON 92) — the primary fuel used by speed boat engines — climbed from **Rp12,300 to Rp16,250 per liter**, a jump of **Rp3,950 per liter or approximately 32 percent** in a single adjustment.

For speed boat operators who depend on Pertamax to power their vessels day in and day out, this is a staggering increase. A single boat on a busy day can consume dozens to hundreds of liters of fuel depending on the number of trips and engine capacity.

## The Dilemma: Raise Fares or Absorb the Cost?

Operators on the Kusamba–Nusa Penida route now face a difficult no-win situation:

**If they raise fares:** They risk losing passengers — particularly local residents and Balinese Hindus who frequently cross to Nusa Penida for religious ceremonies at Pura Dalem Ped, one of the island's most sacred temples. A significant fare increase could price out lower-income communities who depend on this route.

**If they keep fares the same:** Profit margins shrink to almost nothing — or disappear entirely. In addition to fuel, operators must also cover vessel maintenance, crew wages, and port fees, all of which continue to rise.

### The Numbers Add Up Quickly

The math is straightforward and painful. If a single speed boat consumes 100 liters of Pertamax per day, the fuel cost increase alone amounts to **Rp395,000 per day** — or more than **Rp11.85 million per month per vessel**. For operators running multiple boats, the financial strain is multiplied accordingly.

## Historical Context: A Recurring Challenge

The Kusamba–Nusa Penida route is no stranger to fare controversies. In previous years, when fuel prices surged, the Klungkung Regency Council (DPRD) publicly questioned the basis for proposed fare increases, arguing they were excessive and placed undue burdens on local communities. Now, with an even steeper fuel price hike than before, the same debate is set to resurface.

### Impact on Tourists

Nusa Penida has grown into one of Indonesia's most sought-after destinations, drawing visitors with iconic sights like Kelingking Beach, Angel's Billabong, and Crystal Bay. The majority of international tourists reach the island by fast boat from either Sanur or Kusamba. If crossing fares rise sharply, it could affect the island's appeal — particularly for budget-conscious travelers.

## Waiting for a Policy Response

Boat operators are now hoping for a clear response from local authorities — both Klungkung Regency and Bali Provincial Government — to address the situation constructively. Possible solutions include:

- Operational fuel subsidies for marine transportation providers
- Establishment of a fair, government-regulated fare ceiling and floor
- A tripartite dialogue between operators, government, and community users

Without meaningful policy intervention, market pressure is likely to push operators toward reduced sailing frequencies, lower maintenance standards, or — most probably — a fare increase that ordinary passengers and religious pilgrims will ultimately have to bear.
