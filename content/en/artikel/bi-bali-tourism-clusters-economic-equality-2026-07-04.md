The Bank Indonesia (BI) representative office in Bali has unveiled a strategic roadmap designed to distribute tourism growth — and its economic rewards — far beyond the island's already saturated southern heartland. The initiative comes as pressure mounts on popular destinations in southern Bali, while vast natural and cultural riches in other parts of the island remain largely untapped.

## Southern Bali: A Success Story Hitting Its Limits

By almost every metric, Bali's tourism is a runaway success. Bali's tourism sector contributed **66 percent of the province's economic growth** in 2024, and the island generated **Rp 176 trillion in foreign exchange** — accounting for a remarkable **55 percent of national tourism foreign exchange earnings**.

But this success has a geographic problem: **88 percent of all investment in Bali is concentrated in just three regencies** — Badung, Denpasar, and Gianyar, known collectively as "Sarbagia." The region is now experiencing visible signs of **over capacity**: traffic congestion, crowded beaches, rising costs, and growing tension between tourism infrastructure and local communities.

## Three New Tourism Clusters

To rebalance this dynamic, BI Bali has mapped out three priority zones across the island, each matched to its natural and cultural strengths:

### West Bali — Jembrana Regency
Development focus: **marine tourism based on marine ecosystem conservation**. Jembrana's extensive coastline along the Bali Strait — from the waters around Gilimanuk to Negara — holds exceptional potential for eco-friendly diving, snorkeling, and blue-economy tourism. The area remains far less visited than southern Bali, meaning it can absorb growth without replicating the pressures of Kuta or Seminyak.

### North Bali — Buleleng Regency
Development focus: **wellness tourism and agro-spiritual tourism**. Buleleng's mountainous landscape, coffee and wine plantations, and historic temples — including Pura Beji and Pura Meduwe Karang — make it ideal for visitors seeking deeper, more contemplative experiences. Wellness retreats, yoga tourism, and spiritual heritage tours are among the highest-growth niches in global travel, and Buleleng is well-positioned to capture them.

### East Bali — Klungkung and Bangli
Development focus: **cultural heritage and culinary tourism**. These two regencies are home to authentic Balinese traditions that remain largely intact precisely because they have been less exposed to mass tourism. The handwoven textiles of Klungkung and the highland culinary traditions of Bangli represent a kind of tourism experience that visitors are increasingly willing to pay a premium for.

## Infrastructure: The Critical Bottleneck

Mapping the clusters is the easy part. BI Bali acknowledges that the strategy will remain aspirational without urgent infrastructure investment:

- An integrated **east-west-north tourism corridor** to reduce inter-regency travel time
- An **MRT line from Denpasar to Klungkung, Bangli, and Singaraja** as the backbone of public inter-regency transit
- **Revitalization of three strategic ports**: Gilimanuk (west), Padangbai (east), and Celukan Bawang (north)

Without better connectivity, travelers will continue defaulting to the familiar — and increasingly congested — southern triangle.

## Part of the Panca Kerti Growth Strategy

The cluster mapping sits within BI Bali's broader **Panca Kerti** (Five Noble Endeavors) economic strategy for 2026. The framework targets quality tourism development, local supply chain strengthening, MSME financing, digital payment acceleration, and creative economy growth as parallel levers to reduce over-dependence on mass tourism.

BI Bali also highlights the potential of a **Financial Special Economic Zone** to attract corporate MICE (Meetings, Incentives, Conferences, and Exhibitions) tourism — a high-value segment that Bali has barely begun to develop relative to its potential.

The goal, ultimately, is for the economic rewards of being one of the world's great destinations to flow not just to Seminyak and Kuta, but to the fishermen of Negara, the farmers of Bangli, and the artisans of Klungkung.
