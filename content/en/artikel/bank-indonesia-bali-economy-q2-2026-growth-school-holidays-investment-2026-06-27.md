As Bali's second quarter of 2026 draws to a close, the economic outlook for the Island of the Gods remains firmly optimistic. Bank Indonesia's (BI) Bali Representative Office has projected that the regional economy will grow between 5.4 and 5.9 percent in the April-June period, underpinned by the school holiday tourism surge, an agricultural harvest season, and an accelerating pipeline of infrastructure investment projects.

## Strong Foundation from the First Quarter

Bank Indonesia's projection is backed by solid recent data. BPS Bali recorded 5.58 percent economic growth for Bali in the first quarter of 2026, a commendable performance achieved in the face of global geopolitical uncertainty. This result demonstrated that Bali's economy — resting on three pillars of tourism, agriculture, and construction — retains significant resilience even in challenging external environments.

From January through April 2026, total international tourist arrivals to Bali reached approximately 4 million, representing a 10 percent increase over the same period in 2025. This steady inflow of foreign visitors sustained household consumption and provided income for the vast web of businesses that depend on tourism.

## Three Engines Driving Q2 Growth

### 1. School Holiday Season and Peak Tourism

June and July represent the peak international travel season for visitors from India — one of Bali's fastest-growing source markets — as well as the beginning of European summer holidays. Simultaneously, Indonesia's nationwide school vacation period is driving a surge of domestic tourists onto flights bound for Bali.

The combined effect is a significant boost in demand for accommodation, dining, tours, and retail. BI notes that the tourism sector continues to serve as the primary engine of Bali's economic growth, with spillover effects reaching into transportation, handicrafts, and the broader services sector.

### 2. Agricultural Harvest and Rural Economic Health

The second quarter also coincides with the rice and horticulture harvest season across key farming regions including Tabanan, Gianyar, and Buleleng. Improved agricultural output raises farmer incomes, strengthens local food security, and contributes to price stability for basic goods — supporting household purchasing power across the island.

The famed Subak irrigation system, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, underpins this agricultural system. Beyond its productive value, the Subak landscape — particularly at Jatiluwih — has become a tourism attraction in its own right, creating an economic multiplier that links farming communities directly to the visitor economy.

### 3. Infrastructure Investment of Rp788 Billion

Construction activity is the third pillar of Q2 growth. Bali's priority infrastructure projects for 2026, with a combined estimated value of Rp788 billion, are actively progressing. These include:

- **Pura Ulun Danu Batur Parking Complex** in Kintamani, Bangli — easing congestion at one of Bali's holiest temples
- **Tukad Unda Raw Water Supply System** in Klungkung — improving freshwater availability for the southeastern region
- **Nusa Ceningan-Nusa Lembongan Bridge** in Klungkung — enhancing connectivity between two popular island destinations
- **Jimbaran Underpass** in Badung — designed to alleviate chronic traffic congestion in southern Bali's busiest tourism corridor

Beyond these projects, the Provincial Government and BI have compiled an investment catalogue covering 22 potential projects, including the Sanur Special Economic Zone for Health, public street lighting, and electric vehicle procurement for eco-friendly tourism transport.

## Resilient Amid Global Headwinds

Despite ongoing geopolitical tensions globally, Bali continues to attract investor confidence. The combination of government investment incentives, Bali's unmatched global brand recognition, and its unique blend of natural beauty and living Hindu-Balinese culture provides a buffer that most other regional economies cannot match.

Bank Indonesia affirms that as long as tourism fundamentals and investment flows remain solid, the 5.4-5.9 percent growth target for Q2 2026 is very much within reach. The official confirmation will come when BPS publishes its Q2 2026 regional GDP data, expected by the end of July 2026.
