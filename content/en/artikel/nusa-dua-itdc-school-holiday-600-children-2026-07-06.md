The Nusa Dua, Bali's premium tourism enclave managed by Indonesia Tourism Development Corporation (ITDC), has entered its peak season as July school holidays draw families from across Indonesia and beyond. The resort area's management has set an ambitious occupancy target of 75 to 80 percent — with several properties already reporting rates above 90 percent in the first week of July alone.

## Holiday Rush Hits Nusa Dua's Five-Star Strip

The start of Indonesia's school holiday season has visibly transformed the atmosphere in The Nusa Dua. Hotel lobbies have filled with families, resort pools are buzzing with children, and the area's private beaches are enjoying their busiest days of the year. ITDC projects that this upward trend will continue through mid-July.

International visitor numbers to the enclave are also growing, with thousands of foreign guests checking in nightly across the lineup of luxury five-star properties that line the Nusa Dua peninsula.

## A Holiday to Remember for 600 Underprivileged Children

Against the backdrop of high-end hospitality, ITDC created one of the most heartwarming moments of the season. On Friday, 3 July 2026, the corporation hosted a School Holiday Celebration themed *"Liburan Nyaman Penuh Makna"* (A Comfortable and Meaningful Holiday) on Peninsula Island, one of the most iconic spots within The Nusa Dua resort zone.

The event, which also marked Indonesia's National Children's Day, welcomed approximately 600 children — including orphans and children with special needs from various social institutions and care homes across Bali. For many of these children, a day inside one of Indonesia's most prestigious resort areas was an experience they could only have dreamed of.

## Hands-On Activities with Real Impact

The programme was thoughtfully curated to balance fun, education, and creativity. Bali Safari brought in live animals — including eagles and pythons — giving children a hands-on encounter with wildlife and an introduction to conservation principles. The direct interaction with these animals was widely regarded as the highlight of the day.

The Jejak Literasi Bali community invited children to paint their dreams and aspirations onto white cloth, turning artistic expression into a vision board for their futures. Each brushstroke became a quiet declaration of hope from young minds given the space to dream freely.

The event was further enriched with storytelling sessions, performing arts showcases, and interactive games involving Museum Pasifika, Bali Collection, and numerous local community partners. The normally serene Peninsula Island transformed into a vibrant outdoor classroom.

## ITDC's Inclusive Tourism Philosophy

This event reflects ITDC's deliberate positioning of The Nusa Dua not merely as an exclusive playground for high-spending visitors, but as a space that gives back to the surrounding community. By bringing hundreds of underprivileged children into a world-class resort setting, ITDC sent a powerful message: luxury and social responsibility are not mutually exclusive.

The initiative forms part of the corporation's broader Environmental, Social and Governance (ESG) commitments, reinforcing The Nusa Dua's identity as a destination that is both globally competitive and locally responsible.

## School Holiday Packages and Promotions

Alongside its social programmes, ITDC rolled out a range of family-targeted promotions and activity packages designed to attract domestic tourists. Discounted accommodation, bundled family activity packages, and collaborative offers with tenants at Bali Collection give Indonesian families compelling reasons to choose Nusa Dua as their school holiday base.

With a blend of commercial ambition and genuine social purpose, The Nusa Dua under ITDC continues to set the standard for what integrated resort management can look like — profitable, inclusive, and deeply rooted in the community it serves.
