## Bali Sets Sights on Global Medical Aesthetic Tourism

Bali is seriously developing medical aesthetic tourism as a powerful new draw for international visitors. Dermatologist dr. Jesslyn Amelia, Sp.DVE, revealed that the arrival of cutting-edge technologies such as XERF presents a major opportunity for the Island of Gods to compete with South Korea and Thailand as a premium aesthetic treatment destination in Southeast Asia.

"Bali can become a center for medical and aesthetic tourism, so patients don't need to fly to Korea or Thailand — they can vacation in Bali while receiving world-class treatments," said dr. Jesslyn at a healthcare practitioners' forum in Denpasar on Monday (29/6/2026).

## What is XERF Technology?

XERF (eXclusive Radio Frequency) is a non-invasive skin tightening technology jointly developed by the United States and South Korea. The device operates using multi-frequency monopolar radiofrequency at 6.78 MHz and 2 MHz, delivering:

- Surgical-free skin tightening
- Collagen reconstruction and stimulation
- Skin rejuvenation from surface to deep dermal layers
- Improved overall skin texture and elasticity

The technology is already well-established in America, Europe, and Australia, and is now expanding into Asian markets — with Bali viewed as one of the highest-potential destinations due to its unique blend of world-class tourism appeal and demand for premium aesthetic services.

## Billion-Rupiah Investment, 15 Percent Annual Growth

The arrival of XERF technology in Bali comes with serious commercial backing. A single XERF unit requires an investment of approximately **Rp2 billion**, reflecting the aesthetic medical industry's confidence in Bali's market potential.

The Vice President of idsMED Aesthetics Indonesia confirmed that Indonesia's aesthetic technology market is currently growing at **15 percent annually**, with Bali as one of the most dynamic markets. Key drivers include high-spending international tourists and rapidly growing local awareness of premium skincare services.

## Bali Wellness and Beauty Expo 2026 as a Springboard

The momentum for Bali's medical aesthetic tourism development intensified following the **Bali Wellness and Beauty Expo 2026**, held June 4–6. The event brought together over **140 leading brands** from 90 national and international exhibitors in the wellness and beauty sectors, cementing Bali's role as a key node in Southeast Asia's wellness landscape.

Deputy Minister of Tourism Ni Luh Puspa stated that the government fully supports developing this segment, as it delivers high economic impact. Medical tourists typically spend significantly more than conventional visitors, while also benefiting accommodation, dining, and activity sectors.

## Riding the Global Wellness Tourism Wave

The global wellness tourism market is projected to grow at over **10 percent per year** through 2029. Bali — already well-established as a holistic and spiritual destination — is one of the strongest candidates to capitalize on this trend.

Clinics across Seminyak, Kuta, Ubud, and Sanur are now competing to offer high-technology aesthetic services to attract international patients. The development of the Sanur Special Economic Zone (KEK) as an international health hub further strengthens Bali's position as Indonesia's leading health and wellness tourism destination.

With a strong tourism foundation, continuously improving infrastructure, and active government support, Bali is well-positioned to make medical aesthetic tourism a sustainable, high-value pillar of the island's economic growth in the years ahead.
