Farmers in **Desa Bebetin, Kecamatan Sawan, Buleleng Regency** now have something to celebrate. PT Bank Rakyat Indonesia (Persero) Tbk, through its Singaraja Branch Office, has completed a concrete road construction project worth **Rp250 million** as part of the company's Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) program — and the impact on the local agricultural community has been immediate and tangible.

## Muddy Paths No More

Before BRI's intervention, several sections of the main access road leading to farming areas in Desa Bebetin remained unpaved. During the rainy season, these stretches would turn into slippery, muddy tracks that were difficult — and sometimes dangerous — for farm vehicles to navigate.

The poor road conditions meant that farmers faced delays in transporting their harvest, increasing the risk of product spoilage, especially for perishable commodities. The economic toll was real: lower productivity, reduced income, and mounting frustration for families whose livelihoods depended on the land.

## Community Effort Meets Corporate Support

What makes this project particularly notable is its collaborative nature. Long before BRI stepped in, residents of Desa Bebetin had already begun concreting portions of the road through community self-help efforts (swadaya). However, the work stalled midway due to limited funds, leaving gaps in the road surface that undermined the entire effort.

BRI's TJSL (Tanggung Jawab Sosial dan Lingkungan) assistance arrived at exactly the right moment — completing and connecting the community's unfinished work into a fully paved, end-to-end access road.

Construction took approximately **five months** to complete, with progress occasionally hampered by heavy rains. Local residents participated actively in the project, working alongside the implementation team to bring it to completion.

## A Measurable Difference for Farmers

Since the road was finished, farmers in Desa Bebetin have experienced a meaningful improvement in their daily operations. Agricultural and plantation products — including cloves, coffee, and horticultural crops that are mainstays of the area's economy — can now be transported swiftly and reliably to markets.

"This road has made such a difference for us. Before, especially during the rainy season, it was really hard to bring our harvest out. Now everything flows much more smoothly and we can sell faster," said one local farmer.

### Multiplier Effects on the Village Economy

Infrastructure improvements like this one tend to generate a ripple effect across the local economy. Better road access encourages traders and buyers to enter previously hard-to-reach areas, opening up new market channels for farmers and reducing their dependence on middlemen who often dictate prices.

## BRI's Ongoing Commitment in Bali

The Desa Bebetin concrete road is one of several TJSL initiatives BRI is implementing across Bali, with a particular focus on Buleleng, where agricultural communities make up a significant portion of the population. BRI Singaraja remains committed to identifying basic infrastructure needs and contributing to the well-being of rural communities and smallholder farmers.

This initiative reflects BRI's core mission as a bank dedicated to empowering small and medium enterprises and rural communities — a mission that extends well beyond financial services.
