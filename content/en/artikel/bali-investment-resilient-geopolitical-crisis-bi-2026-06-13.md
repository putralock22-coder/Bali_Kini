As geopolitical tensions continue to rattle global markets, Bali is defying the trend with a resilient investment climate that refuses to buckle under external pressure. Bank Indonesia (BI) Representative Office for Bali Province has affirmed that the island's investment performance remains strong, underpinned by robust government incentive programs and a growing pipeline of infrastructure projects.

## Investment Momentum Holds Firm

BI Bali Head **Achris Sarwani** delivered a confident assessment of the island's economic trajectory: "Bali's investment performance continues to strengthen amid global uncertainty." His statement is backed by hard data — Bali's economy grew **5.58 percent** in the first quarter of 2026, a figure that outperformed many expectations given the challenging global backdrop.

Looking ahead, BI projects full-year 2026 economic growth for Bali to land in the range of **5.4 to 5.9 percent**. Bali Governor **Wayan Koster** sets an even more ambitious benchmark, targeting growth exceeding **6.1 percent** by leveraging the island's key economic sectors.

## A Rp788 Billion Infrastructure Catalogue

One of the most tangible expressions of Bali's investment push is the strategic infrastructure catalogue jointly compiled by BI Bali and the Provincial Government. The catalogue presents **22 priority infrastructure projects** ready for investor engagement, spanning public facilities, connectivity, and sustainable development.

Highlights from the project list include:

- **KEK Kesehatan Sanur (Sanur Health Special Economic Zone)** — a premium medical tourism hub designed to capture high-value health tourism from global visitors
- **Parking facility at Pura Ulun Danu Batur** — supporting pilgrimage and spiritual tourism access in the Kintamani highlands
- **Tukad Unda reservoir water supply system** — a strategic water infrastructure project to secure freshwater supply for southern Bali
- **Nusa Ceningan–Nusa Lembongan Bridge** — an inter-island connectivity project to replace traditional boat crossings
- **Jimbaran Underpass** — designed to ease congestion along one of Bali's busiest tourist corridors
- Street lighting expansion and electric vehicle procurement

The combined estimated investment value across the full portfolio reaches **Rp788 billion**, reflecting the scale and ambition of Bali's infrastructure agenda.

## Indonesia Financial Center: Bali's Dubai Ambition

Perhaps the boldest initiative under consideration is the development of an **Indonesia Financial Center** in Bali — a concept modeled closely on the Dubai International Financial Centre (DIFC). The proposed zone would operate under a common law legal framework and offer differentiated tax incentives, enabling foreign capital flows into Bali with significantly reduced regulatory friction.

If realized, this initiative would position Bali not only as a world-renowned tourism destination but as a competitive **regional financial hub** in Southeast Asia — a transformational shift for the island's economic identity.

### Bali Jagadhita Investment Forum

Investor confidence in Bali is also visible in the enthusiasm surrounding the **Bali Jagadhita Investment Forum 2026**, which brought together 22 strategic projects from Bali and Nusa Tenggara and presented them to 35 prospective investors. The forum demonstrated that appetite for Bali-linked investments remains healthy, with both domestic and foreign investors showing strong interest in the island's diversified project offerings.

The combination of a proven tourism base, a government actively building enabling infrastructure, and targeted incentive policies continues to make Bali one of Southeast Asia's most attractive destinations for long-term capital deployment — even as global geopolitical headwinds intensify elsewhere.
