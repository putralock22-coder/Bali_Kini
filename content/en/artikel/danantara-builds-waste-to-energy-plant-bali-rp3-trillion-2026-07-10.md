Bali has taken a historic step in solving one of its longest-standing environmental challenges. On July 8, 2026, Danantara Indonesia — through PT Danantara Investment Management (DIM) and PT Daya Energi Bersih Nusantara (Denera) — broke ground on the Pengolahan Sampah menjadi Energi Listrik (PSEL) Denpasar Raya facility in Pedungan Village, South Denpasar. The Rp3 trillion project marks Danantara's first waste-to-energy groundbreaking and represents a landmark moment in Indonesia's push toward modern waste management.

## Capacity and Energy Output

The PSEL Denpasar Raya facility has been designed with ambitious processing targets. At full capacity, the plant will handle up to 1,500 tons of waste per day — equivalent to more than 500,000 tons annually — which accounts for over 40 percent of Bali's total waste generation.

In terms of energy production, the plant is projected to generate enough green electricity to supply approximately 100,000 households across Bali. On the environmental side, the facility is expected to reduce carbon emissions by 640,000 tons of CO₂ per year and cut the land area required for final disposal (TPA) by as much as 80 percent.

## European Environmental Standards

A defining feature of this project is the adoption of European environmental standards for combustion and waste processing. The technology to be deployed has been proven across more than 50 countries, offering strong guarantees of operational efficiency alongside environmental safety.

The partner selection process was rigorous. Proposal evaluation ran through January 2026, followed by a negotiation phase in February 2026, culminating in the signing of a Joint Venture Agreement on March 2, 2026. Of the six consortiums that submitted proposals, only two passed evaluation and advanced to the negotiation stage.

## Construction Timeline

The PSEL Denpasar Raya project is targeted for completion by the end of 2027, with commercial operations set to begin in the first half of 2028. The roughly 18-month construction window reflects the urgency of Bali's waste crisis, which has long been a reputational concern ahead of major international tourism events.

## Social and Economic Impact

Beyond environmental benefits, the project is projected to create around 1,200 green jobs, offering new employment opportunities for local residents in the renewable energy sector. This reflects Danantara's strategy of coupling large-scale investment with domestic workforce development.

For Bali's tourism industry, the strategic value of this plant cannot be overstated. Waste management has been one of the most persistent challenges threatening Bali's image as a premium global destination. A modern, large-scale processing facility capable of handling the island's waste load is expected to meaningfully strengthen Bali's appeal to environmentally conscious travelers.

## Part of a National Transformation

The PSEL Bali project does not stand alone. It is part of a national waste management transformation program initiated under President Prabowo's administration. Danantara has committed to replicating this model in other major Indonesian cities once the Bali facility demonstrates successful operations.

The success of this plant in Bali is intended to serve as concrete proof that waste is not merely a problem — it is a renewable energy resource that can be harnessed for the benefit of communities across the archipelago.
