Far from Bali's crowded southern beaches, a quiet village on the island's northern coast has quietly become a beacon of what sustainable tourism can truly achieve. **Desa Wisata Les** in Buleleng Regency claimed the top honour at the **Anugerah Desa Wisata Indonesia (ADWI) 2024** — the national tourism village award — proving that community-driven, nature-based tourism is a powerful model for the entire archipelago.

## Top Prize Awarded by the Minister of Tourism

The General Champion title was presented by **Minister of Tourism Widiyanti Putri Wardhana** at a ceremony held at Teater Tanah Airku, Taman Mini Indonesia Indah (TMII), Jakarta, on **November 17, 2024**. The award placed Desa Les among thousands of competing tourism villages from across Indonesia, earning recognition for its holistic approach to development that prioritises people, culture, and nature equally.

Behind this achievement lies a sustained partnership between the village community and **Desa Sejahtera Astra**, a corporate social responsibility programme by Indonesian conglomerate Astra that has been actively supporting Desa Les since 2024.

## Real Impact: Incomes Up 25 Percent

The numbers tell a compelling story. Since the Desa Sejahtera Astra programme began, **more than 800 villagers** have directly benefited, with household incomes rising by an average of **25 percent**. This boost stems not from a single intervention but from a comprehensive, four-pillar approach to community empowerment.

## Four Pillars of the Programme

### Health
Primary healthcare services have been strengthened through revitalised Posyandu (community health posts), maternal and child health education, and nutritional support for children affected by stunting and malnutrition. The programme recognises that healthy communities are the foundation of any sustainable tourism destination.

### Education
Young people from Desa Les are equipped with the skills to become active participants in the tourism economy. **English language classes** and tourism capacity training have enabled local youth to develop into professional tour guides without needing to leave their village.

### Environment
The village's extraordinary marine ecosystem is both its greatest asset and its responsibility. The programme prioritises **coral reef conservation**, a community-based waste management initiative called **Les Grow**, and the transformation of organic waste into compost for integrated village gardens. These efforts protect the underwater world that draws visitors while reducing the waste that can so quickly destroy a destination's appeal.

### Economy
Desa Les has a proud tradition of **artisanal sea salt production**, a craft passed down through generations. The programme has supported the preservation of this tradition, which has evolved into a unique cultural tourism attraction — visitors can witness and learn about the rare, labour-intensive process of traditional salt making.

## A Model for Northern Bali

The success of Desa Les carries important implications for Bali's tourism policy. For too long, the island's development has been concentrated in its southern corridor — Kuta, Seminyak, Ubud, Nusa Dua — where overtourism, traffic congestion, and environmental strain are pressing challenges.

Desa Les demonstrates that Bali's north has immense untapped potential: pristine marine environments, authentic village life, living traditional crafts, and communities eager to welcome visitors on their own terms. **Bank Indonesia's Bali representative office** has identified village tourism clusters like Desa Les as a key strategy for spreading economic benefits more evenly across the island.

More broadly, Desa Les offers a blueprint that works anywhere: start with conservation, invest in people, honour tradition, and let the destination's authentic identity become its greatest selling point. The ADWI 2024 championship is not just a trophy — it is validation that this approach can outperform mass tourism in the long run.
