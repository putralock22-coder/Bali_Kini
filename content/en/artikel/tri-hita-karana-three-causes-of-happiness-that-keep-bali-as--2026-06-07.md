## What Is Tri Hita Karana?

Literally, **Tri** means three, **Hita** means happiness or well-being, and **Karana** means cause. Therefore, Tri Hita Karana is **"three causes of happiness"** â a philosophy that teaches that true well-being can only be achieved through the balance of three main relationships in life.

This concept originates from the Hindu-Buddhist era in Nusantara, flourishing in Bali due to its meeting with the indigenous animist beliefs that existed long before. The result is a unique worldview: humans are not the center of the universe, but rather part of a harmonious network that must be maintained with full awareness.

## Three Pillars: Parahyangan, Pawongan, Palemahan

### 1. Parahyangan â Harmony with God

*Parahyangan* (from the word *parahyang* â the abode of the Almighty) is the spiritual dimension of Balinese life. It is manifested through prayer, offerings, religious ceremonies, and gratitude that flow in every daily activity.

In Bali, there is no settlement without a temple. There is no rice field without *pura uluncarik* (rice field temple). There is no house without *sanggah* (family temple). This is not merely tradition â it is how Balinese people remind themselves every day that there is a greater power that needs to be honored and thanked.

> *"When someone prays sincerely, they are not asking â they are maintaining a connection."*

### 2. Pawongan â Harmony with Fellow Humans

*Pawongan* speaks about human relationships: with family, neighbors, village communities, and visitors from afar. Its values are reflected in the tradition of **gotong royong** (mutual cooperation), **ngayah** (voluntary work for temple or village activities), and **menyama braya** (brotherhood without borders).

For Balinese society, individuals never stand alone. A person is part of *banjar* (environmental community), which is part of the village, which is part of Bali. Every action always considers its impact on the community. This is why social solidarity in Bali is so strong â even in the digital era.

The value of Pawongan also underlies Bali's attitude in welcoming tourists: not merely an economic transaction, but a human relationship based on sincerity and respect.

### 3. Palemahan â Harmony with Nature

*Palemahan* is the pillar most often discussed in the era of climate crisis. It teaches that humans are **part of nature, not its master**. Destroying nature is equivalent to hurting oneself.

Palemahan is embodied in the system of **Subak** â traditional Bali rice irrigation that has been recognized by UNESCO as Intangible Cultural Heritage. It is also embodied in *tumpek wariga* (day of offering to plants), *tanduk kandang* (day of offering to animals), and various rituals that celebrate nature as a fellow being worthy of respect.
