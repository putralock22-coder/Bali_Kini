Bali Governor Wayan Koster has set an ambitious economic target for 2026: growth of 6.10 percent. This exceeds the 5.82 percent achieved in 2025 — itself a seven-year high — and was announced at a Coordination Meeting with all regents and mayors across Bali on June 8, 2026.

## More Than Just a Number

Koster was emphatic that the 6.10 percent figure is not merely a macroeconomic statistic. Every percentage point of growth must translate into concrete improvements in the daily lives of Balinese people.

The most visible target: pushing average resident income to above **Rp72 million per year** — equivalent to Rp6 million per month, in line with the minimum wage (UMK) applicable across Bali's regencies and cities. The aim is to reduce economic disparities between the island's prosperous south and its less-developed inland and eastern regions.

## Comprehensive Social Development Targets

Beyond the headline growth figure, Governor Koster has established a set of social indicators to be achieved by the end of 2026:

| Indicator | 2026 Target |
|-----------|-------------|
| Poverty rate | Below 3.42% |
| Unemployment rate | Below 1.45% |
| Income inequality (Gini ratio) | Below 0.333 |
| Stunting prevalence | Below 8% |

These interconnected targets reflect a development vision that goes beyond economic expansion to encompass equity, health, and the quality of life for all Balinese residents.

## "One Island, One Pattern, One Governance System"

To achieve these goals, Governor Koster has introduced a development framework that treats Bali as a single, integrated territorial unit under the principle of **"One Island, One Pattern, and One Governance System."** This approach is designed to strengthen inter-regional synergy so that economic growth is felt island-wide — not just concentrated in Bali's southern tourist corridor.

Each regency and city is expected to maximize the development of its own leading sectors based on its specific potential and competitive advantages. Rather than competing with one another, the regions are expected to function as complementary parts of Bali's broader economic ecosystem.

## Priority Sectors

To reach 6.10 percent growth, the following sectors have been designated as strategic development priorities:

- **Agriculture** — food security strengthening and agro-tourism development
- **Processing industries** — including locally-based small and medium enterprises (SMEs)
- **Construction** — supported by priority infrastructure projects worth Rp788 billion
- **Transportation and logistics** — improving inter-regency connectivity
- **Accommodation and food services** — the cornerstone of Bali's tourism economy

### Supporting Policies

The Governor has also outlined specific operational policies:

1. **10% budget transfers** to regency and city governments will continue and be increased from 2026
2. **Stricter land-use controls** — green land and productive agricultural land protected from conversion
3. **Selective permitting** — new hotel and restaurant construction subject to rigorous approval criteria
4. **Accelerated budget implementation** — all regional governments are urged to speed up APBD spending to stimulate money circulation, particularly heading into Q3 2026

## Bali's Role in the National Economy

Tourism foreign exchange from Bali contributes approximately 55 percent of Indonesia's total tourism foreign exchange earnings — a figure that underscores the island's outsized importance to the national economy. With this strong foundation, the 6.10 percent target is considered achievable, provided all regional leaders align behind a unified development vision.

The June 8 coordination meeting sent a clear signal: Governor Koster is fully committed to pushing Bali's economy beyond national growth averages and delivering prosperity that reaches every corner of the island.
