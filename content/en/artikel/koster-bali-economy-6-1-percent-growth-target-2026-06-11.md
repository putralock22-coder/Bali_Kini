**Denpasar** — Bali Governor Wayan Koster has set an ambitious target: the province's economy must grow by more than 6.1 percent throughout 2026. To achieve this, all regional heads across Bali have been urged to set aside sectoral rivalries and build stronger, more integrated collaboration in budget management and regional development.

The target was announced during a coordination meeting with all regents and mayors across Bali Province in early June 2026 — a critical gathering to align development initiatives so they complement, rather than compete with, one another.

## Building on 2025's Record Achievement

In 2025, Bali's economy grew by 5.82 percent — the highest rate in seven years according to the Provincial Government's own data. This landmark achievement provides the optimistic foundation from which the 6.1 percent target for 2026 becomes credible rather than aspirational.

Bank Indonesia's Bali representative office projects growth for the province between 5.4 and 6.2 percent in 2026 — above the national average — driven by sustained tourism performance, a resilient agricultural sector, and growing investment inflows to the island.

## Six Strategic Sectors Requiring Simultaneous Strengthening

Governor Koster identified six priority sectors that must be pushed forward together for balanced and sustainable growth:

- **Agriculture** — optimizing productive land use and tightening controls on conversion of green and agricultural land
- **Processing industries** — strengthening local products through Regional State-Owned Enterprises (Perumda), ensuring farm and fishery outputs reach markets at fair prices
- **Construction** — accelerating regional budget (APBD) realization to increase money circulation in communities, especially heading into Q3 2026
- **Transportation and logistics** — integrating land, sea, and air transport modes, including water taxi development as a congestion solution
- **Accommodation and food services** — selective permitting for new hotel and restaurant developments to maintain spatial balance
- **Creative economy** — intensifying local food festivals and affordable markets to strengthen community purchasing power

## Increased Budget Transfers to Districts

One of the concrete policy measures announced by Koster is a 10 percent increase in budget transfers to district and city governments from 2026 onwards. This aims to strengthen fiscal capacity at the regional level, allowing each district to run development programs more effectively without depending solely on provincial funds.

Koster also instructed that permits for new hotel and restaurant construction be processed selectively — except for residential needs. This policy is designed to maintain balance between tourism business growth and the living space requirements of local communities who are increasingly feeling the pressure of rapid development.

## Targeting Per-Capita Income Above Rp72 Million

Beyond macroeconomic growth, Governor Koster is targeting per-capita income for Bali residents to exceed Rp72 million per year — above the national average. This figure reflects the government's determination to ensure that economic growth benefits reach ordinary Balinese people directly, not just large corporations and the formal sector.

Bali contributes approximately 55 percent of Indonesia's national tourism economic turnover. The province's success — or failure — in reaching the 6.1 percent target will therefore have ripple effects across the entire Indonesian economy, making inter-regional synergy in Bali a matter of national significance.
