Denpasar is undergoing its most ambitious urban transformation in years. The city government has officially launched the physical execution of its **zero-kilometer zone revitalization** project in June 2026 — a Rp **83.85 billion** investment that will reshape the heart of Bali's capital into a modern, heritage-conscious pedestrian corridor.

## Scope: 2.5 Kilometers Through the City's Historic Core

The project covers the major arteries that define Denpasar's commercial and cultural identity, with a total coverage of **2.5 kilometers** across the following streets:

- **Jalan Gajah Mada** — Denpasar's oldest and busiest trading street
- **Jalan Hasanudin** — a heritage corridor linking the city's administrative core
- **Jalan Thamrin** — connecting the central business district
- **Jalan Sulawesi** — a dense commercial strip currently being rebuilt after severe flooding
- **Jalan Sumatera** — an interconnecting artery within the urban center

The city had targeted the completion of all procurement tenders by **May 2026**, allowing physical construction to begin no later than June — a timeline now being realized.

## Premium Materials: Andesite Stone Pedestrian Lanes

One of the most visible upgrades will be the resurfacing of sidewalks and pedestrian paths using **andesite stone** — the same premium material already used in the well-regarded Sanur waterfront development. This choice signals that the city intends the central zone to match — or surpass — the walkability and aesthetics of Bali's most polished tourist areas.

The full upgrade package includes:

- Widened and resurfaced **pedestrian paths** in andesite stone
- Redesigned **urban green spaces and city parks**
- Upgraded **street lighting** throughout the corridor
- **Synthetic rattan** furnishings and street elements for an organic, Balinese aesthetic
- More comfortable, inclusive, and safe walking infrastructure

## 26 Shophouses to Step Back 3 Meters

The project comes with real-world challenges. A total of **26 commercial buildings** fall within the planned development zone, and all of their owners have already signed agreements to **set their structures back 3 meters** from the riverbank — in compliance with mandatory riparian buffer regulations.

The setback serves a dual purpose: reducing flood risk along the Tukad Badung river that flows through the area, and creating wider public space for pedestrians and street activity.

## Heritage Preserved, Economy Revived

Planners have been deliberate about **protecting the historic character** of this district. Old buildings along Jalan Gajah Mada and surrounding streets will be retained as landmarks, with design elements chosen to harmonize rather than override the existing cultural fabric.

Beyond aesthetics, the revitalization aims to **spark a new economic ecosystem**. A more walkable, comfortable, and visually appealing city center is expected to attract greater foot traffic for shopping, dining, and urban tourism — particularly timely given Bali's peak tourist season in June and July 2026.

## Full Completion Expected by End of 2027

The full scope of the zero-kilometer zone revitalization — including all five target streets and riverbank improvements — is targeted for **completion by the end of 2027**. In 2026, the focus is concentrated on Jalan Hasanudin, Gajah Mada, and Sulawesi.

This transformation positions Denpasar not just as Bali's administrative capital, but as a walkable, culturally rich urban destination worthy of its place at the center of the island's tourism identity.
