Intellectual property (IP) awareness among Bali's residents and business community has grown remarkably. By mid-2026, a total of **5,889 IP applications** had been submitted from Bali between January and June 2026 — a milestone that signals a meaningful shift in legal literacy among local entrepreneurs and creative workers.

## Breakdown of IP Applications in Bali, H1 2026

Data from the Regional Office of the Ministry of Law and Human Rights of Bali Province reveals the following breakdown:

- **Copyright**: 4,312 applications (dominant)
- **Trademark**: 1,504 applications
- **Patent**: 24 applications
- **Industrial Design**: 12 applications
- **Communal Intellectual Property**: 37 applications

**Total: 5,889 applications** — reflecting a sharp increase compared to previous periods. Copyright alone accounts for more than 73 percent of all submissions, highlighting the growing number of creative works, artworks, and digital content now being legally protected by Balinese creators.

## Better-Protected Balinese Products

The 1,504 trademark applications indicate that small and medium enterprises (SMEs) and local business owners increasingly understand the importance of protecting their brand identity. In a competitive market — both nationally and globally — a registered trademark provides legal protection and significant added value to any business.

### Why IP Matters for Bali

Bali, as a tourism hub and center of the creative economy, possesses extraordinary cultural wealth: arts, cuisine, handicrafts, and traditional knowledge. Without adequate IP protection, these assets are vulnerable to imitation, misuse, or unauthorized commercial exploitation — including by foreign parties.

Trademark protection ensures that unique Balinese products such as hand-woven textiles, traditional carvings, or local food specialties cannot be marketed by others using the same name without permission. Copyright protection, meanwhile, grants creators exclusive rights over their artistic and literary works.

## Communal IP: Protecting Bali's Collective Heritage

A particularly noteworthy figure is the **37 communal intellectual property** applications filed during this period. Communal IP encompasses traditional knowledge, traditional cultural expressions, and genetic resources collectively owned by indigenous communities. Registering these protections is essential to ensure that Bali's ancestral heritage is not commercially exploited without the knowledge and consent of local communities.

### Active Outreach Drives the Surge

The spike in IP applications is closely linked to proactive socialization programs and assistance initiatives led by Bali's regional office of the Ministry of Law and Human Rights, in partnership with the provincial government. A socialization event on IP protection held on June 19, 2026, served as a key moment to further educate the public on the benefits and procedures of IP registration.

## Looking Ahead

With 5,889 applications already filed in the first six months of 2026, there is strong optimism that the full-year total will surpass all previous records. The government continues to encourage more SMEs, artists, and indigenous communities to register their intellectual property and secure the legal protection their creative and cultural assets deserve.

This momentum aligns with Bali's broader vision — not just as an island rich in culture, but as a province that is smart about protecting and monetizing its creative assets.
