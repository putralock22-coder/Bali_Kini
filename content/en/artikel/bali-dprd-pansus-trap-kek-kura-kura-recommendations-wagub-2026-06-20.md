The Bali Regional People's Representative Council (DPRD Bali) officially handed over the findings of its Special Committee on Spatial Planning, Assets, and Licensing (Pansus TRAP) to Deputy Governor I Nyoman Giri Prasta on Thursday, June 19, 2026. The submission marks a significant step in the ongoing scrutiny of alleged irregularities at the Kura Kura Bali Special Economic Zone (KEK) operated by PT Bali Tourism and Infrastructure Development (BTID).

## Background: Why Was Pansus TRAP Formed?

The special committee was established to investigate multiple alleged violations related to the development of KEK Kura Kura, a high-profile integrated tourism and business zone in Bali. The committee also examined issues surrounding construction on state-owned land in Pejarakan Village, Buleleng Regency.

The submission session itself was notably tense, with the plenary session interrupted by several council members, underscoring the contentious nature of the issues under review. Despite internal disagreements, DPRD Bali ultimately finalized and delivered nine formal recommendations.

## Key Recommendations

The nine recommendations from Pansus TRAP cover several critical areas:

1. **No legal immunity** — The committee firmly asserted that the KEK Kura Kura's status as a Special Economic Zone does not exempt it from Indonesia's legal framework or environmental regulations.

2. **Mangrove replacement obligations** — PT BTID's commitments to provide replacement mangrove land for Tahura Ngurah Rai forest in Jembrana and Karangasem were found to be administratively unclear and factually unverified.

3. **Return of land to the state** — If violations of the *no net loss of mangrove function* principle are confirmed, the marine and mangrove areas in Denpasar previously transferred to PT BTID must be returned to state ownership.

4. **Resolution of community land rights** — Regional agencies are directed to promptly resolve the rights of communities over land impacted or converted into state land certificates under BTID's name.

5. **Full audit** — A comprehensive audit of all development activities within the BTID-managed zone is strongly recommended.

The remaining four recommendations address enforcement mechanisms, inter-agency coordination, BTID's remedial obligations, and long-term monitoring frameworks.

## Deputy Governor's Response

Deputy Governor I Nyoman Giri Prasta acknowledged receipt of the recommendations and expressed readiness to review all nine points thoroughly in close coordination with Governor Wayan Koster. He emphasized that appropriate and legally sound follow-up actions would be determined based on this joint review.

The provincial government is reportedly prioritizing two of the most pressing issues: the mangrove replacement question and the resolution of affected residents' land rights.

## Implications for Investment Governance

The KEK Kura Kura case has become a litmus test for investment governance in Bali. While the Special Economic Zone was established to attract significant capital and accelerate the development of premium tourism infrastructure, the recommendations from DPRD signal that even major investors must adhere to environmental and spatial planning regulations without exception.

For Bali's long-term vision of sustainable, quality tourism, clear enforcement of land-use rules and environmental commitments is critical — not only to protect the island's natural assets but also to maintain public trust in the governance of large-scale development projects.
