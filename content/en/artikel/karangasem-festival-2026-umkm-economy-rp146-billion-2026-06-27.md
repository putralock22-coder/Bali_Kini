A four-day cultural festival in the heart of Karangasem Regency has demonstrated that traditional events can be powerful engines of local economic growth. **Karangasem Festival 2026**, held from June 19 to 22 at the Candra Bhuana Cultural Park in Amlapura, generated a total economic turnover of **Rp1.446 billion** — a figure that underscores the potential of culture-driven commerce in Bali.

## The Numbers Behind the Festival

The economic impact broke down across two key sectors:

- **32 SME booths** recorded combined transactions of **Rp358.37 million**, showcasing local crafts, fashion, and artisanal products.
- **85 food and beverage vendors** drove the larger share of activity, generating **Rp1.087 billion** in sales.

Together, these figures paint a compelling picture of how a well-organized cultural festival can translate community pride into tangible economic returns — particularly for micro and small enterprises that rarely have access to such high-footfall platforms.

## Closed by the Deputy Governor of Bali

The festival received official recognition when **I Nyoman Giri Prasta**, the Deputy Governor of Bali, presided over the closing ceremony on Monday, June 22. The event was also tied to the **386th Anniversary of Amlapura City**, celebrated under the theme *"Not Easy, But We Must Succeed"* — a motto reflecting Karangasem's determination to grow despite geographic and economic challenges.

In his remarks, Giri Prasta praised the leadership of the Karangasem Regent and Deputy Regent for organizing an event of this scale and quality. He emphasized that cultural activities like this festival create real-world economic ripple effects that benefit the entire community, especially grassroots entrepreneurs.

### A Night of Art and Heritage

The closing evening was a showcase of Balinese artistic excellence. Proceedings began with a performance of the **Puspa Hradhaya Dance**, followed by a video presentation highlighting the achievements of the current Karangasem administration. The event concluded with a **Wayang Sunar** performance — a form of traditional Balinese shadow puppetry that blends narrative, philosophy, and visual artistry into an immersive theatrical experience.

## A Template for Culture-Driven Development

Karangasem Festival 2026 illustrated how local governments can strategically leverage cultural heritage to stimulate economic activity. By creating a dedicated space for SMEs and food vendors alongside artistic performances, the festival served simultaneously as a tourism product, a marketplace, and a community gathering.

For many vendors, the festival's four days equaled weeks or even months of normal income. Artisans and food entrepreneurs reported high visitor interest and strong sales, reflecting growing consumer appetite for authentic local products and culinary experiences.

### Karangasem on Bali's Tourism Map

The festival also served a promotional purpose, shining a spotlight on Amlapura and the surrounding region as a cultural destination worth visiting. Karangasem — home to the iconic Tirta Gangga water palace, the Mother Temple of Besakih on the slopes of Mount Agung, and the traditional village of Tenganan — has significant tourism potential that the festival helps bring to wider attention.

With growing support from the provincial government and increasing local participation, Karangasem Festival is poised to become an anchor event in Bali's annual cultural tourism calendar — one that puts the regency's communities, craftspeople, and cuisines at the center of the stage.
