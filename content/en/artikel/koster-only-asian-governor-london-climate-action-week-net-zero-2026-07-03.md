Bali Governor I Wayan Koster made history on the international stage. At London Climate Action Week 2026, held in London on June 23, 2026, Koster became the only governor from Asia to attend and speak at this prestigious global climate forum.

## Meeting the UN Secretary-General and World Leaders

Koster's presence in London was anything but ceremonial. On the sidelines of the forum, he held direct conversations with UN Secretary-General Antonio Guterres and Bloomberg Philanthropies founder Michael Bloomberg. Both, along with regional leaders from various countries, expressed admiration for Bali's leadership in the low-carbon transition at the subnational level.

"Bali has proven that green policies can be implemented at the provincial level. This becomes an inspiration for other regions of the world," Koster said in an official statement from London.

## Bali Presents Five Green Policies

At the Local Climate Action Summit — a key event within London Climate Action Week 2026 — Governor Koster outlined five flagship green policies already in effect across Bali:

### 1. Bali Energy Independence through Clean Energy
Enshrined in Governor Regulation (Pergub) No. 45/2019, this policy drives the large-scale adoption of renewable energy sources — including solar, wind, and biogas — across the province.

### 2. Battery-Based Electric Vehicles (KBLBB)
Pergub No. 48/2019 mandates the accelerated adoption of electric vehicles in Bali, from government fleet cars to public transportation.

### 3. Restriction of Single-Use Plastics
Since Pergub No. 97/2018, plastic bags, plastic straws, and styrofoam have been banned across Bali — one of the strictest regional plastic policies in Indonesia.

### 4. Organic Farming System
Regional Regulation No. 8/2019 promotes organic agriculture to maintain soil quality and reduce chemical pesticide use on Bali's farmland.

### 5. Protection of Lakes, Springs, Rivers, and the Sea
Pergub No. 24/2020 sets strict provisions to preserve Bali's water sources and coastal ecosystems from the pressures of development and tourism.

## Net Zero 2045 — 15 Years Ahead of the National Target

The most striking announcement Koster made in London was Bali's commitment to achieving Net Zero Emissions by **2045** — 15 years ahead of Indonesia's national target of 2060. This ambitious goal did not merely draw attention; it became the subject of serious discussion among regional leaders present.

During the **Under2 Coalition** dialogue — a global coalition of subnational governments committed to deep decarbonisation — Koster joined governors and ministers from around the world in shaping joint strategies on energy security and the development of affordable clean energy systems.

## Bali as a Global Model of Climate Leadership

London Climate Action Week drew thousands of city leaders, provincial governors, and international organizations from across the world. Koster's presence as the sole governor from Asia made Bali the voice of Asia in core discussions on accelerating subnational climate action.

World leaders at the forum assessed Bali as a tangible proof that environmental commitments need not come at the expense of economic growth — a crucial narrative amid ongoing global debates about balancing development with sustainability.

This London momentum is expected to strengthen Bali's position as both a premier travel destination and a living laboratory for green policy — a reference point for the world in confronting the climate crisis.
