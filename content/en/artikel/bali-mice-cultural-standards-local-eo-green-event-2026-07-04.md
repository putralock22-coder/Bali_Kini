Bali is raising the bar for its meetings, incentives, conferences, and exhibitions (MICE) industry — and the new standard goes far beyond logistical excellence. The Bali Provincial Government, working alongside the Bali Convention and Exhibition Bureau (BaliCEB), has announced a new direction that anchors every MICE event in Balinese cultural identity and environmental sustainability.

## Mandatory Balinese Cultural Elements in Every Event

The most impactful change is the requirement for every MICE event held in Bali — whether national or international — to carry **strong Balinese cultural identity**. This encompasses:

- Venue decoration with traditional Balinese aesthetics
- Guest welcomes using traditional ceremonial customs
- Performances of Balinese art and dance as part of the official program
- Use of traditional Balinese attire in formal sessions

The Bali Provincial Government is currently developing a **Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)** for culture-based event organization, which will be formalized through a Governor's Circular. The goal is to ensure that all event organizers, domestic and international alike, understand and respect Balinese values.

## Empowering Local Event Organizers

Governor Wayan Koster has called for greater involvement of local businesses in organizing events held in Bali. This includes local Professional Conference Organizers (PCOs), Event Organizers (EOs), hotels, and Bali's tourism associations as primary organizers or official partners.

The provincial government is reviewing a policy that would require national and international events in Bali to prioritize **local PCOs/EOs**. This initiative aims not just to strengthen the local creative industry but to ensure that the economic benefits of large events genuinely reach Bali's communities.

### Bali as a Global MICE Destination Rooted in Local Wisdom

Governor Koster emphasizes that Bali holds a unique competitive edge as a MICE destination: the combination of modern infrastructure, natural beauty, and priceless cultural wealth. This sets Bali apart from other major Asian MICE hubs like Singapore, Bangkok, or Kuala Lumpur, which compete primarily on scale and efficiency rather than cultural depth.

## Green Event Principles Become the New Standard

Alongside cultural requirements, the Provincial Government is driving the adoption of **green event principles** across all MICE activities. Concrete measures include:

- Banning single-use plastics
- Using locally sourced and eco-friendly products
- Implementing energy efficiency measures in venue operations
- Responsible event waste management
- Prioritizing catering menus featuring local ingredients

Bali's green energy policies and single-use plastic restrictions are already drawing international attention through the concept of **green MICE**. This positions Bali not only as an aesthetically attractive event destination, but as a model for environmentally responsible event hosting.

## Economic Promise of Upgraded MICE

The MICE industry is widely known to deliver far greater economic impact than recreational tourism. International conference delegates typically spend two to three times more than regular tourists, with longer stays and higher usage of premium services.

By combining cultural standards, empowered local organizers, and sustainability principles, Bali aims to become the leading MICE destination in the Asia-Pacific region in the years ahead. BaliCEB will serve as the frontline agency attracting international event organizers to choose Bali as their preferred venue.
