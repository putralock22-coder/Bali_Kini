Bali's fiscal performance continues on a positive trajectory in 2026. According to the latest data, tax revenue collected in Bali Province **through May 31, 2026** reached **Rp7.03 trillion**, representing **7.89 percent growth** compared to the same period last year. Once again, the tourism sector has proven to be the key engine behind the numbers.

## Total State Revenue Hits Rp9.36 Trillion

Looking at the broader picture, total state revenue collected across Bali through the end of May 2026 reached **Rp9.36 trillion**, equivalent to **30.79 percent** of the annual target. Taxes account for the lion's share at Rp7.03 trillion, with the remainder coming from non-tax state revenues (PNBP) and customs duties.

## Tourism Sector Leads the Charge

Entering May, heightened tourism activity translated directly into stronger tax collection. The **hotel, restaurant, and tourism support services** sectors emerged as the most significant growth drivers — a pattern consistent with Bali's deeply tourism-dependent economic structure.

Bali's economy is uniquely structured: trade, accommodation, and food and beverage services collectively dominate the provincial gross regional domestic product (GRDP). This means that tourism trends almost immediately show up in provincial tax figures.

### Why Tourism Is So Decisive

The Directorate General of Taxes (DJP) Regional Office for Bali has confirmed that the tourism sector **remains the dominant contributor** to tax receipts on the island. From high-end hotels in Nusa Dua and Seminyak to boutique resorts in Ubud, the constant flow of transactions generates Value Added Tax (VAT) and Corporate Income Tax (CIT) revenues that underpin Bali's public finances.

## Administrative Adjustment Starting July 2026

Beginning in July 2026, a notable **administrative change** is taking effect. Several large taxpayers based in Bali will have their tax management transferred to the **National Large Taxpayer Regional Office**. While this technical shift may affect the figures recorded by the Bali regional office on paper, officials are quick to point out that it does not indicate any slowdown in actual economic activity.

"This administrative transition is technical in nature and is entirely unrelated to the financial position of the companies concerned," tax authority sources clarified.

## School Holiday Surge Expected to Boost Figures Further

Beyond structural factors, June and July traditionally mark Bali's **school holiday peak season**, which historically drives a surge in domestic tourist arrivals. Hotel occupancy rates are forecast to climb 10–12 percent during this period, suggesting that hotel and restaurant tax revenues will receive an additional boost when first-half 2026 figures are tallied.

## Bali's Role in National Tourism Revenue

These strong tax figures underscore Bali's outsized contribution to national finances. The province is known to contribute approximately **55 percent** of total national tourism foreign exchange earnings, generating Rp176 trillion out of a national total of Rp319.9 trillion based on 2024 data — a scale that cements Bali's status as Indonesia's premier economic contributor from the tourism sector.

## Risks Ahead

Despite the strong headline numbers, economists caution that Bali's heavy reliance on tourism creates structural **vulnerability**. External shocks — geopolitical tensions, global health crises, or sudden visa policy changes — can swiftly undermine the province's tax base. This reality continues to drive the Bali Provincial Government's push to diversify the economy through organic agriculture, clean energy, and culture-based creative industries.
