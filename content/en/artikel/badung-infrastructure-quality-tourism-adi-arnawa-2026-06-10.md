## Badung's Rp2.9 Trillion Infrastructure Bet

Badung Regent I Wayan Adi Arnawa laid out his regency's development strategy in front of Bali Governor Wayan Koster at a provincial coordination meeting of regency and city heads in Denpasar, Monday, June 8, 2026. The central message: **infrastructure is the backbone of quality tourism**.

"Culture remains the foundation of Bali's tourism, but sustaining it requires a strong ecosystem — good roads, proper waste management, clean water access," Adi Arnawa said.

To fund its priority programs, Badung has taken on a regional government loan of **Rp2.9 trillion**, directed primarily at infrastructure that supports tourism quality while improving the daily lives of residents living alongside it.

## Priority Road Projects Targeting 2026 Completion

### Western Ring Road: Solving the GWK–Uluwatu Bottleneck

The flagship project is the **western ring road**, designed to relieve severe traffic congestion on the GWK-to-Uluwatu corridor — one of Bali's most notorious chokepoints for tourism traffic. Tens of thousands of visitors travel this route daily to reach cliff-side temples and beaches in South Bali, and road capacity has long lagged behind demand.

### Berawa Road and the Canggu Corridor

Construction on **Jalan Berawa** is already underway, targeting completion by end of 2026. This road connects to a new arterial route running from Gatot Subroto Barat toward Canggu, Mengwitani, and Werdi Bhuwana — opening up movement through the island's most chronically congested tourist zone.

### Udayana Underpass

The **Simpang Udayana underpass** is another critical-path project targeting completion this year. The grade-separated crossing will eliminate the vehicle queues that routinely stretch back hundreds of meters at this busy intersection.

### Uluwatu, Melasti, and Southern Ring Road Corridor

Road upgrades across the Uluwatu, Melasti, and Southern Ring routes are also on the 2026 completion list. These corridors serve Bali's rapidly growing premium southern coast destinations and have become a friction point for visitor mobility.

## Flood Control and Waste Management

Infrastructure investment goes beyond roads. Badung is also tackling **flooding** at problem locations including Basangkasa, Jalan Kunti, Umahanyar, and Darmasaba — adding pumping stations and restoring drainage channels that have underperformed for years.

On the waste front, Badung is rolling out the **Source-Based Waste Management Program (PSBS)** at both the household and business level — incentivizing upstream sorting to reduce the volume of waste that reaches landfills and to improve neighborhood cleanliness in and around tourist areas.

## Quality Over Volume: Badung's Tourism Vision

Adi Arnawa was direct about the philosophy behind all of these investments: Badung is building infrastructure not to handle more tourists, but to offer **better experiences** to the visitors it has — and to improve the quality of life for the communities that host them.

As Bali's highest-revenue regency, Badung holds both the fiscal capacity and the political responsibility to lead the island's transition from volume-driven tourism to a quality-first model. With major projects scheduled for completion before the end of 2026, the outcomes will be measurable: less congestion, faster transit, cleaner neighborhoods, and a tourism product that justifies Bali's reputation as one of the world's great destinations.
