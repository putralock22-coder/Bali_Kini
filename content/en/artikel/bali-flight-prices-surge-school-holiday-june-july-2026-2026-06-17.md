The June–July 2026 school holiday season has officially begun, and its impact on Bali-bound flights is immediate and sharp. **Airfares at I Gusti Ngurah Rai International Airport in Denpasar** have climbed significantly across both domestic and international routes, as families across Indonesia and the region prioritize Bali for their holiday getaway.

The price spike, while seasonal, is particularly pronounced this year — driven by a confluence of factors that have turned Bali into an even more magnetic destination than usual during this period.

## Domestic Fare Data: What Travelers Are Paying

Based on data compiled across major Online Travel Agent (OTA) platforms, here is a snapshot of current fare levels compared to off-peak months:

### Denpasar (DPS) – Jakarta (CGK/HLP)
- **April 2026 (off-peak)**: from approximately IDR 1.24 million
- **School holiday period**: IDR 1.5 million – IDR 1.59 million
- **July 1, 2026 (peak holiday date)**: IDR 1.7 million – IDR 2 million one-way

### Denpasar – Yogyakarta (JOG)
- **Before school holiday**: around IDR 1.5 million
- **School holiday**: risen to IDR 1.6 million and above

### International Routes from Denpasar
- **Denpasar – Singapore**: IDR 1.6 million – IDR 2 million
- **Denpasar – Bangkok**: cheapest options via budget carriers such as Scoot starting at IDR 3.5 million for June–July departures

## Why Prices Are Higher Than Usual in 2026

While seasonal fare increases are expected every school holiday, 2026 has additional catalysts stacking demand:

### Hari Raya Galungan and Kuningan
This year's school holiday perfectly overlaps with two of Bali's most sacred religious celebrations: **Galungan (June 17)** and **Kuningan (June 27)**. Both ceremonies are bucket-list experiences for cultural travelers, and the timing has triggered a surge in bookings from visitors who want to witness Bali's spiritual traditions firsthand.

### Pesta Kesenian Bali (PKB) XLVIII 2026
Running from June 13 to July 11, 2026, the Bali Arts Festival at Art Center Denpasar draws performers, art enthusiasts, school groups, and international tourists to the island for an entire month. This sustained influx of visitors puts sustained pressure on air travel demand.

### Strong Post-Pandemic Tourism Momentum
Bali's tourism sector remains on a strong upward trajectory. The Bali provincial government has set an ambitious economic growth target of 6.1 percent for 2026, and visitor numbers continue to outpace expectations. Increased demand across all travel segments tightens seat availability and pushes prices upward.

## Budget Alternative: Land and Sea Travel

For travelers departing from Java who find airfares prohibitive, overland and sea options remain viable and far more affordable:

Combining **train travel** to Surabaya or Banyuwangi, followed by the **Bali Strait ferry crossing** to Gilimanuk, offers a substantially cheaper alternative. The journey takes roughly 12–16 hours from Surabaya, but can reduce transport costs by 50–60 percent compared to flying.

**Tourist buses** operating on the Java–Bali route during school holidays are also popular, offering direct connections from major Java cities to Denpasar at competitive fares.

## Tips for Securing Better Fares

For those committed to flying, these strategies can help reduce costs:

1. **Book early** — tickets reserved 2–3 months in advance are consistently cheaper than last-minute purchases
2. **Consider early morning or late night flights** — off-peak departure times typically carry lower fares
3. **Compare across multiple platforms** — prices on Traveloka, Tiket.com, and Pegipegi can vary for the same flight
4. **Stay flexible on dates** — shifting departure or return by 1–2 days from peak dates can save hundreds of thousands of rupiah

## Outlook: When Will Prices Drop?

Fares are expected to moderate after **mid-July 2026**, once the new school semester begins and holiday travel demand subsides. Travelers without fixed schedules may benefit from waiting, as fares often drop significantly in the third or fourth week of July.

Ultimately, the fare surge is a positive indicator — it reflects Bali's enduring magnetism as a travel destination. Even at elevated prices, travelers continue to choose Bali, underscoring the island's irreplaceable appeal as both a cultural and natural paradise.
