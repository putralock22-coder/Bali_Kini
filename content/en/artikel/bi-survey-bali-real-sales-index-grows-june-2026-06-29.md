Bali's retail sector is maintaining a positive trajectory heading into the school holiday season. Bank Indonesia's Bali Representative Office released its latest survey showing that the province's Real Sales Index (IPR) grew 0.8% month-on-month, reaching a level of 125.3 — comfortably within the optimistic zone above the 100-point threshold.

## Spare Parts and Fuel Lead the Gains

The Head of Bank Indonesia's Bali office attributed the growth to three key commodity groups. Sales of spare parts and vehicle accessories posted the strongest increase at 5.0%, followed by fuel (BBM) at 2.2%. Cultural and recreational goods also contributed positively, reflecting heightened mobility ahead of Bali's busy calendar of religious holidays and the upcoming school break.

The rise in spare parts and fuel sales is a reliable indicator of increased vehicle movement across the island — a pattern typical during major holiday periods such as Galungan-Kuningan and the June school vacation season. Both local residents and rental vehicles used by tourists tend to drive demand for these categories simultaneously.

## Positive Outlook for the Coming Month

Beyond reporting current performance, the BI survey also included projections for the next month. The Real Sales Index is forecast to rise further to level 126, representing an additional 0.6% month-on-month increase. Growth is expected to be driven by clothing and apparel, general goods, and the food, beverage, and tobacco categories.

These continued positive projections signal that household consumption in Bali remains resilient despite global economic uncertainties, including the volatility of the rupiah against the US dollar — a factor that has drawn particular attention from tourism and hospitality businesses.

## Business Confidence Remains Elevated

Two supporting indicators in the survey — the Price Expectation Index (IEH) and the Sales Expectation Index (IEP) — both pointed to sustained optimism among retail business operators regarding Bali's sales prospects. However, the survey also flagged a rise in price expectations, suggesting businesses are bracing for cost pressures from raw material prices and seasonal demand during the holiday period.

Bank Indonesia views the growth in retail sales as consistent with Bali's seasonal consumption patterns, which typically accelerate during the transition from wet to dry season, coinciding with a dense schedule of cultural events and peak tourist arrivals on the island.

## Supporting the Local Economy

The upward trend in Bali's Real Sales Index matters beyond headline statistics. The retail sector employs a large share of Bali's workforce — from convenience stores and auto workshops to fuel stations and souvenir shops. A sustained positive trend month-over-month helps preserve purchasing power for lower-income households while supporting the island's ongoing post-pandemic economic recovery.

Bank Indonesia stated it will continue monitoring this retail indicator as a key reference for formulating regional monetary and price stability policy, particularly ahead of holiday seasons that historically trigger mild inflationary pressure in Bali's local market.
