The traffic congestion that has long plagued the Pecatu and Uluwatu tourist corridor in Badung Regency is set for a permanent fix. The traffic engineering scheme trialled since June 2026 has now been officially designated as a **permanent policy** by the Badung Transportation Office (Dishub) and the Road Traffic and Transportation Forum (LLAJ).

## Trial Period Delivers Results

After a one-month evaluation, Dishub Badung concluded that the traffic management scheme produced measurable improvements: reduced vehicle density, more predictable traffic flow, and shorter travel times to key attractions in the Uluwatu area.

Based on those findings, authorities formally elevated the trial scheme to a standing regulation, making it the new baseline for traffic management in one of Bali's busiest southern tourist zones.

## Six Intersections Under New Rules

The scheme targets **six strategic intersections** that have historically been the main bottlenecks. Two junctions carry the most significance:

### Toya Ning II – Raya Uluwatu Junction
Vehicles travelling from Raya Uluwatu are **prohibited from turning directly** into Jalan Toya Ning II. This single restriction breaks the cross-flow that was the primary cause of queues building at this intersection.

### Baler Setra – Blimbing Sari Junction
Vehicles from Toya Ning II heading toward Raya Uluwatu may **only turn left** toward Pecatu. Turning right toward Ungasan is prohibited during the enforcement window.

## Evening Hours Only

The restrictions apply daily from **5:00 PM to 10:00 PM WITA** — precisely the peak period when visitors converge on the cliffs to watch the sunset from Pura Luhur Uluwatu and the Bukit Pecatu strip. Traffic returns to normal outside those hours.

## Enforcement Awaits Regent's Decree

Although the scheme is now permanent in practice, penalty enforcement for violations still requires the issuance of a **Badung Regent's Decree (Perbup)** as the formal legal basis. Until that decree is published, police can guide but not yet fine violators.

During this transition, authorities are actively socialising the new rules with drivers, transport operators, and businesses along the Pecatu-Uluwatu corridor.

## Ring Road as the Long-Term Answer

Traffic engineering measures are, by their nature, an interim solution. The root problem — road capacity that has not kept pace with the explosive growth of tourism in the Bukit Bali area — requires a more structural response.

Badung Regency Government is advancing plans for a **ring road** that would distribute vehicle loads across multiple access routes instead of funnelling them through two or three existing arteries. Once realised, the ring road is expected to fundamentally reshape mobility across the entire southern Bali tourist belt.

## Why Uluwatu Matters

The Uluwatu-Pecatu area is among Bali's fastest-growing destinations. World-class surf breaks, cliffside cafés, luxury resorts, and the iconic Kecak Fire Dance at Pura Luhur Uluwatu draw hundreds of thousands of visitors each year. Managing the vehicle volume that comes with that popularity is now a governance priority as Bali pivots toward quality tourism over mass visitor numbers.

With a permanent traffic management policy now in place, officials hope the journey to Uluwatu becomes as memorable for its ease as for its spectacular scenery.
