## Hundreds of Students March in Bali Bergerak Action

On **Monday, June 22, 2026**, hundreds of students from universities across Bali staged a demonstration dubbed **"Bali Bergerak"** (Bali Moves) at the Bali Provincial Legislative Assembly (DPRD) building in Renon, Denpasar. Approximately **200 protesters** from the Student Executive Body (BEM) of Udayana University and the Bali BEM Alliance gathered at the eastern field of Renon before marching to the DPRD building, arriving at around **3:30 PM local time**.

The action was part of a broader wave of student demonstrations occurring simultaneously in major cities across Indonesia, including Jakarta and East Java.

## The 16 Demands

The protesters carried **16 concrete demands** addressing fundamental issues in national governance, grouped into five major clusters:

### 1. Economy and Fiscal Policy
Students called on the central government to stabilize the rupiah exchange rate, improve energy subsidies, and thoroughly evaluate the Free Nutritious Meals (MBG) program to ensure it is properly targeted and fiscally responsible.

### 2. Democracy and Governance
The protesters demanded the acceleration of the Asset Seizure Bill and a comprehensive review of the Police Law, which they argued requires substantial reform to better serve the public interest.

### 3. Human Rights and Civil Liberties
Among the most vocal demands was the call for President Prabowo Subianto to **release all political prisoners unconditionally** and halt various forms of state violence. Students also demanded the evaluation and dismissal of the Minister of Human Rights, and called for a thorough investigation of alleged human rights violations in **Papua**.

### 4. Environment, Agrarian Affairs, and Indigenous Rights
Students urged the President, Minister of Environment and Forestry, and Minister of Agrarian Affairs/National Land Agency to **halt deforestation practices** that have been occurring on a massive scale in Papua, Sumatra, and Kalimantan—particularly in connection with national strategic projects and food self-sufficiency programs. Concrete protection of indigenous community rights was also included in this cluster.

### 5. Education and Public Services
Improved quality of education and public services distributed equitably across Indonesia formed the fifth cluster of demands.

## DPRD Bali Pledges to Relay Demands to Jakarta

Representing DPRD Bali, council member **Agung Suyoga** met with the protesters and acknowledged their dedication. The Bali legislature pledged to forward all 16 demands to the central government through faction leaders in the national DPR RI—including the PDI-P, Gerindra, Golkar, and Democratic-NasDem factions—as well as to the Ministry of Home Affairs.

"The struggle is never over," said Agung Suyoga as he addressed the demonstrators.

## Three-Day Ultimatum

However, students were not satisfied with promises alone. The protesters gave DPRD Bali a **three-day deadline** to prove their commitment by actually conveying the demands to Jakarta and delivering concrete results.

"If there is no action within those three days, we will return with an even larger demonstration," declared a representative from BEM Udayana during their speech.

The ultimatum adds urgency to Bali's political landscape in the final days of June 2026, amid mounting economic and social pressures being felt by communities across the island.
