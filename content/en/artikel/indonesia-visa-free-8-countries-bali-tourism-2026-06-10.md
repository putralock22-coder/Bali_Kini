Indonesia's tourism industry is set for a significant boost. The Ministry of Tourism (Kemenpar) is in the final stages of rolling out a selective visa-free entry policy for tourists from eight strategic countries plus one additional territory. Known as the **"8+1 formula"**, this proposal has completed inter-ministerial reviews and is now awaiting final approval at the ministerial level.

## Origins and Policy Background

The initiative was first presented by the National Economic Council (DEN) to President Prabowo Subianto at the State Palace in Jakarta on Tuesday (9/6/2026). The DEN argued that the selective visa-free policy is a cost-efficient strategic move — it **requires no additional state budget** while having the potential to directly boost foreign exchange earnings from the tourism sector.

The backdrop is Indonesia's weakening rupiah, which has created pressure to accelerate foreign exchange inflows. Tourism is seen as the fastest track to generating hard currency without significant fiscal risk.

## The 8+1 Target Countries

The visa-free policy focuses on nations and territories with the greatest potential to deliver high-spending tourists to Indonesia:

- **Japan** — Indonesia's tier-1 inbound tourism market
- **South Korea** — rapid growth driven by the K-culture boom
- **Australia** — the single largest source of visitors to Bali
- **New Zealand** — premium segment with high per-capita spending
- **India** — fastest-growing middle class with rising travel demand
- **Belarus & Kazakhstan** — emerging markets with growing interest
- **Macau** — premium travel segment from East Asia
- **+1: Singapore Permanent Residents** — leveraging Singapore's hub connectivity

Tourism Minister **Widiyanti Putri Wardhana** confirmed that the proposal draft has been thoroughly reviewed across relevant ministries and is ready for a final decision.

## Projected Impact

### ASITA: International Arrivals Could Surge 35%

The Indonesian Travel Agents Association (ASITA) has offered a highly optimistic projection. If the 8+1 visa-free policy is enacted, ASITA predicts international tourist arrivals to Indonesia could jump by as much as **35 percent**.

This would far exceed typical organic growth. In the context of Bali — Indonesia's primary tourism gateway — such a surge could bring in hundreds of thousands of additional visitors within a short period.

### Relevance to the 17 Million Tourist Target for 2026

The government is currently chasing a target of **17 million international visitors** for 2026. With the global environment still uncertain, the visa-free policy is seen as one of the main accelerators needed to reach that figure.

For Bali specifically, Australia, Japan, South Korea, and India are the four biggest historical source markets. Removing the visa barrier is expected to significantly reduce the administrative and psychological friction in planning a trip to the island.

## Adaptive Strategy in a Turbulent Global Environment

This policy is also part of the government's adaptive strategy in response to disruptions in international air connectivity caused by Middle East conflicts, which had temporarily affected flight routes to Indonesia.

By lowering entry barriers for countries less affected by those disruptions, the government aims to sustain and accelerate the flow of tourists to Indonesia, with Bali positioned as the primary beneficiary.
