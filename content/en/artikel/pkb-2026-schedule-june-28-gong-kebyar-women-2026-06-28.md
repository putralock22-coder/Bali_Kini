Sunday, June 28, 2026 is shaping up to be one of the most vibrant days in the 48th Pesta Kesenian Bali (PKB), Bali's annual arts festival, held at the Taman Budaya Bali cultural complex — commonly known as the Bali Arts Centre — in Denpasar. From late afternoon through the night, a rich lineup of performances unfolds across multiple stages within this sprawling cultural complex.

This year's PKB carries the theme **"Atma Kerthi: Jiwa Sidha Parisudha"** — a Sanskrit phrase meaning the glorification and purification of the human soul toward complete, clear, and sacred perfection. The theme runs through every performance on the program, from classical Balinese dance forms rooted in centuries of tradition to bold contemporary works that reimagine those traditions for a modern audience.

## June 28 Performance Schedule

### Afternoon Sessions

The day's events open at **3:00 PM (WITA)** at the Kalangan Angsoka stage with a **Rekasadana (Exhibition) of New Contemporary Dance and Gamelan**, performed by Komunitas Seni Gamelan Busi. In PKB terminology, "Rekasadana" refers to a non-competitive exhibition performance — a showcase rather than a contest.

At **5:00 PM**, two venues run simultaneously:

- **Gedung Ksirarnawa** hosts the **Utsawa (Parade) of Classic Palegongan Dance**, a competitive showcase featuring Sanggar Seni Ghora Yowana Budaya representing Jembrana Regency, and Sanggar Seni Tari Dharmawangsa representing Badung Regency.

- **Kalangan Madya Mandala** stages the **Traditional Joged Bumbung Parade**, bringing together Sekaa Joged Kusuma Sari from Karangasem Regency and Sanggar Seni Joged Mangun Semara from Buleleng Regency.

### Evening Highlights

The night's agenda features two major performances that draw the largest crowds:

At **7:00 PM** in Kalangan Ayodya, the **Rekasadana Prembon** is performed by Sekaa Gong Catur Mandala from Desa Adat Bitra, Bitra Village, Gianyar Subdistrict, Gianyar Regency. Prembon is a classical Balinese theatrical art form combining masked dance drama with dynamic gamelan music — an immersive experience that blends storytelling, humor, and refined musicianship.

Then at **7:30 PM**, the night's most anticipated moment arrives at the **Open Stage Ardha Candra**: the **Utsawa (Parade) of Women's Gong Kebyar**. Two outstanding arts groups take the stage:

- **Sanggar Seni Guntur Madu** — Banjar Pundung, Pangsan Village, Petang Subdistrict, representing Badung Regency
- **Sanggar Seni Lila Janaki** — Banjar Peninjoan, Batuan Village, Sukawati Subdistrict, representing Gianyar Regency

Women's Gong Kebyar is among the most beloved and celebrated disciplines in PKB. Every gamelan musician is female, demonstrating both technical mastery and graceful artistry that captivates audiences across generations and backgrounds.

## PKB XLVIII: An International Scale

This year, PKB has expanded well beyond a local showcase. Within the **Bali World Culture Celebration (BWCC)** series embedded in PKB, no fewer than **10 international arts groups** — involving approximately **260 artists** — have been confirmed to perform. They hail from New York (USA), China, East Timor, South Korea, Japan, India, and Hong Kong.

The presence of these international delegations cements PKB's reputation as a world-class cultural festival and creates a meaningful platform for cross-civilizational artistic dialogue.

Visitors wishing to attend tonight's performances can enter the Arts Centre grounds from 2:00 PM onward. Parking is available around the complex perimeter, and most PKB performances are free and open to the public.
