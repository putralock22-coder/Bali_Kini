Bali's premier youth basketball club has made it to the national stage. **Merpati Bali** has earned their place in the final of the **Mandiri Kejurnas Antarklub U18 2026**, Indonesia's premier under-18 club basketball championship, where they will face the formidable **Aras Gading Muda Jakarta** at GOR Sahabat Arena in Semarang, Central Java.

## A Nationwide Tournament

The Mandiri Kejurnas Antarklub U16 & U18 2026 runs from **June 27 to July 5, 2026** in Semarang, gathering **56 clubs** from across the Indonesian archipelago. The tournament serves both as the country's top competitive stage for youth basketball and as a key scouting ground for the national team program.

GOR Sahabat Arena has hosted the full bracket of matches, with the quality of play rising steadily as the tournament progresses through group stages, playoffs, semifinals, and now the final.

## Merpati Bali's Unbeaten Group Stage

Merpati Bali entered the tournament as underdogs but immediately sent a statement in the group stage, winning all three matches without dropping one:

- June 27: **55-45** over Scorpio Bandung
- June 28: **89-20** over Tangsel Thunder (Banten)
- June 29: **66-18** over Garuda Padang

The dominant run secured them a top position in the group and a berth in the playoff rounds.

## Semifinal: Grit, Rebounds, and a Narrow Win

Merpati Bali's toughest test came in the semifinals against **Airone Basketball Jakarta**. The match was physical and tightly contested throughout, eventually settled in Merpati's favor at **59-54**.

The deciding factor was the battle on the boards. Merpati Bali won the rebounding duel decisively, denying Airone second-chance opportunities while generating fast-break points themselves.

Star player **Revan Surya Winatha** delivered a commanding performance to carry the team through: **17 points, 9 rebounds, and 3 assists**—a complete game from a player who rose to the biggest moment of the tournament so far.

## The Final: Aras Gading Muda Awaits

On the other side of the bracket, Aras Gading Muda Jakarta disposed of Warriors Jakarta **77-52** in a dominant semifinal display, signaling they arrive at the final in peak form.

Aras Gading Muda are no strangers to the final at this level and bring a well-organized offense built around versatile guard Kenneth Leebron Huang. Their ability to score from multiple positions will test Merpati Bali's defense in ways the group stage opponents did not.

For Merpati Bali, the formula is clear: control the paint, win the rebounding war, and lean on Revan Surya Winatha and the team's collective defensive intensity.

## A Milestone for Bali Basketball

Reaching the national final is a significant achievement for Merpati Bali and for youth basketball development across the island. In a landscape where clubs from Java consistently dominate due to stronger competition infrastructure and greater club density, Merpati Bali's run is proof that the talent and dedication exist in Bali to compete at the highest national level.

The outcome of the final will be an important signal—either confirming that Bali can win a national title at this age group, or highlighting the gap that still needs to be closed. Either way, the journey to the final has already given the island reason to take notice.
