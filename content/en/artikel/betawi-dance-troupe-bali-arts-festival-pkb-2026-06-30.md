Audiences at the 48th Pesta Kesenian Bali (PKB) — Bali's premier arts festival — were treated to a captivating cross-cultural performance on Monday, June 29, 2026. **Sanggar Seni Betawi Ratna Sari**, a performing arts troupe from East Jakarta, brought the spirit of Betawi culture to the heart of Bali, taking the stage at Taman Werdhi Budaya Art Centre in Denpasar.

As part of the *Rekasadana* (Traditional Arts Performance) segment of PKB 2026, the troupe presented **Lenggak-Lenggok Betawi**—a dance performance that blends the characteristic swaying movements of Betawi dance with spirited traditional musical accompaniment. The show was received with enthusiastic applause from the thousands of spectators who filled the venue.

## 22 Dancers, 8 Musicians, Months of Dedication

What appeared effortless on stage was the product of rigorous preparation stretching back months. Rehearsals began in **January 2026**, with intensity ramping up sharply during the final month before the performance. A total of **22 dancers** and **8 musicians** were involved in bringing the production to life, forming a tightly coordinated ensemble.

The performance concept was deliberately designed to be accessible and engaging, allowing audiences unfamiliar with Betawi traditions to grasp and enjoy the distinctive characteristics of the art form. Fluid and playful movements, vibrant traditional Betawi costumes, and upbeat music created an atmosphere that was both festive and educational.

### A Mission of Cultural Preservation

The troupe's presence at PKB was not merely a performance booking — it carried the weight of a cultural preservation mission. By performing on the PKB stage, where audiences include not only Balinese locals but also domestic tourists and international visitors from dozens of countries, Betawi culture gained far broader exposure than it would at any venue in Jakarta.

This is precisely what PKB has always offered: an inclusive stage for the diversity of Indonesian arts. From its earliest editions, the festival has welcomed performing groups from other Indonesian provinces and from overseas, transforming what might otherwise be a regional festival into a celebration of national and global cultural heritage.

## PKB XLVIII 2026: One Month of Art, Free for All

The 48th Pesta Kesenian Bali runs for nearly a full month, from **June 13 to July 11, 2026**, across two main venues: Taman Werdhi Budaya Art Centre and the Monument of the Struggle of the Balinese People, both in Denpasar.

This year's festival carries the theme **"Atma Kerthi: Jiwa Sidha Parisudha"** — a Sanskrit phrase meaning *The Glorification of the Perfect Soul*. The theme invites artists and audiences alike to reflect on the importance of nurturing the human spirit through culture and the arts.

Thousands of artists from every corner of Bali and beyond have participated in this year's edition. All performances are **free and open to the public**, making PKB one of the largest and most inclusive arts festivals in Southeast Asia. The event typically draws an average of 1.6 million attendees each year.

### Bali's Stage Belongs to Everyone

The Lenggak-Lenggok Betawi performance was a reminder that PKB is not solely Bali's festival — it belongs to all of Indonesia, and indeed to the world. When Betawi's swaying rhythms met the spiritual aura of Bali's cultural heartland, the result was a meaningful exchange that transcended geography and tradition, leaving a lasting impression on every audience member who witnessed it.
