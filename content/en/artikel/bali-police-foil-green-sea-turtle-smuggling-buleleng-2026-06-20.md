The Directorate of Water and Air Police (Ditpolairud) of the Bali Regional Police has successfully disrupted an attempt to smuggle and trade green sea turtles — a fully protected species — along the coastline of Buleleng Regency. The operation was launched following reports from residents who had grown suspicious of unusual activity in the area.

## Late-Night Raid at Pegametan Beach

On Wednesday night, June 10, 2026, at approximately 10:00 PM local time, Ditpolairud officers descended on Pegametan Beach in Sumberkima Village, Gerokgak District, Buleleng. The raid uncovered 21 live green sea turtles (*Chelonia mydas*) ready to be sold on the black market.

One suspect was arrested at the scene — a 67-year-old man identified only by his initials KS, who allegedly served as the caretaker of the turtles before they could be distributed to buyers.

## Cross-Island Smuggling Network

During initial interrogation, KS disclosed that the 21 turtles had been shipped from the waters of Madura in East Java, supplied by a man named Iwan. This revelation points to a cross-island wildlife trafficking network operating between Java and Bali.

Bali Police have since declared two additional suspects as wanted persons (DPO):
- **Iwan**, a 30-year-old man from Madura, East Java — suspected as the primary supplier who sourced the turtles from Madura waters
- **KMG**, a 35-year-old man from Buleleng — allegedly the fence who planned to resell the turtles

The operation's success was largely due to community vigilance. Local residents of Pegametan alerted authorities after noticing suspicious behavior near the beach, prompting the swift police response.

## Severe Criminal Penalties

Suspect KS now faces a maximum prison sentence of 15 years under Indonesia's Law Number 32 of 2024 on the Conservation of Natural Resources and Ecosystems. The charges relate to hunting, capturing, storing, possessing, keeping, and/or trading protected wildlife in a living state. Financial penalties ranging from Category IV to Category VII fines are also applicable.

## Why This Matters for Bali

Green sea turtles are critically protected in Indonesia due to their endangered status. Bali has long been associated with sea turtle conservation efforts, with dedicated centers in Perancak (Jembrana) and Serangan (Denpasar) working to rehabilitate and protect the species.

Smuggling operations like this one threaten decades of conservation progress and undermine Bali's reputation as an eco-tourism destination. The marine biodiversity that draws thousands of divers and snorkelers to Bali's waters depends on the health of species like the green sea turtle.

Bali Police urged the public to continue reporting any suspected illegal wildlife trade, emphasizing that community participation is essential to protecting endangered species and preserving the island's natural heritage.
