Bali's immigration authorities recorded a significant enforcement milestone in the first half of 2026. A total of 342 foreign nationals were forcibly repatriated to their home countries between January and June 2026, making this one of the most intensive enforcement periods in recent memory.

## Range of Violations

The deportations were driven by a variety of immigration violations rather than a single category of offense. The most common infractions included visa overstay — where visitors remained in Indonesia beyond the duration permitted by their visa — and visa misuse, where foreigners used the wrong type of permit for their activities.

Other violations included working without a valid work permit, involvement in fictitious investment schemes, and activities deemed to disturb public order or violate local customary norms. As a globally popular tourist destination, Bali has long attracted foreigners who attempt to extend their stays beyond legal limits or quietly run businesses without proper documentation.

## Six Regional Units Involved

The enforcement operations across the first semester of 2026 were carried out by all six immigration units operating in Bali:

- **Class I Special Immigration Office at Ngurah Rai TPI** — the main entry point for foreign visitors through the international airport
- **Class I Immigration Office at Denpasar TPI** — serving the Denpasar area and surroundings
- **Class II Immigration Office at Singaraja TPI** — covering northern Bali
- **Denpasar Immigration Detention Center** — the temporary holding facility for immigration violators
- **Class III Immigration Office at Tabanan** — serving western Bali
- **Class III Immigration Office at Klungkung** — covering eastern Bali

This coordinated multi-unit approach allowed for broader geographic coverage beyond the southern tourist corridors of Kuta, Seminyak, and Canggu.

## High-Profile Cases

Beyond routine deportations, the first half of 2026 also saw several high-profile cases involving foreign nationals. In March 2026, immigration officers working in tandem with the National Narcotics Agency (BNN) and Customs uncovered a clandestine drug laboratory operated by two Russian nationals — one of the most alarming cases to emerge in Bali this year.

That same month, Interpol's Red Notice system proved its effectiveness when officers apprehended a British fugitive at I Gusti Ngurah Rai International Airport. The arrest demonstrated the strength of Indonesia's collaboration with international law enforcement bodies.

## Commitment to Consistent Enforcement

Regional Immigration Chief Felucia affirmed that immigration law enforcement will continue to be applied consistently and without exception. Bali's status as a world-class tourism destination requires a safe environment for local communities while still warmly welcoming law-abiding tourists who arrive with proper documents and genuine intentions.

This crackdown aligns with broader government efforts to protect Bali's tourism reputation from being tarnished by the misconduct of a small number of foreign visitors. With international tourist arrivals surpassing 7 million in 2025 and continuing to climb, rigorous oversight has become more essential than ever.

Going forward, Bali's immigration authorities plan to strengthen inter-agency coordination with police, civil service police units (Satpol PP), and regional governments to ensure that all foreigners residing in Bali comply fully with Indonesian law.
