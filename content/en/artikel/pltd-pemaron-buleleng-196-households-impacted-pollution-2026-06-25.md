## Hundreds of Buleleng Families Living in a Pollution Nightmare

For more than 18 months, 196 households living within a 300-meter radius of the Pemaron Diesel Power Plant (PLTD Pemaron) in Buleleng, Bali, have been unable to enjoy peaceful sleep or clean air. The plant, operated by PT PLN Indonesia Power, sits alarmingly close to the nearest residential area — a distance of just 40 meters.

Residents have raised repeated complaints about the two main problems: constant noise from machinery running nearly 24 hours a day, and toxic black smoke billowing from the facility's exhaust, which poses serious respiratory health risks to the local community.

## Cracked Walls, Lost Income

The effects go beyond health. Ground vibrations generated by the diesel engines have caused structural damage to nearby homes, with residents reporting cracked walls and foundations. The economic toll is also real — one resident who works as a massage therapist has lost clients because potential customers refuse to visit a home located next to a noisy industrial facility.

Despite earlier protests that led PT PLN Indonesia Power to limit operations to daytime hours, residents report that the plant continues to run late at night and in the early morning hours.

## Legal Advocacy and Growing Pressure

Thirty-two residents from Pemaron, supported by YLBHI and LBH Bali (Indonesian Legal Aid Foundation), have formally demanded that PT PLN Indonesia Power permanently shut down PLTD Pemaron. Their legal advocates argue that the plant has exceeded its operational lifespan and should have already been decommissioned.

The residents are split between two demands:
- **Relocation** — the majority want to be moved to a safe new location at the company's expense
- **Adequate compensation** — some accept financial compensation, though around 10 percent of affected families have rejected offers as insufficient

## Local Parliament to Conduct Inspection

Responding to the mounting pressure from residents, the Buleleng Regional Parliament (DPRD Buleleng) has announced plans to conduct an unannounced inspection (sidak) of the PLTD Pemaron facility. The parliament also plans to hold a formal hearing (RDP) with PT Indonesia Power management to find a lasting resolution.

The PLTD Pemaron case has become a prominent example of the tension between energy infrastructure needs and residents' rights to a healthy living environment in Bali. With organized community advocacy and legal backing, the pressure on PT PLN Indonesia Power to act decisively is expected to intensify in the coming weeks.
