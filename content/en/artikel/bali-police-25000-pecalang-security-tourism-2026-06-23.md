More than **25,000 pecalang** — Bali's traditional adat-based community security officers — have been formally integrated into a unified security framework by the Bali Regional Police (Polda Bali). The landmark deployment represents a strategic fusion of modern law enforcement with Bali's centuries-old tradition of community-led protection, aimed at safeguarding both residents and the island's millions of annual visitors.

## Thousands Gather at Niti Mandala Renon

The mass integration was marked by a joint readiness inspection at Lapangan Niti Mandala Renon in Denpasar on Tuesday, June 23, 2026. Bali's Police Chief led the ceremony, joined by Deputy Governor I Nyoman Giri Prasta and thousands of pecalang standing shoulder to shoulder with Polri and TNI personnel.

The apel (inspection muster) served as a powerful public signal that all components of Bali's security apparatus are unified — particularly important as tourist arrivals surge during the school holiday season.

## Deputy Governor: Safety Is Bali's Foundation

Giri Prasta used the occasion to reinforce a message that has been central to Bali's tourism strategy for years.

"Regardless of what happens at the global, regional, or national level, Bali must remain safe and inclusive," he said.

The deputy governor called for ongoing strengthening of the synergy between pecalang, military, police, local government, and the broader community — arguing that this multi-layered approach is what keeps Bali competitive as a world-class destination.

## Who Are the Pecalang?

Pecalang are formally recognized traditional security personnel who operate at the banjar (smallest Balinese community unit) and desa adat (customary village) levels. They carry deep social legitimacy with local communities, giving them influence and access that formal law enforcement agencies often cannot replicate — particularly in managing crowds at religious ceremonies, festivals, and customary events.

Their role has existed for centuries as guardians of adat law and community order. The Polda Bali's mass integration formalizes and expands that role into Bali's broader security network.

### Where Pecalang Will Be Deployed

The 25,000 newly integrated pecalang will strengthen security coverage at:

- Major tourist sites and cultural heritage locations
- Residential neighborhoods and banjar communities
- Key transport corridors and high-traffic areas
- Event venues, festivals, and public gatherings

## Polda Bali's Commitment to Quality Tourism

The Bali Police Chief confirmed that the integration of 25,000 pecalang represents a concrete commitment to maintaining public order while supporting Bali's vision of quality tourism. The combination of Polri's institutional authority and pecalang's cultural legitimacy creates a security model that few destinations in the world can match.

## Security as a Tourism Investment

Evidence consistently shows that perceptions of safety directly drive tourism performance. When visitors feel secure, they stay longer, spend more, and become Bali's most effective ambassadors. For an island economy where tourism accounts for more than half of provincial GDP, investing in security is not just a social obligation — it is one of the highest-return economic strategies available.

With 25,000 pecalang now formally part of Bali's security ecosystem, the island's unique blend of cultural tradition and modern governance continues to set it apart.
