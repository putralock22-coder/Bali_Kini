Bali's Regional Police (Polda Bali) has taken a decisive step against the growing threat of digital radicalism. On Saturday, June 20, 2026, authorities organized a major community outreach event titled *"Police Together with Communities: Countering Radicalism and Building a Digital Fortress for Bali's Security and Public Order"* — an initiative calling on all segments of Balinese society to collectively defend digital spaces from extremist ideologies.

## Radicalism Has Gone Digital

The face of terrorism and radicalism has fundamentally changed. No longer limited to face-to-face recruitment or closed-door propaganda, extremist groups now leverage social media, messaging apps, and digital platforms to reach potential members — including young people who are most comfortable in online environments.

The socialization event, held at the Rupatama Hall of Polda Bali, featured two key speakers: representatives from **Densus 88 Anti-Terror National Police** and **Polda Bali's Cyber Directorate**. Participants included community representatives from every regency and city across Bali — a reflection of the understanding that this threat recognizes no geographic boundaries.

## Three Pillars of the Digital Fortress

Polda Bali introduced three practical steps that every citizen can take to build their own "digital fortress":

### 1. Filter Before You Forward
Verify the accuracy of any information before sharing it on social media. Hoaxes and provocative content are frequently used as entry points for spreading radical ideologies. The public is urged not to hastily forward information without first checking it against trusted media or fact-checking websites.

### 2. Counter Hoaxes with Positive Content
Rather than simply avoiding negative content, citizens are encouraged to actively create and share content that promotes peace, tolerance, and national unity. When positive narratives flood social media, they naturally crowd out extremist material.

### 3. Report Immediately
If anyone encounters accounts, groups, or content spreading radical ideology, hate speech, or inflammatory information, they are urged to report it to authorities immediately. Densus 88 and Polda Bali's Cyber Directorate are equipped to act on every report received.

## Why Bali Must Stay Vigilant

As an international tourism destination welcoming millions of visitors annually, Bali occupies a strategically important — and uniquely vulnerable — position. The island's extraordinary cultural, religious, and demographic diversity makes it a potential target for cross-community disinformation campaigns.

Polda Bali emphasized that public safety and order form the bedrock of Bali's tourism success. Without security, tourists stop coming; without social stability, investors stop investing.

## Community-Based Approach

What distinguishes this initiative is its emphasis on **community-level collaboration**. Beyond the Rupatama Hall event, Polda Bali and Densus 88 have conducted educational sessions at secondary schools across various regions, targeting students — the demographic most vulnerable to digital radicalization — as frontline defenders.

Interactive dialogue formed the core format of the event, allowing participants from diverse community backgrounds to share questions, exchange experiences, and collectively devise locally appropriate strategies for maintaining digital security in their own environments.

Polda Bali hopes that by building this collective awareness, Bali can be known not just as a physically beautiful island, but as a digitally resilient society — a genuine fortress against the dangerous currents of misinformation in the age of social media.
