Ahead of the Galungan holiday on June 17, 2026, Bali's Financial Services Authority (OJK) teamed up with the Financial Services Institutions Coordination Forum (FKLJK) and the Bali Provincial Government to organize the **Pekenan Galungan and Kuningan 2026** — a specially curated market event designed to stabilize the prices of essential goods and support local micro, small, and medium enterprises (MSMEs).

## 50 MSMEs Hit the Field

Held at the Niti Mandala Renon Field in Denpasar on Sunday, June 14, 2026, the market brought together **50 OJK-guided MSMEs** — a significant jump from the 25 businesses that participated when the event was first launched.

Vendors offered a wide range of goods at below-market prices, from staple foods like rice, cooking oil, sugar, and spices, to Balinese ceremonial supplies including banten materials and offerings needed for Galungan rituals. Residents from across Denpasar and neighboring areas flocked to the event, welcoming the chance to shop for holiday needs at accessible prices.

## Bali Inflation Held at 2.99 Percent

The proactive move by OJK and FKLJK comes in response to the well-documented inflationary pressure that tends to spike ahead of Galungan and Kuningan. According to Bank Indonesia's Bali Representative Office, **Bali's inflation rate stood at 2.99 percent as of May 2026** — within a manageable range, but at risk of climbing if commodity demand surges unchecked.

Commodities that historically experience the sharpest demand increases ahead of Galungan include:
- **Pork and chicken** — essential for Galungan ritual dishes like babi guling and lawar
- **Fresh fruits** — a core component of Hindu offerings
- **Vegetables and cooking spices** — demand driven by large-scale ceremonial cooking
- **LPG** — spiking as households prepare holiday feasts
- **Banten materials** — palm leaf weavings, flowers, and incense

Bali's Regional Inflation Control Team (TPID) has been actively monitoring commodity availability and prices across traditional markets throughout the province.

## A Dual Mission: Price Stability and Financial Inclusion

The Head of OJK Bali emphasized that Pekenan Galungan is not an ordinary discount market. It carries a dual mandate:

**First**, to protect the purchasing power of Balinese households ahead of Galungan by making essential goods accessible at fair prices, preventing any significant inflation spike during the holiday period.

**Second**, to advance financial literacy and inclusion across Bali. Alongside the market stalls, OJK and FKLJK set up financial education booths, product consultations, and digital banking demonstrations — particularly aimed at helping MSMEs access formal financial services and scale their businesses.

### Official Confirmation: Commodity Stocks Are Safe

The Bali Provincial Government officially confirmed that essential commodity stocks are sufficient to cover community needs throughout the Galungan and Kuningan period. Inventory levels at distribution warehouses and central markets have been verified as safe for the weeks ahead.

The Badung Regency Government ran a parallel program at the kabupaten level, organizing its own affordable market for Badung residents — a region that serves as the epicenter of Bali's tourism industry.

## MSMEs: From Holiday Markets to Broader Growth

For the 50 MSMEs involved, Pekenan Galungan is more than a one-day sales event. It represents a genuine opportunity to introduce their products to a wider consumer base, build brand recognition, and gain access to the formal financial ecosystem supported by OJK.

With Bali's economy growing 5.58 percent in Q1 2026, local MSMEs are increasingly seen as a key pillar of balanced economic recovery — one that reduces the island's heavy reliance on large-scale tourism operators.

As Galungan and Kuningan 2026 approach, Bali demonstrates once again that the strength of its culture and the vitality of its economy are not competing forces — they are deeply intertwined.
