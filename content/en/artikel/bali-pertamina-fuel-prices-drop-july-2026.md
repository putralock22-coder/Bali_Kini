Motorists and business operators across Bali received welcome news at the start of July. PT Pertamina Patra Niaga officially reduced the retail price of three non-subsidized fuel types, effective from midnight (00:00 WIB) on Tuesday, July 1, 2026. The cuts apply across all Pertamina fuel stations in Bali Province and come as relief amid ongoing operational cost pressures faced by the tourism and logistics sectors.

## Which Fuels Got Cheaper?

Three premium fuel products from Pertamina's lineup saw price reductions in Bali, where the Regional Fuel Tax (PBBKB) rate stands at 5 percent:

### Pertamax Turbo (RON 98)
Price dropped from **IDR 20,750 to IDR 19,300 per liter**—a reduction of **IDR 1,450**. Popular among high-performance vehicles and sports motorcycles.

### Dexlite (CN 51)
From **IDR 23,000 down to IDR 19,700 per liter**—a cut of **IDR 3,300**. Widely used by trucks, buses, and commercial diesel vehicles operating in Bali's supply chains.

### Pertamina Dex (CN 53)
The biggest price drop: from **IDR 24,800 to IDR 21,150 per liter**—a reduction of **IDR 3,650**. This premium diesel is used in high-specification diesel vehicles and commercial fleets.

## Prices That Remain Unchanged

Not all fuels at the pump changed today. The following prices hold steady in Bali:

| Fuel Type | Current Price in Bali |
|---|---|
| Pertalite (RON 90) | IDR 10,000/liter |
| Pertamax (RON 92) | IDR 16,250/liter |
| Pertamax Green 95 | Unchanged |
| Subsidized Solar (diesel) | IDR 6,800/liter |

## Why Did Prices Drop?

Pertamina adjusts non-subsidized fuel prices monthly based on a formula that accounts for global crude oil market movements, the rupiah exchange rate, and distribution costs. The reductions on July 1 reflect sustained softening of crude oil benchmarks in recent weeks, which Pertamina has passed on to consumers.

## Impact on Bali's Tourism Economy

The price cuts are expected to have a positive ripple effect across Bali's economy. Tour operators, shuttle providers, and tourist transportation services—which depend heavily on diesel fuel—stand to benefit most from the Dexlite and Pertamina Dex reductions.

A Dexlite drop of IDR 3,300 per liter can translate into savings of hundreds of thousands of rupiah per day for a fleet of minibuses or airport transfer vehicles, helping operators manage costs and potentially maintain competitive pricing for tourists.

Bali's logistics sector, which underpins everything from fresh produce supply to hotel amenities delivery, also benefits directly. Lower distribution costs can help keep food and accommodation prices more stable for visitors.

Bali's statistics agency (BPS) had previously noted that fuel price increases in prior months displaced pork—a traditional Balinese ceremonial commodity—as the leading driver of local inflation. The July price cuts are expected to ease inflationary pressure heading into the second half of the year.

## How to Check Fuel Prices Near You

Fuel prices vary by region due to differences in PBBKB tax rates and distribution costs. Consumers can verify current prices at any Pertamina station or via the MyPertamina mobile application.
