## Bali Hosts Prestigious Asian Swimming Championship

Jimbaran Bay in Badung Regency became the stage for the 12th Asian Open Water Swimming (OWS) Championship 2026, held from June 13 to 15, 2026. The continental-level swimming competition drew **78 swimmers from 18 countries**, with total participants including officials reaching 127 people. The event positioned Bali at the forefront of international aquatic sport while simultaneously boosting the island's growing sport tourism sector.

The venue was set in the waters off the InterContinental Bali Resort, known for its clear blue sea and calm bay conditions. Despite the picturesque setting, gusty winds on the opening day created challenging conditions for competitors in the open water course.

## China and Vietnam Lock Up the Podium

On the first day of competition, Saturday, June 13, both the men's and women's 5-kilometer races were dominated by athletes from China and Vietnam. The two nations demonstrated superior technique, endurance, and race strategy, leaving competitors from other countries struggling to keep pace.

In the women's 5 km event, Vietnamese and Chinese athletes traded the top positions, showcasing the depth of open water swimming talent that has been cultivated across both nations. The men's 5 km saw China's team asserting its dominance from the early stages, with fast splits and strong finishing kicks through Jimbaran Bay's open waters.

## Indonesian Athletes Face Challenges

Indonesia fielded six of its best open water swimmers — four men and two women — competing across three event categories: 5 km, 10 km, and the 4x1,500 meter relay. The team included Made Nadya, Andy Fauzan, Alexander Adrian, Akbar, Sang Arka, and Izzy Dwi Faiva.

On the opening day, Indonesia's performance in the 5 km events reflected the difficulty of competing against Asia's elite swimmers. In the men's 5 km, **Moch Akbar Putra Taufik** finished 18th with a time of 1 hour, 1 minute, and 51.9 seconds, while **Sang Arka Ning Jaladri Prawatya** placed 23rd at 1 hour, 2 minutes, 55.5 seconds. In the women's race, **Gusti Ayu Made Nadya Saraswati** crossed the finish line in 16th place (1 hour, 7 minutes, 30 seconds). Strong coastal winds were cited as a factor affecting the Indonesian swimmers' pacing strategy.

The Indonesian team remained hopeful heading into Sunday's events — the 10 km races and the 4x1,500 meter relay — where they aimed to deliver improved results.

## Sport Tourism Boost for Bali's Economy

Beyond the medal count, the event served as a powerful catalyst for Bali's sport tourism ambitions. Alongside the 78 official competitors, an open series called the **A. Stream OWS Series** attracted approximately **300 participants** from the general public and sports community, including 91 local Balinese entries.

The influx of athletes, officials, and their supporters from 18 countries generated tangible economic benefits for the local hospitality sector, restaurants, and transportation providers. Anindya Bakrie noted that the Asian OWS Championship 2026 represents a strategic opportunity to simultaneously elevate Indonesian swimming performance and solidify Bali's reputation as a world-class sport tourism destination.

### Building Indonesia's International Hosting Reputation

The successful staging of this Asian championship reinforced Indonesia's growing credibility as a reliable host for major international sporting events. The chairman of the Indonesian Aquatics Federation (PB Akuatik) expressed confidence that Indonesia is increasingly trusted by Asian and global sports federations to organize prestigious competitions, with Bali's natural environment making it especially suited for open water swimming events.

The championship concluded on June 15, 2026, with the final medal standings determining the overall champion nations of this edition.
