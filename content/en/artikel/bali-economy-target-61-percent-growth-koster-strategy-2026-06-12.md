Bali Governor **Wayan Koster** has set an ambitious target: grow the province's economy by **at least 6.1 percent** in 2026. This comes on the back of a solid Q1 performance and a clear multi-sector strategy that aims to transform Bali from a tourism-dependent economy into a more diversified, resilient regional powerhouse.

## Six Sectors Driving the Push

To hit the 6.1 percent mark, Koster's administration is concentrating efforts on **six strategic sectors** that together form the backbone of the Balinese economy:

- **Agriculture** — including plantations, livestock, and fisheries
- **Manufacturing and processing industry** — downstream processing of local products
- **Construction** — infrastructure development across the province
- **Transportation and logistics** — improving inter-island and intra-island connectivity
- **Accommodation and food services** — the tourism hospitality core
- **Trade** — strengthening both domestic and export markets

The approach is designed to generate multiplier effects: growth in one sector stimulates demand in others, creating a virtuous cycle that lifts economic activity across all nine regencies and cities in Bali.

## Increased Budget Transfers to Regions

One of Governor Koster's signature fiscal policies — a mandatory **10 percent budget transfer** from the provincial government to district and municipal governments — will not only be maintained in 2026 but **increased in scale**. The policy ensures that development spending reaches beyond the tourist hotspots of southern Bali into less-developed areas like Buleleng, Karangasem, and Bangli.

This decentralised fiscal approach is expected to reduce economic inequality between Bali's regions, generate local employment, and fund priority infrastructure at the district level — from rural roads to clean water systems and local market upgrades.

## Built on Strong Q1 Foundations

Koster's confidence in the 6.1 percent target is grounded in data. Bali's economy expanded by **5.58 percent in Q1 2026**, outperforming the national average despite a temporary slowdown in tourism caused by the fallout from the Middle East crisis on international travel patterns. That result shows the economy has the underlying strength to accelerate further as the peak tourism season kicks in.

### Bank Indonesia's Investment Catalyst Role

Bank Indonesia's Bali representative office is complementing the government's strategy through an **investment project catalogue** of 22 shovel-ready infrastructure initiatives. These include the Sanur Health Special Economic Zone, renewable energy street lighting networks, and electric vehicle fleet programmes. Together, these projects are designed to attract private capital, create skilled jobs, and modernise Bali's infrastructure backbone.

## A Stronger Rupiah Story for Visitors

A somewhat unexpected tailwind for Bali's growth strategy comes from global currency markets. The strengthening US dollar has made Bali **more price-competitive** as a destination for international visitors who pay in hard currencies. Their spending power in rupiah terms has increased, effectively making Bali a more attractive option compared to other Southeast Asian destinations like Thailand or Malaysia.

Governor Koster has highlighted this currency dynamic as an opportunity that Bali must capitalise on quickly — particularly during the June-July peak season — to maximise foreign exchange earnings that feed directly into the local economy.

## Tourism Still the Engine, But Diversification Is the Goal

While tourism remains Bali's primary economic engine, the 2026 growth strategy reflects a broader ambition: to reduce vulnerability to tourism shocks by building up agriculture, manufacturing, and financial services. The aspiration to host Indonesia's International Financial Centre — a project still in development — signals that Bali's policymakers are thinking decades ahead, not just about the next tourist season.

With peak season underway, investment forums attracting global capital, and a clear fiscal roadmap in place, Bali's 6.1 percent growth target for 2026 appears within reach.
