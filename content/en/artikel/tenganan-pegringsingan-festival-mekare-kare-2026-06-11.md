Three days of living heritage came to a close today, Thursday (11/6/2026), as the **Tenganan Pegringsingan Culture Festival 2026** wrapped up its final activities in Tenganan Pegringsingan Village, Manggis District, Karangasem Regency, Bali. Held from June 9–11, the festival carried the theme **"Weaving Heritage, Interpreting Tradition"** with the subtheme *"Maintaining Sacredness, Preserving Ancestral Heritage"*.

## The Village That Time Preserved

Tenganan Pegringsingan is no ordinary Balinese village. Its community belongs to the **Bali Aga** — the original Balinese people who predated the influence of the Majapahit Kingdom on the island. Their customs, architecture, and traditional dress set them apart from mainstream Balinese society, making Tenganan one of the most authentically preserved cultural destinations in Southeast Asia.

This year's festival was officially opened by the Deputy Regent of Karangasem on June 9, 2026, kicking off three days of cultural performances, educational workshops, and ancestral rituals.

## Mekare-Kare: The Star of the Festival

The undisputed highlight of the festival was **Mekare-Kare** — the Pandan War. This centuries-old tradition is performed as a tribute to **Dewa Indra**, the god of war in local spiritual belief.

Participants attack one another using **bundles of thorny pandan leaves** held in the right hand, while the left hand wields a rattan shield. The tradition unfolds over two consecutive days at different sacred sites:

- **Day one** takes place at Petemu Kaja
- **Day two** is performed in front of Bale Agung

What makes Mekare-Kare truly extraordinary is what happens after the battle ends. All participants — regardless of wounds — **embrace one another without conflict**, then proceed to **megibung**: communal dining as a living symbol of brotherhood and unity.

### Deeper Than a Spectacle

Village elders emphasize that behind the dramatic visuals that captivate tourists, Mekare-Kare embodies core values: **responsibility, courage, and solidarity** among Tenganan's young generation. People from outside the village — including international visitors — are welcome to participate in the experience, not just observe it.

## A Full Cultural Programme

Beyond Mekare-Kare, the festival offered a rich menu of cultural activities:

- **Geringsing cloth workshops**: Participants learned about the motifs and philosophy of Tenganan's double-ikat woven fabric, one of only three double-ikat traditions in the world and believed by locals to carry protective power.
- **Traditional architecture exhibition** by Universitas Warmadewa, showcasing Tenganan's unique village layout that has remained largely unchanged for centuries.
- **UMKM and village tourism product fair**, introducing handcrafts and local goods to visitors.
- **Cultural arts competitions** and performances by community members.
- **Daha Mayunan ritual**, a sacred ceremony specific to young women of the village.

## A Tourism Magnet for East Bali

The Tenganan Pegringsingan Culture Festival has grown into one of Karangasem's most sought-after cultural events, drawing visitors from across Bali and overseas. The event not only boosts tourism to eastern Bali but also serves as a living mechanism for cultural preservation — driven by the indigenous community itself in partnership with local government.

For visitors who missed this year's edition, the festival is expected to return next year, following the sacred cycles of Tenganan's ancestral calendar. It remains one of Bali's essential annual cultural pilgrimages.
