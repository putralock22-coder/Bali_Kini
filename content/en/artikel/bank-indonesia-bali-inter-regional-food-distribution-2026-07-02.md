Behind Bali's relatively stable inflation figures lies a structural challenge that policymakers are now moving to address head-on: the island's heavy reliance on food imports from other islands and, within Bali itself, inefficient distribution between districts. To tackle this, Bank Indonesia's (BI) Bali Regional Office and local governments have opened a new chapter of inter-regional cooperation focused on food supply logistics.

## More Than a Routine Coordination Meeting

The initiative goes beyond periodic coordination sessions. The core objective is to open **efficient inter-regional food distribution channels** — connecting agricultural surplus areas in North, East, and West Bali with the high-consumption zones concentrated in the south.

The primary mechanism being optimised is collaboration through **Perumda Pangan** (Regional State-Owned Food Companies) in each district. By strengthening Perumda's operational role, surplus commodities from one regency can flow directly to deficit areas without passing through long and costly intermediary chains.

## Why This Matters in a Tourism Economy

Bali's tourism dominance creates an unusual food demand profile. The island hosts not only its resident population but also more than **7 million international tourists** annually — a figure that does not yet account for the far larger number of domestic visitors. This dual demand intensifies markedly during school holidays and major religious festivals.

Without a robust distribution network, price spikes during peak demand periods are almost inevitable. The Galungan holiday in June 2026 served as a recent case study: commodity prices for shallots, garlic, and several vegetables climbed sharply, partly because supply from other districts did not reach affected markets quickly enough.

## Priority Commodities Under Close Watch

BI Bali and TPID monitor price movements for several volatile commodities on an ongoing basis:

- **Red and bird's eye chillies** — prices spike quickly when weather disrupts highland harvests
- **Shallots and garlic** — supply chains exposed to Java imports and exchange rate risk
- **Rice** — stocks require careful management ahead of dry season
- **Beef and pork** — demand surges during religious ceremonies and cultural festivals

A more integrated distribution system is expected to dampen the volatility that these items have historically exhibited during peak periods.

## LPG Subsidy Distribution Also in Focus

Beyond food, BI and TPID are tightening oversight of **subsidised LPG (3 kg cylinder)** distribution across Bali. Shortages of subsidised gas — particularly in regencies located far from main distribution warehouses — have been a persistent source of hardship for small businesses and lower-income households. Regular inspections are now being conducted to detect and prevent diversion of subsidised cylinders to non-eligible users.

## Subsidised Market Operations Intensified

As an immediate response to June's price pressures, **subsidised market operations (operasi pasar murah)** have been expanded across multiple locations in Bali. These events supply basic commodities below prevailing market prices and are now being held more frequently — structured around the seasonal demand patterns of Bali's religious calendar and tourism calendar rather than as one-off events.

## Building Structural Price Stability

The broader goal is to move Bali toward structural price stability that does not depend on emergency interventions after prices have already risen. By investing now in distribution infrastructure and inter-agency coordination, BI and its government partners are trying to build a supply-side buffer capable of absorbing demand shocks before they translate into inflation.

With Bali's June 2026 inflation at 3.27% — still within target but rising — the timing of this initiative appears well judged. Analysts note that without coordinated pre-emptive action, the combination of school holiday demand and potential El Niño disruptions could push provincial inflation closer to the upper boundary of the national target range in the second half of 2026.
