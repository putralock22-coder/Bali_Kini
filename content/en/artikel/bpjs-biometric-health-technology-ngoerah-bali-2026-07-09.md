## Bali's Main Hospital Goes Digital with BPJS Biometrics

Healthcare services for National Health Insurance (JKN) participants in Bali have taken a major step forward with the deployment of biometric technology at RSUP Prof. Dr. I Gusti Ngurah Rai—widely known as RSUP Ngoerah—in Denpasar. The system, introduced by BPJS Kesehatan (Indonesia's national health insurer), is designed to accelerate patient identity verification and make accessing JKN benefits faster, more accurate, and easier than ever before.

The rollout is part of a nationwide digital transformation of public health services, happening as the number of active BPJS Kesehatan participants in Bali continues to grow and pressure on public hospital capacity intensifies.

## Senior Minister Inspects JKN Services in Bali

The seriousness of the central government in improving JKN quality was underscored by a direct visit from Coordinating Minister for Community Empowerment, Muhaimin Iskandar, to RSUP Ngoerah on Wednesday, 8 July 2026. During his inspection, the minister met with patients and hospital staff, personally verifying that healthcare access under the national insurance programme is seamless, speedy, and free of discrimination.

"We are here to make sure that JKN services are genuinely felt by every layer of society. There must be no discrimination in national health services," the minister stated to hospital staff and waiting patients.

## How the Biometric System Works

### Facial Recognition for Instant Verification

The BPJS Kesehatan biometric system uses facial recognition technology linked to the national civil registration database. When a JKN participant arrives at the hospital, their identity can be verified in a matter of seconds—eliminating the need to present a physical BPJS card or stand in long administrative queues.

The system is particularly beneficial for elderly patients and those who frequently forget their cards, as their identity can be confirmed through a quick face scan connected directly to the central database.

### Integrated with SatuSehat and Mobile JKN

The biometric system at RSUP Ngoerah does not operate in isolation. It is fully integrated with SatuSehat—Indonesia's national electronic health records platform—and the Mobile JKN app. This integration allows medical staff to access a patient's full health history in real time, reducing administrative errors and cutting registration processing time from a typical 15–20 minutes to just a few minutes.

## Major Referral Reform in 2026

The biometric rollout coincides with a significant overhaul of the BPJS referral system that took effect in 2026. JKN participants can now be referred directly to hospitals best suited to their medical needs, bypassing the previously cumbersome multi-step process through primary care facilities.

This reform is expected to dramatically reduce waiting times for patients with serious conditions such as kidney disease, heart conditions, and cancer, who were previously delayed by lengthy bureaucratic procedures before reaching specialist care.

### Ongoing Challenges

Despite its promise, the implementation is not without difficulties. Some RSUP Ngoerah patients with PBI (contribution-assisted) BPJS status previously faced rejection when their membership showed as inactive in the system—even when they were technically eligible for coverage. With the new real-time biometric system connected to the central database, such errors are expected to be significantly reduced.

## Bali's Path to a Digital Health Ecosystem

The deployment of biometric technology at RSUP Ngoerah marks an important milestone in Bali's journey toward a fully digital public health ecosystem. In the longer term, the system is expected to be expanded to hospitals and community health centres (puskesmas) across the island, ensuring that every resident—local or migrant worker—has equal, efficient access to national health coverage.
