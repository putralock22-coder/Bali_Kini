One of Bali's most celebrated traditional villages is officially in festival mode. **Penglipuran Village Festival XIII** opened its doors on Thursday (9/7/2026) and runs through Saturday (11/7/2026), transforming the ancient pathways of Desa Wisata Penglipuran in Bangli Regency into a vibrant celebration of Balinese culture, environmental stewardship, and community pride.

## Opening Day: Dance, Parade, and Tradition

The festival's opening was a spectacular showcase of living Balinese culture. Hundreds of performers took part in a **colossal dance** (*tari kolosal*), presenting the rhythms and movements that have defined Balinese artistic expression for centuries. The surrounding crowd — a mix of domestic tourists, international visitors, and local residents — was treated to a visual feast that few urban festivals could replicate.

The highlight of the day was undoubtedly the **Gebogan Parade**, a procession of ornate ceremonial offerings shaped like towering pyramids of tropical fruits, flowers, and traditional cakes. Gebogan, traditionally a Hindu devotional offering, has been elevated here into a stunning art form that speaks to Bali's dual identity as both a deeply spiritual and aesthetically refined civilization.

The day's program was rounded out by a live performance of the **Palegongan "Raksan Gumi"** dance and the opening of a **UMKM (small business) exhibition** featuring locally made crafts, textiles, and culinary products from Penglipuran and the surrounding Bangli highlands.

## Three Days, Three Themes

The organizers of Festival XIII designed each day around a distinct theme, reflecting the village's holistic vision for sustainable and inclusive tourism:

**Day 1 – Culture (July 9)**: Colossal dance, gebogan parade, formal opening, Palegongan performance, UMKM fair

**Day 2 – Environment (July 10)**: Environmental conservation workshops, traditional fashion competition (*tempoé doloe*), local music performances

**Day 3 – Health (July 11)**: Laughter yoga session, musical entertainment, festival closing ceremony

The inclusion of an entire day dedicated to environmental themes is no accident. Penglipuran has long been recognized internationally as one of the **cleanest villages in the world**, a distinction maintained through strict customary laws that govern everything from waste disposal to the height and width of traditional gates.

## Targeting 3,000–4,000 Visitors Per Day

Festival organizers have set a visitor target of **3,000 to 4,000 guests per day** for the three-day event — an ambitious but realistic goal given the village's recent visitor trends. During the current school holiday period (late June through mid-July), Penglipuran has already been welcoming an average of **3,500 visitors daily**, compared to around 2,000 on regular non-holiday days.

The majority of visitors are domestic travelers taking advantage of the school break to bring their families to one of Bali's most iconic cultural destinations.

## Inclusivity at the Core

A defining feature of Festival XIII is its deliberate focus on inclusivity. Beyond the expected participants — local artists, UMKM vendors, village youth — the organizing committee has specifically invited and integrated **groups of people with disabilities** into the festival programming. This commitment to inclusive participation reflects a broader shift in how Bali's village tourism sector is evolving: away from purely performative culture toward genuine community engagement.

## Why Penglipuran Matters

Penglipuran is not just a picturesque Balinese village. Located approximately 700 meters above sea level in the Bangli highlands, it has become a living laboratory for sustainable tourism and heritage preservation. Every house in the village faces the same cardinal direction (north–south) in alignment with the Hindu *Tri Mandala* spatial philosophy. Motorized vehicles are prohibited on the main village path, ensuring the pedestrian-friendly atmosphere remains intact.

The village has won recognition from multiple international sources, including travel publications and UNESCO-affiliated bodies, as an exemplary model of how traditional communities can maintain their identity and ecological balance while welcoming the modern world.

## A Festival Worth the Journey

For travelers already in Bali during the school holiday season, the remaining two days of Penglipuran Village Festival XIII offer a rare opportunity to witness authentic Balinese village culture in an immersive, participatory setting. Whether it's joining an environmental workshop, admiring traditional batik at the fashion competition, or simply walking the bamboo-lined lanes of this extraordinary village — there is no shortage of reasons to make the trip to Bangli.

The festival runs through **Saturday, July 11, 2026**.
