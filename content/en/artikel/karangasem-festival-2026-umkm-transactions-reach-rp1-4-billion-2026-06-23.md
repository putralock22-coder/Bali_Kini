## Four Days, Over a Billion Rupiah Generated

The 2026 Karangasem Festival closed on a high note on June 22, when Bali's Deputy Governor I Nyoman Giri Prasta officially wrapped up four days of celebrations in Amlapura — the capital of Karangasem Regency. The numbers told an impressive story: total economic transactions by small and medium enterprises (SMEs) during the festival reached **Rp1.445 billion**, cementing this annual event as far more than a cultural celebration.

The festival ran from June 19 to 22, 2026, coinciding with the **386th Anniversary of Amlapura City**.

## Breaking Down the Numbers

Of the total transactions, **85 food and beverage entrepreneurs** recorded sales of **Rp1.087 billion**, while **32 craft-based SME operators** brought in **Rp358 million**. Both groups operated side by side throughout the four-day event, generating real income for small business owners in what is historically one of Bali's less economically developed regions.

Karangasem Regent I Gusti Putu Parwata expressed satisfaction with the results, saying the figures showed that the creativity and determination of Karangasem's people could genuinely move the local economic needle.

## A Theme Built on Resilience

This year's festival carried the theme **"Tidak Mudah, Tapi Harus Bisa"** — meaning *Not Easy, But It Must Be Done*. The phrase honestly reflects the challenges Karangasem faces: infrastructure gaps, volcanic activity risks, and ongoing efforts to lift living standards for local communities.

Over four days, the festival grounds in Amlapura came alive with traditional Balinese performing arts, exhibitions of local products, and popular entertainment that drew thousands of visitors from across the island and beyond.

### Beyond Entertainment: A Multi-Function Platform

Deputy Governor Giri Prasta described the festival's significance in his closing remarks. The Karangasem Festival, he noted, has evolved into a multi-purpose platform — a creative showcase, a vehicle for cultural preservation, a tourism promotion engine, and a grassroots economic driver.

"This festival brings artists, small business owners, farmers, and tourists into one living space. This is the true face of Bali," said Giri Prasta.

## Karangasem Rising Through Culture

Karangasem Regency has historically ranked among Bali's most economically disadvantaged areas. Yet through initiatives like this festival, local authorities have consistently pushed culture and community-based tourism as pathways to broader development.

The success of Karangasem Festival 2026 signals that the culture-economy festival model can be a sustainable development tool — particularly for regions in Bali that have not yet fully benefited from the province's booming tourism industry.

Future editions are expected to attract more SME participants, draw visitors from Denpasar and Bali's southern resort corridor, and extend economic benefits further down to village-level entrepreneurs.
