India is cementing its position as one of Bali's most important international tourism markets. Data released by Indonesia's Central Statistics Agency (BPS) in May 2026 reveals a robust increase in Indian tourist arrivals to the Island of the Gods throughout the first quarter of the year.

## 157,100 Indian Visitors in the First Quarter

Between January and March 2026, Indian tourists made **157,100 visits** to Indonesia—up **3.90 percent** year-on-year. The overwhelming majority of these visitors chose Bali as their primary destination, entering through the island's international gateway, I Gusti Ngurah Rai International Airport.

In March 2026 alone, Indian tourist arrivals stood at **53,800**, a 1.26 percent month-on-month increase compared to February. During this month, Indian visitors accounted for **4.94 percent** of all foreign tourists who entered Bali.

## Ngurah Rai: The Preferred Gateway

Bali's dominance as India's top destination in Indonesia is clearly reflected in entry-point data. A remarkable **78.92 percent** of Indian visitors—approximately **42,460 people** in March 2026—arrived through Ngurah Rai Airport. This figure dwarfs arrivals through other Indonesian entry points, including Jakarta's Soekarno-Hatta Airport and Batam.

The concentration of Indian arrivals through Bali underscores the island's unique draw: spiritual experiences, rich cultural heritage, iconic landscapes, and an already-established community of Indian restaurants and services that make first-time visitors feel welcome.

## Staying Longer, Spending More

One of the standout statistics from BPS is the average **length of stay for Indian tourists: 7.02 nights**. This is considerably longer than the regional average and signals that Indian visitors are immersing themselves in Bali's diverse offerings rather than rushing through.

Longer stays translate directly into higher spending on accommodation, dining, activities, and shopping—all of which benefit Bali's local economy. Across all nationalities, the average foreign visitor to Bali spent **USD 1,345.61 per trip** with an average stay of 10.84 nights.

## Bali Welcomes 1.6 Million Foreign Tourists in Q1 2026

Total foreign tourist arrivals to Bali in the first quarter of 2026 reached **1,645,169 people**, a 2.4 percent increase that added roughly 39,385 more visitors compared to the same period in 2025. Monthly figures:

- **January 2026**: 588,900 arrivals
- **February 2026**: 533,303 arrivals
- **March 2026**: 522,966 arrivals

At the national level, Indonesia welcomed **3.44 million foreign visitors** in Q1 2026—a **8.62 percent** year-on-year surge that marks the country's highest quarterly foreign tourist count since 2020. This growth was led by markets including India, Australia, Malaysia, and Singapore.

## Why India Loves Bali

The rise in Indian tourism to Bali reflects several converging trends. Indian travelers discovered Bali through a wave of Bollywood films and social media influencers showcasing the island's temples, rice terraces, and beach clubs. The expansion of direct flight routes from major Indian cities including Mumbai, Delhi, and Chennai has significantly reduced travel friction.

Bali's tourism authorities are now working to deepen the Indian market by promoting premium experiences—spiritual wellness retreats, yoga and meditation packages, and authentic cultural immersion tours—that command higher per-visitor spending and align with the island's push toward quality tourism.
