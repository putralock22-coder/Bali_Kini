The **Pesta Kesenian Bali (PKB) XLVIII — Bali Arts Festival 2026** is entering its final stretch, and today's lineup is a highlight of the entire month-long celebration. On Wednesday, July 1, 2026, the main stage at Taman Budaya Bali (Bali Cultural Park / Art Center) in Denpasar will host an **angklung gamelan parade** followed by a **gong kebyar dewasa** (adult gong kebyar) performance. Both events are free and open to everyone.

## A Festival of Epic Scale

PKB XLVIII runs from June 13 through July 11, 2026, under the theme **"Atma Kerthi: Jiwa Sidha Parisudha"** — a Sanskrit phrase meaning the Glorification of the Complete Soul. Now in its 48th edition, this is Southeast Asia's largest government-organized arts festival held annually.

The scale of this year's festival is staggering: **23,936 artists** from 307 arts groups, community studios, and cultural foundations are participating. They represent all eight regencies and one city in Bali, plus national and international performers through the **Bali World Culture Celebration** program. Bali's Deputy Minister of Tourism has set a target of more than **1.6 million visitors** over the course of the festival — a figure that reflects PKB's enormous draw as a cultural tourism event.

## Angklung Bali: Sacred Bronze Gamelan

Many visitors may know the angklung as the bamboo instrument from West Java. But **Balinese angklung** is something entirely different — a bronze gamelan ensemble traditionally associated with ritual contexts, especially cremation ceremonies and other rites connected to death and purification.

In today's parade, groups from different villages and arts communities will process through the Art Center grounds, each performing their own interpretations of the angklung repertoire. The variety of styles — some solemn, some surprisingly lively — reveals the richness of regional musical traditions within Bali's broader cultural tapestry. Watching multiple groups perform in sequence offers a compressed musical survey of the island's ceremonial heritage.

## Gong Kebyar Dewasa: Gamelan at Its Most Electrifying

**Gong Kebyar** is the most celebrated gamelan ensemble in the Balinese tradition. Developed in the early 20th century in North Bali, it broke away from older court music forms with its explosive, unpredictable character. The name *kebyar* means "to flash" or "to burst" — and it describes the music perfectly.

A **Gong Kebyar Dewasa** performance features adult musicians who have typically spent years, even decades, mastering their instruments. These performances can shift from whispered pianissimo passages to thunderous unison strikes with no warning — demanding total concentration from both performers and audience. The musicians appear in elaborate ceremonial costumes, their precision and passion elevating the performance into something that feels closer to a spiritual ritual than a concert.

During PKB, Gong Kebyar competitions are among the most fiercely contested events. Even in non-competitive performances like tonight's, the standard of musicianship is extraordinarily high.

## How to Attend

Taman Budaya Bali (Art Center) is located on **Jalan Nusa Indah, Denpasar**. It is accessible by private vehicle, ride-hailing apps (Gojek/Grab), or Trans Metro Dewata, Bali's affordable city bus network. Parking is available on site. Performances are free of charge and require no ticket — just show up and find a seat.

With just ten days remaining in PKB XLVIII, July 1 is an excellent opportunity to catch some of the festival's most prestigious traditional arts performances before the curtain falls on July 11.
