Bali's fiscal performance in the first half of 2026 has delivered encouraging results. The provincial government's Regional Own-Source Revenue (Pendapatan Asli Daerah / PAD) grew **10.25 percent** year-on-year through May 2026, according to official data. Simultaneously, total tax collection in Bali surpassed **Rp7.02 trillion** by the end of May — an 11.27 percent increase compared to the same period in 2025, which recorded Rp6.3 trillion.

These figures signal continued strength in Bali's economy, despite global headwinds that briefly dampened international tourist arrivals in April.

## Accommodation Sector Drives Fiscal Growth

The standout performer in Bali's revenue story is the accommodation and food-beverage sector, which recorded year-on-year growth of **31.09 percent** in Q1 2026 alone. This surge reflects strong hotel occupancy rates, increasing restaurant activity, and the multiplier effect of major religious celebrations — including Galungan in June — and the domestic school holiday season.

Hotel and restaurant tax revenue in Badung Regency — Bali's tourism powerhouse — reached **Rp2.89 trillion** in the first five months of 2026. Hotel tax alone increased from Rp1.7 trillion to Rp1.8 trillion over the same period, underscoring the sector's resilience.

### Key Tax Components

Across different tax categories, nearly all recorded positive growth:

- **Corporate Income Tax (PPh 25/29 Badan)**: Rp1.91 trillion (+4.71% YoY)
- **Personal Income Tax (PPh 25/29 OP)**: Rp313.63 billion (+19.3% YoY)
- **Hotel and restaurant taxes**: Growing strongly in all major tourism regencies

The sharp rise in personal income tax reflects improving household incomes among Balinese working directly in tourism — including tour guides, villa staff, restaurant workers, and small-business owners.

## Q1 2026 GDP Growth at 5.58 Percent

The strong fiscal performance mirrors Bali's overall economic momentum. Statistics Indonesia (BPS) Bali recorded **GDP growth of 5.58 percent** year-on-year in Q1 2026. While this is a slight moderation from the 5.86 percent growth recorded in Q4 2025, the figure is viewed positively by analysts given the challenging global environment at the start of the year.

The Head of BPS Bali noted that 5.58 percent represents a healthy growth rate for the province, considering the geopolitical tensions that affected international arrivals in early 2026.

> "Growth at 5.58 percent is a strong result for Bali. The accommodation and food-service sector was the main engine of our economic recovery," said the Head of BPS Bali.

### Bali-Nusa Tenggara: Indonesia's Fastest-Growing Region

According to data cited by CNBC Indonesia, the Bali-Nusa Tenggara region recorded the **highest economic growth** among all of Indonesia's regions in early 2026. Tourism — which contributes approximately **66 percent of Bali's economy** — remains the primary driver of this regional outperformance.

## Spending Acceleration Urged

On the expenditure side, the Directorate General of Treasury (DJPb) Bali has called on regional governments to **accelerate budget disbursement**, particularly for infrastructure projects and MSME support programmes. Faster spending is expected to amplify the economic multiplier effect and help spread prosperity beyond the main tourism corridors in the south.

## Challenges Remain

Despite the positive fiscal trajectory, Bali faces several structural challenges that could complicate the outlook:

- **Tourism dependency**: With tourism accounting for two-thirds of the economy, Bali remains exposed to external shocks including geopolitical crises, natural disasters, and global health events
- **Geographic investment imbalance**: Most investment and tax revenue remain concentrated in southern Bali (Badung, Denpasar, Gianyar), while northern and eastern regencies lag behind
- **Local inflation**: Bali recorded price pressure in May 2026, driven by rising chilli prices and higher domestic airfares — factors that can squeeze purchasing power for lower-income residents

## Target: 6.1 Percent Growth for Full Year 2026

Governor Wayan Koster has set an ambitious economic growth target of **6.1 percent** for Bali in 2026. The strategies to get there include attracting higher-spending tourists, diversifying investment into inland and northern regencies, and fast-tracking key infrastructure projects — including a new waste-to-energy plant and improved road networks.

With PAD and tax collection comfortably tracking ahead of target at the halfway point of the year, Bali appears well-positioned to achieve — or possibly exceed — its 2026 growth objective.
