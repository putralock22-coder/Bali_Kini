Bali's immigration authority has reported a significant law enforcement achievement for the first half of 2026. A total of **342 foreign nationals (WNA)** were forcibly repatriated from the Island of the Gods between January and June 2026, following a range of violations of Indonesian immigration laws.

## Overstay and Permit Misuse Top the List

**Felucia Sengky Ratna**, Head of the Bali Regional Immigration Office, confirmed that enforcement actions were carried out as part of intensified oversight of foreign national activities on the island. The most common violations found in the field were overstaying beyond the permitted period and misusing existing residence permits for unauthorized purposes.

Other violations recorded during the period include working in Bali without a valid work permit, participating in fictitious investment schemes, engaging in activities that disturb public order, and breaching local customary norms.

## Joint Operations Across Bali

The crackdown was carried out through coordinated operations involving all immigration units across the island:

- **Class I Special Immigration Office for TPI Ngurah Rai**
- **Class I TPI Denpasar Immigration Office**
- **Class II TPI Singaraja Immigration Office**
- **Class III Non-TPI Tabanan and Klungkung Immigration Offices**
- **Denpasar Immigration Detention Center (Rudenim)**

Operations were conducted through routine immigration patrols, independent enforcement missions, and coordination with the **Foreign National Supervision Team (Timpora)**, which brings together multiple government agencies.

## High-Profile Cases

Beyond routine deportations, Bali Immigration uncovered several major cases during the semester that drew national attention.

### Illegal Drug Laboratory Run by Russian Nationals
In **March 2026**, working in concert with the National Narcotics Board (BNN) and Directorate of Customs, Bali Immigration dismantled a clandestine drug laboratory operated by two Russian nationals on the island. The case underscored that permit violations in Bali sometimes mask far more serious criminal activity.

### Interpol Fugitive from the UK Arrested at Ngurah Rai Airport
Also in March 2026, immigration officers at Bali's I Gusti Ngurah Rai International Airport intercepted and detained a British national listed on an Interpol **Red Notice** — an international alert for wanted persons. The arrest was made through the integrated passenger screening system at the airport's international arrival gates.

### Australian Interpol Fugitive Stopped from Departing
In **June 2026**, Bali Immigration blocked the departure of an Australian fugitive wanted by Interpol in connection with criminal offences in their home country. The successful interdiction resulted from close cooperation between Bali Immigration, the Narcotics Crime Directorate of the National Police's Criminal Investigation Bureau (Dittipidnarkoba Bareskrim), and the **Australian Federal Police (AFP)**.

## A Clear Message: No Safe Haven for Lawbreakers

Kakanwil Felucia Sengky Ratna reiterated the firm stance of Bali's immigration authorities: the island will not tolerate violations of immigration law, regardless of a foreign national's nationality or reason for being in Bali. The crackdown aligns with Bali's growing importance as one of Asia's premier international tourism destinations, where the integrity of the visitor experience depends in part on maintaining public order and safety.

Immigration authorities are also deepening collaboration with regional police, district governments, and traditional village communities (banjar) to ensure foreign national oversight reaches all levels of Balinese society.
