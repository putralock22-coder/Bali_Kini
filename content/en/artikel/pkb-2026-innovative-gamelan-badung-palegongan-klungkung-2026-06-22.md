## PKB XLVIII 2026: Celebrating the Purified Soul

The 48th Pesta Kesenian Bali (PKB), or Bali Arts Festival XLVIII, continues to unfold in spectacular fashion at the Taman Budaya Bali (Bali Cultural Park / Art Centre) in Denpasar. Entering its second week, Monday, June 22, 2026, brings a packed program of traditional performances and parades representing Bali's diverse regencies.

PKB 2026 runs from June 13 to July 11, 2026, under the theme **"Atma Kerthi: Jiwa Sidha Parisudha"** — a philosophical concept encapsulating the pursuit of purifying the human soul toward perfection through artistic expression rooted in Balinese tradition. This annual cultural festival engages an impressive **20,929 artists** from **673 groups**, delivering a total of **879 performances** across a full month, cementing its status as one of Southeast Asia's largest arts festivals.

## Innovative Gamelan: Badung Celebrates Sangeh Forest

The standout performance today is the **Rekasadana (Recital) of Innovative Gamelan**, scheduled for **3:00 PM WITA** at Kalangan Angsoka. Representing Badung Regency, Sanggar Seni Gargita Santhi from Banjar Pacung, Desa Sangeh, Kecamatan Abiansemal, takes to the stage.

This innovative gamelan composition from Badung draws its inspiration from the history and ecological richness of **Sangeh Forest**, a sacred woodland renowned for its population of gray Balinese long-tailed macaques and towering centuries-old nutmeg trees. Through fresh compositional approaches and creative use of traditional instruments, Gargita Santhi translates Sangeh's spiritual and environmental significance into a musical language that is both contemporary and deeply Balinese.

## Classical Palegongan Parade: Heritage from Klungkung and Gianyar

At **5:00 PM WITA**, the **Utsawa (Parade) of Classical Palegongan** takes the stage at Gedung Ksirarnawa, featuring two distinguished delegations:

- **Sekaa Gong Panji Gita Semara** from Banjar Budaga, Kelurahan Semarapura Kauh — representing Klungkung Regency
- **Gambelan Suling Gita Semara** from Banjar Tengah, Desa Peliatan, Ubud — representing Gianyar Regency

Classical Palegongan is among Bali's oldest dance-music traditions, combining the refined Legong dance with the ethereal tones of the Semar Pegulingan gamelan ensemble. Its presence at PKB represents not merely artistic display but a serious commitment to preserving ancestral heritage for younger generations.

## International Participation at PKB 2026

This year's PKB XLVIII also welcomes international performers, including **Yowana Sari from Queens College, New York**, who will present a fusion of Talujon Percussion and Gamelan Salukat — a blend of Balinese gamelan with contemporary Western percussion. This international involvement underscores PKB's role as a global cultural bridge for Balinese arts.

All performances at Taman Budaya Bali are **free and open to the public**.
