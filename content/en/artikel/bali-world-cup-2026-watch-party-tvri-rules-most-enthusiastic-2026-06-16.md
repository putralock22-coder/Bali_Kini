Bali has claimed another unexpected record this week — not in tourism arrivals or cultural exports, but in World Cup enthusiasm. Data compiled by TVRI Bali, Indonesia's state broadcaster and official rights holder for the 2026 FIFA World Cup, shows that Bali has the highest number of registered watch party organizers of any province in the country, outpacing regions traditionally associated with strong football culture.

## Bali Leads the Nation

TVRI Bali Station Chief I Gede Mustito confirmed the figures in an official statement, noting that Bali's commercial and international tourism character has translated into outsized demand for World Cup screenings.

"Bali is currently the region with the highest watch party registration activity in Indonesia. This reflects both local enthusiasm and the huge number of international visitors who want to watch their national teams compete," Mustito said.

Thousands of foreign tourists currently in Bali — from Australia, Europe, the Americas, and across Asia — are fueling demand for live screenings across the island's bars, beach clubs, and hotels.

## TVRI's Strict Rules: Register or Face Consequences

Behind the excitement lies a strict regulatory framework. TVRI, as the official broadcaster holding FIFA's rights in Indonesia, has issued detailed rules for all watch party organizers — commercial and non-commercial alike.

### Non-Commercial Events

Community screenings at schools, neighborhood halls, and public spaces fall under the non-commercial category. These organizers **may not charge admission fees** of any kind, and **may not involve sponsors or brand promotions**. The broadcast must be sourced directly from TVRI's official feed without modifications.

### Commercial Events

Bars, restaurants, hotels, and entertainment venues that screen matches as part of a for-profit event must obtain an **official license agreement** from TVRI covering broadcast rights and technical specifications.

### No FIFA Assets Allowed Without Permission

One of the most heavily emphasized restrictions is the **ban on using FIFA visual assets**. This includes the World Cup name, logo, trophy image, mascot, and official branding. These cannot appear in event titles, banners, digital flyers, stage backdrops, or merchandise — without FIFA's explicit authorization. Violations may result in direct sanctions from FIFA.

## Popular Venues Across Bali

The Canggu, Kuta, Seminyak, and Denpasar areas have become the epicenters of watch party culture this World Cup season. Notable venues include:

- **The Bench Sports Bar, Canggu** — one of the largest sports bars in the Batu Bolong area, featuring multiple screens and an international atmosphere.
- **De Berry Beerhouse** — actively targeting foreign visitors from participating nations, with dedicated seating areas by team nationality.
- **Starters Sports Bar** — a go-to venue for live international sporting events, from AFL and NRL to the World Cup.

## Bangli Joins in With an Official Public Screening

Local government has also joined the World Cup spirit. Bangli Regency officially organized a free public watch party at the Alun-Alun (town square) of Bangli, with an open invitation to residents and visitors alike. This public event falls under the non-commercial category and complies with TVRI's requirements.

## Economic Impact for Bali's Tourism Sector

The World Cup season is expected to deliver a meaningful economic boost to Bali's tourism and hospitality industries. Hotels, bars, restaurants, and venues are ramping up staffing to accommodate higher footfall throughout the tournament. Travel industry analysts note that the combination of peak season tourism, cultural events like Galungan and PKB, and the World Cup makes June–July 2026 one of the busiest and most economically active periods in Bali's recent history.
