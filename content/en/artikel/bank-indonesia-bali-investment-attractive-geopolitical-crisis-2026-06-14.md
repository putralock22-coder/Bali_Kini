## Bali Holds Its Ground as a Global Investment Hub

While geopolitical tensions have shaken economies worldwide through much of 2026, the **Bank Indonesia (BI) Representative Office for Bali Province** is sending a clear message: Bali remains one of Indonesia's most attractive investment destinations. Three core strengths keep investor confidence resilient — a world-class tourism ecosystem, structured government incentives, and Bali's irreplaceable global brand.

This positive outlook was affirmed during the **Bali Jagadhita Investment Forum 2026**, a high-level event that presented **22 strategic investment projects in Bali and Nusa Tenggara** to an audience of **35 investors** from across the region and beyond. The forum's strong attendance demonstrated that global uncertainties have done little to dampen investor appetite for Bali's long-term potential.

## Economy Projected to Grow 5.4 to 5.9 Percent in 2026

Bank Indonesia Bali projects the island's economy will expand within a range of **5.4 to 5.9 percent** in 2026 — a robust figure that stands well above average global growth, which has been weighed down by ongoing conflicts and market volatility.

Key factors supporting this projection include:

- **Recovering tourism** — international visitor arrivals to Bali for January to April 2026 reached approximately 4 million, representing a roughly 10 percent increase compared to the same period in 2025
- **School holiday season** (June–July) historically drives significant uplift in accommodation occupancy, dining, and leisure spending
- **Government infrastructure projects** continuing uninterrupted despite global headwinds
- **Contained inflation** creating a stable environment for medium and long-term investment

## 22 Strategic Projects Ready for Investment

In collaboration with the Bali Provincial Government, Bank Indonesia has compiled an **investment catalogue** featuring 22 priority infrastructure projects. Among the most significant:

### Special Economic Zone (SEZ) for Healthcare in Sanur
Bali's flagship project for positioning the island as a global health tourism destination. The Sanur Health SEZ is designed to attract investment in premium hospitals, specialist clinics, and wellness retreats catering to international visitors seeking medical care combined with a world-class travel experience.

### Electric Vehicle and Transport Electrification
A green mobility initiative integrated with the tourism sector. This aligns with Bali's commitment to sustainable tourism and reducing air pollution in high-traffic visitor areas including Kuta, Seminyak, and Ubud.

### Street Lighting Infrastructure
Renewable energy-powered street lighting upgrades across multiple regencies, enhancing safety for tourists and residents while contributing to Bali's low-carbon development goals.

## Government Incentives: The Competitive Edge

A key differentiator highlighted by BI Bali is the **concrete and structured package of government incentives** available to investors. These include streamlined licensing processes, tax relief in priority investment sectors, and legal certainty that protects long-term capital commitments.

*"A strong economic outlook, contained inflation, solid business confidence, active government support, and Bali's outstanding regional competitiveness — all of these factors are creating meaningful opportunities for increased investment,"* said a BI Bali spokesperson.

## The "Bali" Brand: An Asset No Crisis Can Diminish

Unlike many destinations that lose their appeal when global conditions deteriorate, Bali possesses an exceptionally powerful and resilient brand identity. The name "Bali" is globally synonymous with premium tourism, authentic cultural immersion, and spiritual lifestyle — a combination that remains compelling regardless of the external economic climate.

This brand resilience explains why Bali continues to appear on the radar of international investors even during periods of global stress. With a curated portfolio of 22 strategic projects, an active investment forum, and demonstrably strengthening investment performance quarter after quarter through 2026, Bali is positioning itself to attract the next wave of capital that will underpin its economic foundation for the coming decade.

Data from BI also shows that **Bali's investment performance has strengthened consistently** across each quarter of 2026, proving that geopolitical turbulence has not eroded the confidence that businesses and investors place in the island's enduring appeal.
