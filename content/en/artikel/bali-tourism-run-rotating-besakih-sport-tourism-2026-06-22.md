The inaugural **Bali Tourism Run (BTR) 2026**, held on June 21 at the UNESCO World Heritage site of Jatiluwih in Tabanan, has exceeded all expectations — drawing **1,715 runners** from across Indonesia and abroad, and generating up to **Rp 2 billion** in local economic activity. Now, Bali's provincial government is wasting no time: the format is to become a **rotating event**, with the sacred highland complex of **Besakih** identified as the next destination on the circuit.

## A Debut That Exceeded All Expectations

The Bali Tourism Run was launched by ASITA (the Association of Indonesian Travel Agents) Bali as a flagship sport tourism initiative marking **100 Years of Bali Tourism**. Jatiluwih — with its dramatic terraced rice fields recognized as a UNESCO World Cultural Heritage site since 2012 — provided the perfect backdrop for the event's first chapter.

Over approximately one month of open registration, more than 1,700 participants enrolled for the 5-kilometer trail run through Jatiluwih's iconic landscape. ASITA Bali projected the combined festival and race would drive **Rp 1.5 billion to Rp 2 billion in economic circulation**, spanning registration fees, purchases at local SME stalls, food and beverage consumption, and overnight stays in the Tabanan area.

To reinforce direct community benefit, every runner received a **Rp 25,000 shopping voucher** redeemable at one of the 40+ local SME booths — ensuring that event spending translated immediately into revenue for small farmers, food producers, and artisans.

## Sekda Bali: Bali's Beauty Deserves to Be Run Through

The event's success prompted a swift and enthusiastic response from **Bali's Regional Secretary, Dewa Made Indra**, who publicly called for the Bali Tourism Run to adopt a rotating model — moving from one premier destination to another across the island.

"I want this type of nature running event to travel from one tourism area to another. Bali has so much natural beauty that deserves to be promoted through sport tourism," Sekda Dewa Made Indra stated at the event's conclusion.

**Besakih**, the largest and most sacred Hindu temple complex in Bali, has been named as the leading candidate for the next edition. Set dramatically on the slopes of Mount Agung — Bali's highest and most revered volcano — Besakih would offer a running route of an entirely different character: spiritually charged, volcanically dramatic, and scenically breathtaking.

## Tabanan Deputy Regent Eyes More Local Destinations

Beyond the provincial conversation, **Tabanan's Deputy Regent** also expressed interest in developing additional running events across Tabanan's own diverse tourism portfolio. Known as Bali's "rice barn," Tabanan is home to forested highlands, lakeside villages, and cultural landscapes that would each offer a unique trail running experience.

This enthusiasm signals that the Bali Tourism Run model is not a one-off event, but rather a **replicable and scalable formula** that could be adopted by multiple regencies and cities across Bali.

## Sport Tourism: A Growing Global Market

Bali's pivot toward sport tourism is strategically well-timed. According to UN Tourism, sport tourism currently accounts for roughly **10 percent of global tourism spending**, with growth projections of **17.5 percent** through 2030. Indonesia's Ministry of Tourism has prioritized sport tourism as a key lever for regional economic development and international destination marketing.

What makes the Bali Tourism Run concept particularly effective is its **triple impact**: it simultaneously promotes a specific destination, drives local economic activity, and generates organic social media promotion — as thousands of runners share their scenic race photos with global audiences.

With a rotating format across Bali's most iconic landscapes — from the UNESCO-recognized rice terraces of Jatiluwih to the volcanic grandeur of Besakih and beyond — the Bali Tourism Run has the architecture to become one of Southeast Asia's most distinctive annual sporting events. For each destination that hosts it, the benefit is clear: global visibility, economic circulation, and a compelling new reason for visitors to come, run, and return.
