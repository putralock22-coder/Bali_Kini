## Tabanan Regent Opens Jatiluwih Festival VII

Tabanan Regent I Komang Gede Sanjaya officially opened the **7th Jatiluwih Festival 2026** on Saturday, June 20, 2026. The two-day event running through Sunday, June 21, solidifies Jatiluwih Village's standing as one of Bali's most prestigious tourism destinations and a genuine engine of local economic growth.

Regent Sanjaya emphasized that this festival transcends entertainment—it serves as a new economic catalyst for local communities. When thousands of visitors arrive, the benefits flow directly to farmers whose harvests—particularly Jatiluwih's signature organic red rice—sell out rapidly. Local warungs, restaurants, and small business owners all share in the windfall.

## First-Ever Entry into the National Karisma Event Program

The standout achievement of Jatiluwih Festival VII is its selection for the **Karisma Event Nasional (KEN) 2026** program by Indonesia's Ministry of Tourism. This marks the first time this locally rooted Tabanan festival has been recognized as a national flagship event—a milestone that significantly elevates Bali's tourism promotion on the national stage.

KEN status brings direct promotional support from the central government, enabling far broader marketing reach to both domestic and international visitors. The festival carries the theme **"In Balance with Nature, Inspired by Tradition"**—a concept that aligns seamlessly with Bali's current priority of sustainable tourism development.

## Bali Tourism Run: Racing Through UNESCO Heritage

A highlight of this year's festival is the **Bali Tourism Run 2026**—a sport tourism initiative launched in the spirit of Bali's centenary of tourism. Around 2,000 runners were invited to experience the breathtaking panorama of Jatiluwih's terraced rice fields while running through the subak irrigation channels, a **UNESCO World Heritage Site** since 2012.

This five-kilometer run is no ordinary race. Participants experience something rare: the sight of lush green paddies against the backdrop of Mount Batukaru, while gaining an appreciation for the subak system's deep philosophy of balancing humanity, nature, and the divine.

## Full Occupancy and Thriving Local Economy

The economic impact was immediate and measurable. All accommodations in the Jatiluwih area—from family-owned homestays to premium villas—reached **100% occupancy** during the festival. This is concrete evidence that culture- and nature-based events can serve as powerful catalysts for rural economic growth.

An UMKM (small and medium enterprise) exhibition showcasing local culinary offerings and artisan products added to the festive atmosphere. Visitors savored organic red rice dishes, locally grown coffee, and handcrafted traditional goods that reflect the richness of Balinese culture.

## Sustainable Tourism Built on World Heritage

Jatiluwih is no ordinary destination. Its terraced rice fields form part of the **Subak System as a Cultural Landscape of Bali: Forest, Paddy Fields, and Subak Temples**—recognized by UNESCO since 2012. Organizing a festival that prioritizes harmony with nature and local wisdom is a living example of how tourism can flourish without eroding ancestral heritage.

Regent Sanjaya confirmed that the success of Jatiluwih Festival VII will lay the groundwork for developing this event into an increasingly large and internationally recognized annual fixture—while preserving Jatiluwih's identity as a premium destination grounded in environmental and cultural conservation.
