Traffic congestion has long been one of Bali's most persistent headaches, especially along the busy corridor between Ngurah Rai International Airport and the increasingly popular Canggu area. A journey that covers only a few kilometers by road can easily stretch to two hours during peak traffic. Now, the Indonesian government is preparing an innovative solution never seen before in the country: a **water taxi** service that promises to connect the airport and Canggu in just 30 minutes.

## Indonesia's First Water Taxi

The Bali water taxi project will be the **first of its kind in Indonesia** — a landmark step in the transformation of the island's tourism transportation network. Minister of Transportation **Dudy Purwagandhi** has been actively championing the accelerated development of this project, positioning it as a key pillar in Bali's push toward smarter, more efficient mobility.

The water taxi is designed as an integrated alternative connecting land, sea, and air transport, while simultaneously relieving the chronic road congestion that plagues Badung Regency.

> "We want to give tourists and locals alike a fast and comfortable transport option. Water taxis are our answer to the traffic that has been a chronic problem for too long," said Minister Dudy Purwagandhi.

## Priority Route: Sekeh to Berawa (Canggu)

Technical studies have identified the **Sekeh Beach** (adjacent to Ngurah Rai Airport) to **Berawa Beach, Canggu** corridor as the priority first route. This stretch was selected for its immediate impact potential — it directly serves the thousands of tourists who arrive at the airport daily and need to reach Canggu, now a hub for digital nomads, surf culture, and modern tourism.

### Travel Time Comparison

| Mode of Transport | Normal Travel Time | Peak Hour |
|---|---|---|
| Road vehicle | 30–45 minutes | 1–2 hours |
| Water taxi (planned) | Max 30 minutes | Max 30 minutes |

The key advantage of water taxis is **time consistency** — completely unaffected by road traffic conditions. For travelers with tight schedules, this predictability is invaluable.

## Rp1.21 Trillion Investment

The ambitious project carries a price tag of **Rp1.21 trillion** (approximately USD 73 million). The investment will cover:

- **Pier and dock construction** at strategic locations along the route
- **Vessel procurement** for the water taxi fleet
- **Supporting infrastructure** including operational systems, navigation technology, and maritime safety facilities
- **Passenger facility development** at Celukan Bawang Port in Buleleng Regency

State-owned ferry company **PT ASDP Indonesia Ferry** has been designated to lead implementation and is currently finalizing the *Detailed Engineering Design* (DED) — the comprehensive technical blueprint required before construction can begin.

## Construction Timeline

The project is planned in three stages:

1. **DED Phase (Current):** PT ASDP finalizing technical designs
2. **Construction Phase:** From **August 2026** through **July 2027**
3. **Trial Operations:** Planned to begin **late 2026** on a phased basis

The government is optimistic that with streamlined procurement and design processes, the service could potentially launch ahead of schedule.

## Celukan Bawang Port Development

Beyond the airport-Canggu route, the project also includes developing **Celukan Bawang Port** in North Bali's Buleleng Regency. Pier I will handle general cargo and liquid asphalt vessels with a 92-meter extension, while Pier II will be developed as a multipurpose facility serving passengers, general cargo, and dry bulk shipments.

This northern Bali development aligns with the broader policy objective of distributing tourist flows beyond the heavily congested southern Bali corridor.

## Bali's Chronic Traffic Problem

Traffic congestion has become a defining challenge for Bali's modern tourism industry. The commute from Ngurah Rai Airport to Canggu routinely tops 90 minutes to two hours during peak periods — a frustration consistently flagged by international travelers on review platforms and in tourism surveys.

The severity of the issue has forced the government to look beyond road-based solutions. In addition to water taxis, authorities are also evaluating potential MRT routes and dedicated cycling lanes at strategic points across the island.

### Expected Benefits

- **Tourists:** Faster, more comfortable, traffic-free journeys
- **Digital nomads:** Seamless access to Canggu — Bali's co-working epicenter
- **Local residents:** An efficient daily transport alternative
- **Environment:** Reduced vehicular emissions on congested roads

## Challenges Ahead

Transportation analysts have welcomed the initiative while also pointing to challenges that must be addressed — including maritime safety regulations, the impact of ocean swells on vessel operations, and the need for seamless integration between the water taxi terminals and onward land transport.

The Bali Provincial Government and the Ministry of Transportation have pledged to resolve these technical and regulatory hurdles to ensure the water taxi service operates safely and reliably.

## A Maritime Tourism Pioneer

If successful, Bali's water taxi will be far more than a traffic solution. It will position the island as **Indonesia's pioneer in maritime tourism transport** — a model that other congested destinations across the archipelago could eventually follow.

With construction set to begin in just a few months, the countdown is on for Bali to redefine how visitors move through the Island of the Gods.
