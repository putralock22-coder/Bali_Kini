Indonesia's Corruption Eradication Commission (KPK) conducted a seven-hour raid on the Class I Immigration Office (Kantor Imigrasi Kelas I TPI) in Denpasar, Bali, on Friday, June 19, 2026. The operation is part of an ongoing investigation into alleged corruption and extortion in the processing of immigration documents for foreign nationals, with estimated losses reaching Rp145.5 billion.

KPK investigators departed the premises carrying three suitcases filled with documents and key evidence. The search was also expanded to two visa service agencies in Bali: PT Visa Empat Bali and CV Visa Agung Bali Teratai Promanende, suggesting the involvement of private intermediaries in the scheme.

## How It All Started: A Sting Operation

The case traces back to an undercover sting operation (OTT) conducted by KPK on June 2–3, 2026. Within 24 hours, on June 4, KPK formally named eight individuals as suspects. The suspects are accused of extorting foreign nationals who sought immigration services in Indonesia between 2022 and 2026.

The accused are connected to what was formerly the Ministry of Law and Human Rights, which has since been reorganized into the Ministry of Immigration and Correctional Institutions. The most prominent suspect in the case is **Silmy Karim**, currently serving as Deputy Minister of Immigration and Correctional Institutions, and previously Director General of Immigration from 2023 to 2024.

## Who Are the Other Suspects?

Two other key suspects have been identified:

- **Saffar Muhammad Godam** — Acting Director General of Immigration (2024–2025)
- **Jaya Saputra** — Head of the West Java Regional Office of the Directorate General of Immigration, formerly Director of Residence Permits and Immigration Status (2024–2025)

The alleged scheme involved systematically levying illegal fees on foreign nationals applying for various immigration documents — from visit visas and temporary residence permits (KITAS) to permanent residence cards (KITAP). The total illegal proceeds from this practice are estimated at Rp145.5 billion over four years.

## Raids Expand Across Bali

Between June 17 and 19, 2026, KPK broadened its investigation to Bali, a region with a high concentration of foreign residents and long-stay visitors. In addition to raiding the Denpasar Immigration Office, investigators also searched the two private visa agencies, indicating the alleged extortion network extended beyond government offices into the private sector.

## Impact on Bali's Expat and Tourism Ecosystem

Bali is home to one of the largest communities of foreign nationals in Indonesia. Every year, thousands of digital nomads, retirees, investors, and long-stay tourists navigate the island's immigration system. Revelations of systemic extortion within this system raise serious concerns about the integrity of Bali's reputation as a welcoming and safe destination for foreigners.

Business owners and the expat community in Bali hope the KPK's investigation will serve as a catalyst for reforming immigration services — making them more transparent, accessible, and free from corruption.

KPK has confirmed that the investigation will continue, with the possibility of additional suspects and charges as evidence from the Bali raids is analyzed.
