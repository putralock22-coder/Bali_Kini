## Three Days. 60,000 Visitors. One MURI Record.

Bank Indonesia's Bali Representative Office (KPw BI) has wrapped up the 7th edition of Bali Jagadhita — Bali's premier integrated economic promotion event — after three days at Mal Bali Galeria, Kuta, June 5–7, 2026. The results were outstanding: **60,000 visitors**, **Rp1.9 billion** in direct on-site transactions, and a **MURI world record** for the largest-ever participatory creation of endek-motif painted tote bags.

The record-breaking tote bag event — blending Balinese textile tradition with green economy messaging — became an unlikely symbol of what Bali Jagadhita VII was all about: economic ambition grounded in cultural identity and environmental responsibility.

## Theme: "Suddha Bumi Parahita"

Bali Jagadhita VII 2026 was organized under the theme **"Suddha Bumi Parahita"**, asserting that economic development must be anchored in environmental stewardship. The theme reflects Bali's governing vision, *Nangun Sat Kerthi Loka Bali* — sustaining the harmony of nature, humanity, and Balinese culture across generations.

Bank Indonesia used the event to drive the adoption of green economy principles across Bali's business community, from major investors down to small artisans and food vendors.

## Three Pillars, Three Standout Results

### Trade and SMEs

More than **60 small and medium enterprises (SMEs)** from across Bali and Nusa Tenggara participated in the trade exhibition, representing food and beverage, crafts, fashion, and the creative sector. Total SME export potential identified and recorded during the event: **Rp23.03 billion**. These businesses are being supported in their transition to become export-oriented, globally competitive enterprises.

### Quality Tourism

The **12th Bali and Beyond Travel Fair (BBTF)**, integrated within Jagadhita, attracted **407 international buyers from 44 countries** and **208 tourism sellers from 12 Indonesian provinces**. Total confirmed tourism business deals: a remarkable **Rp6.9 trillion** — underscoring Bali's enduring pull as a global travel destination.

### Sustainable Investment

Investment forums focused on green infrastructure projects, eco-tourism development, and long-term economic diversification strategies for Bali in an era of shifting global conditions.

## Green Economy Activities That Drew Crowds

Beyond the numbers, Bali Jagadhita VII filled three days with hands-on sustainability programming:

- **Eco-fashion runway show** — produced in collaboration with the Bali Provincial Craft Council (Dekranasda)
- **Waste management education booths** and live sustainability talks
- **Upcycled bag-making competition** from repurposed materials
- **Endek motif painting contest** celebrating Bali's traditional weaving heritage
- **Traditional gebogan flower offering competition**
- **Fish-based cooking competition** promoting local Balinese ingredients

## Bali's Economic Outlook for Q2 2026

Bank Indonesia projects Bali's economy will grow positively in the second quarter of 2026, driven by school holiday demand, the agricultural harvest season, and government infrastructure investments coming online. Bali Jagadhita VII served as a powerful catalyst — boosting SME capacity, driving tourism business, and demonstrating that sustainability and economic growth are not competing forces, but complementary ones.
