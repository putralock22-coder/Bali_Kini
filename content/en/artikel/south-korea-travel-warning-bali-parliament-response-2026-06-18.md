## Travel Warning Shakes Bali's International Tourism Image

The South Korean government issued an official travel warning (*travel warning*) for Bali in early April 2026. The formal announcement from the South Korean Embassy was published on April 14, 2026, citing a series of serious security incidents involving foreign nationals in several of Bali's most popular tourist areas.

The embassy's report highlighted cases including kidnapping, severe physical violence, and sexual harassment against foreign tourists. The areas specifically named in the warning include **Jimbaran**, **Seminyak**, and **Canggu** — three neighborhoods that have long been among the busiest international tourist hubs on the island.

## Parliament Calls on Tourism Ministry to Take the Lead

Member of the Indonesian House of Representatives (DPR) Commission VII, **Bambang Haryo Soekartono**, responded swiftly, calling on the Ministry of Tourism (Kemenpar) to immediately take charge of coordinating Bali's image recovery. He assessed that cross-sector coordination in protecting tourist safety has remained dangerously weak.

> "The Ministry of Tourism must act as the leading sector in resolving this image crisis. This isn't just about security — it's about the trust of millions of tourists who choose Bali as their primary destination."

Bambang stressed that without a firm and rapid response, one country's travel warning could trigger a domino effect, prompting other major source markets to issue similar advisories.

## Government Response: Coordination with Foreign Affairs Ministry

Deputy Minister of Tourism **Ni Luh Puspa** stated that the Indonesian government has been maintaining close coordination with the Ministry of Foreign Affairs regarding South Korea's travel advisory. Follow-up measures are being formulated to reassure the international community that Bali remains a safe destination.

The Deputy Minister also emphasized that these isolated incidents do not reflect the overall security situation in Bali, and that police and immigration authorities have been stepped up their presence in the affected tourist zones.

## Why the South Korean Market Matters

South Korea is one of Bali's significant international source markets. Korean tourists are known for their relatively high spending patterns, particularly in dining, shopping, and entertainment. A decline in Korean visitor numbers resulting from the travel warning could have a tangible economic impact on businesses in Seminyak, Canggu, and Jimbaran.

Bali's visitor arrivals in the first quarter of 2026 recorded 1.47 million trips — an 11 percent decline compared to Q4 2025. This backdrop makes a swift and credible response to South Korea's travel warning all the more urgent.

## What Parliament Is Demanding

The DPR is pushing for several concrete steps to be implemented without delay:

- **Strengthened immigration oversight** in high-risk tourist areas
- **Integrated security patrols** involving the National Police (Polri), Tourism Offices, and industry associations
- **International communications campaigns** to counter negative narratives about Bali's safety
- **Diplomatic dialogue** with embassies of major tourist source countries to present accurate information

### Bali Still Ranks at the Top

Despite the travel warning, Bali's global standing remains strong. In early 2026, TripAdvisor named Bali the **world's best destination** in its *Travelers' Choice Awards Best of the Best*, outranking London, Dubai, Hanoi, Paris, and Rome. This prestigious recognition serves as a powerful asset for the government as it works to restore international confidence.

Both the Bali provincial government and the central government now face pressure to translate words into action — ensuring that South Korea's travel warning does not evolve into a broader erosion of Bali's hard-won reputation as the world's favorite island.
