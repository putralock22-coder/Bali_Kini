July 2026 is one of the richest months in Bali's cultural calendar. As the island reaches peak season under clear, dry skies, a string of vibrant festivals gives visitors far more to experience than beaches and temples. Here is a complete guide to Bali's major cultural events in July 2026.

## Jatiluwih Festival VII — July 18–19, 2026

**Venue:** Jatiluwih Rice Terraces, Tabanan, Bali

The Jatiluwih Festival returns for its seventh edition, staged amid the sweeping green steps of the **UNESCO World Heritage-listed Jatiluwih rice terraces**. The event weaves together the beauty of Bali's traditional subak irrigation system, local agricultural knowledge, and living arts into a two-day celebration unlike anything else on the island.

### What's On

- **Traditional farming demonstrations** showcasing the subak system that has sustained Balinese rice cultivation for centuries
- **Nature photography exhibitions** featuring work from local and international photographers inspired by the Jatiluwih landscape
- **Painting competitions** open to artists from across Bali and Indonesia
- **Balinese performing arts** — traditional dance, gamelan music, and evening stage shows
- **Bali Tourism Run (5K)** — a scenic run along rice paddy paths, popular with sporty visitors

### Why Visit

Jatiluwih offers a quieter, more expansive alternative to the Tegalalang terraces near Ubud. Located roughly 35 km from Denpasar in Tabanan regency, the area sees fewer visitors outside festival time, making the July event an ideal opportunity to witness the landscape and culture at their most animated.

## Lovina Festival — July 24–26, 2026

**Venue:** Kalibukbuk and Kaliasem villages, Buleleng, North Bali

The Lovina Festival shines a spotlight on the culture and coastline of North Bali — a region that travelers rushing between Seminyak and Ubud often overlook. Set against the backdrop of Lovina's distinctive black-sand beach and famous dolphin-watching waters, the three-day festival is a genuine community celebration.

### Programme Highlights

- **Collaborative music performances** bringing together musicians from across Bali
- **Bungbung traditional dance** — a rare performance form rooted in North Bali's heritage
- **Wayang kulit (shadow puppet) shows** — a centuries-old storytelling tradition
- **Cultural parade** through the villages of Kalibukbuk and Kaliasem featuring traditional costumes and gamelan processions
- **Beach party at Kaliasem Beach** with a fireworks display as the grand finale
- **Local handicraft and food fair** showcasing Buleleng's artisans and culinary traditions

### Beyond the Festival

Attending the Lovina Festival is the perfect excuse to explore North Bali for a few days. The area is home to:
- **Dolphin-watching tours** departing at dawn from Lovina Beach
- **Pura Beji at Sangsit** — a stunning Balinese temple known for intricate red-brick carvings
- **Pulau Menjangan** (Menjangan Island) — one of Bali's best diving spots, a short drive west
- **Gitgit Waterfall** near Singaraja for a refreshing mid-trip detour

## Bali International Choir Festival — July 26–30, 2026

**Venue:** Multiple venues across Bali

Closing out the month on a soaring note, the **Bali International Choir Festival (BICF)** gathers choral groups from dozens of countries for five days of competition and collaboration. The festival is among Southeast Asia's most prestigious choral events and draws ensembles ranging from children's choirs and youth groups to mixed adult and chamber choirs.

Concerts and competitions take place at venues across Bali, often set within striking cultural and natural backdrops that amplify the already-powerful experience of live choral music.

## FOTO Bali Festival — Closing July 12, 2026

**Venue:** Nuanu Creative City, Tabanan

If you arrive in Bali before mid-July, catch the final days of the **FOTO Bali Festival**, an international photography festival running through **July 12** at Nuanu Creative City in Tabanan. The festival presents large-format exhibitions by world-class photographers exploring themes of nature, culture, and the human experience.

## Tips for Attending Bali Festivals in July

1. **Book accommodation early.** July is peak season across Bali. Hotels near festival venues — especially in Tabanan and Buleleng — fill up quickly. Aim to book at least four to six weeks ahead.
2. **Arrive early in the day.** Bali in July is warm and sunny. Morning arrivals beat the midday heat and let you explore the surroundings before crowds build.
3. **Dress respectfully.** Some festival events are intertwined with Balinese religious tradition. Wearing a sarong or covering your shoulders at ceremonial areas is expected.
4. **Bring cash.** Local food stalls, craft vendors, and small ticket booths at these festivals rarely accept digital payments.
5. **Explore beyond the crowds.** Attending a festival in Tabanan or Buleleng is an ideal launch point for exploring parts of Bali that see far fewer tourists than the southern resort belt.

## Part of Bali's 2026 Calendar of Events

These festivals are official entries in **Bali's Calendar of Events 2026**, a government-curated program featuring **56 festivals and cultural agendas** designed to distribute tourism more evenly across Bali's nine regencies and one city. The initiative reflects Bali's broader push for quality-driven, culturally enriching tourism rather than mass volume.

With so much on the programme in July, this is the month to look beyond the obvious and discover what makes Bali's cultural life so enduringly compelling.
