The 48th Pesta Kesenian Bali (PKB XLVIII) — Bali's premier annual cultural festival held at Taman Budaya Bali (Art Center) in Denpasar — is proving to be more than a showcase of traditional arts. This year, it has also become a powerful economic engine for the island's culinary small and medium enterprises (SMEs, or *UMKM* in Indonesian).

## Rotating Stall System: A New Model for Shared Prosperity

A standout feature of PKB 2026 is the newly introduced **rotating stall system** for the culinary zone, implemented by the Bali Provincial Office of Cooperatives and SMEs (Diskop UKM). Rather than allowing the same set of vendors to occupy stalls for the entire duration of the festival, the organizers divided participation into two batches of 36 vendors each — bringing the total number of participating culinary SMEs to **72**.

This model was specifically designed to extend economic benefits to more small business owners rather than concentrating them among a select few.

### Batch One: Rp2.5 Billion in 15 Days

The first batch of vendors operated from **June 13 to June 27, 2026** — the opening two weeks of PKB. In that window, 36 culinary SMEs collectively recorded transactions totalling **Rp2,507,173,000** (approximately US$155,000), surpassing expectations and setting a new benchmark for culinary revenue at the festival.

The most popular food items during this period included **babi guling** (Balinese roast suckling pig), **cendol daluman** (a traditional green jelly drink), **laklak** (Balinese rice pancakes), and a range of other traditional Balinese snacks that drew long queues from both domestic tourists and foreign visitors.

### Batch Two: New Faces, Same Flavors

Starting **June 28 through July 13, 2026**, the second batch of 36 vendors took over. To maintain consistency for festival-goers, organizers ensured that each stall continued to offer the same type of cuisine as before — only the vendors themselves changed. A babi guling stall, for example, would still sell babi guling, just operated by a different business owner.

Stall handovers are conducted at night to avoid disrupting daytime and evening trading activities.

## Food Safety Inspections in Progress

To uphold hygiene standards, all second-batch vendors were scheduled to undergo inspection by the **National Agency of Drug and Food Control (BPOM)** on Thursday, July 2, 2026. This forms part of the routine food safety oversight system established by the festival committee.

## PKB as an Economic Platform

Officials from Diskop UKM emphasized that PKB is not just a cultural celebration — it is a genuine economic platform for ordinary Balinese people. By bringing in 72 SMEs across two batches, the 2026 edition distributes economic opportunity more widely across the island's local business community.

If second-batch sales match the pace set by the first, total culinary transactions at PKB 2026 could surpass **Rp5 billion** by the festival's close on July 13 — a remarkable figure that demonstrates how cultural preservation and grassroots economic empowerment can go hand in hand.
