The **Financial Services Authority (OJK)** Regional Office VIII for Bali and Nusa Tenggara has reported a striking milestone in the island's capital market: the total value of stock ownership in Bali reached **Rp7.95 trillion** (approximately USD 490 million) in the first quarter of 2026 — a sharp jump that signals the province's deepening engagement with formal financial markets.

## 48 Percent Growth in One Year

Compared to the same period in 2025, when stock ownership stood at Rp5.35 trillion, the figure has grown by **48.40 percent** year-on-year. In absolute terms, Bali investors collectively added over Rp2.6 trillion in market exposure within just 12 months.

OJK Bali Head **Parjiman** acknowledged the strong performance while also flagging the context: global economic uncertainty, particularly the fallout from ongoing Middle East geopolitical tensions, had created headwinds for emerging market sentiment broadly. Despite this, Bali's investor community held firm.

"Even with global pressure, investor confidence in Bali's capital market remains positive," Parjiman said on Wednesday, June 18, 2026.

## Over 392,000 Active Investors

As of March 2026, the total number of investors in Bali based on *Single Investor Identification* (SID) reached **392,841 SID** — up nearly 30 percent from approximately 302,000 SID recorded in March 2025.

Breaking down by asset class:

- **Mutual funds (Reksadana)**: 369,562 investors — the largest segment
- **Stocks (Saham)**: 199,394 investors
- **Government Securities (SBN)**: 34,085 investors

The dominance of mutual funds suggests that many Balinese are using managed, lower-risk instruments as their first foray into investing — a typical pattern in markets undergoing rapid financial inclusion expansion. However, the growth in direct stock investors indicates a maturing investor base seeking higher returns.

## Bali's Financial Sector Remains Stable

The capital market data forms part of OJK's broader quarterly assessment of Bali's financial services industry (IJK). Banking, insurance, and financing sectors were all described as solid through end of March 2026, giving OJK confidence in the province's overall financial health.

OJK continues to flag the risk of **illegal investment schemes** targeting communities in tourist-heavy areas like Bali, where high disposable income and financial newcomers can attract fraudulent operators. Residents and tourists alike are urged to verify any investment product or platform via OJK's official channels before committing funds.

## Why This Matters for Bali's Economic Future

Bali's economy grew **5.58 percent** in Q1 2026, outpacing the national average. A rising investor class deepens that economic base — channeling private savings into productive assets, building a local ownership stake in Indonesia's broader economy, and creating a buffer against the island's traditional over-reliance on tourism revenues.

OJK's national goal is for at least **25 percent of each province's population** to hold a registered investor account by the end of 2026. With a growing tech-savvy, entrepreneurial community and a tourism industry that attracts mobile capital, Bali is well positioned to meet — or exceed — that benchmark.
