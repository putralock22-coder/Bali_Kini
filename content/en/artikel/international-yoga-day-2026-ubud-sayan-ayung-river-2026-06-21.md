## 72 Elementary Students Celebrate Yoga Day in Bali's Iconic River

On Sunday, June 21, 2026, Sayan Village in Ubud District, Gianyar Regency, became the setting for a truly memorable celebration of the 12th International Day of Yoga. A total of 72 students from SD Negeri 1 and SD Negeri 2 Sayan were invited to practice yoga in an extraordinary location: the middle of the flowing Ayung River.

The initiative was organized by Bambu Indah, a nature-based boutique resort nestled in the lush hills of Sayan, Ubud. The resort brought in local yoga instructors to guide the young students through foundational yoga poses, breathing exercises, and mindfulness practices — all while surrounded by the sound of flowing water and tropical birdsong.

## An Unforgettable Setting: Between River Rocks and Giant Trees

What made this yoga session truly exceptional was its setting. Rather than a conventional studio or school hall, the students gathered in an open area within the Ayung River itself — one of Bali's most iconic rivers, carving its way through the verdant valleys that define the Ubud landscape.

Encircled by towering trees and untouched nature, the students found themselves immersed in a living meditation. The combination of gentle yoga movement, the sound of flowing water, and the fresh morning air of Ubud created an atmosphere that no indoor studio could replicate.

Bambu Indah, long recognized as a pioneer of eco-conscious hospitality in Bali, deliberately chose this riverside location to embody yoga's core philosophy: the union of body, mind, and nature.

## 2026 Theme: "Yoga for Healthy Ageing"

This year's International Day of Yoga carries the theme **"Yoga for Healthy Ageing"** — a message from the United Nations emphasizing yoga's role in supporting physical and mental well-being across all stages of life.

While the 2026 theme focuses on healthy aging, the Ubud celebration wisely extended the message to children, reflecting the belief that the earlier young people are introduced to yoga, the stronger the foundation for lifelong health and emotional resilience they will build.

### Why June 21? The Power of the Summer Solstice

International Yoga Day is celebrated every June 21 because this date marks the summer solstice in the Northern Hemisphere — the longest day of the year, considered sacred in many ancient and yogic traditions. Since the United Nations declared June 21 as the International Day of Yoga in 2014, the date has been observed by millions across more than 180 countries.

## Ubud: The Global Capital of Yoga and Wellness

It comes as no surprise that one of the most creative celebrations of International Yoga Day took place in Ubud. Long regarded as Asia's premier wellness destination, Ubud has attracted yoga teachers, spiritual seekers, wellness entrepreneurs, and retreat centers from around the world for decades.

The presence of Bambu Indah in Sayan — known for drawing international guests seeking authentic, nature-immersed experiences — adds a cross-cultural dimension to this local event, bridging the village community with the global wellness movement.

## Bali's Commitment to Wellness Tourism

Events like this align perfectly with Bali's broader ambition to develop **wellness tourism** as a pillar of quality, sustainable tourism. As mass tourism gives way to more meaningful, experience-driven travel, Bali's natural beauty, spiritual depth, and wellness culture make it uniquely positioned to attract high-value visitors seeking genuine transformation.

For the 72 young students of Sayan who bent, breathed, and balanced on the banks of the Ayung River that Sunday morning, the day's lesson was simpler but no less profound: that health, joy, and peace can be found anywhere — even in the middle of a river.
