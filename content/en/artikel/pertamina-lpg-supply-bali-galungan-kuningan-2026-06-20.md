As Bali prepared for two of its most sacred Hindu holidays — Galungan on June 17 and Kuningan on June 27, 2026 — state energy company PT Pertamina Patra Niaga rolled out a major supplemental LPG distribution across the island. A total of 258,160 cylinders of 3-kilogram LPG were made available through an "extra dropping" scheme — supply beyond the regular daily quota — to ensure stable energy access for households throughout both holidays.

## Why LPG Demand Surges During Galungan

The Galungan-Kuningan cycle is among the most important in the Balinese Hindu calendar. Households prepare elaborate ceremonial meals and offerings for ancestors and deities, leading to a dramatic surge in cooking activity and, consequently, LPG consumption. Pertamina's distribution boost — reaching 95 percent above standard daily supply volumes — was designed precisely to absorb this predictable demand spike.

Ahad Rahedi, Area Manager Communication, Relations & CSR for Pertamina Patra Niaga's East Java-Bali-Nusa Tenggara (Jatimbalinus) region, confirmed that the preparations began well ahead of the holidays. All supply chain infrastructure — from regional depots to local retail agents and neighborhood distribution points — was reinforced with on-standby technical personnel.

## No Need to Panic Buy

Pertamina urged the public to remain calm and purchase only according to their actual needs. Panic buying, the company warned, can create artificial shortages and disrupt supply for other consumers. With stocks confirmed as adequate and logistics teams on alert, households were reassured that LPG would be consistently available across Bali throughout both holiday periods.

## Fuel Supply Also Secured

Beyond cooking gas, Pertamina also confirmed adequate vehicle fuel stocks for the Galungan-Kuningan period. Bali typically sees a significant surge in road mobility during major religious holidays, compounded by the influx of domestic tourists taking advantage of the extended holiday break to visit the island.

## About Galungan and Kuningan

Galungan commemorates the victory of dharma (righteousness) over adharma (evil) in Balinese Hindu tradition. The celebration involves elaborate temple ceremonies, family prayers, and the iconic penjor — tall bamboo poles decorated with woven coconut leaves and fruit — displayed in front of every home as symbols of gratitude and reverence for ancestors.

Kuningan, celebrated ten days after Galungan, marks the moment when divine spirits and ancestral souls return to the heavens. The day is observed with final offerings and prayers at household and village temples across the island.

With energy supply thoroughly secured by Pertamina, Bali's 2026 Galungan-Kuningan season is expected to proceed smoothly and with full community participation across the island.
