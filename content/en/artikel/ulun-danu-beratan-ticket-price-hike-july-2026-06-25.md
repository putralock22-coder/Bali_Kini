Visitors planning a trip to one of Bali's most photographed landmarks have less than a week to take advantage of current entry prices. The Ulun Danu Beratan tourist destination (DTW) in Bedugul, Tabanan Regency, will officially implement new, higher admission rates from **July 1, 2026**.

## New Ticket Price Breakdown

The price adjustments apply to all visitor categories. Here is a full comparison of old and new rates:

### Domestic Visitors

| Category | Old Price | New Price (from July 1) |
|---|---|---|
| Adult (weekdays) | Rp40,000 | Rp50,000 |
| Adult (weekends/holidays) | Rp50,000 | Rp50,000 |
| Children | Rp20,000 | Rp25,000 |

One notable change for local visitors is the **standardization of adult admission** to a flat Rp50,000 — eliminating the previous weekday discount and creating a single unified rate regardless of the day of visit.

### Foreign Visitors

| Category | Old Price | New Price (from July 1) |
|---|---|---|
| Adult | Rp75,000 | Rp100,000 |
| Children | Rp50,000 | Rp75,000 |

Foreign adult admission rises by **Rp25,000**, representing an increase of approximately 33 percent over the previous rate.

## Why the Increase?

The management of Ulun Danu Beratan has stated that the additional revenue generated by the price adjustment will be directed toward three key areas:

- **Improving visitor services** and overall experience quality
- **Better site management** and environmental stewardship
- **Developing cultural attractions** within the tourist area

The move aligns with Bali's broader tourism strategy of shifting from a high-volume, low-spending model to one centered on **quality tourism** — attracting visitors who stay longer and spend more per visit.

## About Ulun Danu Beratan

Pura Ulun Danu Beratan is a Hindu temple complex perched on the shores of Lake Beratan in Bali's cool highland region of Bedugul. Built in the 17th century, the temple is dedicated to Dewi Danu — the Balinese Hindu goddess of water, lakes, and rivers.

The temple's iconic silhouette appearing to float on the misty lake surface, framed by lush mountain greenery, has made it one of the most recognized and photographed sights on the island. It has even appeared on Indonesia's older paper currency.

### What's on Offer at the Site

Beyond the iconic temple views, the Ulun Danu Beratan complex offers a range of attractions and facilities:
- Expansive, well-maintained flower gardens
- Traditional rowing boats for lake excursions
- The distinctive Prabha Ing Ranu traditional dance performance
- Souvenir shops and local food stalls

The upcoming price increase aims to provide sustainable funding for maintaining and upgrading all of these facilities.

For visitors hoping to visit before the new rates take effect, there are just a few days left in June 2026 to experience Ulun Danu Beratan at the current admission prices.
