One of the world's best tourism villages is about to host its most anticipated annual event. **Penglipuran Village Festival XIII** kicks off tomorrow, Thursday July 9, 2026, and runs through Saturday July 11 in the traditional village of Penglipuran, Bangli Regency. Now in its 13th edition, the festival is set to become one of Bali's most vibrant cultural events of the 2026 school holiday season.

## Theme: Harmony of the Earth

This year's festival carries the theme **"Harmoni Bhumi Penglipuran: Menuju Pariwisata Inklusif, Berkelanjutan dan Regeneratif"** (Harmony of Penglipuran's Earth: Towards Inclusive, Sustainable, and Regenerative Tourism). The theme is an invitation for visitors to experience tourism not as passive consumption, but as a meaningful exchange that enriches both guest and host.

Festival organizers say the theme emerged from a collective reflection on the pressures of modern mass tourism — and Penglipuran's commitment to proving that a traditional Balinese village can thrive as a world-class destination without losing its cultural soul.

## The 4S Framework

Penglipuran Village Festival XIII is structured around the **4S concept**:
- **Something to Do** — hands-on cultural activities and workshops
- **Something to See** — traditional Balinese performing arts
- **Something to Buy** — local UMKM products, culinary specialties, handicrafts, and souvenirs
- **Something to Learn** — education on Balinese culture, ecology, and the Tri Hita Karana philosophy

### Three-Day Programme

**Day 1 — Thursday, July 9**
The opening day features a formal welcome ceremony followed by a **kolosal (mass) dance** and a **gebogan parade** — a traditional Balinese offering procession involving hundreds of village residents. The evening spotlight falls on a performance of **Tari Palegongan "Raksan Gumi"**, a philosophical Balinese court dance rarely seen outside of ceremonial contexts. The UMKM exhibition also opens on day one.

**Day 2 — Friday, July 10**
The second day shifts to education and creativity, with a **environmental workshop** exploring traditional Balinese agriculture, a **vintage costume competition** (*lomba busana tempoe doloe*) showcasing traditional attire across different eras, and an evening of local music performances.

**Day 3 — Saturday, July 11**
The festival closes on a joyful note with **Laughing Yoga** (*Yoga Tertawa*) — a symbol of health, community spirit, and positive energy — followed by a final local music showcase.

## 4,000 Visitors Targeted Over Three Days

Organizers are targeting **3,000 to 4,000 visitors** across the three-day event. The momentum is already strong: during the current school holiday period, Penglipuran has been welcoming an average of **3,500 visitors per day** — up dramatically from its normal daily average of around 2,000.

The festival is primarily aimed at domestic tourists seeking an authentic cultural experience during the school break, while remaining open to international visitors eager for a deeper understanding of Bali's village life.

### Economic Benefits for the Community

The festival is designed so that economic benefits flow directly to Penglipuran's residents through food stalls, handicraft markets, and village-based experience packages. This approach reflects the broader vision of regenerative tourism — one where the local community is not merely a backdrop, but the primary actor and beneficiary.

Penglipuran Village Festival XIII stands as living proof that tradition and modern tourism development can coexist — harmoniously, just as its theme suggests.
