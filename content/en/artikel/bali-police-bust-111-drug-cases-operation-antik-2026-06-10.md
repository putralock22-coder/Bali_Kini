The Bali Regional Police (Polda Bali) has released the results of **Operation Antik Agung 2026**, a 16-day intensive anti-narcotics campaign that ran from **May 13 to 28, 2026**. The operation delivered significant results: **111 narcotics cases uncovered**, **138 suspects arrested**, and illegal drugs worth **Rp 13.15 billion** confiscated and subsequently destroyed.

## Case and Arrest Breakdown

Of the 111 total cases, **74 were pre-planned targeted operations**—networks and individuals already identified through prior intelligence—while **37 were non-targeted cases** discovered incidentally during patrols. This distinction highlights the broad vigilance maintained by officers throughout the operation, well beyond the planned scope.

Among the 138 arrested suspects, **77 were operation targets** and **61 were non-target arrests**. Suspects ranged from active distributors and dealers to buyers and brokers operating across multiple areas of the island.

## What Was Seized and Destroyed

The full inventory of confiscated narcotics was subsequently destroyed in a formal ceremony. The breakdown by substance:

| Substance | Quantity |
|-----------|----------|
| Methamphetamine (sabu) | 6,279.22 grams |
| Cannabis (ganja) | 2,123.41 grams |
| Mephedrone | 937 tablets |
| Sedative pills (pil koplo) | 1,282 tablets |
| Ecstasy | 584 tablets |
| Synthetic cannabis | 53.46 grams |
| Cocaine | 23.5 grams |

The total destruction of these drugs is estimated to have **protected 40,846 people** from potential narcotics abuse.

## International Supply Chains Exposed

Beyond local distribution networks, the operation uncovered evidence of **mephedrone being smuggled from abroad via air transport** for distribution within Bali. This finding underscores that Bali's narcotics problem is not purely domestic—international supply chains are actively funneling drugs onto the island through its busy airport infrastructure.

In one notable regional operation, the Buleleng Police successfully **intercepted a cocaine distribution network in Desa Tajun**—suggesting that harder drugs are now reaching communities well outside Bali's main tourist corridors.

## Bali's Vulnerability as an International Destination

Bali's global profile as a premier tourist destination makes it an attractive market for drug syndicates. In a separate earlier operation, Polda Bali had already exposed two narcotics trafficking cases involving foreign nationals, with a combined street value of **Rp 19.8 billion**—confirming that international networks view Bali as a viable marketplace.

With Operation Antik Agung 2026 concluded, Polda Bali has reaffirmed its commitment to sustained enforcement. Protecting Bali from narcotics is not only a public safety priority but also essential to maintaining the island's reputation as a safe, welcoming destination for tourists from around the world.
