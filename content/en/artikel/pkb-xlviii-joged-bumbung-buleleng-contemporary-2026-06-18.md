## Buleleng Steals the Show at PKB XLVIII

Sunday, June 14, 2026 — The performance arena of **Kalangan Madya Mandala Ardha Candra** at Taman Budaya Bali (Bali Cultural Park) in Denpasar erupted with applause as **Sanggar Seni Werdhi Suara** took the stage. The arts group, hailing from Banjar Dinas Unusan, Gobleg Village, Banjar District, Buleleng Regency, had come to represent their region at the **48th Bali Arts Festival (PKB XLVIII 2026)** — and they delivered something special: a revitalized Joged Bumbung that bridged the old and the new.

Among the hundreds of enthusiastic audience members were Bali Governor **Wayan Koster** and Brigadier General **Ida I Dewa Agung Hadisaputra** (Commander of Regional Military Command 163/Wirasatya), who watched the performance with visible admiration.

## Reviving a Nearly Forgotten Tradition

**Joged Bumbung** is a traditional Balinese social dance performed to music created predominantly from **bumbung** — hollow bamboo tubes that produce a distinctive resonating sound when struck. In Buleleng (North Bali), this dance form developed its own regional identity during the **1960s**, featuring unique movements and musical arrangements different from those found in the south.

But like many traditional art forms, Joged Bumbung gradually faded from public consciousness as younger generations gravitated toward modern entertainment. PKB XLVIII 2026 became the perfect stage for Sanggar Seni Werdhi Suara to breathe new life into this near-forgotten tradition.

"We went back to the roots — studying the original form of Buleleng's Joged Bumbung by engaging directly with senior artists who still remember how it used to be," said **Gede Nikolas Candra Wiadi**, the group's percussion arranger (Penata Tabuh).

## Ancient Instruments, Fresh Sound

What made this performance truly distinctive was the deliberate incorporation of two rare ancient instruments: the **genggong** and the **mandolin**.

The **genggong** is a jaw harp-like instrument made from the rib of a sugar palm leaf. Held near the lips, it creates sound through the resonance of the mouth cavity and a plucked tongue of material — producing a uniquely ethereal, buzzing tone that is rarely heard on major performance stages today.

The **mandolin** in this context refers to a traditional Balinese string instrument — distinct from its Western counterpart — with a warm, folkloric sound that complements the bamboo percussion of the bumbung ensemble.

Together, these ancient instruments infused the Joged Bumbung with a sound that felt simultaneously timeless and refreshingly unexpected. It was innovation in the truest Balinese sense: not abandoning tradition, but rediscovering and honoring it.

### Months of Dedicated Preparation

The group did not take this opportunity lightly. Upon receiving their mandate from the Buleleng Regency Cultural Affairs Office, the members of Sanggar Seni Werdhi Suara began intensive rehearsals in **early April 2026** — giving themselves nearly three months of preparation time.

The process involved archival research, interviews with elderly cultural figures, and careful choreographic development to ensure that the performance honored authentic Buleleng Joged Bumbung while remaining compelling for modern audiences.

## PKB XLVIII 2026: A Month of Bali at Its Cultural Best

The 48th Bali Arts Festival opened on **June 13** and runs through **July 11, 2026** — a full month of cultural programming at Taman Budaya Bali in Denpasar. This year's theme, **"Atma Kerthi: Jiwa Siddha Parishudha"** (The Soul Purified and Strengthened), reflects the festival's deeper purpose: to reconnect communities with the spiritual and cultural foundations of Balinese life.

PKB XLVIII features hundreds of performances across dance, music, shadow puppetry (wayang), crafts exhibitions, and cultural seminars. All nine regencies and cities of Bali send their best artists, and Buleleng alone is presenting **14 distinct art performances** across the festival period.

Deputy Minister of Tourism **Ni Luh Puspa** has set a visitor target of over **1.6 million people** for this year's PKB, boosted by the overlap with the national school holiday period and international summer travel season.
