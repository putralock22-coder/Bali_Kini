The 48th Bali Arts Festival (Pesta Kesenian Bali / PKB) XLVIII 2026 continues to dazzle on Tuesday, June 16, 2026. Held at the iconic Taman Budaya Bali (Bali Cultural Park / Art Center) in Denpasar, today's lineup features three major performances spanning the sacred and the spectacular — running from late afternoon well into the evening.

## Nglawang Barong Rengas Parade: Spirit Walkers of Tabanan

The day's festivities kick off at **17:00 WITA** with the Utsawa (Parade) Nglawang, featuring the Barong Rengas Community from Banjar Bongan Pala, Desa Bongan, Tabanan — performing as the proud representative of Tabanan Regency.

The Nglawang is a sacred Balinese procession in which the Barong — a lion-like protective deity — is paraded through the streets to ward off negative forces and restore spiritual balance. At PKB, this ancient ritual is transformed into a stunning parade that showcases the artistry and spiritual significance of the Barong tradition across different regions of Bali.

## Joged Bumbung: Bali's Playful Folk Dance Takes the Stage

Simultaneously at **Kalangan Madya Mandala**, two joged troupes compete in the Joged Bumbung Tradition Parade:

- **Sekaa Joged Kumbang Dedari** from Desa Batubulan, Sukawati — representing Gianyar Regency
- **Sekaa Joged Madu Bratha Kusuma** from Banjar Umadui, West Denpasar — representing Denpasar City

Joged Bumbung is Bali's beloved social dance accompanied by bamboo gamelan instruments (bumbung). Known for its lively, playful, and sometimes flirtatious energy, Joged Bumbung is one of Bali's most joyful art forms — and often invites audience participation, making it an interactive crowd favorite.

## Calonarang: The Night's Grand Finale

The evening's centerpiece is the **Rekasadana (Performance) Calonarang**, scheduled to begin at **19:30 WITA** at Kalangan Ayodya. The performance is staged by Sanggar Seni Majalangu from Banjar Padang, Desa Kerobokan, Kuta Utara — representing Badung Regency.

Calonarang is one of Bali's most powerful and mysterious sacred dance dramas. It tells the story of Calonarang, a fearsome widow-witch whose dark magic brings plague and suffering to the kingdom — until ultimately defeated by the righteous king and the protective Barong. The performance features iconic characters including Rangda (the demon queen), Barong (the protector), and the Barong's army of kris-wielding followers.

Beyond its artistic complexity — combining dance, live gamelan music, elaborate costumes, and theatrical storytelling — Calonarang is considered to carry genuine spiritual power. For many Balinese, watching or participating in a Calonarang performance is as much a ritual act as it is entertainment.

### Why Calonarang at PKB Matters

The Calonarang performance tonight is especially meaningful, given that it falls on **Penampahan Galungan** — the eve before the sacred Galungan holiday. In Balinese Hindu belief, Penampahan is when malevolent forces are said to be at their most active, making the Calonarang's themes of spiritual combat and purification particularly resonant tonight.

## PKB XLVIII 2026: 23,936 Artists, One Grand Stage

Running from **June 13 to July 11, 2026**, PKB this year carries the theme **"Atma Kerthi: Jiwa Saking Parisuda"** — a philosophical call to honor the purified soul. The festival brings together more than **23,936 artists** from 307 art groups, studios, and cultural organizations, ranging from local community troupes to international performers participating through the Bali World Culture Celebration (BWCC).

PKB 2026 also serves as a platform for the next generation of Balinese artists — offering young performers the opportunity to share the stage with masters while exchanging inspiration with international counterparts.

All performances at PKB are **open to the public and free of charge**. Tonight's combination of Barong parade, folk dance, and Calonarang drama offers an unmissable cultural experience for visitors in Bali.
