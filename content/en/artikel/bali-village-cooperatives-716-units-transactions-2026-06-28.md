## Bali Achieves Full Coverage of Village Cooperatives

Bali has become one of the first provinces in Indonesia to achieve complete coverage under the national Koperasi Desa/Kelurahan Merah Putih (KDMP) program. A total of **716 cooperative units** have been established across the island — 636 in villages and 80 in urban sub-districts (kelurahan) — ensuring that every community in Bali now has its own Merah Putih cooperative.

The data was confirmed by **Supendi**, Head of the Regional Office of the Directorate General of Treasury (DJPb) for Bali Province, who stated that the cooperatives have already begun delivering tangible economic benefits for local communities.

## Rp765 Million in Transactions and Counting

In their initial months of operation, these cooperatives collectively recorded **Rp765.76 million in transactions** from **32,763 individual deals** through the end of May 2026. For a program still in its early stages, these figures represent a promising start.

The cooperatives have also begun building their financial base:
- **Principal savings (simpanan pokok)**: Rp900.96 million
- **Mandatory savings (simpanan wajib)**: Rp303.70 million

These funds form the foundation for future expansion of cooperative services, from microlending and basic goods distribution to marketing local MSME products.

## Administrative Progress

On the administrative side, all 716 KDMP units in Bali have registered for **tax identification numbers (NPWP)**. Progress on further formalization is ongoing:
- **496 units** have obtained Business Identification Numbers (NIB)
- **238 units** have active system accounts for cooperative management

The pace of NIB registration and account activation is being accelerated so cooperatives can quickly access government financing facilities and begin offering a full range of services.

The cooperatives are spread across all regencies and cities in Bali:

Buleleng leads with 148 units, reflecting its position as the regency with the most villages in Bali.

## Governor's Target: 120 Fully Operational Cooperatives by End of July

Bali Governor **Wayan Koster** has set a target of at least **120 cooperatives fully operational** by the end of July 2026. Achieving this requires cooperatives to have trained management, sufficient capital, and transparent accounting systems in place.

To accelerate progress, the provincial government is promoting an **inter-cooperative trade scheme** — enabling KDMP units from different villages to supply each other's needs. This approach aims to build a local distribution network that reduces dependency on external supply chains.

## KDMP as a Distribution Channel for MSMEs

The Deputy Minister of Cooperatives has positioned KDMP as the **primary distribution channel** for products from Micro, Small, and Medium Enterprises (MSMEs). Under this model, farmers, artisans, and small business owners in rural areas will gain access to broader markets through the cooperative network — without having to rely entirely on middlemen or city-based digital platforms.

---

With 716 KDMP units established across all of Bali and initial transactions exceeding Rp765 million, the groundwork for a village-based cooperative economy is firmly in place. The challenge now shifts from establishment to effective operation — ensuring these cooperatives become genuine engines of rural prosperity rather than administrative checkboxes.
