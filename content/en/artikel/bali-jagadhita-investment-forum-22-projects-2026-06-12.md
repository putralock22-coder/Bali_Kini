Bank Indonesia and the Bali Provincial Government jointly hosted the **Bali Jagadhita Investment Forum (BJIF) 2026**, a high-profile platform designed to connect Bali and Nusa Tenggara's most promising investment opportunities with domestic and international capital. The event sent a clear message: despite global geopolitical turbulence, Bali remains firmly open for business.

## 22 Projects on the Table

The forum put **22 strategic projects** from Bali, West Nusa Tenggara (NTB), and East Nusa Tenggara (NTT) before an audience of more than **35 potential investors**. The portfolio spans a wide range of sectors: infrastructure, tourism, downstream processing, trade, and several high-growth emerging industries.

The headline project was the **Sanur Health Special Economic Zone (KEK Kesehatan Sanur)** — Indonesia's first dedicated healthcare SEZ, designed to establish Bali as a world-class medical tourism hub. Other projects included green street-lighting networks powered by renewable energy and a fleet electrification programme for public transport, aligning with Bali's long-term sustainability agenda.

## Ambassadors from Six Nations in Attendance

One of the forum's most notable features was the **direct participation of ambassadors from six countries**: Bulgaria, Bahrain, Oman, Armenia, Pakistan, and Romania. Bank Indonesia's Bali representative office used the occasion to give these senior diplomats a firsthand introduction to the KEK Sanur project and the wider investment catalogue.

The diplomatic angle is deliberate: by engaging ambassadors directly, the forum aims to accelerate government-to-government investment facilitation, opening doors for capital flows that might not materialise through conventional commercial channels alone.

### One-on-One Business Meetings

Beyond the showcase presentations, the forum featured structured **one-on-one business meetings** between project proponents and prospective investors. This format allows for frank discussions about investment structures, return projections, risk profiles, and site conditions — conversations that are difficult to have in a large-group setting. Participants rated these bilateral sessions as among the most productive elements of the event.

## Resilient Despite Geopolitical Headwinds

The timing of the forum was deliberate. With conflicts in the Middle East creating uncertainty in global investment flows, Indonesia — and Bali specifically — is positioning itself as a **safe and lucrative alternative** destination for international capital. The Indonesian government has reinforced this narrative with a package of fiscal incentives, simplified licensing procedures, and strategic national projects designed to create a level playing field for foreign investors.

Bank Indonesia projects Bali's economy to grow within a range of **5.4 to 5.9 percent in 2026**, citing strong fundamentals in tourism, agriculture, and services as buffers against global volatility.

## Bali's International Financial Centre Ambition

Looking further ahead, Bali is also positioning itself as the home of **Indonesia's International Financial Centre** — a concept modelled loosely on the Dubai International Financial Centre. The proposed zone would operate under a separate legal framework based on common law and offer preferential tax treatment to attract global capital, particularly for financing large-scale national strategic projects.

The Bali Jagadhita Investment Forum 2026 reinforces a broader narrative: Bali is no longer just a beach destination. It is steadily building the institutional infrastructure, regulatory frameworks, and investment pipeline to become a genuine economic powerhouse in Southeast Asia.
