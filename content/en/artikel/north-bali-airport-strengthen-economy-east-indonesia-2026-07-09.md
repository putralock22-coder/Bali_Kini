## FSKN: North Bali Airport a Gateway for Economic Equity

The Forum Silaturahmi Keraton Nusantara (FSKN), a prominent royal palace network in Indonesia, has expressed strong support for the North Bali International Airport (BIBU) project, stating that it will serve as a powerful new engine for economic growth. The forum believes the airport will not only benefit Bali but also strengthen trade, investment, connectivity, and sustainable development throughout eastern Indonesia.

The project has gained significant regulatory backing after the Indonesian government included the development of Bali's northern region in the National Medium-Term Development Plan (RPJMN) 2025–2029 under Presidential Regulation No. 12 of 2025. This inclusion signals a clear national commitment to ending the long-standing economic imbalance between Bali's booming south and its neglected north.

## Buleleng's Urgent Need for Better Connectivity

Buleleng Regency, where the airport is planned, faces stark economic disparities compared to South Bali. Of the 16.3 million tourists who visited Bali in 2025, only around 600,000 made their way to Buleleng. Yet Buleleng is Bali's most populous regency, home to approximately 826,000 residents, and simultaneously records the island's highest rates of unemployment and poverty.

A major barrier is the grueling road journey: travelling from Buleleng to Ngurah Rai International Airport in South Bali takes three to four hours along narrow, winding mountain roads. This has kept investors at bay and left the region's tourism potential largely untapped.

## A Mega-Project on an Artificial Island

The North Bali International Airport is designed to be built on a 900-hectare artificial island off the coast of Kubutambahan, Buleleng. Far beyond a simple airport, the project envisions an entirely new metropolitan hub incorporating:

- **Five-star hotels** and premium resorts
- An **international convention centre** for MICE (Meetings, Incentives, Conferences, and Exhibitions) events
- **"Baliwood"**, a film industry precinct aimed at attracting national and international productions
- A **60-km toll road** connecting the airport to South Bali
- A **railway line** linking the airport to urban centres and tourist destinations

The airport is projected to handle more than 20 million passengers per year, significantly reducing Bali's total dependence on a single airport.

### Investment and Institutional Support

PT BIBU Panji Sakti, the project developer, has already secured investment commitments worth Rp50 trillion. The airport's design is grounded in the Balinese philosophy of Tri Hita Karana—harmony between humans and God, among people, and with nature—as a framework for sustainable development.

FSKN formally petitioned the President as early as September 2025, requesting an accelerated groundbreaking for the project. Indonesia's Ambassador to Singapore has also endorsed the project as a concrete step toward economic equalisation, noting its potential to position eastern Indonesia as a compelling destination for global investment.

## A Catalyst for the Eastern Archipelago

Strategically positioned at Bali's northern tip, the new airport is seen as an ideal hub for flights into Nusa Tenggara, Sulawesi, Maluku, and Papua—regions historically underserved by international air routes. By creating a second major international gateway in Bali, the project could fundamentally reshape travel patterns and economic flows across Indonesia's eastern half.

If completed as planned, the North Bali International Airport stands to transform Buleleng from one of Bali's most economically marginalised areas into a thriving new growth centre with global reach.
