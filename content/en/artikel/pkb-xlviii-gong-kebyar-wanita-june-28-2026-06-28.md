The **48th Bali Arts Festival (PKB XLVIII 2026)** continues to captivate audiences with breathtaking cultural performances. On Sunday, June 28, 2026, the Taman Werdhi Budaya Art Centre in Denpasar once again served as the grand stage for Bali's finest artists, presenting a diverse lineup from contemporary gamelan compositions to the highly anticipated Women's Gong Kebyar Parade.

## Full Schedule for June 28, 2026

Day 16 of PKB 2026 was packed with performances running from late afternoon to evening, filling multiple performance spaces simultaneously.

### Rekasadana Gamelan Busi — Tabanan (3:00 PM WITA)

At 3:00 PM WITA, **Kalangan Angsoka** hosted the **Rekasadana (Performance) of New Dance and Tabuh Compositions** presented by the **Gamelan Busi Arts Community** from Tabanan Regency. This community showcased original works blending traditional Balinese musical structures with contemporary creativity, demonstrating how Tabanan's artists continue to innovate while honoring their cultural roots.

### Classic Palegongan and Traditional Joged Bumbung (5:00 PM WITA)

As the afternoon turned to early evening at 5:00 PM WITA, two simultaneous performances took the stage at separate venues. **Gedung Ksirarnawa** hosted the **Utsawa (Parade) of Classic Palegongan**, while **Kalangan Madya Mandala** presented the **Utsawa (Parade) of Traditional Joged Bumbung**. Classic Palegongan is one of Bali's most revered dance traditions, featuring the delicate and graceful movements of highly trained dancers. Joged Bumbung, by contrast, is a joyful social dance that frequently invites audience participation.

### Prembon Performance (7:00 PM WITA)

At 7:00 PM WITA, **Kalangan Ayodya** presented a **Rekasadana (Performance) of Prembon** by Sekaa Gong Catur Mandala. Prembon is a hybrid performing art that combines elements of Gambuh and Arja, integrating dialogue, singing, and dance into a unified dramatic presentation.

### Grand Finale: Women's Gong Kebyar Parade (7:30 PM WITA)

The evening's highlight was the **Utsawa (Parade) of Women's Gong Kebyar** at the **Open Stage Ardha Candra** beginning at 7:30 PM WITA. Two outstanding groups competed:

- **Sanggar Seni Guntur Madu** from Pangsan Village, Petang District — representative of Badung Regency
- **Sanggar Seni Lila Janaki** from Batuan Village, Sukawati District — representative of Gianyar Regency

Women's Gong Kebyar is one of PKB's most prestigious categories, featuring the complete kebyar gamelan composition performed and danced entirely by women. This event consistently draws thousands of enthusiastic spectators to the Ardha Candra Open Stage.

## PKB XLVIII: Atma Kerthi — Honoring the Human Soul

PKB XLVIII 2026 carries the theme **"Atma Kerthi: Jiwa Sidha Parisudha"** — an expression of the purification of the soul toward complete perfection. Bali's largest arts festival runs from June 13 to July 11, 2026, involving more than 20,000 artists from all regencies and cities in Bali, along with delegations from various Indonesian provinces.

## International Participation Through BWCC

Alongside the main festival, **Bali World Culture Celebration (BWCC)** brings together approximately 260 international artists representing 10 foreign art groups from the United States, China, Timor-Leste, South Korea, Japan, India, and Hong Kong. Additionally, 955 participants from Nusantara — representing Jakarta, East Java, Central Java, West Java, Yogyakarta, Banten, and Central Kalimantan — add to the festival's rich cultural tapestry.

## Free Admission

All PKB 2026 performances are **completely free** and open to the public at the Taman Werdhi Budaya Art Centre, Jalan Nusa Indah, Denpasar. The full schedule is available at **pestakesenianbali.id**.
