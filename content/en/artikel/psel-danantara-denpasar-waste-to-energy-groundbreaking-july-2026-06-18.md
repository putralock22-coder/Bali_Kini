## Bali's Landmark Waste Solution Takes Shape

A major turning point in Bali's environmental management is approaching. The **Proyek Pengolahan Sampah menjadi Energi Listrik (PSEL) Denpasar Raya** — Bali's flagship waste-to-energy facility — is scheduled for groundbreaking on **July 8, 2026**, at a six-hectare site in Banjar Pesanggaran, South Denpasar.

Backed by **Danantara**, Indonesia's sovereign investment management arm, the project forms part of a national wave of waste-to-energy initiatives also targeting Bekasi, Yogyakarta, and Bogor in the first batch.

## Scale and Technology

The PSEL Denpasar Raya facility is designed to process up to **1,200 tonnes of waste per day**, making it one of Indonesia's largest and most ambitious waste management infrastructure projects. The plant will primarily serve Denpasar and Badung — two of Bali's most densely populated and heavily visited regions.

The central government has designated **Zhejiang Weiming Environment Protection Co., Ltd.** — a Chinese company with an extensive portfolio of international waste-to-energy plants — as the winning bidder and operator. The incineration technology to be deployed is designed to convert waste into usable electricity while meeting rigorous emissions standards.

### Target: Operational by December 2027

Construction is expected to be completed by **end of 2027**, with commercial operations targeting **December 2027**. Land preparation work at the Pesanggaran site has already begun ahead of the official groundbreaking ceremony, reflecting the government's urgency in tackling Bali's waste backlog.

## The Crisis That Sparked Action

The urgency behind PSEL stems from a genuine crisis. TPA Suwung — Bali's main landfill serving Denpasar and Badung — had long exceeded its designed capacity, with open dumping officially halted in early 2026.

A shock therapy measure banning organic waste from entering the landfill, enacted on April 1, 2026, proved dramatically effective: waste volumes dropped by **60 percent** in Denpasar and Badung within just weeks. This behavioral shift laid the groundwork for deeper, infrastructure-level reform.

## Bali as Indonesia's National Model

Governor of Bali Wayan Koster has positioned the island as a **national model** for modern, sustainable waste management. The PSEL project is one pillar of a comprehensive roadmap that also includes mandatory waste sorting from source beginning July 1, 2026, and the full elimination of open dumping nationally by August 2026.

With Danantara's investment backing and a clear construction timeline, Bali is on track to transform its most persistent environmental challenge into an asset — literally generating power from the problem.
