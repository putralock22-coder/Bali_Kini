A golden moment from Polish soil. Balinese speed climbing sensation **Desak Made Rita Kusuma Dewi** delivered an emphatic victory at the **World Climbing Series (WCS) Krakow 2026** in Poland on Saturday (4/7), claiming the top step of the podium in the women's individual speed event and sending Indonesia's national anthem echoing through the arena.

## 6.54 Seconds to the Top of the World

The final was run in a 4-lane format, placing all four competitors on the wall simultaneously. Desak Made clocked a blazing **6.54 seconds** to leave her rivals in her wake. Poland's Natalia Kalucka took silver with 6.62 seconds — a margin of barely a tenth — while American Emma Hunt secured bronze at 11.37 seconds. Fellow Polish athlete Aleksandra Miroslaw rounded out the field in fourth.

The result was a statement performance from Desak Made, whose explosive technique and razor-sharp focus continue to place her among the elite of global speed climbing.

## A Three-Medal Haul for Indonesia

WCS Krakow ran from 3 to 5 July, with Indonesia fielding a seven-strong team across multiple disciplines. The squad didn't just meet expectations — they surpassed them. Against a pre-event target of at least one gold, Indonesia returned with **three medals** in total.

Beyond Desak Made's individual triumph, the Indonesian pair of **Robby and Desak Made** teamed up to claim silver in the **mixed relay** — an event requiring precise coordination and trust between partners. **Raharjati** added a bronze in another individual speed category, underlining the remarkable depth of Indonesian talent in this discipline.

## A Champion Built in Bali

Desak Made Rita Kusuma Dewi is no stranger to the international podium. The Bali-born athlete has been a fixture in Indonesia's national team for years, making speed climbing — a discipline that demands explosive power, precise footwork, and unwavering focus over a 15-metre vertical wall — look almost effortless.

### Why Speed Climbing Matters

Speed climbing is one of the most spectacular disciplines in competitive climbing. Athletes sprint up a standardised 15-metre wall on an incline of exactly 5 degrees off vertical. Times at the elite level now regularly dip below 7 seconds for women, a feat that would have seemed improbable just a decade ago. Indonesia has quietly become one of the world's leading nations in this event, with Desak Made at the spearhead.

## Bali Celebrates a World-Class Champion

News of the gold spread quickly through Bali's sporting community, drawing an outpouring of pride from locals, fans, and officials alike. For an island world-famous for its temples, rice terraces, and surf breaks, Desak Made's performance is a powerful reminder that Bali produces champions of a different kind too.

With her Krakow title secured, the focus now turns to the next stops on the World Climbing Series calendar — and whether Desak Made can build further on what is shaping up to be one of the standout performances of her career.
