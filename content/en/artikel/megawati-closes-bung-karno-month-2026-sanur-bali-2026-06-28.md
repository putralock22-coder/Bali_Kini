## A Sea of Red in Sanur: Bung Karno Month 2026 Closes with a Flourish

The Bali Beach Convention Center — The Meru in Sanur, Denpasar — was awash in red on Sunday (28/6/2026) as the **Bulan Bung Karno (Bung Karno Month) 2026** officially came to a close. The ceremony was led by **Megawati Soekarnoputri**, Chairwoman of the Indonesian Democratic Party of Struggle (PDI-P), who was accompanied by her son **Prananda Prabowo**, Chairman of PDI-P's Creative Economy and Digital Economy Division.

The closing event brought together the full structure of PDI-P's organization in Bali, from provincial and regency-level party executives to members of parliament and regional heads who are PDI-P cadres.

## 625 Out of 636 Villages Joined the Celebration

Bali Governor and PDI-P Bali Chairman **Wayan Koster** reported directly to Megawati that this year's Bung Karno Month had achieved remarkable grassroots participation across the island.

A total of **625 out of 636 villages** in Bali participated in the Bung Karno Month celebrations simultaneously — a testament to the party's deep penetration into the fabric of Balinese village society. The total funds mobilized for all activities reached **Rp10.5 billion**, drawn from village budgets (APBDes) and community self-funding.

## Youth at the Forefront

One of the most striking aspects of this year's Bung Karno Month was the significant involvement of young people. Across the various events held since March 2026, **14,569 young participants** took active roles in cultural and artistic performances.

Among the highlights:
- **Matembang Sastra Bali** — traditional Balinese literary recitation performed by young artists
- **Traditional Arak Juggling** — a unique acrobatic performance combining local skill with Bali's traditional spirit
- Photo exhibitions, culinary festivals, and multi-regency cultural showcases

## The Theme: "Be Loyal to Your Source"

The entire series of Bung Karno Month 2026 activities was carried out under the theme **"Setialah Kepada Sumbermu" (Be Loyal to Your Source)**, with a sub-theme emphasizing that the strength of both the party and the nation must continue to draw from the power of the people.

In his remarks before Megawati, Koster invoked the spirit of **Marhaenism** — Sukarno's political philosophy centered on championing farmers, laborers, and the ordinary people — as a relevant framework for addressing today's economic inequalities.

The **Tri Sakti of Bung Karno** — political sovereignty, economic self-sufficiency, and a distinct Indonesian cultural identity — served as the guiding framework for the month-long program.

## Why Bali Takes Bung Karno Month So Seriously

Bung Karno Month is an annual tradition for PDI-P, held every June to mark the birth month of Indonesia's founding president, Ir. Soekarno, who was born on June 6, 1901. In Bali, the occasion carries particular weight because PDI-P commands deep loyalty in this predominantly Hindu province, where Sukarno's family roots — his mother was Balinese — lend a special personal connection.

The scale of this year's celebration — 625 participating villages, Rp10.5 billion in funds, and nearly 15,000 young people involved — signals that PDI-P is actively using this tradition not just as a commemorative event but as a political mobilization exercise ahead of future electoral cycles.

As Megawati departed Sanur and the banners came down, Bung Karno Month 2026 left behind something more than a party event: a demonstration of organizational depth and community reach that few political organizations in Indonesia can match, at least in Bali.
