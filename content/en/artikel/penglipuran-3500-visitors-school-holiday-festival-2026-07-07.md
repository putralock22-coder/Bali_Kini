Penglipuran Tourist Village in Bangli Regency, Bali, has recorded a significant surge in visitors during the mid-2026 school holiday season. Daily arrivals reached **3,500 people** — nearly double the usual average of 2,000 visitors on regular days.

## Visitor Surge During School Holidays

The spike has been observed from June 23 through early July 2026, coinciding with the start of the national school holiday period. Village management expressed optimism that the upward trend will continue through mid-July, driven by the upcoming Penglipuran Village Festival XIII.

This growing popularity is not surprising given Penglipuran's reputation as one of the world's cleanest and best-preserved traditional villages. In 2024, the village received the prestigious **Best Tourism Village** award from UN Tourism, an accolade that has firmly cemented its status as a world-class cultural destination.

## Festival XIII: Harmony of the Penglipuran Earth

The school holiday season dovetails perfectly with the preparations for the **Penglipuran Village Festival XIII 2026**, scheduled for **July 9–11, 2026**. This annual cultural celebration has become a major draw for visitors eager to experience authentic Balinese traditions.

The festival's theme — *"Harmoni Bhumi Penglipuran: Towards Inclusive, Sustainable, and Regenerative Tourism"* — reflects the village's long-term vision of developing tourism that delivers economic benefits without compromising its natural environment and cultural heritage.

### Festival Schedule Highlights

The three-day program is packed with cultural and educational activities:

- **Thursday, July 9:** Official opening ceremony featuring a mass dance performance, *gebogan* (ceremonial offering) parade, and the *Tari Palegongan "Raksan Gumi"* performance, plus a local SME exhibition.
- **Friday, July 10:** Environmental workshops, a traditional costume contest, and performances by local musicians.
- **Saturday, July 11:** Closing day with Laughter Yoga and live music sessions symbolizing joy, health, community spirit, and positive energy.

## The 4S Concept: An Immersive Experience

The festival is built around the **4S concept** — *Something to Do, Something to See, Something to Buy,* and *Something to Learn* — ensuring visitors leave with a truly enriching experience. This goes beyond sightseeing: guests can join creative activities, sample local culinary delights, shop for handmade products from village artisans, and learn about the indigenous customs that have sustained Penglipuran for centuries.

## Target: 4,000 Visitors Per Day

Village management has set a target of **3,000 to 4,000 visitors per day** during the festival period. Notably, this cap is deliberately set below the previous year's peak of 5,000 daily visitors, which was deemed to have exceeded the village's comfortable carrying capacity and negatively affected the visitor experience and environmental conditions.

## Regenerative Tourism, Not Just Numbers

Behind the visitor boom, Penglipuran remains firmly committed to regenerative tourism principles. Every visit should not only generate economic returns for the community but also actively contribute to environmental preservation, cultural continuity, and the well-being of future generations.

Visitors are urged to use designated waste bins, minimize single-use plastics, respect local customs, and support the village economy by purchasing products from local SME vendors.
