## Amlapura Marks 386th Anniversary with Grand Cultural Festival

East Bali is gearing up for its biggest cultural celebration of the year. **Karangasem Festival 2026** will unfold over four days — from **June 19 to 22, 2026** — at the **Taman Budaya Candra Bhuana arts complex in Amlapura**, coinciding with the **386th anniversary of Amlapura City**, the capital of Karangasem Regency.

Far from being just a birthday party for a city, Karangasem Festival 2026 is designed as a multi-layered cultural showcase that brings together traditional Balinese arts, youth creativity, sports communities, and mainstream entertainment under one festival umbrella. Best of all: **entry is completely free** for everyone.

## Theme: "Not Easy, But We Must"

This year's festival carries a rallying cry as its official theme: **"Tidak Mudah, Tapi Harus Bisa"** — which translates to "Not Easy, But We Must." The theme was crafted to reflect the resilience and determination of Karangasem's people in navigating development challenges, expressing a collective belief that no obstacle is too great when faced with genuine commitment.

The main stage design draws inspiration from the Mahabharata tale of **Nakula and Sadewa** — twin warrior brothers whose complementary strengths symbolize cooperative leadership. The stage concept is meant to represent the partnership between local government and the community in building Karangasem together.

## 1,200 Local Artists in a Grand Cultural Parade

The festival's centerpiece is a spectacular **cultural parade** featuring approximately **1,200 local artists and performers**. Participants span the full breadth of Karangasem's arts community, including:

- Traditional Balinese dance and gamelan groups
- Local and regional music bands
- Motorcycle community representatives
- The **Amlapura Running Club**
- Reigning **Jegeg Bagus Karangasem** tourism ambassadors

Adding to the competitive spirit, the festival will host a **Tapel Ogoh-Ogoh competition** — a contest to create elaborate Balinese mythological masks and giant effigies — with separate categories for elementary school students, middle school students, and adults. This competition is expected to draw enthusiastic participation from communities across the regency.

## Donnie Sibarani Headlines the Grand Finale

The festival's closing night on June 22 will be headlined by **Donnie Sibarani**, the celebrated former frontman of Indonesian pop-rock band **Ada Band**, now a successful solo artist. His performance at the grand finale is expected to attract thousands of additional visitors from across Bali and beyond.

Supporting acts include a lineup of Balinese local artists and other national bands, ensuring vibrant entertainment across all four evenings of the festival.

## Economic Catalyst for East Bali

Karangasem Festival 2026 carries ambitions that extend well beyond cultural celebration. Organizers have positioned the event as a significant **economic driver** for the local community. Hundreds of UMKM (small and medium enterprise) stalls will be set up throughout the venue, offering Karangasem's distinctive handicrafts, traditional foods, woven textiles, and agricultural products.

The regional government is counting on the festival to amplify awareness of Karangasem's tourism offerings — a regency that boasts some of Bali's most dramatic natural and spiritual landmarks, including the majestic **Mount Agung**, the Mother Temple of **Pura Besakih**, the royal water garden of **Tirta Gangga**, and the world-renowned diving sites of **Amed** and **Tulamben**.

## Getting to Amlapura

Taman Budaya Candra Bhuana is located in central Amlapura, approximately 75 kilometers east of Denpasar. The journey from Denpasar takes about 1.5 to 2 hours via the Gianyar–Klungkung–Amlapura route. Visitors are advised to arrive early, as the festival is expected to draw tens of thousands of attendees each day.
