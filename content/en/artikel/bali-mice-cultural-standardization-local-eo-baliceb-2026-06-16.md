Bali is making a bold push to cement its position as one of the world's premier destinations for **MICE (Meetings, Incentives, Conventions, and Exhibitions)** — and it is doing so on distinctly Balinese terms. The island's government and the newly installed leadership of **Bali Convention and Exhibition Bureau (BaliCEB)** have laid out a comprehensive strategy that places cultural identity, local economic empowerment, and environmental sustainability at the heart of the island's MICE ambitions.

The BaliCEB leadership for the **2026–2031 period** was inaugurated on **June 5, 2026**, at Bali Beach Convention Center, The Meru Sanur, signaling a new chapter in Bali's organized approach to the global conventions industry.

## Five Strategic Pillars

The new BaliCEB leadership has committed to five core agendas:

1. **Global positioning** — Strengthening Bali's brand as a culturally rooted, sustainable MICE destination on the world stage
2. **International promotion** — Expanding marketing reach and building stronger networks with global associations and corporate buyers
3. **Active bidding strategy** — Systematically pursuing and winning international events through structured bid campaigns
4. **Human resource development** — Upgrading the skills and professionalism of Bali's MICE workforce
5. **Standards and sustainability** — Raising service standards, embracing technology, and embedding green practices across all events

## Culture as a Non-Negotiable Standard

Governor **Wayan Koster** has made it unambiguous: every MICE event held in Bali must carry a **strong Balinese cultural fingerprint**. This extends to:

- **Venue decoration** incorporating traditional Balinese motifs and materials
- **Guest reception protocols** following adat (customary) practices
- **Cultural performances** by Balinese artists at official events
- **Traditional Balinese attire** worn by hosts and organizers at formal ceremonies

This cultural mandate is not merely aesthetic — it is a deliberate strategy to differentiate Bali in a competitive global MICE market and ensure that international delegates leave with an authentic, immersive experience that no other destination can replicate.

## Local-First Economic Policy

Koster has also pushed for strong **local economic participation** in Bali's MICE industry. Event organizing companies (EOs) operating in Bali are expected to:
- Register their business domicile in Bali
- Pay taxes through Bali-based tax offices
- Use local financial institutions, particularly Bank BPD Bali

This policy is designed to keep the economic value generated by major international events circulating within the Balinese economy, benefiting MSMEs, artists, traditional craft producers, and the broader community — not just large hospitality corporations.

## Green MICE: Sustainability as a Selling Point

**Green MICE** emerged as one of the most discussed themes at the BaliCEB inauguration. Event standards in Bali are being reformed to require:
- Use of renewable energy sources at event venues
- Single-use plastic elimination across all event operations
- Energy-efficient lighting, air conditioning, and transportation
- Preference for locally sourced food and materials in catering
- Comprehensive green event management plans as a condition of permits

Koster noted that Bali's existing green policies — including renewable energy development, single-use plastic bans, and organic farming promotion — are already drawing international interest, creating a natural competitive advantage that the MICE industry can leverage.

## The Scale of the Opportunity

The Vice Minister of Tourism reinforced the ambition by stating that "**Bali has enormous assets to become a world-class MICE destination**" — citing the island's international airport connectivity, world-class hotels, diverse natural and cultural attractions, strong security record, and an established global brand.

MICE travelers typically spend **three to five times more** than leisure tourists per day, making this segment one of the highest-value opportunities in tourism. With the right standards in place, Bali's MICE industry has the potential to generate substantial economic spillover for local communities while simultaneously raising the island's profile as a destination that means serious business.

BaliCEB and the Provincial Government's shared vision — a globally recognized MICE hub that stays firmly rooted in Balinese identity — represents an increasingly rare and attractive proposition in a world where convention business often looks identical wherever it lands.
