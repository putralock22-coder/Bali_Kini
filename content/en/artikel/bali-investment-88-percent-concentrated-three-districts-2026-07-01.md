Investment inequality in Bali remains a pressing structural challenge. The latest data released on July 1, 2026 reveals that 88 percent of total capital investment in Bali is still concentrated in just three administrative areas: Badung Regency, Denpasar City, and Gianyar Regency—a cluster collectively referred to as "Sarbagia."

## Badung Leads, Others Lag Far Behind

According to data from the Bali Statistics Agency (BPS) and Bank Indonesia (BI), the distribution of investment realization is starkly uneven. **Badung Regency** leads with investment totaling **IDR 22.21 trillion**, followed by **Denpasar City** at **IDR 10.61 trillion**, and **Gianyar Regency** at **IDR 4.96 trillion**.

Outside the Sarbagia cluster, Tabanan Regency recorded IDR 2.04 trillion in investment—already far behind the top three. The remaining eight regencies across Bali, including Buleleng, Karangasem, Jembrana, and Klungkung, share the residual 12 percent of the province's total investment pie.

## Sarbagia: Engine of Growth, and a Risk

The Head of the Economic Assessment Division at Bank Indonesia's Bali office explained that Sarbagia's dominance stems directly from its role as Bali's tourism hub. The region contributes over 60 percent of Bali's Gross Regional Domestic Product (GRDP), with the accommodation, food, and beverage sub-sector accounting for roughly 22.76 percent of total GRDP.

Yet BI has also sounded a note of caution. Heavy reliance on tourism creates structural vulnerability. "Dependency on a single sector like tourism means events such as pandemics could happen again and cause outsized damage," a BI official noted.

## Government Moves to Spread the Wealth

Bali Governor Wayan Koster has initiated a coordinated development push under the "Bali Bergerak Bersama" (Bali Moves Together) program, which links Badung, Denpasar, and Gianyar under 22 strategic projects aimed at creating more balanced regional growth.

The plan includes developing new economic zones outside Sarbagia, upgrading infrastructure in North Bali, and unlocking the natural and cultural tourism potential of under-invested regencies such as Buleleng and Karangasem.

## Strong Growth Despite Uneven Spread

Despite structural imbalances, Bali's overall economy grew at 5.58 percent year-on-year in Q1 2026—outpacing the national average. Bank Indonesia projects Bali's economy to expand at 5.4 to 6.2 percent through the full year of 2026.

Economists warn, however, that growth anchored to a single geographic cluster remains inherently fragile. Diversifying investment flows to all of Bali's nine regencies and cities is considered urgent to ensure economic prosperity is genuinely felt island-wide—not just in the glamorous south.
