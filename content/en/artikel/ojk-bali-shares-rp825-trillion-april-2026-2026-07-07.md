## Bali's Capital Market Surges in April 2026

Bali's Financial Services Authority (OJK) has released its latest capital market report, revealing that the total value of stock ownership by investors in Bali reached Rp8.25 trillion as of April 2026. This represents a remarkable 46.8 percent increase compared to the same period in 2025, when the figure stood at Rp5.6 trillion.

OJK Bali Head Parjiman announced the figures as part of a broader assessment of the province's financial services sector, which he described as "solid and stable" despite ongoing global economic uncertainty.

## Over 404,000 Registered Investors

The growth in asset value is matched by a significant expansion in the investor base. According to Single Investor Identification (SID) data, the total number of investors in Bali reached 404,965 in April 2026 — a 32 percent increase from the 306,643 investors recorded in April 2025.

Breaking down the numbers by investment type:
- **Mutual fund investors**: 381,253 — the largest segment
- **Stock investors**: 202,807
- **Government bond (SBN) investors**: 34,683

The dominance of mutual funds reflects a growing preference among Balinese investors for diversified, professionally managed products as an entry point into formal investment.

## Consistent Month-on-Month Growth

The April 2026 figures also show consistent momentum on a monthly basis. Stock ownership values grew 3.75 percent from Rp7.95 trillion in March 2026 to Rp8.25 trillion in April — continuing an unbroken upward trend throughout the first months of the year.

## Financial Sector Remains Robust

Beyond the capital market, OJK Bali notes that the broader financial services sector remained stable through April 2026. Credit growth in the banking sector also showed positive momentum, supporting the overall resilience of Bali's economy amid external pressures.

The authority highlighted that despite global headwinds — including geopolitical tensions and international currency fluctuations — Bali's financial ecosystem has continued to perform strongly.

## Tourism Economy Underpins Investor Confidence

Bali's investment appeal is closely tied to its tourism-driven economy, which accounts for 66 percent of provincial economic growth. The island contributed Rp176 trillion in national foreign exchange earnings — representing 55 percent of Indonesia's total tourism-related foreign exchange of Rp319.9 trillion.

Strategic infrastructure developments such as the Kura-Kura Special Economic Zone (KEK) and the proposed international financial center — valued at over Rp104 trillion — are also expected to further attract domestic and foreign investors in the coming years.

## Encouraging Financial Literacy

OJK Bali continues to run public education programs to improve financial literacy across the province. Officials are particularly focused on drawing in younger generations, aiming to build a new wave of retail investors who can benefit from Bali's ongoing economic expansion.

With international visitor arrivals to Bali rising more than 768 percent from January to May 2026 compared to the same period last year, investor sentiment toward the island remains highly positive.
