## The Price Gap Problem That Has Long Plagued Nusa Penida

Residents and businesses on Nusa Penida, Klungkung Regency, have long dealt with a frustrating reality: essential goods cost significantly more on the island than on the Balinese mainland. The price disparity for basic food commodities, building materials, and everyday necessities can reach **10 to 30 percent** compared to central Klungkung.

The core reason is a logistical bottleneck: the Roro ferry (Roll-on/Roll-off vessel KMP Nusa Jaya Abadi) that serves the Padangbai Port–Nusa Penida route currently operates only **twice a day**. With Nusa Penida's population needs growing rapidly alongside its booming tourism sector, this frequency is no longer sufficient.

## Proposal: Three Daily Trips to Boost Supply and Reduce Costs

The Klungkung Regency Government has formally proposed increasing the Roro ferry service from two to **three trips per day**. Regent I Made Satria emphasized that new regulations will be needed to make this a sustainable reality.

"New regulations are required to realize this plan," Regent Satria stated. As a practical next step, the Bali Provincial Government plans to revise the Governor's Regulation (*Peraturan Gubernur* or Pergub) on crossing tariffs, while the Klungkung Regency Government will pilot the new fare structure over the next **six months**.

### Governor Koster Backs the Push

The proposal has strong support from Bali Governor Wayan Koster, who has called for increased cargo vessel schedules to Nusa Penida. His reasoning is straightforward: smoother supply chains translate directly into stable and more affordable prices for island residents.

"Smooth supply means prices can stabilize," the Governor said in reference to the importance of adequate maritime connectivity to Nusa Penida.

## Long Queues at Padangbai: An Additional Headache

Beyond the price disparity issue, long queues at Padangbai Port have been a recurring complaint from ferry users, particularly during peak periods. Adding a third daily trip would help distribute freight volume more evenly throughout the day, relieving congestion at the port while improving delivery timelines for perishable goods.

The Bali Provincial Transportation Agency (*Dinas Perhubungan*) confirmed the proposal is currently undergoing technical and administrative review, covering vessel operational readiness, crew scheduling, and port capacity at both Padangbai and Nusa Penida.

## Fiscal Impact: Subsidy Rises from Rp1.4 to Rp2.1 Billion

The additional trip comes at a cost. Current subsidies for the two daily Roro ferry services amount to approximately **Rp1.4 billion per year**. Adding a third trip would push the estimated subsidy to **Rp2.1 billion annually** — an increase of Rp700 million.

However, local government officials argue the social and economic benefits — more stable food prices, improved supply security, and greater equity for Nusa Penida's residents — far outweigh the additional budgetary expenditure.

## Nusa Penida: A Fast-Growing Island That Needs Stronger Logistics

Nusa Penida has rapidly emerged as one of Bali's most sought-after tourism destinations. With its stunning underwater world, dramatic cliffs like the iconic Kelingking Beach, and a quieter atmosphere compared to Kuta or Seminyak, the roughly 200 km² island now attracts hundreds of thousands of visitors annually.

This rapid tourism growth has amplified the urgency for stronger logistical infrastructure. Hotels, restaurants, dive shops, and local vendors all rely on regular, reliable deliveries of supplies from the mainland. With every delay or shortage, the ripple effects are felt in both prices and quality of service.

Expanding the Roro ferry service is therefore not just about cheaper groceries — it is about building the foundation for Nusa Penida's long-term sustainable development, where local communities and tourism operators can grow together without being held back by logistical isolation.
