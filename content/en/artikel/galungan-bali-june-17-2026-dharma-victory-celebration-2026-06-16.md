The Island of the Gods is preparing to welcome one of the most sacred celebrations in the Hindu calendar — **Galungan** — falling on Wednesday, June 17, 2026. Observed every 210 days according to the Balinese Pawukon calendar, Galungan commemorates the triumph of Dharma (righteousness) over Adharma (evil), making it one of the most spiritually significant and visually stunning events in Bali.

## Penampahan: The Day of Preparation

Today, Tuesday June 16, Bali celebrates **Penampahan Galungan** — the eve of the great holiday. Across the island, households buzz with activity as families prepare for tomorrow's festivities.

The most iconic sight of Penampahan is the raising of **penjor** — tall bamboo poles adorned with coconut leaves, tropical fruits, rice, flowers, and traditional ornaments — planted in front of every home along the length of every road. These graceful arching bamboo poles represent gratitude for the abundance of life and the blessings of the earth.

Another central tradition is the preparation of **lawar** — a ceremonial dish made from minced pork or chicken, grated coconut, herbs, spices, and in the traditional version, fresh blood. Lawar is an essential offering during Galungan. The ritual slaughter of pigs on Penampahan carries deep symbolic meaning: it represents the killing of animalistic tendencies and negative desires within oneself.

## The Peak of Galungan

On Wednesday, June 17, millions of Balinese Hindus will gather at **family shrines (merajan)** and village temples for communal prayer. Roads across the island will be transformed into corridors of penjor, creating breathtaking scenes that are uniquely and unmistakably Balinese.

Galungan is also believed to be the time when **ancestral spirits descend to earth** to visit their descendants. Every family prepares **punjung** — offering trays with food, flowers, and incense — to welcome the returning souls of their loved ones.

## The Full Galungan Season (June 2026)

Galungan is not a single day but a complete cycle of sacred observances:

| Date | Name | Significance |
|------|------|--------------|
| June 15 | Penyajaan Galungan | Making traditional rice cakes (jajan) |
| June 16 | Penampahan Galungan | Erecting penjor, cooking lawar, ritual slaughter |
| June 17 | Hari Raya Galungan | Peak celebration, temple prayers |
| June 18 | Umanis Galungan | Family visits, dharma santi gatherings |
| June 26 | Penampahan Kuningan | Preparation for Kuningan |
| June 27 | Hari Raya Kuningan | Closing celebration of the Galungan season |

## A Magical Time for Visitors

Galungan is widely considered the **best time to visit Bali** for travelers seeking an authentic cultural experience. The island takes on a profoundly spiritual atmosphere that is difficult to describe and impossible to forget.

Travelers can witness:
- Thousands of penjor lining every street from Denpasar to Ubud to Singaraja
- Processions of elaborately dressed worshippers carrying temple offerings
- Prayers at iconic temples including Besakih, Tanah Lot, and Uluwatu
- Traditional dance performances staged in village temples
- The warmth of Balinese families celebrating with open hearts

Visitors are encouraged to dress respectfully when approaching temple areas — wearing a sarong and sash — and to observe ceremonies from a respectful distance without disturbing the worshippers.

## The Universal Message of Galungan

At its core, Galungan teaches a timeless lesson: that light will always prevail over darkness, that truth will always outlast deception. This message — embedded in centuries of Balinese Hindu philosophy — resonates far beyond religious boundaries, making Galungan a celebration of human values that speaks to people of every culture and background.
