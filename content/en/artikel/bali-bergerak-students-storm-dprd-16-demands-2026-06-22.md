## Hundreds of Students Surround Bali's Provincial Legislature

On Monday, June 22, 2026, hundreds of students united under the "Bali Bergerak" (Bali Moves) Alliance staged a demonstration in front of the Bali Provincial House of Representatives (DPRD) in Denpasar. The protest was part of a coordinated national wave of demonstrations that simultaneously took place in Jakarta and East Java.

The crowd began marching from the eastern field of Lapangan Renon at approximately 3:15 PM local time and arrived at the DPRD building by 3:30 PM. Protesters came from diverse groups, including students from 13 faculties at Udayana University (Unud), the Student Executive Bodies (BEM) of Undiknas and Warmadewa universities, the Indonesian Catholic Student Association (PMKRI), the Indonesian National Student Movement (GMNI), and elements of civil society.

## 16 Demands Across Five Clusters

The Bali Bergerak Alliance presented 16 demands organized into five main clusters:

**1. Democracy and governance** — calling for stronger democratic institutions and meaningful anti-corruption measures.

**2. Human rights and civil liberties** — demanding the unconditional release of all political detainees, an end to intimidation of activists, and resolution of alleged human rights violations in Papua.

**3. Economy and public welfare** — highlighting rising fuel (BBM) prices and a weakening rupiah exchange rate as compounding burdens on ordinary Indonesians. Students also urged the House of Representatives to swiftly pass the Asset Forfeiture Bill, which is on the 2025–2026 National Priority Legislative Program.

**4. Education and public services** — calling for a comprehensive review of the Free Nutritious Meal Program (MBG), including investigations into mass food poisoning incidents and alleged corruption surrounding the flagship welfare initiative.

**5. Environment and agrarian rights** — raising concerns about deforestation and land disputes affecting indigenous communities, demanding concrete government action to protect forests and customary land rights.

## DPRD Bali Pledges to Relay Demands to Jakarta

The protest remained peaceful throughout the afternoon. Deputy Speaker III of the Bali DPRD, I Komang Nova Sewi Putra, alongside several council members, came out to meet the students and pledged to forward all 16 demands to the central government.

"I accept these demands and will convey them to our leaders in Jakarta through all existing factions — PDIP, Gerindra, Golkar, and others — so that each can deliver the students' demands to their respective leaders at the national level," Nova Sewi Putra said.

The DPRD also promised to engage with Bali's representatives in the national parliament to ensure the grievances are heard at the highest level of policymaking.

## Three-Day Ultimatum Issued

The students, however, were not satisfied with verbal commitments alone. BEM PM Udayana University issued a strict three-day ultimatum to the Bali DPRD to demonstrate tangible follow-through. If no concrete developments emerge by the deadline, the alliance warned it would return with an even larger mobilization.

The "Bali Bergerak" action reflects growing unrest among Bali's academic community and civil society over national policies perceived as falling short of public interest. It also signals that beyond its tourism image, Bali remains an active and critical participant in Indonesia's democratic discourse.
