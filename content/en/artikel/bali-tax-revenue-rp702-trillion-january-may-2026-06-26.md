The Regional Office of Indonesia's Directorate General of Taxes (Kanwil DJP) for Bali Province has reported strong tax collection figures for the first five months of 2026. Total tax revenue from January through May reached **Rp7.02 trillion** — an **11.27 percent increase** compared to the same period in 2025, which recorded Rp6.3 trillion.

The achievement signals sustained economic momentum in Bali despite ongoing global uncertainties, particularly geopolitical tensions that had weighed on tourism early in the year.

## Realization Stands at 29 Percent of Annual Target

Head of the Regional Office of the Directorate General of Treasury (DJPb) for Bali, Supendi, noted that while the Rp7.02 trillion figure is encouraging, it represents only about **29 percent** of the 2026 annual tax target of **Rp24.3 trillion**. Significant ground remains to be covered in the remaining months of the year.

"The 11.27 percent growth reflects continued economic improvement in Bali as tourist activity and business transactions pick up," Supendi said in an official statement.

## Tax Breakdown by Category

Growth was recorded across nearly all major tax categories:

### Income Tax (PPh)

- **Corporate Income Tax (PPh 25/29 Badan)**: Rp1.91 trillion, up **4.71 percent**
- **Individual Income Tax (PPh 25/29 Orang Pribadi)**: Rp313.63 billion, up **19.3 percent**

The stronger growth in individual income tax suggests rising personal incomes and improving taxpayer compliance among Bali's residents and business owners.

### Value Added Tax (PPN) and Luxury Goods Tax (PPnBM)

Combined PPN and PPnBM revenues totaled **Rp1.76 trillion**, growing **23.60 percent** — the highest rate among major tax categories. This surge reflects robust consumer spending across retail, property, and the hospitality sector.

### Land and Building Tax (PBB)

PBB posted an extraordinary **262.42 percent** jump to **Rp760 billion**, largely driven by adjustments in the assessed value (NJOP) of land in Bali's prime tourist areas, where property prices have risen sharply in recent years.

## Sector Contributions

By economic sector, **trade** emerged as the single largest contributor to tax revenue at **Rp1.24 trillion** (up 17.7%), driven by thriving commercial activity across local businesses and tourism supply chains.

**Accommodation and food services** ranked second with **Rp1.14 trillion** (up 16.34%), reflecting Bali's vast and growing hospitality industry comprising thousands of hotels, villas, cafés, and restaurants.

**Financial services and insurance** contributed **Rp969.1 billion** (up 13.79%), indicating healthy banking and investment activity as Bali continues to attract capital from domestic and international investors.

## A Strong Fiscal Signal

The double-digit growth in several tax categories and sectors points to a healthy fiscal picture for Bali. Improved tax revenue not only strengthens the regional budget but also expands the government's capacity to invest in infrastructure, public services, and sustainable development.

Risks remain, however. Tourist arrivals from abroad dipped early in 2026 due to global geopolitical pressures and rupiah depreciation. But the June–July school holiday season and peak tourism period are expected to accelerate tax collection in the second half of the year.

## Broader Revenue Context

DJPb Bali also reported that total state revenue collected across Bali — including non-tax revenue — reached **Rp10.43 trillion** through May 2026. This provides a strong fiscal foundation amid national budget challenges.

Bali's performance demonstrates that a well-managed tourism economy can generate consistent and growing fiscal revenues. The multiplier effect of tourism on tax collection — through spending in accommodation, food, trade, and financial services — remains one of the island's most powerful economic assets.
