As Bali's Hindu community prepares for the sacred **Galungan** holiday on Wednesday, June 17, 2026, government agencies across the island are working to ensure that staple food supplies remain sufficient and prices stay under control. The Regional Inflation Control Team (TPID) of Bali Province is intensively monitoring market conditions across all regencies and cities.

## Government Assures No Shortage

Bali's Regional Secretary confirmed that basic food commodities are available in adequate quantities to meet community needs throughout the Galungan–Kuningan period (June 17–27, 2026). Residents have been urged not to engage in panic buying, which can artificially drive up prices and create shortages where none exist.

The TPID is conducting intensive market surveillance, especially at major distribution hubs in Denpasar, Badung, Gianyar, and Klungkung — the four main commercial centers of Bali.

## Market Prices at Pasar Kreneng, Denpasar

The latest price survey at Pasar Kreneng in Denpasar shows most commodities remain within reasonable range, though upward pressure is evident on several items:

### Red Chili in Jembrana Reaches Rp 75,000

The sharpest increase has been recorded in Jembrana Regency, where red chili prices have surged to **Rp 75,000 per kilogram**. The spike is driven by a combination of elevated Galungan demand — chili is a staple ingredient in ceremonial Balinese dishes — and distribution bottlenecks from producing regions.

Flower prices and religious offering supplies in Denpasar and Badung have also climbed significantly, reflecting the broad economic ripple effect of the Galungan season.

## Abundant Stocks in Buleleng Regency

Data from Buleleng Regency demonstrates that aggregate supply far exceeds local demand:

| Commodity | Available Stock | Requirement |
|-----------|-----------------|-------------|
| Chili | 76.48 tonnes | 5.34 tonnes |
| Shallots | 145.93 tonnes | 5.93 tonnes |
| Garlic | 191.42 tonnes | 9.52 tonnes |

These ratios indicate that regional-level food scarcity poses a minimal risk, though logistical distribution to end markets requires continued monitoring.

## Market Inspections and Affordable Food Initiatives

The Bali Provincial Police's Food Task Force (*Satgas Saber Pangan*) has deployed personnel across traditional markets to enforce price stability regulations and investigate potential hoarding activities.

In Gianyar Regency, the Food, Marine, and Fisheries Agency (DKPKP) launched an **Affordable Food Movement** (*Gerakan Pangan Murah*), enabling residents to purchase essential items below market rates. The Provincial Agricultural and Food Security Agency also organized a **Farmers' Market** (*Pasar Tani*) that connects producers directly with consumers, eliminating middlemen.

Similar monitoring and stabilization efforts are underway in Klungkung, where local authorities have confirmed stable supplies across all key commodities.

## The Economic Pulse of Galungan Season

The Galungan 2026 holiday follows a structured sequence of sacred observances, each driving distinct waves of consumer demand:

- **June 11** – Sugihan Jawa: purification of the cosmos
- **June 12** – Sugihan Bali: purification of the self
- **June 14** – Penyekeban: mental fortification
- **June 15** – Penyajan: preparation of ritual offerings
- **June 16** – Penampahan: installation of the ceremonial *penjor* bamboo poles
- **June 17** – **Galungan**: the sacred day itself
- **June 27** – Kuningan: closing ceremony

Each stage generates economic activity spanning food, flowers, ceremonial accessories, traditional clothing, and ritual supplies. The government is calling on households, traders, and market operators to cooperate in maintaining price stability and ensuring that the season's abundance is shared equitably across all segments of Balinese society.
