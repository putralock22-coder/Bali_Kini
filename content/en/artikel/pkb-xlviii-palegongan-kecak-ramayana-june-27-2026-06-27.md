The Bali Arts Festival (BAF) XLVIII 2026, known locally as Pesta Kesenian Bali (PKB), delivers another night of cultural splendor this Saturday, June 27, 2026. Tonight's lineup at the Bali Cultural Park (Taman Budaya Bali) in Denpasar brings together two of the island's most celebrated classical art forms: the refined Palegongan dance and the thunderous Kecak Ramayana.

## Classic Palegongan Parade at Gedung Ksirarnawa

The evening begins at 5:00 PM WITA with the Utsawa (Parade) Palegongan Klasik at Gedung Ksirarnawa. Two groups take the stage representing different regions: Sekaa Gong Palegongan Sekar Ganjira representing Tabanan Regency, and Sekaa Gong Waja Swara representing Denpasar City.

Palegongan is among Bali's most delicate dance genres, combining fluid hand movements, expressive eyes, and harmonious gamelan rhythms. The "Classic" designation refers to the traditional style that preserves the original movement patterns and costuming from Bali's artistic golden age. Each gesture carries philosophical meaning drawn from Hindu-Balinese cosmology.

### A Living Cultural Heritage

The BAF has been the most important platform for preserving Classic Palegongan since the 1970s. Several groups performing this year have revived repertoires that had been dormant for decades, unearthing choreography and musical arrangements from older masters to present before contemporary audiences.

## Kecak Ramayana Titi Situbanda at Kalangan Madya Mandala

At 7:30 PM WITA, the night reaches its climax at Kalangan Madya Mandala with the Rekasadana Kecak Ramayana Titi Situbanda, performed by Sekaa Cak Girang Bawening Duta from Ungasan Village, Kuta Selatan District, Badung Regency.

The Kecak is a uniquely Balinese performance where a chorus of hundreds of men seated in concentric circles create rhythmic chanting — "cak-cak-cak" — to accompany the dramatization of the Ramayana epic. Unlike most Balinese performing arts, the Kecak uses no gamelan instruments; the human voice alone produces the sound landscape for the entire performance.

Tonight's rendition, subtitled "Titi Situbanda," focuses on the episode where Rama's monkey army builds a legendary bridge to reach the demon king Rahwana's kingdom in Lanka, seeking to rescue the kidnapped Princess Sita. Groups competing at BAF are expected to bring fresh choreographic interpretation while honoring the story's sacred narrative.

## Traditional Arts Performance at 7:00 PM

Between the two headline events, at 7:00 PM at Kalangan Ayodya, Sanggar Tari dan Tabuh Prabha Semara Jaya representing Badung Regency presents a Rekasadana (Pergelaran) of traditional arts, keeping the festival energy flowing through the evening.

## Getting There

All tonight's events are held at the Bali Cultural Park complex, Jalan Nusa Indah, East Denpasar. Visitors are advised to arrive early, particularly for Gedung Ksirarnawa which has limited seating. The BAF XLVIII continues through July 11, 2026, with dozens of performances scheduled daily. Admission to most events is free for the public.

For visitors and tourists seeking an authentic immersion in Balinese performing arts, tonight offers a rare opportunity to watch two of the island's greatest living traditions in one venue — a reminder of why Bali's cultural richness remains unmatched in Southeast Asia.
