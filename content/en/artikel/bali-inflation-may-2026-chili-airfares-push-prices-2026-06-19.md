Bali's Central Statistics Agency (BPS) reported a monthly inflation rate of **0.42 percent** in May 2026 compared to the previous month. This is an unusual phenomenon: in both May 2024 and May 2025, Bali experienced deflation during the same period—making this year's uptick a notable departure from recent trends.

## Annual Inflation Climbs to 2.99 Percent

On an annual basis, Bali's year-on-year inflation in May 2026 reached **2.99 percent**, climbing sharply from 2.08 percent recorded in April 2026. While still within the national inflation target range, the figure signals growing price pressure in Bali's tourism-driven economy.

## What's Driving Prices Up?

### 1. Chili Peppers Top the Chart
Red chili peppers recorded the highest inflation of any single commodity at **11.76 percent**, contributing 0.03 percentage points to overall inflation. Bird's eye chilies (cabai rawit) also rose by **7.63 percent**, adding another 0.04 percentage points. BPS attributed this spike to intense rainfall that damaged crops and increased pest infestations at key agricultural production centers.

### 2. Domestic Airfares Soar
Domestic flight ticket prices posted inflation of **8.61 percent**, contributing 0.03 percentage points. The Ministry of Transportation's new policy imposing additional charges on economy class tickets for scheduled domestic flights directly triggered this increase—hitting travelers heading to and from Bali hardest, particularly ahead of the school holiday season.

### 3. Non-Subsidized LPG Aftershock
Adjustments to non-subsidized LPG prices made in April 2026 continued to reverberate through May's inflation figures. The Food, Beverages, and Tobacco spending group was the largest contributor to monthly inflation, accounting for **0.17 percentage points**.

## Regional Breakdown Across Bali

| Region | Monthly Inflation | Annual Inflation |
|---|---|---|
| Denpasar City | 0.50% | 3.19% (highest) |
| Tabanan Regency | 0.54% (highest) | 2.78% |
| Singaraja City | 0.37% | 3.17% |
| Badung Regency | 0.25% | 2.64% |

Notably, Badung—Bali's tourism heartland—recorded the lowest monthly inflation among monitored areas, reflecting relatively stable supply chains supported by active tourism commerce.

## What This Means for Tourism and Locals

Rising food costs, especially for chili—a staple ingredient in Balinese cuisine—are squeezing restaurant and warung operators across the island. Meanwhile, pricier airfares may dampen domestic tourism demand, particularly from mid-distance cities where flying is the only practical option.

Economists expect inflation pressure in Bali to ease modestly in June 2026 as chili supplies normalize after harvest and heavy rains subside. However, continued uncertainty around the national government's fuel pricing policies remains a variable to watch.
