Indonesia's Meteorology, Climatology, and Geophysics Agency (**BMKG**) Regional Office III Denpasar has issued an **early warning of strong winds** for two regencies in Bali on Sunday, June 28, 2026. The two areas flagged are **Buleleng Regency** in North Bali and **Karangasem Regency** in East Bali. Residents and visitors in those areas have been advised to remain vigilant throughout the day.

## Strong Wind Warning for Buleleng and Karangasem

According to BMKG's weather forecast, winds are blowing from the **East–Southeast** direction at speeds ranging between **10 and 35 km/h** across parts of Bali. Wind speeds at the stronger end of this range can potentially disrupt outdoor activities, particularly in coastal areas and highland zones within Buleleng and Karangasem.

Residents near Lovina Beach (Buleleng) and along the eastern coastline around Amed, Tulamben, and Candidasa (Karangasem) are advised to monitor sea conditions carefully. Wave heights in the southern waters of Bali could reach **up to 2 meters**, meaning operators of small vessels should exercise extra caution before setting sail.

## Most of Bali Remains Clear and Sunny

Despite the warnings in two regencies, weather conditions across most of Bali on June 28, 2026 are forecast to be **clear to partly cloudy**. Areas including Denpasar, Badung (Kuta, Seminyak, Nusa Dua), Gianyar (Ubud), Tabanan, and Jembrana are expected to enjoy favorable conditions throughout the day.

Temperatures are forecast to range between **24 and 32 degrees Celsius** with moderate humidity levels — ideal conditions for a wide range of outdoor tourism activities. This comes at an opportune time, as Bali enters the school holiday season in late June 2026 with increased visitor arrivals expected.

## Impact on Tourism Activities

For visitors planning maritime activities such as **snorkeling in Tulamben, diving in Amed**, or **boat trips to Nusa Penida**, it is advisable to check updated weather conditions throughout the day and consult local tour operators before departure.

Operators of traditional fishing boats (jukung) along Bali's northern coast have been asked to exercise caution and refrain from departing if sea conditions are deemed unsafe.

## BMKG Safety Recommendations

BMKG has outlined several precautionary steps for both residents and tourists:

- **Avoid sheltering under large trees** when strong winds are blowing
- **Secure lightweight objects** around accommodation areas, including beach umbrellas, garden chairs, and signage
- **Delay open-water activities** if winds are noticeably strong and seas are rough
- **Monitor the latest weather updates** via the Info BMKG app or the official website at bmkg.go.id

## Understanding Bali's Dry Season Weather Patterns

June through August marks Bali's **dry season**, generally characterized by clearer skies and less rainfall compared to the wet season (October–April). However, southerly winds — known locally as **angin kemarau (dry season winds)** — can cause choppy conditions in parts of the sea, particularly on Bali's southern and eastern flanks.

Overall, weather conditions across Bali on June 28, 2026 remain favorable for tourism, especially in the south where the island's main tourism activities are concentrated. With awareness and appropriate precautions, the 2026 school holiday experience in Bali is expected to remain safe and enjoyable for all visitors.
