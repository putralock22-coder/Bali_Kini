## Ministry of Tourism Hosts Strategic 3B Forum in Denpasar

Indonesia's Ministry of Tourism organized the **Bangga Berwisata di Indonesia (BBWI) 3B Table Top Forum** in Denpasar, Bali, on Wednesday, June 20, 2026. The forum was specifically designed to accelerate the redistribution of tourist flows away from Bali's dominant Southern corridor toward three strategically linked regions known as the **3B axis: Banyuwangi, West Bali (Jembrana), and North Bali (Buleleng)**.

Marketing Deputy Minister Ni Made Ayu Marthini stated that the forum serves as a strategic platform for generating genuine business transactions and partnerships. She expressed hope that the gathering would produce compelling new tourism packages ready to capture the June–July 2026 school holiday surge.

## South Bali Concentration: A Long-Standing Challenge

For years, both domestic and international tourists visiting Bali have gravitated overwhelmingly toward the southern regencies of Badung, Denpasar, and parts of Gianyar. Meanwhile, the kabupaten of Buleleng in the north and Jembrana in the west have historically been left behind in sharing the economic rewards of Bali's booming tourism sector.

The BBWI 3B Forum addresses this imbalance head-on by directly connecting tourism businesses from the 3B region with travel agents, tour operators, and travel organizers. The table-top format—where sellers and buyers of tourism products negotiate directly across the same table—has proven highly effective in catalyzing concrete business agreements.

## 30 Curated Businesses, Rp3.67 Billion in Potential

The forum brought together **30 curated tourism businesses** representing accommodation, travel services, and leading village tourism sites across the three 3B regions. Results were encouraging: the estimated potential transactions generated from this single event reached **Rp3.67 billion**, covering potential bookings for tourism products, packages, and accommodation for up to **6,830 visitors**.

These figures demonstrate that the economic potential in North Bali and Banyuwangi is enormous—provided there is a functional bridge connecting local tourism products with broader markets. Village resorts, hidden beaches, tropical forests, and cultural sites across the 3B axis are beginning to attract serious attention from the travel industry.

## Strengthening the #DiIndonesiaAja Campaign

The BBWI 3B Forum also serves as a pillar of the national **#DiIndonesiaAja** (Travel in Indonesia) campaign, which encourages Indonesian citizens to prioritize domestic destinations for their holidays. By spotlighting the unique offerings of Banyuwangi, Buleleng, and Jembrana—each with untouched natural beauty, distinct culture, and authentic cuisine—the campaign seeks to reframe the idea that "quality vacation" does not require going abroad.

North Bali, for instance, offers Bali Barat National Park, the peaceful dolphin-watching beaches of Lovina, and agro-tourism experiences at Hatten Wines' vineyards near Singaraja. Jembrana features sea turtle conservation programs and mangrove forests still largely unknown to most tourists.

## School Holiday Season as a Pivotal Opportunity

The Ministry of Tourism sees the June–July 2026 school break as a golden window to redirect visitor flows toward alternative destinations. With the right promotional push and competitively priced packages, the 3B region has real potential to absorb millions of domestic travelers eager to explore a different side of Bali—far from the crowds of Kuta, Seminyak, or Ubud.

The success of this program is expected to serve as a replicable model for other provinces pursuing regionally balanced tourism development, while reinforcing Indonesia's profile as a world-class destination with extraordinary variety.
