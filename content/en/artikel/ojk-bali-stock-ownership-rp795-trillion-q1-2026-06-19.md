Bali's Financial Services Authority (OJK) has released encouraging data on the growth of capital market investment across the province. As of the end of March 2026, the total value of stock ownership by investors in Bali reached **Rp7.95 trillion** — a remarkable jump of **48.40 percent year-on-year (YoY)** compared to Rp5.35 trillion recorded in the same period of 2025.

The figures signal growing public confidence in capital market instruments, even as global economic pressures continue to test investor sentiment worldwide.

## Investor Count Approaches 400,000

The number of registered investors in Bali's capital market has also expanded significantly. As of March 2026, the total investor count based on **Single Investor Identification (SID)** reached **392,841 SID** — up nearly **30 percent** from 302,000 SID in March 2025.

### Breakdown by Investment Type:

| Instrument | Number of Investors |
|------------|---------------------|
| Mutual Funds | 369,562 investors |
| Stocks | 199,394 investors |
| Government Bonds (SBN) | 34,085 investors |

Among all instrument categories, **stock SID** recorded the fastest annual growth at **31.97 percent YoY**, indicating that a growing number of Balinese residents are moving beyond mutual funds into direct equity investment.

## Financial Sector Stability Remains Solid

Despite the rapid growth in investor numbers, OJK Bali stressed that the overall **stability of the Financial Services Industry (IJK)** in Bali remained solid through the end of Q1 2026. Several factors have contributed to this resilience:

- A robust recovery in the tourism sector, Bali's economic backbone
- Rising financial literacy among the Balinese population
- Easier access to digital investment platforms and mobile apps
- Sustained investor confidence amid global geopolitical volatility

OJK also reminded the public to remain vigilant against illegal investment schemes. The OJK Pasti Task Force continues to take enforcement action against unlicensed financial entities operating in Bali — as evidenced by the recent shutdown of Universal Peak and Bafi Group Indonesia for fraudulent investment activities.

## Bali as a Barometer for Retail Investment

Bali's consistent investor growth positions the province as one of Indonesia's leading indicators for the development of retail capital market participation. With nearly 393,000 registered SIDs, Bali demonstrates that investment culture is no longer confined to major urban centers like Jakarta or Surabaya.

This achievement also reflects the success of financial education programs run by OJK in partnership with banks, securities firms, and universities across Bali — nurturing a generation of investors who understand the value of long-term financial planning.
