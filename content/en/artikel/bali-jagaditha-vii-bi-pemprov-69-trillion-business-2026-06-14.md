The Bali Provincial Government and Bank Indonesia (BI) wrapped up the **Bali Jagaditha VII 2026** forum with remarkable results, closing the event at the Atrium of Mall Bali Galeria, Kuta, Badung on Sunday, June 7, 2026. Held over three days (June 5–7), the forum carried the theme **"Bhumi Parahita"** — symbolizing Bali's land as a foundation for shared prosperity and sustainable growth.

## Rp6.9 Trillion in Potential Tourism Business

The flagship event within Bali Jagaditha VII was the **Bali and Beyond Travel Fair (BBTF) 2026**, one of Indonesia's premier international tourism expos. This year's edition brought together **407 buyers from 44 countries** and **208 sellers** from across Indonesia, generating an estimated **Rp6.9 trillion (approx. USD 425 million)** in potential business agreements.

The figure reaffirms Bali's role as the main engine of Indonesia's national tourism industry and signals strong global confidence in Indonesian travel destinations.

## Investment Forum: 35 International Investors

The **Bali Investment Forum 2026**, co-hosted as part of Bali Jagaditha VII, facilitated direct meetings between **21 project owners** from Bali and the Lesser Sunda Islands (Nusa Tenggara) and **35 international investors**, including ambassadors from several partner countries. The forum was accompanied by field visits to investment sites and direct business matching sessions, creating tangible opportunities for capital inflows into strategic projects.

### SMEs Record Rp23 Billion Export Potential

Bali's small and medium enterprises (SMEs) also registered impressive results. Participating SME businesses logged **Rp23.03 billion in export potential**, underscoring that locally-made Balinese products hold competitive appeal in international markets.

## 60,000 Visitors, Rp1.9 Billion in Spot Transactions

Public enthusiasm for the event was unmistakably high. Over the three-day run, Bali Jagaditha VII drew an estimated **60,000 visitors**, generating **Rp1.9 billion in direct on-site transactions** — a testament to the forum's value not just as a business event, but as a direct economic driver for local communities.

## Bali Economy Projected to Hold at 5.4–5.9% in 2026

Despite global geopolitical pressures, Bank Indonesia's Bali provincial office projects the island's economy to remain solid at a growth rate of **5.4–5.9 percent** in 2026. This resilience is largely attributed to the ongoing synergy between the provincial government and BI, sustained through strategic forums like Bali Jagaditha, which integrates trade, tourism, and investment into a single cohesive ecosystem.

## Tri Hita Karana-Based Investment Commitment

At the closing of Bali Jagaditha VII, the Bali Provincial Government reaffirmed its commitment to fostering **sustainable investment guided by the philosophy of Tri Hita Karana** — the Balinese principle of balance between humans, nature, and God. Looking ahead, Bali Jagaditha is set to expand into strategic sectors including renewable energy and green infrastructure, supporting Bali's ambitious goal of achieving net-zero emissions by 2045.
