Indonesia's Meteorology, Climatology, and Geophysics Agency (BMKG) — through its Regional Office III in Denpasar — has issued an **early warning for coastal flooding (rob)** in multiple areas along the Bali coastline. The alert covers the period from **Friday, June 12 through June 20, 2026**, linked to the astronomical phenomenon known as a **Super New Moon**.

Residents living or working near Bali's coastal areas are urged to remain alert and to monitor BMKG's regularly updated maritime weather bulletins.

## What Is a Super New Moon?

A Super New Moon occurs when the new moon phase coincides with the moon reaching its **closest point to Earth**, known as the *perigee*. At this orbital position, the moon's gravitational pull on Earth's oceans is significantly stronger than during a regular new moon.

The Super New Moon in the June 2026 lunar cycle is expected to peak around **June 15, 2026**. This gravitational amplification causes sea levels to rise above normal tidal ranges, creating coastal flood risk in low-lying shoreline areas.

## Six Coastal Areas at Risk

BBMKG Region III Denpasar has identified six coastal zones in Bali that face elevated flooding risk from June 12 to 20:

- **Southern coast of Jembrana Regency**
- **Southern coast of Tabanan Regency**
- **Badung Regency coastline** (including Kuta, Seminyak, and Jimbaran areas)
- **Denpasar City coastline**
- **Gianyar Regency coastline**
- **Southern coast of Klungkung Regency**

These areas are characterized by relatively flat, low-elevation beaches and in some locations sit at or near mean sea level, making them more susceptible to tidal surges.

## Potential Impacts

The anticipated coastal flooding during this period could affect several sectors:

- **Port operations** — particularly small and traditional harbours with low deck elevations
- **Coastal settlements** — fishing villages and densely populated shoreline communities face inundation risk
- **Salt farms and aquaculture** — seawater intrusion can damage salt pans and fish ponds
- **Coastal road access** — some beachside roads may experience temporary flooding during peak high tides

## BBMKG Recommendations

BBMKG Region III Denpasar urges all individuals — especially those who live, work, or conduct recreational activities near the coast — to take the following precautions:

1. Remain alert and monitor sea conditions, particularly during high tide windows
2. Regularly check BMKG's official **maritime weather updates**
3. Avoid activities on very low-lying beach areas during peak tidal conditions
4. Secure valuables, equipment, and infrastructure in elevated locations where necessary

The warning is especially relevant for marine tourism operators, fishing boat crews, and owners of beachfront villas and cottages along Bali's southern and western shores.

## Timing: Flooding Risk During Peak Holiday Season

This coastal flood alert arrives at a particularly busy time for Bali. The school holiday season has begun, Galungan celebrations fall on June 17, and the Bali Arts Festival (PKB XLVIII) opens on June 13. Visitors planning beach activities along Bali's southern coastline this week are advised to check current tidal conditions and exercise caution near the waterline, especially during the morning and early evening tidal peaks.
