## Bali Buzzes During Eid al-Adha Holiday

The **Eid al-Adha 2026 long weekend** has delivered a significant boost to Bali's tourism industry, with the island's Dinas Pariwisata (Dispar), or Tourism Office, reporting a lively and busy holiday period. Domestic visitor arrivals surged an estimated **10 to 15 percent** above a regular weekend, demonstrating that Bali's appeal as Indonesia's premier holiday destination remains undiminished.

Daily domestic tourist arrivals during the holiday peak reached approximately **17,000 visitors per day**, compared to a daily average of around 12,000 on normal days. The figure reflects pent-up travel demand and strong public enthusiasm for Bali vacations during national holidays.

## Hotel Occupancy Climbs to 80 Percent

The Indonesian Hotel and Restaurant Association (**PHRI**) reported that hotel occupancy rates across Bali during the Eid al-Adha long weekend averaged **80 percent** — a result that has brought considerable relief and optimism to the island's hospitality sector, which had faced questions earlier in the year about visitor sentiment.

Key tourism corridors including **Kuta, Seminyak, Ubud,** and **Sanur** saw their properties fill up weeks in advance, with room rates rising noticeably above standard weekday pricing. Smaller boutique hotels and villa properties in Canggu, Nusa Dua, and Ubud's rural hinterland also reported strong bookings.

## Multiple Factors Behind the Tourism Surge

Several converging forces drove unusually strong visitation to Bali during this holiday window:

### Bali and Beyond Travel Fair (BBTF)

The long weekend coincided with the **Bali and Beyond Travel Fair (BBTF)**, a major international tourism trade event that attracts both industry professionals and general visitors. The event's presence amplified Bali's profile as a destination during this period and drew additional attendees from across Indonesia and abroad.

### Galungan and Kuningan on the Horizon

The long weekend also sits within the lead-up to two of Bali's most sacred and visually spectacular cultural celebrations. **Galungan** falls on **June 17, 2026**, followed by **Kuningan** on **June 27, 2026**. Many visitors deliberately chose to arrive early — or planned to extend their stays — to witness the unique atmosphere that descends on Bali during these Hindu ceremonial periods. Streets lined with **penjor** (bamboo poles adorned with offerings) and temple processions throughout villages make this one of the most atmospheric times of year to experience Bali.

### PKB XLVIII Running Concurrently

The **48th Bali Arts Festival (PKB)**, which opened on June 13, is running throughout this period, offering daily performances of traditional Balinese dance, music, and theater at Taman Budaya in Denpasar. Tourists visiting during the long weekend had the rare opportunity to experience both the holiday atmosphere and one of Asia's most important living arts festivals at the same time.

## Economic Ripple Effects Across Sectors

The tourism surge generated widespread economic benefits for Bali's local communities. The culinary sector, transportation providers, souvenir and craft sellers, and natural attraction venues all recorded above-average revenues. Street vendors near major tourist areas reported significantly higher transaction volumes compared to regular weekends.

The Bali Provincial Government and its tourism stakeholders coordinated actively ahead of the holiday period to ensure adequate security, smooth traffic management, and high service standards for all incoming visitors — a logistical effort that contributed to the positive visitor experience throughout the long weekend.

### Optimism for the Rest of 2026

The strong performance during the Eid al-Adha holiday reinforces an upbeat outlook for Bali tourism through the second half of 2026. With major cultural events, a robust international air schedule, and continued interest from both domestic and international markets, Bali's tourism industry is well-positioned for a strong full-year performance.
