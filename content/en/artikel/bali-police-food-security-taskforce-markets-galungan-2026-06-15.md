## Police Task Force Takes to the Markets

With the Hindu holy day of Galungan falling on Wednesday, June 17, 2026, the Bali Regional Police's Food Security Task Force (Satgas Saber Pangan) launched surprise inspections at traditional markets across Denpasar. On Monday morning (15/6/2026), a joint team conducted an unannounced inspection at **Pasar Kreneng, Jalan Kamboja, Denpasar**, monitoring the availability and pricing of essential food commodities.

The operation is no mere formality. As one of Bali's most important religious festivals, Galungan traditionally drives a surge in demand for basic foodstuffs — from rice and eggs to meat and fresh spices — creating opportunities for unscrupulous traders to inflate prices. The task force aims to prevent that from happening.

## Key Commodities Under Watch

Officers from Satgas Saber Pangan personally checked prices and availability of **strategic food commodities** including:

- **Rice** — the most essential daily staple
- **Eggs and meat** (beef and pork) — in high demand for ceremonial offerings (yadnya)
- **Cooking oil and sugar** — sensitive to price fluctuations
- **Chili, garlic, and shallots** — indispensable ingredients for Galungan preparation
- **Soybeans and corn** — raw materials for home-based food industries

Preliminary findings showed that prices at Pasar Kreneng remained within reasonable bounds, with no significant spikes detected. Stock levels were found adequate to meet community needs throughout both Galungan (June 17) and Kuningan (June 27).

## Multi-Agency Coordination

What sets this operation apart is the **breadth of inter-agency collaboration** involved. Beyond the Bali Police's Ditreskrimsus leading the effort, the task force included:

- **National Food Agency (Badan Pangan Nasional)** — overseeing distribution and food reserves
- **Bali Agriculture and Food Security Office** — monitoring supply from the production side
- **Industry and Trade Office** — ensuring prices comply with Maximum Retail Price (HET) regulations
- **Perum BULOG Bali** — holding a stock of 10.79 million kilograms of food, sufficient for 10 months of community needs

This multi-stakeholder coordination reflects the government's commitment to maintaining food price stability during periods of heightened seasonal demand.

## Affordable Markets and Enforcement

In addition to inspections, Satgas Saber Pangan has also organized **affordable market events (pasar murah)** at various locations across Bali, giving residents direct access to food staples at subsidized prices.

For those who break the rules, there is zero tolerance. The task force has pledged strict enforcement against hoarding, price manipulation, and LPG adulteration — practices that tend to surface around major religious holidays. Such fraudulent behavior not only harms consumers but also undermines trust in the national food distribution system.

## Inflation in Check, But Vigilance Maintained

Bali's inflation rate stood at **2.99 percent as of May 2026**, still within the national target range — a positive sign of relatively stable regional economic conditions. Nevertheless, with community spending expected to spike around Galungan, authorities are maintaining heightened vigilance.

Residents are urged to shop wisely and avoid panic-buying, as this can artificially drive up prices and create temporary shortages. The Bali Regional Inflation Control Team (TPID) has confirmed that monitoring will continue through several days after Kuningan on June 27 — covering not just the lead-up but the aftermath of the festive period.

Through coordinated action between law enforcement, government agencies, and BULOG, Bali aims to ensure residents can celebrate Galungan in peace, without the added burden of soaring food prices.
