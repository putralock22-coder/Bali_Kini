Denpasar City Government is accelerating its digital transformation in local taxation. At the High Level Meeting (HLM) of the Regional Digital Expansion and Acceleration Team (TP2DD) and the Regional Inflation Control Team (TPID), held Wednesday June 24, 2026, Mayor I Gusti Ngurah Jaya Negara reaffirmed the city's commitment to building an inclusive and efficient digital financial ecosystem.

## 95 Percent Non-Cash Tax Transactions Achieved

The most significant achievement highlighted at the meeting was the local tax digitalization rate reaching 95 percent. This means nearly all types of local taxes in Denpasar can now be paid through digital and non-cash platforms.

This milestone is the result of a series of digital innovations consistently developed by the city government. Key digital products that are already operational include: **Reditia**, **Melodi Sanur**, **Paon Gatsu**, **KLADI 5B**, **Pagi Bersinar BPHTB**, **SIPARKIR**, and **SIPERDI**.

## Two New Innovations Officially Launched

During the forum, Mayor Jaya Negara officially launched two new digital breakthroughs:

### Kedai Pasar (Integrated Digital Economic Zone)
This innovation creates digital transaction clusters targeting the Panjer, Sesetan, and Sidekarya areas. Through Kedai Pasar, merchants and MSME players in these districts are encouraged to fully adopt digital payment systems, building an integrated technology-based economic ecosystem.

### SMALDING (Smart Building Dharma Negara Alaya)
The second innovation introduces a smart building concept at the Dharma Negara Alaya complex — Denpasar's modern public service center. This system is designed to improve building operational efficiency while delivering a better service experience for residents.

## Collaboration with Bank Indonesia

This HLM also reinforced synergy between Denpasar City Government and Bank Indonesia in maintaining regional economic stability. Inflation control was equally prominent on the agenda, given its direct impact on public purchasing power and the local investment climate.

The collaboration between local government and Bank Indonesia is seen as a cornerstone for building a stable and sustainable economic foundation, especially amid ongoing shifts in the global economy.

## Denpasar as a Digital City Model

Denpasar's steps toward tax and financial digitalization are becoming a reference point for other cities across Indonesia. Through a consistent, phased approach, Denpasar has built a digital financial ecosystem that not only improves bureaucratic efficiency but also delivers genuine convenience to residents and business operators.

These efforts align with Denpasar's broader vision as a technology-driven city capable of delivering high-quality public services while continuously driving local economic growth.
