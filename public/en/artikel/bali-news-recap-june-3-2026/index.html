Bali News Recap: High Waves, Clear Skies & Sacred Calendar — June 3, 2026

Today's summary of key events in Bali — from BMKG high-wave warnings and weather forecasts to the Hindu sacred calendar for June 2026.

Wednesday, June 3, 2026. Here is today’s summary of the key news, alerts, and information from around Bali — from natural conditions and safety warnings to the sacred calendar you need to know this month.

⚠️ High Wave Warning: June 1–4

BMKG (Indonesia’s Meteorology, Climatology and Geophysics Agency) has issued a warning for high waves of 2.5–4 metres in the Badung Strait and South Bali waters. Coastal flooding risk applies to areas along Jembrana, Tabanan, Badung, Denpasar, Gianyar, and Klungkung.

Fishing boats and small vessels are advised to remain in port. Beachgoers along southern Bali should exercise caution.

North Bali 0.5–1.25 m Safe

🌤️ Today’s Weather

Bali’s weather on June 3, 2026 is largely clear to partly cloudy across most regencies, marking the gradual onset of the dry season:

  • Denpasar: 24–31°C, partly cloudy
  • Badung (Kuta/Seminyak): 22–31°C, cloudy
  • Ubud (Gianyar): 21–31°C, partly cloudy
  • Buleleng (Singaraja): 20–30°C, strong wind advisory
  • Bangli: 15–22°C, foggy and cool

🔥 2026 Dry Season Outlook

BPBD (Bali Regional Disaster Management Agency) warns that 30% of Bali’s territory has already entered dry conditions since April, particularly Nusa Penida, Klungkung and Gianyar. By September, 90% of the island is expected to be affected. The peak of the dry season — and highest wildfire risk — is forecast for August 2026.

The provincial government has activated a IDR 50 billion emergency reserve fund (BTT) to manage drought and fire responses.
