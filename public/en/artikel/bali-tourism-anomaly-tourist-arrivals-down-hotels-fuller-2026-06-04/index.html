BPS Bali Finds Anomaly: Foreign Tourists Down 6%, Hotels Fuller Than Ever

Bali's statistics agency BPS recorded a rare phenomenon in April 2026 — foreign tourist arrivals fell 6.41% year-on-year, yet star-rated hotel occupancy climbed to 57.94%.

Bali’s Central Statistics Agency (BPS) has flagged an unusual phenomenon in the island’s tourism landscape for April 2026: the number of foreign tourist (wisman) arrivals fell on a year-on-year basis, yet hotel room occupancy rates rose. The anomaly raises new questions about the quality-versus-quantity debate at the heart of Bali’s tourism strategy.

April 2026 Tourist Data: Monthly Rebound, Annual Decline

According to the official BPS Bali release, foreign tourists arriving directly in Bali in April 2026 totalled 553,328 visits. Month-on-month, that figure rose 17.21 percent compared to March 2026 — a seasonal rebound typical of the lead-up to the Northern Hemisphere summer.

Year-on-year, however, arrivals were down 6.41 percent from April 2025. The cumulative January–April 2026 figure stands at 2,019,892 visits, slightly below the 2,042,666 recorded in the same period last year — a decline of 1.11 percent.

Top Source Markets

Australia remained Bali’s largest single source market. Australian passport holders accounted for 146,414 visits in April 2026, up a strong 22.24 percent month-on-month. India ranked second with 46,513 visits (up 9.55 percent), followed by China at 44,447 visits.

The Anomaly: Hotels More Crowded Despite Fewer Tourists

This is where the data becomes compelling. Even as year-on-year foreign arrivals fell, the room occupancy rate (TPK) of star-rated hotels in Bali for April 2026 reached 57.94 percent — up 0.71 percentage points from April 2025. Non-classified hotels recorded a TPK of 34.81 percent, up 1.49 percentage points.

BPS Bali head Agus Gede Hendrayana Hermawan openly acknowledged the peculiarity.

“This is somewhat contradictory — foreign tourist numbers are down but hotel occupancy is up. April usually tends to see rising TPK figures when you look at the past two years, but now there is an anomaly because visitor numbers are actually declining.”

One likely explanation, according to BPS, is a reduction in available hotel room inventory: if fewer rooms are on the market while demand remains steady, per-property occupancy rises even when total arrivals decrease.

Tax Revenue Keeps Growing

The paradox extends to fiscal data. Governor of Bali Wayan Koster revealed that combined Hotel and Restaurant Tax (PHR) revenue from 1 January to 27 May 2026 reached IDR 2.89 trillion (approximately USD 177 million) — up from IDR 2.62 trillion in the same period of 2025. Put simply, while there are fewer foreign visitors, those who do come are spending more per head.

This aligns with the central government’s push toward quality tourism — fewer tourists, but greater economic contribution per visitor.

Global Turbulence and Its Effect on Bali

BPS Bali also noted that global geopolitical uncertainty — including ongoing tensions in the Middle East and broader economic headwinds — has weighed on inbound foreign visitor patterns. Despite this, markets from Australia, India, and Southeast Asia have shown resilience.

At the national level, Indonesia recorded 1.25 million foreign arrivals in April 2026, with Bali’s I Gusti Ngurah Rai International Airport remaining the country’s single largest entry point for international tourists — confirming that Bali retains its status as Indonesia’s premier tourism gateway even under external pressure.

What This Means for Bali’s Tourism Industry

The anomaly carries a dual message for Bali’s hospitality sector:

  • Positive signal: Rising revenue per room and growing tax receipts point to a higher-spending visitor profile.
  • Caution flag: A sustained year-on-year decline in foreign arrivals needs monitoring to prevent it from deepening through the second half of 2026.

Industry players are being encouraged to look beyond raw visitor volumes and instead strengthen destination appeal for high-value travellers from growth markets such as India, Western Europe, and the United States.

Source: BPS Bali (Statistics Indonesia – Bali Province), ANTARA News Bali, Detik Bali, Bali Bisnis — April 2026 data
