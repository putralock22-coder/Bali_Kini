Bali Tourism Earns Rp176 Trillion in 2025, Economy Grows 5.58% in Q1 2026

Governor Koster reveals Bali's tourism foreign exchange reached Rp176 trillion in 2025, contributing 55% of Indonesia's national total. Economy grew 5.58% in Q1 2026.

Bali has once again reaffirmed its position as the backbone of Indonesia’s tourism industry. Governor Wayan Koster revealed that Bali’s tourism sector generated Rp176 trillion in foreign exchange in 2025 — equivalent to 55 percent of Indonesia’s total national tourism foreign exchange of Rp320 trillion.

Koster’s statement, backed by data from the Central Statistics Agency (BPS), is a testament to the Island of the Gods’ outsized contribution to the Indonesian economy.

7.05 Million Foreign Tourists Chose Bali in 2025

Behind those staggering foreign exchange figures lies a steady stream of international arrivals. Throughout 2025, Bali welcomed 7.05 million foreign tourist visits — representing 45.8 percent of Indonesia’s total 15.39 million international arrivals.

Each foreign visitor spent an average of $1,522.44 per trip while in Bali. At an exchange rate of Rp16,500 per US dollar, this collective spending produced Rp176 trillion that flowed through Bali’s local economy — from hotels and restaurants to transportation services and micro, small, and medium enterprises (MSMEs).

“Bali’s tourism foreign exchange contributes 55 percent of the national total. This demonstrates how vital Bali’s tourism is to Indonesia’s economy,” said Governor Koster.

Economy Grows 5.58% in Early 2026

The positive momentum extends into 2026 as well. Bali’s economy has demonstrated remarkable resilience amid global geopolitical uncertainty. BPS data shows that Bali’s economy grew 5.58 percent year-on-year in Q1 2026.

The Gross Regional Domestic Product (GRDP) for the January–March 2026 period reached Rp44.28 trillion, up from Rp41.94 trillion during the same quarter a year earlier.

Driven by Holiday Momentum and Tourism

This growth was largely driven by the festive season and strong tourism activity in the early months of the year. On the production side, the accommodation and food service sector was the largest contributor, accounting for 20.95 percent of total GRDP.

On the expenditure side, household consumption dominated at 54.15 percent, reflecting the relatively strong purchasing power of Bali’s population.

Note: Seasonal Contraction Is Normal

On a quarter-to-quarter basis, Bali’s economy contracted 4.57 percent — but this is a normal seasonal pattern that typically occurs at the start of the year, when tourism activity and consumption tend to be lower compared to year-end.

Bright Outlook for Q2 2026

Bank Indonesia projects that Bali’s economic growth will strengthen further in Q2 2026. Two key drivers are expected:

  • School holidays, which historically trigger a significant surge in domestic tourist arrivals
  • Accelerating investment across various sectors, including tourism infrastructure

For the full year 2026, Bank Indonesia projects Bali’s economy to grow within a range of 5.0–5.8 percent, with some projections reaching as high as 5.9 percent — outpacing the national growth forecast of 4.9–5.7 percent.

The Challenge: Balancing Dominance with Sustainability

Behind the impressive numbers lies a structural challenge that cannot be ignored. Minister of Tourism Widiyanti Putri Wardhana has cautioned that over-concentration of tourism investment and arrivals in Bali — particularly in areas like Canggu, Seminyak, Uluwatu, and Ubud — risks capacity saturation and mounting pressure on the island’s environment and infrastructure.

The central government is now pushing for a more even distribution of tourism investment toward 10 other Priority Tourism Destinations across Indonesia, ensuring that economic benefits are not monopolized by Bali alone.

Despite these challenges, Bali’s position as a world-class destination appears stronger than ever. With Rp176 trillion in foreign exchange earnings, above-average economic growth, and school holiday season approaching, the Island of the Gods is heading into the second half of 2026 with well-founded optimism.
