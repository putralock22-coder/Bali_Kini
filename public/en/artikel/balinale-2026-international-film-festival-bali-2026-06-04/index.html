Balinale 2026: 94 Films from 38 Countries Illuminate Sanur

Bali International Film Festival (Balinale) 19th edition runs June 1-7 2026 in Sanur, showcasing 94 films from 38 countries as Indonesia's only Oscar-qualifying film festival.

Sanur, Bali has become the center of the international film world’s attention. The Bali International Film Festival, widely known as Balinale, is celebrating its 19th edition from June 1–7, 2026. Ninety-four films from 38 countries are being screened across multiple venues in the Sanur area, cementing Balinale’s position as one of Southeast Asia’s most prestigious film festivals.

Theme and Spirit of the 19th Edition

Carrying the theme “Stories Worth Experiencing”, Balinale 2026 arrives with a spirit of celebrating the power of cinema as a universal storytelling medium. This edition is particularly significant: Balinale has been officially recognized as an Academy Award®-qualifying film festival — the only one in Indonesia — meaning every short film award carries real weight in the global film industry.

Opening Film: Euphoria by Julian Rosefeldt

The festival opened with the world premiere of “Euphoria” by German director Julian Rosefeldt. Known for his cinematic installation art and visually striking, boundary-pushing narratives that blur the line between fine art and cinema, Rosefeldt’s choice as opening director signals Balinale’s commitment to presenting the cutting edge of contemporary filmmaking.

Closing Film: The End by Joshua Oppenheimer

Closing the festival on June 7, “The End” by director Joshua Oppenheimer is set to be a major highlight. This multinational production involves Denmark, Germany, Ireland, Italy, the United Kingdom, Sweden, and the United States. Oppenheimer, widely celebrated for his controversial documentary works The Act of Killing and The Look of Silence, returns to Bali screens with his latest fiction feature.

Over 50 World and Asian Premieres

Balinale 2026 features more than 50 premieres, including:

  • 20 World Premieres
  • 10 International Premieres
  • 26 Asian Premieres

These numbers reflect the global film community’s trust in Balinale as a launchpad for outstanding works.

26 Indonesian Films in Competition

A remarkable 26 Indonesian films made it into Balinale 2026’s curated program — a testament to the rapidly improving quality of Indonesian cinema on the world stage. Among those drawing attention is “Holy Salt”, a short film by students of Jakarta Institute of the Arts (IKJ). Inspired by the real-life phenomenon of widespread ruqyah salt sales in urban Jakarta, the film transforms an everyday social observation into a compelling cinematic narrative.

The strong showing of local films is more than symbolic: with Balinale’s Oscar-qualifying status, Indonesian filmmakers now have a genuine pathway to the Academy Awards.

Oscar-Qualifying Status: A Door to Hollywood

Balinale’s recognition as an Oscar® Qualifying Film Festival marks a historic milestone for Indonesian cinema. Winners from three short film categories — short narrative, short documentary, and short animation — are considered directly for Academy Award nominations in the Best Short Film category.

The track record is already being written: at Balinale 2025, two winning films were shortlisted for the 2026 Oscars — “The Boy with White Skin” (France) for Best Live-Action Short Film, and “Retirement Plan” (Ireland) for Best Animated Short Film.

New Award: Tapestry of Indonesia

As a tribute to the richness of local cinema, Balinale 2026 introduces a brand-new competitive category: Best Indonesian Short — Tapestry of Indonesia. This award is designed specifically to celebrate the diversity of stories from Indonesian filmmakers, encouraging more short-form works that reflect the archipelago’s cultural mosaic.

A High-Caliber International Jury

A 10-member jury — five from Indonesia and five from abroad — will determine this year’s winners.

Indonesian Jurors:

  • Yosep Anggi Noen — filmmaker, producer, and screenwriter
  • I Made Denny Chrisna Putra — filmmaker and cinematographer
  • Marcella Zalianty — actress
  • Annissa Nurul Shanty Kusuma Wardhani Heryadie — actress and singer
  • Nirartha Bas Diwangkara — filmmaker and producer

International Jurors:

  • Joseph J.U. Taylor (New Zealand) — director
  • Denise Castelli (Italy) — television network executive
  • Eros Zhao (United States) — documentary filmmaker
  • Richard Todd (Australia) — documentary filmmaker
  • David Hanan (Australia) — film researcher and academic

Venues: The Heart of Sanur Shines

The festival’s main hub is Icon Bali Mall, with film screenings at Cinema XXI, while special events are held at The Meru Sanur and Bali Beach Hotel. The Sanur district — known for its calm, artistic atmosphere — provides the ideal backdrop for a festival that celebrates narrative and imagination.

Bali: A Global Stage for Cinema

The 19th edition of Balinale further solidifies Bali’s identity not merely as a tourist destination, but as a world-class center for culture and the arts. With its natural beauty, spiritual depth, and long-established creative energy, Bali is proving itself capable of hosting meaningful cross-cultural cinematic dialogue.

For film lovers visiting Bali between June 1–7, 2026, the opportunity to witness dozens of world premieres is a rare privilege — the kind of experience typically reserved for Cannes, Berlin, or Toronto.
