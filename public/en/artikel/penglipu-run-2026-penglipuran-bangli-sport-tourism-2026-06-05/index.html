Penglipu-Run 2026: A 5KM Race Through the World's Best Tourism Village

Penglipuran Village in Bangli, Bali, hosts the Penglipu-Run Fun Run 2026 on June 7 — a 5 km sport tourism event through its legendary bamboo forest and historic traditional lanes.

Penglipuran Tourism Village in Bangli Regency, Bali, is set to become a world-class sport tourism stage on Saturday, June 7, 2026, as hundreds of runners weave through its iconic bamboo corridors during the Penglipu-Run Fun Run 2026. This 5-kilometer event is designed to blend sport, natural beauty, and Balinese cultural heritage into an unforgettable running experience.

A World’s Best Tourism Village Becomes a Running Arena

Penglipuran is no ordinary village. It has been recognized by the United Nations World Tourism Organization (UNWTO) as one of 54 Best Tourism Villages in the world in 2023 — joining leading tourism villages from across the globe. This international recognition places Penglipuran among Indonesia’s most prized destinations.

Now, that recognition is being leveraged to birth a sport tourism event that goes beyond a simple race. Penglipu-Run is a cultural journey that invites participants to breathe fresh mountain air and absorb the centuries-old tranquility of Penglipuran’s living heritage.

A Route Through the Heart of Culture and Nature

The Penglipu-Run 2026 route spans 5 kilometers and has been carefully designed to deliver the best possible experience for every participant. The starting point is located at the monument in the southern part of the village, from where runners circle the village’s core area — celebrated for its uniformly arranged traditional Balinese houses lining the main ceremonial road.

The highlight of the route comes when runners enter the Penglipuran Bamboo Forest — one of Bali’s most sought-after natural icons. Towering bamboo corridors, reaching tens of meters high, create a cool, shaded, and magical atmosphere found nowhere else but here. After traversing the forest, runners return to the village’s main tourism area at the finish line.

What Participants Experience

  • Iconic bamboo corridors — cool, shaded, and incredibly photogenic
  • Traditional Balinese architecture — neatly arranged ancestral homes along the route
  • One of the world’s cleanest villages — Penglipuran is renowned for its immaculate environment
  • Warm local community — residents actively welcome and cheer on participants

Targeting 500 Participants from All Backgrounds

The organizer, Penglipuran Tourism Village Management, is targeting 500 participants for this inaugural event. Registration is open to international tourists, domestic visitors, and members of Bali’s active running communities.

The event is deliberately positioned as a fun run rather than a competitive race, ensuring that participants of all ages and fitness levels can join. This spirit of inclusivity aligns with the values of togetherness (gotong royong) that have formed the foundation of Penglipuran’s community life for generations.

Advancing Sustainable Sport Tourism in Bali

Against a global backdrop of growing enthusiasm for sport tourism, Penglipu-Run 2026 represents Bali’s answer to the rising demand from travelers who want to enjoy destinations while staying physically active. The Bali Provincial Government has made diversification of tourism products — including sports and wellness tourism — a strategic priority for 2026, as the island shifts its focus toward attracting high-quality visitors.

Penglipuran is seen as the perfect laboratory for nature- and culture-based sport tourism. Unlike city runs, racing through Penglipuran offers an authenticity that is increasingly rare: connecting with nature, experiencing living culture, and directly supporting the local village economy.

How to Register

Interested participants can register through the Penglipuran Tourism Village management office in Bangli. The event is scheduled to begin in the morning on Saturday, June 7, 2026. Beyond medals and a goodie bag, participants will have the opportunity to explore the full beauty of Penglipuran and engage with the community’s traditional way of life.
