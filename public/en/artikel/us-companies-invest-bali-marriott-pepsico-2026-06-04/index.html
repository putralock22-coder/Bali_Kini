US Companies Deepen Investment in Bali: Marriott to PepsiCo

Marriott International, PepsiCo, and Herbalife reaffirm their commitment to expand investments in Bali at a US-ASEAN Business Council event in June 2026.

Several major US corporations have officially reaffirmed their commitment to expand and deepen their investments in Bali. The announcements came at an Executive Briefing titled “Impact of Innovation on Bali’s Economic Development: A Showcase of U.S. Companies’ Initiatives”, held in Bali on Thursday, June 4, 2026.

The event was organized by the US-ASEAN Business Council (USABC), which spotlighted the various initiatives of American companies contributing to Bali’s economic development amid global economic uncertainty.

Marriott International: 27 Hotels and Counting

Marriott International, the hospitality giant currently operating 27 hotels across Bali, reaffirmed its commitment to further grow its presence on the Island of the Gods. The company plans to add new properties while also increasing the use of locally sourced Balinese products in their hotel operations.

This move aligns with Bali’s evolving tourism policy, which now prioritizes quality over quantity — welcoming high-caliber investments that create a tangible impact on local communities.

PepsiCo: Environmental Sustainability Partner

PepsiCo’s engagement goes beyond business. The global food and beverage company is supporting environmental sustainability initiatives in Bali through its Greenhouse Accelerator program, focused on empowering local enterprises to adopt greener, more sustainable practices.

PepsiCo’s commitment reflects a broader trend among foreign investors, who are increasingly factoring in environmental, social, and governance (ESG) considerations when strategizing for premium destinations like Bali.

Herbalife: Investing in Human Capital

Herbalife brought a social mission to the table. The nutrition and health supplement company is directing its programs toward improving children’s nutritional quality in Bali — a long-term investment in the human capital that will underpin the island’s tourism industry for generations to come.

US Investment Position in Bali

While not yet the top foreign investor, the United States shows a consistent upward trend. According to the latest data, US investment in Bali reached Rp661.58 billion through Q1 2026, placing it fifth behind France, Australia, Russia, and Singapore.

The figure signals strong growth potential, particularly across tourism, technology, and environmental sustainability — the three key pillars that the participating US companies are focusing on.

Context: Bali’s Economy in Q2 2026

These investment commitments arrive at a pivotal moment. Bank Indonesia’s (BI) Bali Representative Office projects positive economic growth for Bali in Q2 2026, driven by several key factors:

  • School holiday season running June–July 2026
  • Peak international tourist arrivals, particularly from India
  • Government investment projects entering the realization phase
  • Harvest season across various agricultural regions of Bali

The combination of domestic momentum and the confidence demonstrated by foreign investors like these American companies reinforces optimism for Bali’s economic performance throughout the second half of 2026.

Bali as an International Investment Magnet

Bali continues to prove itself as a destination that is not only attractive for tourism, but also compelling for economic and social investment. The presence of Marriott, PepsiCo, and Herbalife at a single investment forum reflects the international business community’s enduring confidence in Bali’s long-term stability and prospects.

The Bali Provincial Government and key stakeholders are now expected to capitalize on this momentum — building a more accessible, transparent, and sustainable investment ecosystem that benefits local communities while enhancing the quality of the visitor experience.
