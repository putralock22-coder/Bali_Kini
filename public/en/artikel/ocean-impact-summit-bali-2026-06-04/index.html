Bali Hosts 'Blue Davos' — Ocean Impact Summit 2026

Indonesia and the WEF hold the Ocean Impact Summit 2026 in Bali, gathering world leaders to advance the blue economy and ocean sustainability.

Bali is once again capturing global attention. The Island of the Gods has been chosen to host the Ocean Impact Summit (OIS) 2026, a landmark global forum dubbed “Blue Davos” — an initiative led by Indonesia in partnership with the World Economic Forum (WEF). The event was originally scheduled for June 8–9, 2026, though Indonesia’s government has also announced the possibility of rescheduling to October 2026.

What Is the Ocean Impact Summit?

The Ocean Impact Summit is a high-level, action-oriented global platform bringing together governments, international organizations, investors, industry leaders, and civil society to advance sustainable and inclusive blue economic development.

Unlike conventional discussion forums, OIS is designed to produce tangible outcomes. Just as the World Economic Forum’s Davos meeting shapes global economic policy, OIS 2026 is envisioned as Davos for the oceans: a space where real investment commitments, private sector partnerships, and blue economy financing initiatives are decided.

The Indonesia–WEF Partnership

The formal partnership between Indonesia and the WEF for OIS 2026 was sealed through a letter of intent signed by Maritime Affairs and Fisheries Minister Sakti Wahyu Trenggono and WEF officials in Davos, Switzerland — witnessed personally by President Prabowo Subianto.

During the Davos 2026 Annual Meeting, President Prabowo actively championed the Bali summit, issuing personal invitations to world leaders. Among those invited were Australian Prime Minister Anthony Albanese and representatives from Monaco. Renowned investor and ocean philanthropist Ray Dalio — founder of OceanX — is also expected to attend.

Three Core Agenda Themes

The summit will address three strategic global issues:

1. Freshwater Management and Access

As water scarcity worsens globally, the forum will explore ecosystem-based solutions to guarantee freshwater availability for billions of people worldwide.

2. Blue Food Security

The ocean provides more than 17% of the world’s animal protein. OIS 2026 will push for innovation in sustainable marine aquaculture and blue food supply chains.

3. Ocean Protection and Blue Economy Development

This is the heart of the summit: how to protect marine ecosystems while unlocking their economic potential — spanning marine tourism, ocean energy, biotechnology, and green shipping.

Indonesia’s Ambition: A Global Ocean Economy Hub

As the world’s largest archipelagic nation — with over 17,000 islands and the second-longest coastline in the world — Indonesia holds a strategic position that few nations can match. The Prabowo administration has declared a bold ambition: to make Indonesia not merely a Southeast Asian maritime power, but a global leader in ocean economics.

Bali’s selection as the venue is no coincidence. As one of the world’s most recognized destinations, Bali offers world-class international conference infrastructure and symbolically represents both the beauty and the vulnerability of tropical marine ecosystems that urgently need protection.

Concrete Expected Deliverables

OIS 2026 is designed to generate real, measurable outcomes:

  • Investment commitments in the blue economy from corporate leaders and sovereign wealth funds
  • Private sector partnerships in marine research and technology
  • Financing initiatives for sustainable fisheries and marine development
  • Cross-border policy frameworks for marine conservation

Latest Update: Possible Rescheduling

While preparations continue, Indonesia’s government has announced that OIS 2026 is under review for rescheduling to October 2026, with confirmed dates to be announced as soon as a final decision is made. As of June 4, 2026, no official announcement had been made.

Regardless of any date changes, the momentum built around this summit — including WEF backing, presidential diplomacy, and enthusiastic engagement from the global community — keeps OIS 2026 one of the most anticipated geopolitical-economic events in the Asia-Pacific region this year.

What It Means for Bali

Hosting OIS 2026 brings multiple benefits to Bali:

  1. Global exposure: Bali will be showcased to thousands of delegates from hundreds of countries as a world-class event destination
  2. Economic boost: Thousands of attendees will need premium accommodation, transportation, and hospitality services
  3. MICE credentials: Bali’s standing as an international Meetings, Incentives, Conferences, and Exhibitions (MICE) hub grows stronger

Bali is far more than beaches and rice terraces — the Island of the Gods is now a stage for global diplomacy and economic leadership.
