Galungan 2026: The Complete Guide for Visitors and Locals

Galungan falls on June 17, 2026. A complete guide to traditions, the best spots to see penjor bamboo poles, and tips for visiting Bali during this sacred Hindu festival.

Galungan 2026 falls on June 17, 2026, followed by Kuningan on June 27, 2026. For the Balinese, this is the most sacred moment in the Hindu Balinese calendar — and for visitors, it is simply the best time to see Bali in its most authentic form.

What Is Galungan?

Galungan celebrates the victory of dharma (good) over adharma (evil). The festival occurs every 210 days according to the Balinese Pawukon calendar. For 10 days — from Galungan to Kuningan — the entire island is adorned with penjor: tall curved bamboo poles decorated with coconut leaves, fruits, and yellow cloth lining every road and temple entrance.

Best Spots to See the Penjor

Almost every road in Bali will be lined with penjor, but some locations stand out:

  • Ubud — traditional villages around Ubud such as Penestanan and Mas
  • Gianyar — the Sukawati and Blahbatuh areas
  • Klungkung — around Kerta Gosa, with a deeply traditional atmosphere
  • Karangasem — small villages on the slopes of Mount Agung

Tips for Visitors

Visit Bali during Galungan and you’ll see a version of the island that no travel brochure can capture.

  1. Arrive early — penjor start going up 1–2 days before Galungan (around June 15–16)
  2. Respect temple ceremonies — do not enter areas where private ceremonies are in progress
  3. Dress respectfully — bring a sarong and sash if you wish to enter temple grounds
  4. Use small vehicles — some village roads are narrow and busy
  5. Slow down — savour the experience, not just the photos

Impact on Tourism

Based on historical data, tourist arrivals to Bali typically increase 15–20% in the week of Galungan. Hotels in Ubud and Canggu tend to fill up fast — early bookings are strongly recommended.

The 2026 Galungan season coincides with the Bali Arts Festival XLVIII (June 13–July 11) at Taman Budaya Denpasar, making June 2026 the busiest month in Bali’s tourism calendar this year.

Kuningan: The Sacred Closing

Ten days after Galungan, Kuningan (June 27, 2026) marks the return of ancestral spirits to the afterlife. Ceremonies take place in the morning before noon — this is the best time to witness processions at major temples.
