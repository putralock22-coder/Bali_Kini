Bali Tourism June 2026: 553K Visitors in April, Target 6.6M for the Year

Bali recorded 553,000 foreign visitors in April 2026, a 6.41% drop year-on-year, but hotel occupancy rates surprisingly increased — driven by domestic tourists.

Bali welcomed 553,000 foreign visitors in April 2026, according to the latest data from BPS Bali (Statistics Indonesia). While this represents a 6.41% decline year-on-year, the island’s tourism economy tells a more nuanced story.

The Visitor Paradox

Despite fewer international arrivals, hotel occupancy rates across Bali actually increased during the same period. Analysts attribute this anomaly to a surge in domestic tourism — Indonesian travelers from Java and other islands filling the rooms that fewer foreign visitors left empty.

Australia remains Bali’s top source market with 146,414 visitors in April, accounting for roughly 26% of all foreign arrivals. India came in second with 46,513 visitors (+9.55% YoY), followed by China with 44,447 visitors.

Revenue Still Strong

The Hotel and Restaurant Tax (PHR) for January–May 2026 reached IDR 2.9 trillion — up IDR 300 billion compared to the same period in 2025. This suggests that while visitor numbers dipped, spending per visitor may have increased.

Annual Target: 6.625 Million

Bali’s Provincial Tourism Office (Dispar) has set a 2026 target of 6.625 million foreign visitors — slightly below the 2025 record of 6.9 million, which exceeded all expectations. With 2.02 million visitors recorded in January–April 2026, the island is pacing at roughly 30.5% of its annual target.

Bali & Beyond Travel Fair Boost

The Bali & Beyond Travel Fair (BBTF) 2026 recently concluded with positive results, strengthening Bali’s brand among international travel buyers and operators. The event is expected to translate into increased bookings for the second half of 2026.

Diversification Efforts

Governor I Wayan Koster’s administration has been actively working to reduce dependency on Australian tourists. A new direct flight agreement between Bali and Mumbai (4 flights per week) is projected to add 40,000 additional Indian visitors annually. Talks are also underway for new routes to Saudi Arabia and South Korea.

Source: BPS Bali, May 2026 · Antara Bali · Bisnis.com
