Jatiluwih Festival 2026: Run 5K Through UNESCO Heritage Rice Terraces

Jatiluwih Festival VII & Bali Tourism Run 2026 takes place June 20-21 in Tabanan, combining sport tourism with the breathtaking UNESCO World Heritage rice terraces, targeting 2,000 runners.

Running Through a World Heritage Landscape

Bali is set to host one of its most unique sport tourism events of the year. Jatiluwih Festival VII & Bali Tourism Run 2026 is scheduled for June 20-21, 2026 at the Jatiluwih Tourist Attraction Area (DTW), in Penebel Village, Tabanan Regency — right in the heart of the iconic rice terraces recognized as a UNESCO World Cultural Heritage Site.

This is no ordinary race. Participants will run 5 kilometers along specially designed trails winding through Jatiluwih’s legendary stepped rice fields, breathing in the crisp mountain air of Tabanan, and experiencing firsthand the stunning agricultural landscape built on centuries of traditional Balinese subak irrigation practices.

ASITA Bali Drives the Initiative

The event is organized by ASITA Bali (the Association of Indonesian Travel Agencies, Bali chapter) as part of a broader push to develop sport tourism across the island. ASITA chose Jatiluwih as the venue for its extraordinary potential as both a natural tourism destination and an internationally recognized cultural heritage site.

The Bali Tourism Run 2026 is also linked to the countdown toward 100 Years of Bali Tourism, a milestone to be celebrated in 2027. Bali Governor Wayan Koster has reportedly been personally involved in the event’s preparations, setting an ambitious target of 10,000 participants from across Indonesia and beyond.

Event Details and Registration

For running enthusiasts and travelers looking to have a truly memorable experience, here are the key details:

  • Date: June 20-21, 2026
  • Venue: DTW Jatiluwih, Penebel Village, Tabanan, Bali
  • Race category: 5K Fun Run
  • Participant target: 2,000 – 10,000 runners
  • Registration: Online via runon.id
  • Early bird price: IDR 250,000 (until May 21, 2026)
  • Regular price: IDR 300,000 (from May 22, 2026)

One Important Rule: Respect the Rice Fields

Event organizers and DTW Jatiluwih management have emphasized that participants must not enter or damage the rice paddies along the route. The running trail has been carefully planned so that participants can enjoy the panoramic views of the terraces without disturbing the sacred subak agricultural ecosystem.

Boosting Tabanan’s Local Economy

Beyond the athletic and scenic appeal, Bali Tourism Run 2026 is deliberately designed as a genuine economic stimulus for the local communities of Tabanan. At least 40 local small and medium enterprises (SMEs) will participate in the event, selling locally made products such as:

  • Jatiluwih red rice — a prized local product beloved by health-conscious tourists
  • Jatiluwih coffee — robusta beans grown in the cool highlands of Tabanan
  • Handicrafts and other local culinary specialties

To directly support these vendors, each race participant will receive a shopping voucher worth IDR 25,000 redeemable at the local SME stalls. This initiative ensures that the economic benefits of a large-scale event flow meaningfully to the grassroots communities surrounding Jatiluwih.

Run and Stay: A New Tourism Model

One of the distinctive features of Bali Tourism Run 2026 is its strong encouragement for participants to stay overnight in Jatiluwih. Organizers actively recommend that runners book accommodation at local homestays and village tourism hubs nearby, allowing the economic impact to ripple further throughout the surrounding area.

This “run and stay” concept reflects Bali’s broader shift in tourism strategy — moving away from maximizing visitor numbers toward encouraging travelers to stay longer, engage more deeply with local culture, and spend more meaningfully in the destinations they visit.

Jatiluwih: A Living Heritage Landscape

Jatiluwih is no ordinary tourism attraction. Sitting at approximately 700 meters above sea level in the foothills of Tabanan, its vast rice terraces spanning over 600 hectares were inscribed by UNESCO in 2012 as part of the Cultural Landscape of Bali Province: the Subak System as a Manifestation of the Tri Hita Karana Philosophy.

The subak — Bali’s traditional cooperative irrigation system that has sustained rice cultivation for over a thousand years — is the living backbone of this landscape. Far more than a functional water management system, subak embodies the Balinese philosophy of Tri Hita Karana: the pursuit of harmony between humans, nature, and the divine.

Walking or running through Jatiluwih is, in many ways, walking through the history of Balinese civilization itself.

Sport Tourism: A Growing Global Trend

The Bali Tourism Run 2026 reflects a powerful and growing global movement. Today’s travelers are no longer content with passive sightseeing — they want to be physically present, to feel the terrain under their feet, and to return home with stories of genuine adventure.

With its unparalleled natural and cultural assets, Bali is exceptionally positioned to become a world-class sport tourism destination. Events like the Bali Tourism Run at Jatiluwih represent concrete steps toward realizing that potential.

Whether you plan to race or simply want to experience the energy of Jatiluwih Festival VII, mark your calendar: June 20-21, 2026, Jatiluwih, Tabanan. An unforgettable run across one of humanity’s great living landscapes awaits.
