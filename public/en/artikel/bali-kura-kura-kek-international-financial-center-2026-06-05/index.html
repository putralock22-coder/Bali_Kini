Bali Eyes Dubai & Singapore: Kura Kura SEZ Set to Become Indonesia's Financial Hub

Kura Kura Bali Special Economic Zone on Serangan Island is being prepared as Indonesia Financial Center with a Rp104.4 trillion investment target and a 0% tax incentive.

Bali is making headlines once again — this time not just as a world-class tourist paradise, but as a contender for the title of Southeast Asia’s next major international financial center. The Kura Kura Bali Special Economic Zone (SEZ), located on Serangan Island in Denpasar, is being positioned by the Indonesian government to become the Indonesia Financial Center (IFC) — a bold ambition designed to rival the financial dominance of Dubai and Singapore.

What Is Kura Kura Bali SEZ?

Kura Kura Bali SEZ is a 498-hectare special economic zone situated on Serangan Island, Denpasar City. Managed by PT Bali Turtle Island Development (BTID), the zone was originally designed to integrate premium tourism, creative industry, and sustainable development. It is now being steered toward becoming a full-scale international financial hub.

As of Q1 2026, Kura Kura Bali SEZ has recorded:

  • Rp1.62 trillion in realized investment
  • 2,146 workers directly employed
  • Only about 15% of the land developed so far, indicating enormous room for growth

Indonesia Financial Center: Acting on President Prabowo’s Directive

The concept of transforming Kura Kura SEZ into the Indonesia Financial Center emerged as a direct response to President Prabowo Subianto’s directive to strengthen Indonesia’s global economic competitiveness. Coordinating Minister for the Economy Airlangga Hartarto personally visited the site to assess its readiness as a dedicated financial zone.

The Financial Services Authority (OJK) is actively drafting the regulatory framework needed to establish a Special Economic Zone for the financial sector in Bali. These regulations are designed to accommodate the full range of requirements for a world-class financial center — from governance structures to investor-attracting facilities.

The Key Draw: Zero Percent Tax Incentives

Perhaps the most compelling offer on the table for prospective investors is a zero percent tax incentive — the very policy that has long underpinned the competitive advantage of the Dubai International Financial Centre (DIFC) and Singapore’s financial district in attracting multinational financial institutions.

With this incentive in place, the government hopes to attract:

  • International investment banks
  • Global asset management firms
  • International Islamic finance institutions
  • Fintech and financial technology companies

Rp104.4 Trillion Investment Target by 2052

The government has set a total investment target of Rp104.4 trillion for Kura Kura Bali SEZ upon its full operation in 2052. Beyond that, the zone is projected to contribute a cumulative foreign exchange earning of Rp477 trillion by the same year — a figure that underscores the enormous economic potential locked within Serangan Island.

The scale of this ambition firmly places Kura Kura Bali SEZ in the category of a national strategic project that will shape Indonesia’s economic landscape for generations to come.

Parliament Approves, But Urges Sustainability

Commission VII of the House of Representatives (DPR RI) conducted a working visit to Kura Kura Bali SEZ on May 4, 2026. Deputy Chair Evita Nursanty praised the development as a promising model for integrated zone development across Indonesia.

However, lawmakers also flagged several critical concerns:

1. Independent Water Management

The DPR pushed for Kura Kura SEZ’s management to establish a sustainable, self-sufficient water management system, given Bali’s increasingly serious groundwater crisis. Large-scale development without an independent water solution risks exacerbating the island’s water stress.

2. Protecting Bali’s Local Identity

Despite welcoming large investment, legislators emphasized that Bali’s local identity must not be eroded. All development must continue to respect adat customs, traditional arts, sacred sites, and cultural heritage that form the true soul of the Island of the Gods.

3. Community Involvement

Commission VII stressed the importance of actively involving local communities at every stage of development — from planning to operations — so that economic benefits genuinely reach the Balinese people.

Academic Voices: Beware of Inequality and Environmental Risk

Not everyone is celebrating. Academics have raised concerns about social inequality that could arise if the IFC development is not accompanied by robust equity-focused policies. Property prices in the surrounding areas are expected to surge, potentially displacing middle and lower-income communities.

Environmental risks are also a serious concern. The coastal ecosystem and marine biodiversity of Serangan Island could face significant pressure from large-scale reclamation and construction. Strict and consistent environmental impact assessment (AMDAL) standards must be enforced throughout the development process.

Bali: From Tourist Island to Financial Island?

If the IFC vision at Kura Kura Bali SEZ is successfully realized, Bali will achieve a remarkable transformation in its modern history — from a world-famous holiday destination to an international financial powerhouse. The blend of natural beauty, cultural richness, and cutting-edge financial infrastructure could position Bali as a truly unique and unrivaled destination on the global stage.

However, the success of this project depends heavily on the government’s and developer’s ability to balance economic ambition with environmental sustainability and cultural preservation — the very qualities that have always made Bali irresistible to the world.
