Bali Tackles Uluwatu Traffic Chaos: New Flow System Launched June 2026

Badung Transportation Agency officially launched a traffic management trial in the Pecatu-Uluwatu area on June 2, 2026. Restrictions run daily from 2 PM to 10 PM WITA, with 24 officers stationed at 6 strategic points.

The chronic traffic congestion that has long plagued the road to Uluwatu is finally receiving serious attention. Badung Regency’s Transportation Agency (Dishub) officially launched a traffic management trial in the Pecatu area, South Kuta, beginning Tuesday, June 2, 2026. This marks the first concrete government action to address gridlock that has routinely trapped tourists and local residents in jams lasting more than three hours on roads less than 20 kilometres long.

Why Is Uluwatu Always Congested?

Uluwatu and Pecatu rank among the most visited destinations in South Bali. Every late afternoon and evening, thousands of visitors flock to the area for two headline attractions: the breathtaking cliff-top sunset at Pura Luhur Uluwatu, and the iconic Kecak Fire Dance performed there at dusk.

The resulting surge in vehicle volume creates severe bottlenecks, particularly along Jalan Toya Ning and Jalan Belimbing Sari — the two main arteries connecting the area. The Head of Badung’s Transportation Agency acknowledged that congestion in the Uluwatu-Pecatu corridor is “a very serious problem”, with visitors and locals alike sometimes stuck for over three hours on a route that should take under 20 minutes.

How the Traffic Trial Works

The Dishub Badung trial introduces several technical measures:

  • 6 traffic control points along the main Pecatu-Uluwatu corridor
  • Operating hours: restrictions in force daily from 2:00 PM to 10:00 PM WITA
  • Access closures and restrictions at congestion hotspots during peak hours
  • 24 Dishub Badung officers deployed in two shifts:
    • First shift: 12 officers from 2:00–6:00 PM WITA
    • Second shift: 12 officers from 6:00–10:00 PM WITA

The 2:00–10:00 PM window was chosen specifically because it aligns with the area’s peak period — when tourists begin arriving to witness the sunset and the Kecak performances typically staged at dusk.

Trial Duration and Evaluation

The trial is scheduled to run for 14 days from its June 2 launch date. However, it may be extended to one month depending on field results. Dishub Badung will monitor the scheme’s effectiveness regularly and make adjustments as needed.

What the Trial Aims to Achieve

The traffic management scheme targets:

  • Clearing vehicle queues at the main congestion points
  • Shorter travel times for tourists heading to Uluwatu
  • More orderly traffic flow, especially during peak hours
  • Improved road safety on the narrow lanes of the Pecatu area

Longer-Term Infrastructure Plans

The traffic trial is one part of a broader effort to address congestion in South Bali. Badung Regency has also finalised the boundaries for the final three sections of the long-awaited South Ring Road — an ambitious infrastructure project that will link Balangan Beach to Jimbaran. Construction is targeted to begin during 2026–2027.

In addition, the regency government has allocated IDR 20 billion to revitalise Bingin Beach — one of Uluwatu’s most iconic surf spots. Plans include a cultural performance stage and a new 3-metre-wide cliff stairway to improve visitor access up and down the dramatic cliffside.

Tips for Visitors

If you’re planning a trip to Uluwatu in the coming weeks, here are some practical pointers:

  1. Arrive early — aim to be in the area before 1:00 PM WITA to avoid the restrictions
  2. Use a motorbike if possible — they’re more flexible at traffic control points
  3. Check the latest updates from Dishub Badung for which access points are open or closed
  4. Consider shuttle services offered by some resort and venue operators in the area

Uluwatu remains one of Bali’s must-see destinations — with its dramatic cliff views over the Indian Ocean and spellbinding sunset Kecak performance. This traffic trial is hoped to be a meaningful step toward making the experience more comfortable and efficient for everyone who makes the journey.
